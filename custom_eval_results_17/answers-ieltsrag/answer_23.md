### Structured Workflow for Resource Optimization  
At the core of Decision Lens's effectiveness is its **structured four-stage workflow**, which encompasses **Requirements Intake**, **Mission Prioritization**, **Budgeting & Funding**, and **Resource Capacity**. This systematic approach allows organizations to gather and analyze data on various projects and initiatives, ensuring that all relevant factors are considered during the decision-making process. Each stage is designed to build upon the previous one, creating a cohesive framework that facilitates informed investment decisions.

1. **Requirements Intake** involves collecting data on potential projects and initiatives, allowing organizations to understand the needs and expectations of stakeholders. This initial stage sets the foundation for effective prioritization.
   
2. **Mission Prioritization** enables organizations to evaluate the strategic importance of each initiative. By aligning projects with organizational goals, Decision Lens helps ensure that resources are directed toward the most impactful investments.

3. **Budgeting & Funding** focuses on the financial aspects of resource allocation. This stage allows organizations to assess the costs associated with each initiative and determine how best to allocate limited financial resources.

4. **Resource Capacity** evaluates the organization's ability to execute the prioritized initiatives. This stage ensures that the selected projects are feasible given the available resources, thereby minimizing the risk of overcommitment.
