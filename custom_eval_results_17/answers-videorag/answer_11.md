### Criteria Used by the National Park Service in Prioritizing Environmental Cleanup Projects

The National Park Service (NPS), through its Environmental Compliance and Cleanup Division (ECCD), utilizes a comprehensive hierarchical model for prioritizing environmental cleanup projects. This structured approach ensures that resources are allocated efficiently and effectively, addressing a range of environmental risks and maintaining the integrity of national park lands.

#### Goals and Subcriteria

The criteria are organized around several key goals, which include:

1. **Protecting Human Health and Safety**: One of the primary concerns for the NPS is ensuring that the cleanup efforts protect both visitors and park staff from environmental hazards. Projects that directly mitigate risks to human health are prioritized.

2. **Enhancing Access to Public Lands**: Projects aimed at improving public access to national parks are also given priority. This includes removing hazards that could impede visitor access or create unsafe conditions.

3. **Stimulating the Economy**: Cleanup projects that contribute to local and park economies can influence prioritization. Economic stimulus can arise from increased visitation or improved park facilities, which benefit local communities.

4. **Reducing Negative Impacts on Park Management**: Projects are also assessed based on their potential to reduce adverse effects on park management. This includes limiting ongoing operational costs tied to environmental hazards.

5. **Addressing Climate Change**: Consideration is given to how cleanup efforts can contribute to broader climate goals. This may involve remediating sites to enhance resilience against climate impacts.

Each of these goals is supported by specific subcriteria, which can include the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions and improving visitor safety, among others.

#### Source Policies and Evaluation Metrics

Additionally, the criteria are supported by a range of source policies that guide the overall assessment and decision-making process. This multifaceted approach ensures that not only is the immediate environmental concern addressed, but also that considerations for human health, economic impacts, and long-term sustainability are taken into account. 

The NPS also employs tools such as the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), which allows for the weighing and comparison of different projects based on their individual merits and how they fulfill the established criteria. This systematic method enhances transparency and inclusivity in the decision-making process, involving stakeholders and prioritizing based on both immediate and long-term benefits.

#### Conclusion

Through these comprehensive criteria, the National Park Service aims to ensure that environmental cleanup efforts are effectively prioritized, balancing the need for immediate action with broader social, economic, and environmental benefits. By focusing on protecting health, improving access, stimulating economies, and addressing climate challenges, the NPS underscores its commitment to the preservation of public lands. The structured prioritization process also reflects an ongoing effort to adapt and respond to emerging environmental challenges.
