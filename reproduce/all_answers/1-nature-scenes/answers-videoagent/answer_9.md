Arctic wolves and cheetahs have different hunting strategies due to their distinct habitats and prey. Arctic wolves, which live in packs, often rely on teamwork and endurance to pursue caribou over long distances in the Arctic tundra. They use coordinated efforts to isolate and exhaust their prey. In contrast, cheetahs are solitary hunters that rely on their incredible speed and agility to chase down prey in short bursts on the African savannah. Cheetahs typically hunt smaller, faster animals like gazelles rather than caribou, which are not native to their habitat.
