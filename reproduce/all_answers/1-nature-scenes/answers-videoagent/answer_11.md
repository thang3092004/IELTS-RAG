The symbiotic relationship between saddleback clownfish and carpet anemones benefits both species in several ways. The clownfish gain protection from predators by living among the anemone's stinging tentacles, which they are immune to. In return, the clownfish help the anemone by providing nutrients through their waste, improving water circulation around the anemone, and sometimes luring prey closer to the anemone. This mutualistic relationship enhances the survival and well-being of both species.
