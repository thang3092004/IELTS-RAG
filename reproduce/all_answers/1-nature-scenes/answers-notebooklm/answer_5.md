Army ants are formidable hunters that operate as a collective unit, exhibiting remarkable coordination and adaptability.

**Collective Action and Sensory System:**

* Army ants function as a "superorganism," with the colony acting as a single entity rather than a collection of individuals.
* Their sensory system comprises 2 million antennae, enabling them to effectively detect and respond to their surroundings.
* This intricate network of senses allows for rapid communication and coordinated movement throughout the colony.

**Hunting Strategies and Prey Subjugation:**

* Army ants employ a "vast search party" that advances into uncharted territory, systematically seeking out any signs of life.
* Unlike other ant species that utilize scouts, army ants rely on their collective sensory input to locate prey.
* Their hunting technique involves physically contacting their target to initiate an attack.
* Once prey is detected, it is swiftly immobilized and overwhelmed by the sheer number of ants.
* The ants dismantle their prey by dismembering it at its joints.
* The intensity of the attack escalates in response to the prey's struggles, ensuring efficient subjugation.

**Overcoming Obstacles:**

* Army ants demonstrate resilience and adaptability when confronted with obstacles.
* Notably, they exhibit a unique behavior when faced with rising floodwaters.
* In such situations, the ants link their legs together, forming a "living raft."
* Each ant's body is covered in fine hairs that trap air, providing buoyancy for the entire raft.
* This collective action allows the colony to navigate flooded areas, protecting the vulnerable members, including larvae and the queen, positioned at the raft's center.
