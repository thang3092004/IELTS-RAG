Darwin's bark spiders are known for building the **world's largest orb webs**, which can reach up to 2 meters wide. They construct these webs using a remarkable strategy.

* **The female spider sprays strands of silk in a long, continuous flow**.
* **These threads fan out like a sail, drifting on air currents blowing across the water**.
* **She crimps the strands together every few seconds to control their spread**.
* **The breeze carries the threads, forming a single line, creating a 25-meter bridge**.
* **She reinforces the bridge, as her web will hang from it**.
* **With the bridge secured, she constructs her trap, a large orb web, in a few hours**.

**What makes their silk unique?**

* **Darwin's bark spider silk is the toughest natural fiber on the planet, tougher than steel**.
* **This strength is necessary to span the wide rivers where they build their webs**.

Scientists are baffled by how a spider so small can produce such a large amount of silk so quickly.
