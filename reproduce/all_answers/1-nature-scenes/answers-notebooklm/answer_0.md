Flying fish use a unique ability to evade predators like the dorado and frigate birds. They are able to **push themselves out of the water and glide through the air**, sometimes for hundreds of meters with a good wind.

Here's how this strategy helps them evade specific predators:

* **Dorado:** These fish hunt flying fish underwater. When flying fish launch themselves into the air, they can escape the dorado's reach.
* **Frigate birds:** These birds hunt flying fish from the air. While flying fish are in the air, they are still at risk of being caught by frigate birds. However, by diving back into the water, flying fish can evade attacks from above.

Essentially, flying fish use the air and water as different mediums of escape, putting their predators in a "between the devil and the deep blue sea" situation.
