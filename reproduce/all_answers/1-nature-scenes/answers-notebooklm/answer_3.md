Poison dart frogs demonstrate extraordinary teamwork and memory when raising their young. The male poison dart frog places each of his tadpoles in its own tiny pool of water to keep them safe. He may have up to five tadpoles, so he must remember where he put each one. If a tadpole’s puddle dries out, the father will give it a piggyback ride to a new pool. Because he is no bigger than a human thumbnail, the father can reach places that others cannot. Although he can move his tadpole to a new location, he can’t provide food. To do this, the father leads the mother to their hungry tadpole, where she lays a single unfertilized egg for the tadpole to eat. The parents continue making their rounds for the next six weeks.
