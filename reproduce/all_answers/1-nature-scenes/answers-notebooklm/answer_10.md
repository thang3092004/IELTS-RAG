The sources describe the reproductive strategies of giant cuttlefish.

 **Females mate with multiple partners to ensure the greatest genetic diversity for their young**. They signal their receptivity to males through bands of color that sweep across their skin. After mating with a male, the female displays a white stripe along her side to indicate she no longer wants to mate with him.

**Males compete for access to females**.  Large males, like the “Goliath” described in the source, can use their size to intimidate rivals. Smaller males may use trickery to gain access to females. For example, one small male in the source mimicked a female’s coloring and posture to slip past a larger male and mate with a female he was guarding.
