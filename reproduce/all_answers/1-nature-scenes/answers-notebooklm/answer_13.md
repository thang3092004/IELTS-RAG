The silk of Darwin's bark spider is **the toughest natural fiber on the planet, tougher than steel**. This extraordinary strength is crucial for the spider's web-building strategy, which involves constructing **the world's largest orb webs, up to 2 meters wide**, over wide rivers.

 Here's how the spider utilizes its silk's strength:

* **Bridge Lines:** The spider initially sprays a continuous stream of silk that stretches 25 meters, forming a bridge line across the river. The silk's toughness ensures this line can withstand the tension and weight.
* **Orb Web Construction:** Once the bridge is secured, the spider uses the same strong silk to create a massive orb web suspended from the bridge. The web's size and strength allow it to capture a variety of flying insects over the river.

The combination of the Darwin's bark spider's silk strength and web-building strategy makes it a highly effective predator in its riverside habitat.
