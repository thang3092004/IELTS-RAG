Giant cuttlefish, the largest of all cuttlefish, live for only one or two years. At the end of their lives, as the Australian summer ends, they reproduce.  There are over 100,000 males competing for the females in a single bay. **The females mate with multiple partners to ensure the greatest genetic diversity for their young.**

Here is how this happens, according to the source:

* A large male, called Goliath in the source, mates with a female while under his protection.
* Other rival males are still interested in the female, even though a small male would not normally be a match for Goliath.
* The female displays a white stripe along her side nearest to Goliath, signaling that she no longer wants to mate with him.
* This encourages a small male to use trickery.
* **The smaller male disguises himself as a female by toning down his colors and tucking in his arms.**
* This deceives Goliath.
* **The smaller male then displays a white stripe just like the female, and is able to slip past Goliath and mate with the female.**

The sneaky smaller male then leaves, having completed his final act.
