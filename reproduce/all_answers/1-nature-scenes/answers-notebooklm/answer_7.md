The red crabs of Christmas Island face several challenges during their annual migration to the sea to breed.

* **The crabs must travel from the forests of the island to the coast, a journey that can be several kilometers long.** This journey takes them across roads, through gardens, and over rocky terrain.
* **The crabs are vulnerable to dehydration during the migration, as they need to keep their gills moist.**
* **The crabs are also vulnerable to predators, such as birds, reptiles, and other crabs.**
* **In recent years, the crabs have faced a new threat: yellow crazy ants.** These ants, which are an invasive species on Christmas Island, attack the crabs by spraying acid into their eyes and mouths. The acid blinds and confuses the crabs, making them easy prey for the ants. As a result of the ant invasion, millions of red crabs have been unable to reach the sea to breed.

Humans are working to control the yellow crazy ant population on Christmas Island in an effort to protect the red crabs.
