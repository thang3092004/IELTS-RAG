Puffin parents face numerous challenges when raising their chicks, especially with declining fish numbers.

*   **Both parents share the responsibility of raising their week-old chick (called a puffling), who requires five square meals a day.  The parents alternate fishing trips, flying as much as 50 kilometers out to sea when fish stocks are low.**
*   Good fishing spots are hard to come by and puffins often have to compete with guillemots, who also have short wings that are good for diving.  Puffins can hold their breath for over a minute and dive as deep as 40 meters.
*   **Returning parents with food are frequently robbed by Arctic skuas, who have long, narrow wings that make them faster and more maneuverable.**  Puffins have to choose their moment carefully in order to avoid being robbed and successfully return to their burrow.
* **With fish numbers in decline, many puffins now find it hard to get enough food for their chicks.  It can be even harder to be a successful puffin parent in the changing seas of today.**
