The Portuguese man-of-war is a cnidarian that floats on the ocean surface with the help of a gas-filled bladder and a vertical membrane that acts as a sail. Trailing behind it are **long tentacles armed with thousands of stinging cells**.  A single tentacle can kill a fish and even a human in rare cases. When a tentacle catches prey, it reels it in and paralyzes it. The prey is then transferred to specialized muscular tentacles that digest the catch, liquefying it with powerful chemicals. Eventually, all that is left of the prey is a scaly husk. One Portuguese man-of-war can consume over 100 small fish in a single day.
