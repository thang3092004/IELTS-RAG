Poison dart frogs are an extraordinary species of fish that inhabit the Galapagos Islands. They have evolved a complex system of care for their young, which is both fascinating and efficient. After a few days after hatching, the tadpoles are vulnerable and need protection from predators. So the adults take the tadpoles to a safe location to keep them safe. They use their bodies as a decoy to lure predators away from the young. The adults also guard the young tadpoles, ensuring they don't get eaten. They take turns fishing to provide food for the young tadpoles. The adults alternate fishing and guarding, so the young tadpoles get both protection and feeding. This system of care allows the young tadpoles to grow up safely and be ready to survive on their own when the adults return to the ocean.
