Eden's whales have adapted their hunting behavior by changing the way they catch their prey. Previously, they would swim out to catch fish directly from the water. However, with fewer fish in the ocean, the whales have found that simply opening their mouths and letting the fish jump in has become a successful hunting technique. This allows them to catch fish more efficiently. The whales no longer need to expend much energy swimming around to find fish, instead they just wait for the fish to come to them. This new strategy has been effective for them in the changing ocean environment where fewer fish are available due to agricultural pollution and other factors.
