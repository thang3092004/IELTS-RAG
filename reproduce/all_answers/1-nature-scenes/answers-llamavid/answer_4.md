Humboldt penguins face many challenges when trying to reach the sea for food in Peru. Some of the major challenges are:

1. They must cross through deep snow, which requires massive amounts of energy and strength to move through. This is especially difficult for young chicks. 

2. They must navigate through the crowded beach where there are many sealions already there and compete for food. 

3. They have to swim through the treacherous surf of the ocean. The currents can be strong and dangerous.

4. They need to avoid predators like sealions that are also hunting for food.

To overcome these challenges, Humboldt penguins have developed several strategies:

- They have an incredible ability to locate and catch fish using their beaks. This allows them to catch more food.

- They have a strong sense of smell to detect fish in the water. This helps them identify potential targets.

- They use their speed and agility to outmaneuver predators.

- They can also work together in groups to protect their young chicks from danger.

- They have a strategy of eating fish on the beach and then transporting the fish back to the sea to continue the hunt.

- They have learned to use the seaweed as a temporary protection while feeding, allowing them to rest and conserve energy.

So in summary, Humboldt penguins have developed a range of skills and strategies to help them overcome the challenges of reaching the sea for food in Peru.
