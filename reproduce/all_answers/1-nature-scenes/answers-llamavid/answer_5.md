Army ants have evolved an incredible ability to work together and coordinate their hunting efforts. They can quickly detect movement and communicate with each other to locate and capture prey. When ants detect a potential prey, they send out scouts to locate it. The scouts then bring back information to the colony and the ants launch an attack. The ants move in a coordinated manner, with some ants attacking from the front while others surround it from the sides. The ants use their powerful mandibles and stings to overpower their prey. When the prey is subdued, the ants then eat it. The ants have an amazing ability to detect movement through vibrations under the sand and use their paradoxical blindness to their advantage. This allows them to locate prey buried under the sand. Overall, the ants' highly evolved communication and coordination skills allow them to overcome obstacles and work together as a team to hunt effectively.
