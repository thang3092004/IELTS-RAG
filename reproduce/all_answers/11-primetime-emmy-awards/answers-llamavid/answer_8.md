The 2022 Emmy Awards reflected the diverse community of LA through the recognition of various nominees and presenters. For example, the Governor's Award went to Gail Anderson, a long-time LA journalist and news anchor. The nominees for Informational Programming were also diverse, including KCET's Indigenous Land Stewardship and Spectrum News 1's Pearl Harbor 80 Years Later. The presenters included representatives from Spanish-language TV stations like Univision and Telemundo, as well as English-language TV stations like KTLA and KNBC. The LA Area Emmy Awards ceremony itself was also held at the Microsoft Theater, which is located in downtown LA. Overall, the nominees and winners represented a wide range of backgrounds and perspectives, reflecting LA's diverse community.
