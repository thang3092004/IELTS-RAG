Journalists face many challenges while striving to report the truth accurately. Some of the major challenges are:

1. Disinformation and fake news: False information has become a major issue, with many people sharing and spreading false stories online. This can make it difficult for journalists to accurately report on news. 

2. Limited resources: Many journalists do not have the resources to conduct extensive investigations and research. This can make it harder to report the truth.

3. Pressure to report quickly: Journalists are often under pressure to report news quickly, which can lead to errors in reporting.

4. Lack of diversity: Many newsrooms lack diversity, which can make it difficult for journalists to report on stories from different perspectives.

5. Lack of access: Newsrooms often lack access to sources of information, which can limit the ability to report on certain issues.

6. Bias: Newsrooms can be biased in their reporting, which can affect the accuracy of the news reported.

7. Conflict of interest: Newsrooms can have conflicts of interest that can impact the accuracy of reporting.

8. Lack of accountability: Newsrooms often lack accountability mechanisms to ensure the accuracy of their reporting.

In summary, journalists face a range of challenges that make it difficult to accurately report the truth, from disinformation to lack of resources and accountability.
