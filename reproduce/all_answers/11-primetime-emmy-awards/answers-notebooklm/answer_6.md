Giselle Fernandez has had a long and illustrious career in broadcast journalism.

*   She began her career in Los Angeles at KTLA on August 11, 1993.
*   During her time at KTLA, she co-hosted a show with Sam Rubin called “Project X,” where she interviewed notable figures like Rosa Parks.
*   Fernandez is a devoted community advocate and passionate voice for those in need, tirelessly supporting organizations such as the American Cancer Society, the Hirshberg Foundation for Pancreatic Cancer Research, the American Diabetes Association, the Arthritis Foundation, and several literacy and education programs.
*   She also hosted a show that highlighted the stories of people working to get their GEDs.
*   Fernandez is the host of “L.A. Stories with Giselle Fernandez” on Spectrum News 1, where she does long-form reporting.
*   **In 2022, she won an Emmy for the “Jose Martinez” episode of “L.A. Stories,” which tells the story of an Afghanistan war veteran. **
*   She was the host of the 74th Los Angeles Area Emmy Awards.
*   **In 2022, Fernandez was awarded the Los Angeles Area Governors Award, which recognizes an individual's unique and immense contribution to television and news, as well as their service to the greater Los Angeles community.**
