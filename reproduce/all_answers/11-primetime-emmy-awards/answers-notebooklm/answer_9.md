Journalists face several challenges in their pursuit of truthful reporting.  Here are some key challenges highlighted in the sources: 

* **Maintaining mental health while covering difficult and painful news**: The constant barrage of negative news can take a toll on journalists' mental health. Viewers often express the need to disconnect from the news for their well-being, but journalists must find ways to adapt, respond, and reach as many people as possible.

* **Earning and maintaining public trust**: Building trust with viewers is crucial for journalists, especially in the current climate of uncertainty and negativity. Local news is seen as a more trustworthy source of information, making it essential for journalists to uphold this trust.

* **Adapting to the rapidly changing news cycle**:  News events are constantly changing, requiring journalists to adapt quickly and remain informed. The rise of social media and short-form content consumption presents a challenge in providing in-depth reporting in a way that captures viewers' attention.

* **Balancing courage and professionalism in covering unpredictable events**: Live coverage of unscheduled events demands courage and professionalism, as journalists often face high-stakes situations with lives in danger. This requires a unique blend of skills and composure to report accurately and safely.

* **Shining a light on injustices**: Investigative journalism, which aims to expose injustices and bring about change, can be a challenging and often slow process. Despite the difficulties, these stories are crucial for holding those in power accountable and promoting positive change.
