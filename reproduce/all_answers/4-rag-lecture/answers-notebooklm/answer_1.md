Vision-based RAG offers several advantages over text-based RAG, especially when dealing with documents containing images and tables. Here are some key benefits:

* **Simplified Preprocessing:** Vision-based RAG eliminates the need for complex preprocessing steps like OCR, layout detection, and chunking that are typically required in text-based RAG systems. Instead, it directly processes documents as images, simplifying the pipeline and reducing potential errors.
* **Preservation of Visual Information:** Traditional text-based RAG systems often struggle to extract information from images and tables, leading to a loss of crucial data. Vision-based RAG, on the other hand, leverages vision language models to directly understand and retrieve information from images, preserving the visual context.
* **Enhanced Information Retrieval:** By understanding both visual and textual content, vision-based RAG can retrieve information more accurately, particularly for queries related to images and tables. It can even answer questions like "Can you explain Figure 14?" which would be challenging for a text-based RAG system.
* **Improved Explainability:** Vision-based RAG models, like those using ColPali, offer better explainability because they can highlight the specific image regions that contribute to the answer. This transparency allows users to understand how the model arrived at a particular conclusion.
* **Reduced Error Accumulation:** Traditional RAG pipelines involve multiple steps, each with its own potential for errors. These errors can accumulate, impacting the overall accuracy. Vision-based RAG, with its streamlined approach, minimizes the number of steps and thus reduces the risk of cumulative errors.

**Overall, vision-based RAG offers a more efficient and powerful approach to document understanding, especially for documents rich in visual content.** It simplifies the RAG pipeline, preserves visual information, enhances retrieval accuracy, and provides better explainability.
