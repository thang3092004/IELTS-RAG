Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems have several limitations:

1. **Dependency on Retrieval Quality**: The quality of the generated response heavily depends on the quality and relevance of the retrieved documents. If the retrieval component fails to find relevant information, the generation component may produce inaccurate or irrelevant responses.

2. **Scalability Issues**: As the size of the knowledge base increases, the retrieval process can become slower and more resource-intensive, potentially affecting the system's performance and response time.

3. **Contextual Understanding**: RAG systems may struggle with understanding the context of a query, especially if it requires deep comprehension or reasoning beyond the surface-level information present in the retrieved documents.

4. **Bias and Misinformation**: If the knowledge base contains biased or incorrect information, the RAG system may propagate these issues in its responses, leading to biased or misleading outputs.

5. **Complex Queries**: RAG systems may have difficulty handling complex queries that require synthesizing information from multiple sources or understanding nuanced language.

6. **Maintenance and Updates**: Keeping the knowledge base up-to-date is crucial for the system's accuracy, which can be challenging and resource-intensive, especially in rapidly changing domains.

7. **Limited Creativity**: While RAG systems can generate responses based on retrieved information, they may lack the creativity and flexibility of purely generative models in producing novel or imaginative content.
