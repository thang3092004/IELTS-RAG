Keyword-based search mechanisms like BM25 play a crucial role in improving Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) performance by efficiently retrieving relevant documents or passages from a large corpus. BM25, a probabilistic information retrieval model, ranks documents based on the query terms' presence and frequency, considering factors like term frequency, document length, and inverse document frequency. By providing a robust ranking of relevant documents, BM25 enhances the quality of the retrieved context, which is then used by the generative model in RAG to produce more accurate and contextually relevant responses. This synergy between retrieval and generation components is essential for tasks requiring access to external knowledge, such as question answering and summarization.
