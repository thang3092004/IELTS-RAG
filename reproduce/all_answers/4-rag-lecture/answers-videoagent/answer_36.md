'Late chunking' refers to the process of dividing data into chunks at a later stage, often during or after retrieval, rather than at the time of data storage. 

Potential benefits of late chunking include:
1. Improved retrieval effectiveness: By chunking data based on the specific query or retrieval context, it can lead to more relevant and precise results.
2. Flexibility: It allows for dynamic chunking strategies that can be adapted to different types of queries or retrieval tasks.

Potential drawbacks of late chunking include:
1. Increased computational cost: Performing chunking at retrieval time can be computationally expensive, as it requires additional processing during query execution.
2. Latency: It may introduce delays in retrieval times, as the chunking process needs to be completed before the data can be fully retrieved and presented.

Overall, the choice between early and late chunking depends on the specific requirements and constraints of the retrieval system in use.
