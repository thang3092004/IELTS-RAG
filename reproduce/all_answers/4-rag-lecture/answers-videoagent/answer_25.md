The concept of 'late chunking' in Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) challenges traditional notions of chunking by suggesting that information should be chunked or segmented later in the processing pipeline, rather than at the initial stages. Traditional chunking involves breaking down information into smaller, manageable pieces early on, which can limit flexibility and adaptability. 'Late chunking' proposes that keeping information in larger, more holistic forms until later stages allows for more dynamic and context-sensitive processing, potentially leading to more accurate and relevant retrieval and generation of information.
