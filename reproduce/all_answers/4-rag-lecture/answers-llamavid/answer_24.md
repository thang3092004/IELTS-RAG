Using vision-language models like Quin-2 for local multimodal RAG implementations has some limitations that should be considered. First, the models require substantial resources, including high amounts of VRAM and computational power. This limits the number of documents and chunks that can be processed, especially for long documents. Second, the models are not as powerful as the latest cloud-based models, so they may not be able to extract all the information needed for a complete answer. Finally, the models may struggle with complex documents that contain a mix of text and images, making them less suitable for multimodal RAG. Overall, while vision-language models can be useful for RAG, they should be used in conjunction with other techniques to provide the best results.
