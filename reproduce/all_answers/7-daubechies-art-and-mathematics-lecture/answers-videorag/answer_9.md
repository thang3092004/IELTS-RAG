Ingrid Daubechies, a renowned mathematician with a focus on image analysis and art conservation, expressed several motivations for her engagement in collaborations with art historians and conservators during her presentations. 

### Passion for Interdisciplinary Work
One of Daubechies' primary motivations is her passion for the interdisciplinary nature of combining mathematics with art conservation. She has repeatedly emphasized the crucial role that mathematical tools and methodologies can play in preserving and analyzing artworks. During her presentations, she noted how her mathematical expertise allows her to contribute meaningfully to the field of art conservation, and she often aimed to demonstrate how these artistic practices can benefit from mathematical insight.

### Collaborative Synergy
Daubechies also highlighted the importance of collaboration with experts in art history and conservation. She noted that working together can lead to innovative solutions and enhanced understanding. For instance, her previous collaborations included projects with the North Carolina Museum of Art, where she was able to apply her mathematical methods to real-world art conservation challenges. This synergy benefits not only the artworks in question but also enriches the academic and practical experiences of all the professionals involved.

### Dedication to Preservation and Restoration
Another significant motivation expressed by Daubechies is her dedication to the preservation and restoration of historical artworks. The opportunity to engage in projects that aim to restore damaged pieces of art or to reconstruct missing elements, such as in the Ghissi project, greatly appeals to her. She has recognized the collaborative efforts that aim to reunite fragmented artworks and enhance our understanding of art history through scientific approaches to art conservation.

### Contribution to Art History
Ingrid Daubechies also conveyed a desire to contribute to the wider field of art history through her work. By applying mathematical algorithms to help art historians authenticate works and analyze styles, she aims to deepen the appreciation and knowledge surrounding many artworks, thus enriching the cultural landscape.

Through her engaging presentations, Daubechies has effectively communicated her motivations in fostering relationships with art historians and conservators, showing a commitment to furthering both the scientific and artistic endeavors of these fields.
