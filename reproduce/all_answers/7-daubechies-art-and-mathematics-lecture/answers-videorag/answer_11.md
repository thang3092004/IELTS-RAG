The mathematical concept employed by Ingrid Daubechies to analyze variations in scale and detail within Van Gogh's brushstrokes is known as **wavelet decomposition**. This technique is particularly effective in examining information locally at different scales, which is crucial in understanding the intricacies present in artworks such as Van Gogh's paintings.

### Understanding Wavelet Decomposition

Wavelet decomposition allows for the breakdown of signals (or images, in the case of artwork) into constituent parts at various frequencies. This means that it can capture both the coarse and fine details of the visual data. In Daubechies’ application, the technique involves taking images of Van Gogh's works and analyzing them in terms of pixel values. By manipulating the scale of the image and examining how details emerge or diminish when the image is blurred, researchers can glean insights into the artist's brushwork and texture.

### Application in Art Analysis

In the case of Van Gogh, Daubechies illustrated how a sharp image could be transformed into a blurred one, allowing for a quantitative analysis of different levels of texture and detail. The method involves comparing nuanced changes that occur at each scale of detail, which helps in understanding the characteristic brushstrokes and techniques Van Gogh employed. This mathematical approach not only supports art conservation efforts but also enhances the appreciation and interpretation of the artwork through a scientific lens.

By using wavelet analysis, Daubechies combines artistic inquiry with mathematical rigor, showcasing an innovative intersection of the two fields that permits a deeper exploration of historical art techniques and their implications.
