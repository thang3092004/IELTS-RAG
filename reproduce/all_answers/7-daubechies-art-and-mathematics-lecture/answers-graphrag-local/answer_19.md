### Concerns Regarding the Reconstruction of the Gisi Altarpiece

The North Carolina Museum of Art (NCMA) faced several concerns during the initial stages of reconstructing the Gisi Altarpiece, which is a significant medieval artwork comprised of several panels depicting religious narratives.

#### Historical Integrity of the Artwork

One primary concern was maintaining the historical integrity of the artwork during the reconstruction process. The museum wanted to ensure that any reconstructed pieces remained faithful to the original artistic intent and historical context of the Gisi Altarpiece. This involved challenging discussions about how to approach the missing panel, which has been absent for a considerable time. Experts, including those from NCMA, were engaged in investigating historical accuracy, considering how the original artist, Francesco di Vanni Ghissi, may have approached the work.

#### Authenticity and Representation Issues

Another concern was the authenticity and representation of the reconstructed panels. The museum highlighted the importance of distinguishing between the original medieval paintings and any newly created pieces. This involved critical dialogue about the methodologies used in reconstruction, especially in how to authentically represent the missing elements of the Altarpiece while ensuring that the new additions did not mislead viewers regarding what is original versus what is restored or newly created.

#### Collaboration and Resource Sharing

Additionally, the collaborative nature of projects like the Gisi reconstruction required a delicate balance of resources and knowledge-sharing between various institutions, including Duke University and other museums. This inter-institutional collaboration was essential but also raised concerns about aligning different methodologies and standards for restoration work among the contributors.

#### Educational and Public Engagement

Lastly, NCMA had to consider how the reconstruction efforts would impact public engagement and education. The museum aimed to create a dialogue with the audience surrounding the significance of the Altarpiece and the complexities involved in art restoration. Communicating these nuances effectively to the public without oversimplifying the complexities of art reconstruction was crucial to maintain educational integrity.

### Conclusion

The reconstruction of the Gisi Altarpiece by the North Carolina Museum of Art raised substantial concerns about the preservation of historical integrity, authenticity, inter-institutional collaboration, and effective public engagement. Addressing these issues was vital for the museum to not only restore an important piece of art history but also to foster a deeper understanding of the art conservation process among audiences and stakeholders alike.
