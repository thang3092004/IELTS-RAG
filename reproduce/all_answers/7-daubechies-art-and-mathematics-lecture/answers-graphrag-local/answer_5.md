## Collaboration Overview

Ingrid Daubechies, a prominent mathematician associated with Duke University, plays a significant role in advancing the intersection of mathematics and art conservation through her collaboration with the North Carolina Museum of Art. This partnership exemplifies how academic research can be integrated with practical applications in art preservation, showcasing a blend of scientific methodology and cultural heritage.

## Key Initiatives

One of the notable projects arising from this collaboration is the **Ghissi Project**, which focuses on the virtual aging and rejuvenation of paintings. This initiative highlights how mathematical techniques can be applied to analyze and restore artworks, particularly in the context of preserving the Ghissi Altarpiece, which resides at the North Carolina Museum of Art. The project emphasizes the importance of interdisciplinary approaches in addressing challenges faced in the field of art conservation.

In addition to the Ghissi Project, the collaboration facilitates ongoing discussions and educational programs that enhance public understanding of the relationship between mathematics and art. Through various events and presentations, including the involvement of Daubechies and other mathematicians, the partnership amplifies the role of mathematics in enriching the practices of art conservation.

## Educational Impact

The collaboration extends beyond research and technical applications; it also encompasses educational outreach. By engaging with audiences through lectures and workshops, Daubechies and her colleagues aim to demystify complex mathematical concepts and illustrate their relevance in the context of art. This initiative seeks to stimulate interest and excitement around both fields, inspiring younger generations to appreciate the interplay between mathematics and artistic expression.

## Conclusion

Ingrid Daubechies's collaboration with the North Carolina Museum of Art represents a fruitful intersection of academic research and cultural preservation. Through initiatives like the Ghissi Project and various educational efforts, this partnership seeks to foster a deeper understanding and appreciation of how mathematics can significantly contribute to the field of art conservation.
