Ingrid Daubechies, a prominent mathematician, has articulated a variety of motivations for engaging in collaborations with art historians and conservators, emphasizing the interdisciplinary nature of her work. 

### Intersection of Mathematics and Art

One of Daubechies' primary motivations is to explore the intersection of mathematics with art history and conservation techniques. She has highlighted the relevance of mathematical analysis in understanding art, particularly in areas like style attribution, restoration methodologies, and art preservation practices. By collaborating with art historians, she aims to apply mathematical concepts to enhance the analytical tools available for art conservation, supporting efforts to analyze and restore artworks more effectively.

### Educational Endeavors

Engaging in collaborations also serves educational purposes. Daubechies is passionate about expanding educational outreach, particularly in how mathematical principles can inform and improve practices in the arts. She often discusses the importance of creating accessible tools for conservators, such as a user-friendly software package aimed at facilitating the restoration and maintenance of artworks. This commitment to education underlines her desire to make complex mathematical methods more approachable for those working in art conservation.

### Shared Goals in Cultural Preservation

Daubechies recognizes that both mathematicians and conservators share common goals in preserving cultural heritage. By working together, they can leverage their distinct expertise to confront challenges in art conservation, such as crack analysis and color rejuvenation. This collaborative effort not only fosters innovation but also ensures the integrity and longevity of artworks for future generations.

### Interdisciplinary Collaboration

Daubechies promotes interdisciplinary collaboration as a source of innovation. She believes that combining insights from mathematics with historical and technical knowledge from art conservators can lead to novel solutions that benefit both fields. Her approach often includes exploring complex problems, such as analyzing the effects of aging on paintings or developing mathematical algorithms for image analysis in restoration projects.

### Impact on Community and Research

Finally, Daubechies expresses a desire to impact both the academic community and the public through her collaborations. By merging the rigorous analytical methods of mathematics with the narrative and cultural elements of art, she hopes to inspire a broader appreciation of both fields. 

In summary, Ingrid Daubechies is motivated by the potential for mathematical methods to advance art conservation, the importance of educational outreach, shared goals in preserving cultural heritage, the benefits of interdisciplinary collaboration, and the desire to enrich both academic and public understanding of these interconnected domains.
