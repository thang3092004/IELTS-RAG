The North Carolina Museum of Art (NCMA) had several significant concerns regarding the reconstruction of the Gisi Altarpiece, primarily related to authenticity, historical accuracy, and the integrity of the original artwork.

### Authenticity and Historical Accuracy
First and foremost, the NCMA was apprehensive about the authenticity of the reconstructed altarpiece. They questioned whether the reconstruction efforts would accurately represent the original intent of the artists and maintain the historical context of the artwork. The museum emphasized the importance of ensuring that any restoration would not lead to a loss of the original narrative inherent in the altarpiece's panels. 

### Preservation of Cultural Heritage
Concerns were also raised about the potential loss of historical context due to the redesign of panels or alterations that could change the narratives depicted in the altarpiece. This reflects the broader issues surrounding the preservation of cultural heritage, as the NCMA sought to ensure that their techniques and materials align with the practices of the time when the original work was created. The integrity of the artwork must be preserved while any modifications are made.

### Techniques and Methodologies
The NCMA highlighted the need for thorough documentation and research in the reconstruction process. They were particularly focused on the methods and materials used in restoration, stressing the importance of employing appropriate techniques that honor the original craftsmanship. This includes a commitment to both modern interventions and traditional methods, balancing preservation with the desire for a faithful representation of the artwork.

### Future Preservation Implications
Another concern involved the long-term implications of contemporary reconstruction methods on the altarpiece's preservation. The museum was worried about how these choices could affect future study and appreciation of the piece. Concerns extended to the complexity of reuniting fragmented components of the altarpiece, which posed logistical challenges as the pieces are held across various institutions.

### Public Perception
Lastly, the NCMA raised concerns about how the reconstructed altarpiece might be perceived by the public and scholars alike. There were fears that alterations could mislead audiences regarding the original state of the artwork, potentially diminishing its historical value and significance.

In summary, the NCMA’s concerns encompassed a multi-faceted approach to the reconstruction of the Gisi Altarpiece, emphasizing the necessity for authenticity, historical fidelity, proper methodologies, and consideration for the altarpiece’s future legacy in the art historical narrative.
