Ingrid Daubechies employed the concept of **wavelet transformation** to analyze variations in scale and detail within Van Gogh's brushstrokes. This mathematical technique is especially effective for analyzing signal frequency, as it allows for the decomposition of a signal into different frequency components. 

### Understanding Wavelet Transformation

Wavelet transformation enables a multi-resolution analysis of data, which is particularly advantageous in the context of art analysis. By examining different frequency components at various scales, this method captures both high-frequency details, such as intricate brushstrokes, and low-frequency information that represents broader patterns within the artwork. 

### Implications for Art Analysis

Utilizing wavelet transforms in the analysis of Van Gogh's brushstrokes provides deeper insights into the textures and patterns that characterize his unique artistic style. The ability to localize and analyze the frequency information can unveil the complexities inherent in his works, offering a more nuanced understanding of his technique. This mathematical framework thus not only aids in art analysis but may also enhance the appreciation of the subtleties involved in the creation of such masterpieces.
