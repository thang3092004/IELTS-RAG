Ingrid Daubechies's collaboration with the North Carolina Museum of Art (NCMA) revolves around the innovative intersection of mathematics, art conservation, and digital reconstruction. Daubechies, a prominent mathematician known for her contributions in the field of wavelets and mathematical modeling, has been actively engaged in projects that leverage mathematical frameworks to aid art historians and conservators in their efforts to restore and reconstruct artworks.

One of the focal points of this collaboration is the "Reunited" project, which aims to piece together fragments of historical artworks that have been separated over time. The initiative includes collaboration with various contributors, such as student Rachel Yin and fellow associates like William Brown and Noelle O'Connor, who are conservators, as well as David Steel, a curator at NCMA, and artist Charlotte Caspers. This multifaceted team works towards reconstructing missing panels from altarpieces and other significant works of art.

During presentations, Daubechies has highlighted the specific projects undertaken with the NCMA, particularly focusing on the Ghisi altarpiece, a historical piece that had been fragmented and scattered across multiple museums. Throughout her talks, Daubechies has described the methods used in the reconstruction process, including mathematical algorithms that assist in inferring and visualizing missing elements based on existing features of the artworks. This collaboration not only emphasizes the blending of disciplines—combining art history with mathematical insights—but also fosters partnerships that enhance the preservation of cultural heritage.

Overall, Daubechies’s work exemplifies a successful integration of advanced mathematical techniques with practical applications in art conservation, illustrating the significant role that mathematicians can play in supporting the arts and humanities.
