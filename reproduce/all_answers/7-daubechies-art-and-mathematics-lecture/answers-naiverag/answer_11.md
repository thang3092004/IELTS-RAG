## Wavelet Transform

The mathematical concept employed by Daubechies, which is instrumental in analyzing signal frequency, is known as the **Wavelet Transform**. This technique allows for the decomposition of a signal into its constituent frequencies, facilitating an analysis of variations in both scale and detail. 

### Application in Art Analysis

In the context of analyzing artworks, particularly Vincent van Gogh's brushstrokes, the Wavelet Transform enables researchers to dissect the intricacies of the painter's technique. By applying this method, they can pinpoint specific features in Van Gogh's paintings, such as textures and patterns at different scales. The process involves transforming the image data into a wavelet representation, where the details can be examined more closely according to varying resolutions.

### Insights Gained

The use of wavelet analysis provides several distinct advantages:
- **Localized Analysis:** Unlike Fourier transforms, which provide global frequency information, wavelets can capture both frequency and location information, making them ideal for analyzing textures in paintings.
- **Multi-Resolution Capability:** This feature enables the examination of details across various levels of granularity, allowing researchers to appreciate the subtleties of Van Gogh's brushwork, which might be lost in traditional analysis methods.

### Conclusion

In summary, Daubechies utilized the Wavelet Transform to analyze the intricate variations in scale and detail present within Van Gogh's brushstrokes. This mathematical approach provides profound insights, enhancing our understanding of the artistic techniques employed by the painter and contributing to the broader field of art analysis through quantitative methods.
