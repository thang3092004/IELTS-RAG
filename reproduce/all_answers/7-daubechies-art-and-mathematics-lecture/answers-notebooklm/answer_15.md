The "Munt Lab" was a **computer lab set up in the center of Padua** in the late 1990s to reconstruct frescoes in the Eremitani Church that had been destroyed during World War II. The lab contained computer workstations where **volunteers could help identify fresco fragments**.  

* Volunteers would select a fresco fragment and run a program that used circular harmonics to analyze the fragment and find possible matches within the digitized collection of fragments.
* The program would generate a table of the best possible matches, and the volunteer would then use their own judgment to select the most likely match.
* Art historians would then place the identified fragment on a full-scale printout of the fresco.

The Munt Lab played a crucial role in the reconstruction project because it allowed for the efficient and accurate identification of a large number of fresco fragments. The lab also provided a way for the community to participate in the reconstruction of the frescoes.
