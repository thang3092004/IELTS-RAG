Ingrid Daubechies employed **wavelet decomposition** to analyze the variations in scale and detail present in Van Gogh's brushstrokes. Wavelet decomposition is a mathematical tool used to analyze signal frequency by examining information locally in an image at different scales. 

Daubechies explains the process by suggesting one imagine taking a black and white picture and slightly blurring it. By converting the image to a digital format and comparing the difference in numerical values of the original and blurred images, one can isolate the fine details lost in blurring. This process can be repeated, blurring and analyzing the differences each time, to examine details at various levels. Daubechies visualized these differences using false color to highlight details. This technique allowed her to analyze not only the edges of objects, which stand out across multiple scales of blurring, but also more subtle details which appear and disappear at different levels of blurring.  

Daubechies conducted this analysis in multiple directions to gain a comprehensive understanding of the style. This process allowed her to synthesize the information about brushstrokes and details to create a kind of “statistical synthesis,” capturing the implicit style of Van Gogh.
