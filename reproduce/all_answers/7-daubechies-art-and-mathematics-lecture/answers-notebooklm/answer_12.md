The key mathematical tool used to reconstruct the Padua frescoes was the **decomposition of images into circular harmonics**. Circular harmonics are special functions with predictable behavior under rotation. This property was critical for the project because the fresco fragments needed to be placed in their correct orientation, which was unknown.

Here's how circular harmonics were applied:

* Each fresco fragment was analyzed to find the largest possible circle within it. 
* The image content within that circle was decomposed into a sum of circular harmonics. This is analogous to representing a musical sound as a sum of sine waves of different frequencies. 
* To determine the fragment's orientation, the algorithm would virtually rotate the circular harmonic representation and compare it to a black and white image of the original fresco. 
* This comparison allowed for fast and accurate identification of the correct placement and rotation for each fragment.

The use of circular harmonics, along with the dedicated work of volunteers, led to the successful reconstruction of the frescoes.
