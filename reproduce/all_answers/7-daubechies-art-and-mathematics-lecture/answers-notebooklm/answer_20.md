The Gisi Altarpiece project had a broader significance beyond the physical reunification of the artwork:

*   **The project highlighted the potential of mathematics and computer science to aid art conservation and art history.** The project used advanced image processing techniques to virtually age a reconstructed panel, making it visually compatible with the original panels. This demonstrated that technology can be used to preserve and understand artworks in new ways.
*   **The project created a multi-layered and engaging exhibition experience for the public.** The exhibition featured not only the reunited altarpiece but also a documentary about the reconstruction process, a display of the materials used, and a digital wall explaining the role of image processing and mathematics in the project. This multi-faceted approach provided visitors with a deeper understanding of the artwork, its history, and the conservation efforts involved.
*   **The project fostered collaboration between different disciplines.** The project involved art historians, conservators, mathematicians, computer scientists, and artists. This interdisciplinary collaboration led to new insights and innovative solutions that would not have been possible within a single field. 
*   **The project underscored the importance of digital tools in enhancing our understanding and appreciation of art.** The virtual aging and rejuvenation of the panels, along with the creation of a 3D model to showcase the gleaming gold leaf work, provided viewers with a unique and insightful perspective on the artwork. These digital tools offered a way to experience the artwork in its original splendor, something that would not have been possible with the physical object alone.

The Gisi Altarpiece project served as a compelling example of how technology can be harnessed to preserve, understand, and share art, ultimately enriching our appreciation of cultural heritage.
