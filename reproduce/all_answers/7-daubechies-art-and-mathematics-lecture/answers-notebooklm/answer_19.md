The North Carolina Museum of Art (NCMA) had two main concerns about the reconstruction of the Gisi Altarpiece:

*   **They were worried that even with all the panels from across the country, the altarpiece would be incomplete because one panel was missing.** 
*   **They were concerned that a newly reconstructed panel placed alongside the original 14th-century panels would draw attention away from the authentic artwork due to its bright and vibrant colors.**

To address these concerns, the museum decided to:

*   Commission a reconstruction of the missing panel from an artist trained in art history and conservation.
*   Virtually age a copy of the reconstructed panel to match the appearance of the original panels using image analysis and processing techniques.

This approach allowed the NCMA to present a complete and unified altarpiece while preserving the focus on the authentic artwork. The reconstructed panel was displayed separately in its original bright colors, along with a documentary about its creation and information about the digital processes used to age the copy.
