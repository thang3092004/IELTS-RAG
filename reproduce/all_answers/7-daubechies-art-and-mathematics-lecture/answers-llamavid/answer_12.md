The mathematical tool central to the reconstruction of the Padua frescoes was circular harmonics. Circular harmonics is a special function that transforms under rotation, allowing the image to be decomposed into a sum of smaller circular harmonics. This decomposition allowed the team to reconstruct the entire fresco by piecing together the different fragments and computing the correspondence between them. The circular harmonics decomposition was performed by Massimo Fornasier and his team, using a computer program that they developed. They used the correspondence between the different fragments to reconstruct the missing pieces of the frescoes. This mathematical tool allowed the team to reconstruct the Padua frescoes, which had been destroyed and disbanded over centuries.
