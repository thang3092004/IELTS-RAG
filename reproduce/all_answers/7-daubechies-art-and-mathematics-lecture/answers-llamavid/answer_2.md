Circular harmonics allowed the reconstruction of the fresco fragments by decomposing the image into a superposition of circularly rotated versions of the original image. This was done by propagating the image through circular harmonics functions. The circular harmonics coefficients were then computed and compared to determine the most likely match. This allowed the fragments to be reconstructed by piecing them together. This technique was used to reconstruct the frescoes of St. Francis of Assisi and St. Peter's Basilica.
