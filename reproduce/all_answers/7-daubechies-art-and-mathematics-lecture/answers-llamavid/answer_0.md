Mathematical tools can be used to analyze and reconstruct art pieces in a number of ways. For example, techniques like Fourier transforms and circular harmonics can be applied to reconstruct images from fragmented frescoes. X-rays and other imaging tools can also be used to study the composition of paintings and identify artists' styles.


---Supporting evidence---

Ingrid Daubechies discusses this in her lecture, "Bones, Teeth and Animation." She mentions how Fourier transforms were used to reconstruct images from fresco fragments, and how x-rays and other imaging techniques can be used to study the composition of paintings and identify artists' styles.
