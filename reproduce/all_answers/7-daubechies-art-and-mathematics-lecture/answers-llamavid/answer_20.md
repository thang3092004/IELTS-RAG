The Gisi Altarpiece project had broader significance beyond the physical reunification of the artwork. It demonstrated how mathematics and computer science could be used to uncover new insights into art history and artistic techniques. The project involved using mathematical tools like Fourier analysis and computer graphics to study the cracks and repairs in the artwork. This led to the discovery that the cracks in the painting were not random, but instead corresponded to specific text, which had been illegible to art historians. The project also highlighted the importance of digital preservation and the potential for mathematics to be applied in the field of art history and art conservation.
