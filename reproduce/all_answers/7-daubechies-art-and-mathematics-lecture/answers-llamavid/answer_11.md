Daubechies used the concept of Fourier transform to analyze variations in scale and detail within Van Gogh's brushstrokes. The Fourier transform is a mathematical technique that decomposes an image into its individual components, allowing for the analysis of frequency and scale. By applying the Fourier transform to the brushstrokes, Daubechies was able to identify the underlying circular harmonics and coefficients that correspond to the variations in scale and detail. This allowed him to reconstruct the brushstrokes in detail and analyze the differences between the brushstrokes of different paintings. The Fourier transform was a powerful tool that enabled Daubechies to gain insights into Van Gogh's artistic technique and style.
