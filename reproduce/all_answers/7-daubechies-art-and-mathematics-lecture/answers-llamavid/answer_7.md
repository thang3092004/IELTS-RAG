The Giotto altarpiece was virtually aged and rejuvenated using mathematical techniques. The first step was to digitize the altarpiece into black and white images. Then, mathematical algorithms were used to reconstruct the missing parts from other Giotto paintings. The missing parts were then reconstructed using the circular harmonics method. This involved computing the coefficients of the circular harmonics for each fragment, rotating the fragments, and then summing the results. Finally, the reconstructed fragments were combined into a single image. The result was a virtually aged Giotto altarpiece that was then put on display in the Giotto Chapel in Padua.
