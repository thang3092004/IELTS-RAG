The 'Munt Lab' was a computer lab that played a crucial role in the reconstruction project. The lab was used to process the high-resolution black and white images of the fresco fragments, which were then used to reconstruct the missing parts of the frescoes. The lab was set up by Massimo's former student Rick Johnson, who developed a virtual chapel program that allowed people to view the fragments and interact with them. Rick also developed a virtual cradle removal tool that allowed people to remove wooden cradles from the paintings, which was used to help reconstruct the missing parts. The lab allowed volunteers to sign up and contribute their efforts to the project. The lab's use of computer graphics and computer processing helped make the reconstruction process more efficient and accurate.
