Ingrid Daubechies discussed several motivations for her collaborations with art historians and conservators in her lecture. She emphasized the need to use mathematical techniques to analyze artwork and develop new methods to study and preserve it. She also highlighted the value of interdisciplinary collaborations in generating new knowledge and insights. Daubechies pointed out that art historians and conservators bring their own expertise and knowledge of artwork, while mathematicians bring their skills in image analysis, pattern recognition and computational methods. By combining these perspectives, she argued that new research communities can be created that can transform the world.
