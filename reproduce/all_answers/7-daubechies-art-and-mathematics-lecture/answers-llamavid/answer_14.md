The challenge of identifying the correct rotation of fresco fragments was significant for the reconstruction project. Traditional methods of analyzing individual fragments in isolation would not work, as the rotations were not uniform. 


To overcome this, Massimo Fornasier proposed using circular harmonics. He showed that if you take a black and white image and represent it as a sum of circular harmonics, then rotating the blocks by different amounts, you can compute the correct rotation of each block by examining the coefficients. This allowed for identifying the correct rotation of each fragment in the collection.

This was an innovative approach that was able to handle the non-uniform rotations of the fresco fragments, and it played a key role in enabling the successful reconstruction of the Ghent fresco.
