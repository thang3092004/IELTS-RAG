Daubechies's team addressed the challenge of 'cradle' interference in X-ray images of panel paintings by using a technique called 'cradle filtering', which involves filtering the X-ray data to remove the interference caused by the cradle. The impact of this work was significant in the field of art conservation, allowing for non-destructive analysis of panel paintings and leading to the development of new techniques for analyzing artwork, which have been used by conservators and art historians worldwide.
