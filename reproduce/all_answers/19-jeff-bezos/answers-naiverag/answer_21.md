Jeff Bezos's rationale for not negotiating the purchase price of The Washington Post is rooted in his trust in Don Graham, the former owner of the newspaper. Bezos indicated that he had complete confidence in Graham's honesty and integrity, stating that he did not require any due diligence or price negotiation because he trusted the information Graham had relayed to him. In his remarks, Bezos emphasized that he was made aware of both the positive and negative aspects of the Washington Post before making the decision to purchase it, referring to this information as “every wart and pimple” that had been disclosed to him. 

Bezos expressed that every piece of information provided by Graham was accurate, which eliminated the need for extensive negotiation or investigation on his part. He framed this decision as an intuitive choice rather than a calculated business negotiation, showcasing a belief that such an investment in a media outlet was important and worth the asking price stated, which was $250 million. Ultimately, he portrayed this lack of negotiation as a reflection of the trust he placed in Graham and the understanding he had with him concerning the newspaper’s value and potential challenges.

In summary, Bezos relied on a foundation of trust and transparency with Don Graham, which led him to proceed with the purchase without question or negotiation. This reflects a broader perspective on business dealings that values relationships and trust as core elements of decision-making.
