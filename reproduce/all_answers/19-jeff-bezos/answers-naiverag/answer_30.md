Jeff Bezos, the founder of Amazon and Blue Origin, has shared insights into his approach to managing time and energy across multiple ventures, particularly as highlighted during his discussions at the New York Times DealBook Summit. 

### Focused Structure
Bezos emphasizes the importance of having defined expectations regarding work. He suggests that while not all work may be enjoyable, obtaining a good balance where a portion is engaging can lead to a fulfilling work life. He notes the necessity of hard work in establishing a company and maintains a structured workday filled with meetings and reading to stay informed and engaged. He studies the dynamics of various enterprises and acknowledges the significant energy demands that come with running multiple businesses.

### Delegation and Leadership Philosophy
In his leadership philosophy, Bezos firmly believes that no one should be indispensable within a company. This principle extends to his approach with Amazon, where he seeks to create an environment where the organization can thrive independently, even in his absence. He regularly reflects on his role at Amazon, reinforcing the notion that the company should be self-sufficient, allowing him to focus on other ventures, such as Blue Origin.

### Personal Investment and Mission-Centric Focus
Bezos also approaches his time management and energy allocation through a mission-oriented lens. He has made it clear that he intends to invest a substantial portion of his wealth into Blue Origin, showcasing his commitment to space exploration. He segments his time between the various enterprises based on their missions and goals. As he puts it, he examines ways to achieve these missions profitably, relying on what he has termed “missionaries” in contrast to “mercenaries”—the former being those who are passionate about their work and committed to their company's cause.

### Overall Engagement
Overall, Bezos’s strategy includes a blend of structured, deliberate management of his time with a firm focus on mission alignment and organizational independence. By committing himself to achieving high expectations and nurturing a supportive environment for autonomous growth, he adeptly balances his responsibilities across multiple significant ventures.
