There are both advantages and disadvantages to using the moon as a base for further space exploration. 

Advantages:

- The moon is a convenient stepping stone to Mars and beyond. It can be used as a launching point for missions to other planets in the solar system.

- The moon has resources such as water and minerals that could be used to support a lunar colony. 

Disadvantages:

- The moon has limited resources compared to other planets. It has no atmosphere, so it cannot support life indefinitely.

- The moon is small, so it would not be able to support a large lunar colony.

- The moon is in constant orbit around the Earth, so it is not an ideal location to build a self-sustaining base.

- The moon is also a high-radiation environment, which could be dangerous for astronauts.

In summary, while the moon has some resources that could be used as a base for lunar exploration, it has many limitations that make it an unsuitable location for a long-term self-sustaining colony.
