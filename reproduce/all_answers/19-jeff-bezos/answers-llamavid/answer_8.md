Bezos's leadership style is characterized by a strong emphasis on truth-seeking and continuous improvement, which is evident in several ways at Amazon:

1. Bezos encourages a culture of experimentation and willingness to try new things, as seen in the early days of Amazon when they launched with a small amount of money and little knowledge about the business model. This allowed them to identify mistakes quickly and improve their approach. 

2. Bezos is known for his "no powerpoint" leadership style, favoring meetings with just a few key decision makers and minimal presentation. This allows for more focused discussions and debates rather than relying on data.

3. Bezos has a habit of reading books and taking time to think about decisions, which helps him make better decisions. He believes in being "the customer" and putting customer needs first.

4. Bezos has a strong focus on metrics, which allows Amazon to measure the success of their products and services. This helps them make data-driven decisions.

5. Bezos values "cadence" over efficiency, valuing a balanced life over a punishing work schedule. This allows for more time for reflection and creativity.

In summary, Bezos's leadership style promotes a culture of truth-seeking through his emphasis on experimentation, customer-centricity, and reflection, which helps Amazon make better decisions and improve their products and services.
