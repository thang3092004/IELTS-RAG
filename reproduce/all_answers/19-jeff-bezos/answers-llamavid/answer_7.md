Bezos has a reputation for being a data-driven decision-maker, but he also values intuition and gut feelings in his decision-making process. He often relies on his intuition to identify promising ideas, even when the data does not initially support them. For example, when he first conceived the idea for Amazon, he did not have the data to show that the business model would work, but he had an intuitive sense that it was a good idea. He also relies on intuition when making high-stakes decisions, like buying Whole Foods and investing in Blue Origin. However, Bezos also believes that intuition must be backed by data and analysis.
