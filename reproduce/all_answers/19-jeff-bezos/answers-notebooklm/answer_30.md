Jeff Bezos prioritizes a **growth-oriented mindset** and believes in **spending energy on a small number of high-quality decisions** rather than a large quantity of potentially lower-quality decisions.

Bezos believes **big companies have a responsibility to be scrutinized and inspected by the public**. He thinks it's important that **politicians understand the value big companies provide and avoid demonizing businesses in general**.  For Bezos, **big companies have the capacity to achieve things small companies cannot**. He prefers to **focus on customers rather than competitors** and believes this has been key to Amazon's success.

Bezos **prioritizes getting eight hours of sleep** and believes **adequate rest improves his energy, mood, and thinking**.  He **prefers to schedule high-IQ meetings, which require a lot of mental energy, before lunch**.  By 5:00 PM, he feels less able to engage with challenging topics.

**Bezos values his "puttering time" in the morning**, which involves activities like reading the newspaper, drinking coffee, and interacting with his children before they go to school. He **prefers to have his first meeting of the day scheduled for 10:00 AM**.

When managing meetings, Bezos **prefers to receive a six-page, narratively structured memo beforehand and have the meeting participants spend 30 minutes in study hall silently reading**. He sees the value in both **crisp documents and messy meetings** to allow for adequate preparation and open discussion.  **Bezos welcomes dissenting opinions in meetings and finds it unproductive when meetings have been rehearsed**.  He believes **messy meetings are a sign people are genuinely seeking the truth**.  **He feels it's important to hear dissenting opinions and seeks to uncover the points of controversy in meetings**.  Bezos likes to **be involved in the problem-solving process from the beginning** and not simply receive a polished presentation of a completely formed solution. 

Bezos describes himself as **easily influenced** but notes that a **small percentage of the time he will not be moved by any force because he feels certain about the decision**. He uses the example of Fulfillment by Amazon, which many people tried to discourage him from pursuing.  **Bezos values intimacy in personal and professional relationships**, and sees vulnerability and expressing negative emotions like sadness and fear as a way to achieve that intimacy and depth. He views **emotions as an early warning system** that something may be wrong. Bezos has found **expressing vulnerability in professional settings to be productive**.

Bezos has a **long-term vision for companies and believes they should be structured in such a way that they will outlast any individual, including the founder**. **He believes no one is indispensable** and views this as an especially important perspective for CEOs to maintain. He feels **leaders should focus on identifying big ideas, enforcing execution of those ideas, and developing the next generation of leaders**. 

**Bezos currently dedicates the majority of his time to Blue Origin, but still spends a significant amount of time working on Amazon initiatives related to artificial intelligence**.  He prefers to **work from 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM in meetings** and reads documents outside of those hours.
