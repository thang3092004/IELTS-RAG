Jeff Bezos believes that **excessive regulation hinders economic growth and innovation**. He argues that the United States has a strong foundation for growth with its natural resources, energy independence, and risk capital system. However, he feels the country is “burdened by excessive permitting and regulation”, which prevents the construction of crucial infrastructure like bridges and solar fields.

 Bezos is optimistic about President Trump’s regulatory agenda and believes it has a good chance of succeeding. He emphasizes the importance of a “growth orientation” and a “growth mindset” to address economic challenges like the national debt.  He argues that the solution is not shrinking the national debt itself, but rather outgrowing it by boosting GDP growth to 3-5% annually. This, in his view, will make the debt more manageable as a percentage of GDP.

 Bezos believes that **large companies play a crucial role in driving innovation and that vilifying them is detrimental**. He argues that certain endeavors, such as building a fuel-efficient Boeing 787 or an iPhone, require the resources and capabilities of large companies. He cites examples like Boeing, Apple, and Samsung, stating that the world would be worse off without them.

 Bezos acknowledges that large institutions, including corporations, should be subject to scrutiny and inspection, but emphasizes that this is a normal and healthy aspect of a functioning society. He believes that **regardless of regulations, companies like Amazon will continue to innovate and serve customers** by focusing on fundamental values like low prices, fast delivery, and vast selection.
