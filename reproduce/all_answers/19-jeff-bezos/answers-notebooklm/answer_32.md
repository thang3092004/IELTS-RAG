Jeff Bezos believes that **customer obsession, rather than competitor obsession, is key to business success**. He focuses on understanding customer needs and delivering exceptional experiences, rather than solely trying to outmaneuver rivals. This is evident in Amazon's relentless focus on customer satisfaction through features like one-click shopping and Prime, both of which aimed to address customer pain points.

Bezos acknowledges the existence of competition but **doesn't see it as the primary driver of his business decisions.** For example, he claims Amazon's main competition comes from physical stores, not other online retailers. Similarly, in the space industry, he welcomes competition from companies like SpaceX, viewing it as healthy for the overall growth of the sector.

His approach to competition is further characterized by a focus on long-term goals and a willingness to take risks. Bezos famously endured years of losses at Amazon while building the infrastructure and customer base needed for long-term success. This long-term vision allows him to make bold decisions like investing in seemingly unprofitable ventures, like Blue Origin, believing in their potential for future returns.

Bezos’s approach to innovation is also influenced by his view of competition. Rather than simply copying existing ideas, he seeks to pioneer new concepts and create differentiated offerings. Amazon Web Services (AWS), for example, was born out of Amazon's internal need for efficient computing resources, and ultimately revolutionized the way companies access computation. 

He encourages a culture of continuous innovation and invention, constantly challenging the status quo and seeking improvements. This "Day One" mentality, characterized by a relentless pursuit of improvement and a willingness to embrace new ideas, fosters a dynamic environment where innovation can flourish.

In essence, Bezos's view of competition shapes his business approach by:

* **Prioritizing customer needs over competitor analysis**.
* **Focusing on long-term goals and enduring short-term losses**.
* **Pioneering new concepts and creating differentiated offerings**. 
* **Fostering a culture of continuous innovation and invention**. 

This approach has allowed him to build successful businesses that revolutionize industries and create lasting value for customers and stakeholders.
