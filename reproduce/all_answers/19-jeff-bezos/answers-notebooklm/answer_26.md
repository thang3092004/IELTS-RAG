Jeff Bezos explains the significance of the escape system in the design of New Shepard, emphasizing its importance for a tourism vehicle where safety is paramount. 

**The escape system is crucial because it ensures the safety of the passengers in case of a malfunction during the flight.** Even though the booster is designed to be safe and reliable, harnessing the immense power of rocket engines in a compact space always carries inherent risks.

Here are some key points about New Shepard's escape system:

* **It employs a solid rocket motor located at the base of the crew capsule.** This motor is designed to quickly propel the capsule away from the booster if an anomaly is detected during ascent.
* **The escape system is fully automated, allowing for rapid reaction times.** This automation ensures that the system engages promptly and effectively, even if human intervention is delayed or impossible.
* **New Shepard's escape system is unique in its design.** It uses a "pusher" system where the solid rocket motor is embedded in the base of the capsule, as opposed to traditional tower escape systems.
* **This "pusher" system is also reusable.** If the escape system is not activated during a nominal mission, it can be recovered and used again. 

Bezos highlights that the presence of a reliable escape system is the main reason he feels comfortable sending people on New Shepard. He acknowledges that space travel cannot be perfectly safe, but for a tourism vehicle, every effort must be made to mitigate risks and prioritize passenger safety.
