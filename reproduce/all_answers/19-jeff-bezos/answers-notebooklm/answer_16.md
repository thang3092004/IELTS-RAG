Jeff Bezos describes the process of developing a “crisp document,” or six-page, narratively structured memo, at Amazon and Blue Origin. This document is intended to be the starting point for “messy” meetings focused on solving hard problems and truth-seeking.

**The process is as follows:**

*   **The author creates a memo, anticipating the questions of meeting attendees.** The goal is for attendees to have their questions answered simply by reading the document.
*   **The memo is written and rewritten, edited, and reviewed.** The author seeks input from others and allows them to find flaws in their ideas.
*   **The process can take as long as two weeks.**
*   **At the meeting, all attendees silently read the document and take notes in the margins for 30 minutes.** Bezos refers to this as “study hall.”
*   **Following the study hall, the meeting attendees begin a discussion.**

Bezos outlines a number of benefits of this meeting structure, which he contrasts with the problems of presentation-based meetings:

*   **The memo is hard to write, forcing the author to think clearly, but is easy to read, making it more accessible to the audience.** Presentations tend to be easy to assemble but difficult to fully grasp in the moment.
*   **The memo is narratively structured, with topic sentences, verbs, and nouns.** This makes it more difficult to conceal poor reasoning. Conversely, presentations rely heavily on bullet points, which may obscure incoherent thinking.
*   **The memo promotes vulnerability because it requires the author to fully articulate their ideas.** They must clearly state their thinking, rather than being influenced by the opinions of more senior meeting attendees. Bezos feels this is valuable because it prevents the author’s ideas from being “trampled on.”

Bezos states that the goal of the “messy meeting” is **to arrive at the truth**. To achieve this goal, it is important for the author to be vulnerable and for senior team members to speak last.

**Bezos believes that this meeting structure leads to better decision making and higher velocity.**
