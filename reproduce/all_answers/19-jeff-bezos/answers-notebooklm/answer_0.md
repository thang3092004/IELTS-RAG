Jeff Bezos’s problem-solving approach appears to be heavily influenced by his upbringing and early experiences.

*   **His grandfather was a major influence on his approach.** Bezos spent a lot of his childhood on his grandfather’s ranch. His grandfather was very resourceful, fixing everything himself, from veterinary tools to a D6 Caterpillar bulldozer.  This taught Bezos the value of self-reliance, persistence, and ingenuity, which he has carried with him throughout his life. 
*   **He draws on his experiences at Princeton.** Bezos changed his major from theoretical physics to electrical engineering after a humbling experience with a classmate, Yosanta, who could solve complex math problems with ease. This experience showed Bezos that he needed to find a field where he could excel and made him more aware of his own strengths and weaknesses. 
*   **He values and uses both intuition and analysis.** Bezos describes his decision-making process as a balance between analysis and intuition. He believes that you should use analysis when possible, but instinct and intuition are essential in making important decisions.
*   **He embraces experimentation and is comfortable with risk.** Bezos is not afraid to take risks and sees failure as a necessary part of innovation. He is willing to experiment and learn from his mistakes. At Amazon, he encourages his team to try new things and sees big wins as paying for thousands of failed experiments. 
*   **He involves others in the process.** Bezos advocates for "messy meetings" that allow for diverse perspectives and foster healthy debate, even if it means challenging his own ideas. He encourages his team to bring up dissenting opinions and fosters a culture of disagreement and debate.

Bezos's problem-solving approach is a blend of these influences, allowing him to identify big ideas, execute effectively, and foster future leaders. He sees the world as full of opportunities that are often underestimated and believes in the power of thinking big.
