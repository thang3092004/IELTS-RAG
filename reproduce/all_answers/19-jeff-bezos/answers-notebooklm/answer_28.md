Jeff Bezos believes it’s human nature to **overestimate risk and underestimate opportunity**.  He thinks leaders should be aware of this bias and compensate for it when making decisions.

Here are some examples of how this belief has influenced his leadership style and business decisions:

*   **Fundraising for Amazon**: Bezos acknowledged the high risk of failure when seeking seed capital for Amazon, telling potential investors there was a 70% chance they would lose their money. He believes his willingness to be transparent about the risks, and his own confidence in the potential of the idea, helped him secure the funding needed to launch the company. 
*   **Fulfillment by Amazon**: Bezos faced internal pushback when introducing Fulfillment by Amazon, a service that handles storage, packaging, and shipping for third-party sellers. Despite the doubts, Bezos’s belief in the potential of the idea led him to push forward with the program, which has since become a major revenue generator for Amazon.
*   **Investing in Blue Origin**: Bezos has consistently stated that he sees Blue Origin as a long-term investment, acknowledging that it may not be profitable for some time. This perspective aligns with his belief that people tend to underestimate the opportunities associated with bold endeavors.

In addition to his views on risk and opportunity, Bezos also emphasizes the importance of **optimism and energy** in leadership. He believes that leaders need to be able to inspire and motivate their teams, especially during challenging times. This belief has informed his approach to building company culture and fostering innovation.

Here are some examples of how this emphasis on optimism and energy has influenced his leadership:

*   **Day One Mentality**:  Bezos stresses the importance of approaching each day with a fresh perspective and a willingness to embrace change. This mentality encourages a culture of continuous improvement and innovation, which has been central to Amazon’s success.
*   **Messy Meetings**: Bezos prefers meetings that are open and exploratory, where participants feel comfortable sharing ideas and challenging assumptions. He believes this approach leads to better decision-making and fosters a more creative and dynamic work environment. 

Bezos’s beliefs about human nature, particularly our tendency to overestimate risk and underestimate opportunity, have significantly shaped his approach to leadership and decision-making. These beliefs, combined with his emphasis on optimism and energy, have contributed to his success in building and leading innovative companies.
