Jeff Bezos **did not negotiate the purchase price of *The Washington Post* because he trusted Don Graham's assessment of the paper's value, he did not feel the need to conduct due diligence, and he had already determined he wanted to buy the paper if it was an important institution and he was optimistic about its future**. Bezos said that after a series of conversations, Graham, whom he had known for 15 years, convinced him that his lack of experience with newspapers was not an impediment to buying the paper. Bezos decided he would only buy *The Washington Post* if he believed it was an important institution. He determined that it was indeed an important institution, noting, “This is this is an important institution it's really it is the newspaper in the capital city of the most important country in the world the Washington Post has an incredibly important role to play in this democracy.” Bezos said that he looked in the mirror to “be sure that I was optimistic that it could work” because he would not want to get involved in something hopeless. Once he determined he was optimistic about the paper’s future, Bezos decided to buy the paper.  When Graham offered a price of $250 million, Bezos accepted without negotiation or due diligence, saying that he “wouldn’t need to.” Bezos explained that Graham had already given him a detailed account of the paper’s strengths and weaknesses, and “every single thing he told me on both sides of that ledger turned out to be true.”
