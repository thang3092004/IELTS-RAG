Large language models (LLMs) have been shown to be very effective at generating natural language text, but they still have limitations when it comes to reasoning tasks. 

Some of the main limitations include:

1. They are not very good at understanding the context of their input. They can make mistakes when asked to do complex chain-of-thought reasoning tasks. 

2. They struggle with understanding the implications of their responses. They do not provide good justifications or explanations for their answers.

3. They are not very good at understanding and following instructions. They do not seem to be able to understand the nuances of language and follow complex language rules. 

4. They do not have a strong sense of self. They do not have a good grasp of their own capabilities and limitations.

5. They are not very good at handling ambiguous or complex prompts. They struggle to provide consistent answers when asked to do multiple things.

In summary, while LLMs are very good at generating natural language text, they still have limitations when it comes to reasoning, understanding the context of their input, providing justifications, understanding and following instructions, and handling ambiguous prompts.
