Current agentic frameworks have several limitations:

1. They are designed for specific tasks and use a single model. They do not have the ability to integrate multiple models that have different strengths. 

2. They do not have the ability to dynamically transfer control between models during a conversation. They have a fixed state machine that determines the flow of the conversation.

3. They do not have the ability to fine-tune the models and retrain them on new data. They are based on pre-trained models.

4. They do not have the ability to use multiple models that have been optimized for different tasks. They only use a single model.

5. They do not have the ability to evaluate the models and compare their performance. They do not have a way to evaluate the models' performance.

In summary, current agentic frameworks have limited capabilities due to their fixed state machines, single model use, and lack of ability to dynamically transfer control between models. They also lack the ability to fine-tune the models and evaluate their performance.
