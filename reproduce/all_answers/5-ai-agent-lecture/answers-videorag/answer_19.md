### Limitations of Current Agentic Frameworks

The development of agentic frameworks—systems designed to automate tasks using artificial intelligence—has made significant advances, yet several limitations remain that affect their efficacy and reliability. These limitations can be categorized into various aspects such as functionality, diversity, and integration with existing technologies.

#### 1. **Performance and Effectiveness**

Many current agentic frameworks struggle with performance, particularly in real-world applications. As noted in various presentations, many of these systems still have a lot of room for improvement. For instance, it's reported that most frameworks do not operate effectively at present, indicating that users may encounter inconsistent results when relying on them for complex tasks. The video information suggests that while many frameworks are designed to work with large language models (LLMs), they still lack reliability, achieving only marginal success in certain applications.

#### 2. **Diversity of Opinions and Approaches**

Another significant issue is the lack of diversity in the opinions presented by the agents. Using a single model or approach (for example, using the same LLM across various stages of a task) can lead to homogeneity in problem-solving methods. This uniformity may not only limit creativity but also reduce the capability of the systems to tackle complex or varied issues effectively. The probability of converging to the same solution hinders adaptation and flexibility in dynamic environments.

#### 3. **Integration with Existing Systems**

The integration of agentic frameworks with existing systems poses a challenge. Many frameworks require extensive modification of current architectures to function efficiently, leading to additional workload for developers. Furthermore, frameworks like AutoGen often demand users to implement their custom workflows and skills manually, which can be a barrier for new developers or those unfamiliar with the technology. This complexity detracts from the user-friendly nature that many tools strive to achieve.

#### 4. **Limited Learning and Adaptation Capabilities**

Current agentic frameworks often lack robust mechanisms for learning from past experiences or adapting to new situations. While some frameworks incorporate memory elements to keep track of plan execution and decision-making states, they may not be sufficient to cope with unexpected changes in task requirements or environments. This limitation presents risks, particularly in situations requiring adaptive responses, such as customer service interactions or real-time data processing.

#### 5. **Security and Safety Concerns**

Another significant limitation is the risk of security threats when agents interact with external systems or the internet. Current frameworks face challenges with malicious instructions embedded in web pages, highlighting vulnerabilities that could be exploited. Ensuring that agentic systems can operate safely in unpredictable online environments without executing harmful code is critical but remains a major concern within the field.

#### Conclusion

In summary, while the advancements in agentic frameworks are notable, challenges such as performance issues, lack of diversity in solutions, integration difficulties, limited learning capabilities, and safety concerns highlight the need for ongoing development. As these frameworks continue to evolve, addressing these limitations will be crucial for unlocking their full potential in various applications. The future of agentic frameworks will likely rely on enhancing their capabilities while ensuring they can operate safely and effectively alongside existing technologies.
