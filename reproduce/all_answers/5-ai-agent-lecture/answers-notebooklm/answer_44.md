When using smaller open-source LLMs, there are several challenges to consider:

* **Limited Reasoning Abilities:** Smaller LLMs may struggle with tasks that require complex reasoning or critical thinking. They may rely heavily on patterns in their training data and fail to adapt to slight variations in prompts or scenarios. For instance, when tested with modified versions of well-known thought experiments or riddles, smaller LLMs often fail to recognize the variations and provide inaccurate answers based on the unmodified problem.
* **Difficulty with Function Calling and Tool Usage:** Smaller LLMs may have difficulty effectively using function calling or external tools. This limitation can hinder their ability to interact with external systems, retrieve real-time information, and perform actions beyond their internal knowledge.
* **Performance Degradation with Quantization:** While quantization techniques like 4-bit can reduce VRAM requirements, they can significantly impact the performance of smaller LLMs. Lower quantization levels can lead to noticeable drops in accuracy and performance, especially for smaller models.
* **Sensitivity to Prompt Templates:** Smaller LLMs can be more sensitive to the specific prompt template used. A prompt that works well for a larger LLM might not produce good results with a smaller one.
* **Limited Context Window:** Smaller LLMs typically have shorter context windows compared to larger models. This limitation restricts the amount of information they can process and retain, potentially affecting their performance on tasks requiring longer sequences of text.
* **Susceptibility to Hallucination:** Smaller LLMs can be more prone to hallucination, generating inaccurate or nonsensical information. This tendency highlights the need for careful evaluation and error handling when using smaller models.

Despite these challenges, smaller LLMs offer advantages like faster inference speeds and lower computational requirements. They can be suitable for specific tasks and applications where these factors are prioritized. However, it's crucial to be aware of their limitations and choose models and techniques that align with the task's complexity and performance requirements.
