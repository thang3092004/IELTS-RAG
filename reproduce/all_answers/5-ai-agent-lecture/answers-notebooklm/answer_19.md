Current agentic frameworks have a number of limitations, including:

*   **Many are still in early stages of development and don't work well.**
*   **Open-source LLMs struggle to effectively perform function calling**, a key component of agentic workflows. For example, one open-source model was able to access a skill to find research papers, but it provided instructions for using Google Scholar instead.
*   **Smaller open-source LLMs struggle with agentic workflows** in general.
*   **LLMs are not good at reasoning**. When presented with slight variations of thought experiments, LLMs often fail to come up with the correct solution. This suggests that LLMs are not reasoning through the problem, but rather relying on pattern recognition.
*   **Agents that control computers pose unique security risks.** Anthropic recommends using these agents in a sandboxed environment to limit their access to sensitive information.
*   **Current benchmarks are not adequate for testing agentic capabilities**, as they primarily measure human preferences in chatbot interactions.
