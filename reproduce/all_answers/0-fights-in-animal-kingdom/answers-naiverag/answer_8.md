Caribou migration is a remarkable phenomenon that highlights their incredible adaptability and instinctual navigation skills. During their long journeys across the tundra and various terrains, these animals utilize several key navigational cues that help them maintain their course and reach their destination.

### Environmental Cues

1. **Landmarks**: Caribou are known to use prominent geographical features as reference points. This includes mountains, rivers, and other distinct natural formations that can be easily recognizable during their migration.

2. **Wind and Weather Patterns**: Changes in the weather can also play a significant role in navigation. Caribou are sensitive to wind direction, which can help them determine their movement relative to their surrounding environment.

3. **Sunlight**: While moving during daylight, caribou may rely on the position of the sun to orient themselves. This natural phenomenon offers a way to gauge direction, especially during the long days of summer when the sun does not set in polar regions.

### Biological Cues

1. **Instinctual Behaviors**: Caribou possess innate migratory instincts that drive them to travel between seasonal ranges. Over generations, these animals have developed a strong migratory pattern in response to environmental changes and resource availability.

2. **Social Groups**: Migrating in herds allows caribou to benefit from social learning. Experienced members within the herd can guide younger individuals based on their own navigational knowledge and instinct.

3. **Sensory Perception**: Similar to other wildlife, caribou have acute sensory perceptions, including olfactory cues that can lead them to food sources or water along their migration routes.

### Summary

In summary, caribou employ a combination of environmental landmarks, wind and weather patterns, the sun's position, innate instincts, social dynamics, and sensory perceptions for navigation during their migrations. This multi-faceted approach enables them to traverse vast and often challenging landscapes in search of sustenance and breeding grounds. Their survival and success in navigating these routes highlight the incredible adaptations of these resilient creatures in their natural habitats.
