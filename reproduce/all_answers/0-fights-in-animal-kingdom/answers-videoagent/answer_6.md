The development of the killer whale's beaching behavior is driven by both ecological and evolutionary factors. Ecologically, this behavior allows killer whales to access prey that is otherwise difficult to catch, such as seals resting on beaches. Evolutionarily, this behavior may have developed as a successful hunting strategy that provides a competitive advantage, allowing killer whales to exploit a niche food resource. Over time, individuals that mastered this technique would have had better survival and reproductive success, leading to the propagation of this behavior in certain populations.
