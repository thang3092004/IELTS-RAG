Mother bears face multiple challenges when trying to secure salmon for themselves and their cubs. The sources highlight the following:

* **Competition from large males**: Mother bears often have to compete with larger, more dominant males for the best fishing spots. For example, one mother bear had to cautiously approach a large male who had taken over the prime fishing spot at a waterfall. The male's presence limited her movement and access to the salmon.  She had to be assertive and eventually build up the nerve to challenge the larger male for the fishing spot.

* **Safety concerns**: Mother bears must prioritize their own safety, especially when large males are nearby. In the example above, the mother bear was initially hesitant to approach the male due to a previous encounter.

* **Limited access to salmon**: Even with plenty of salmon, the presence of a large male can hinder a mother bear's ability to move freely and catch fish effectively.  The mother bear in the example was unable to take advantage of a male salmon that the larger bear let go because she was too close to the dominant male.

* **Need to provide for cubs**: Mother bears need to catch enough salmon not only for themselves, but also for their growing cubs. The pressure to provide enough food for their offspring adds another layer of difficulty to their already challenging task.

Ultimately, securing salmon is a critical task for mother bears, and they must overcome various obstacles to ensure their own survival and that of their cubs.
