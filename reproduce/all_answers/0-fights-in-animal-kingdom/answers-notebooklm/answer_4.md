When a hippopotamus mother gives birth, she must reintegrate herself into the pod with her calf, which can be dangerous. **The mother must first re-establish her place within the pod, and during the process, a careless adult hippo could accidentally crush the calf. ** The mother must also contend with other members of the pod, such as her previous calf. One mother hippo had to stop her previous calf from attacking the new baby.  The older calf was jealous of the new baby or curious about it, and began pushing the new calf around. The new calf was frightened by the encounter, and clung to its mother. While the mother and her calves remained separate from the pod, the sounds of the encounter could be heard by the rest of the family. **Hippos communicate underwater using a variety of sounds.  The sounds are made by vibrations from their head and jaw bones that travel into their ears. ** These sounds can travel for up to a mile in calm waters.
