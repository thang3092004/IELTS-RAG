In the stony canyons of the Middle East, Nubian ibex gather at precious waterholes, especially during the summer months. The water is vital for their survival, but it also plays a crucial role in their mating rituals. The male ibex who controls a waterhole gains exclusive access to the females who come there to drink. This makes the waterholes highly contested areas, leading to dramatic fights between rival males.

The source describes one such fight, where the dominant male ibex guarding the pool senses competition when he smells the air. **To gain the upper hand, the challenging ibex must secure the higher ground.** The fight is fierce, as their skulls are reinforced to withstand the impact of head-butting. **The clash is described as a battle of equals, potentially lasting for an hour.** However, the intensity of the duel is disrupted by the arrival of a third male, causing confusion among the combatants about who to fight. The arrival of Bedouin hunters further disrupts the fight, causing the ibex to scatter due to their fear of these skilled predators.
