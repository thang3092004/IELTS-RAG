Chimpanzees have a highly evolved communication system that allows them to coordinate their roles as drivers, blockers, and ambushers in their complex hunting strategies. 

Drivers are the first to spot and pursue prey. They use a variety of calls, facial expressions, and body movements to alert other chimpanzees of the potential meal. They also use tools like sticks and rocks to create distractions. 

Blockers are the chimpanzees that cover the escape routes and try to block the prey from fleeing. They are positioned in front of the prey to prevent it from escaping. They use their strength and size to intimidate the prey.

Ambushers are the chimpanzees that hide and wait for the prey to pass by, then launch an ambush from the rear. They use their speed and stealth to catch the prey by surprise.
