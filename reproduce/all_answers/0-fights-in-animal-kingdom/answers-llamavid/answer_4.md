Hippos are known for their aggressive nature, but they manage to integrate a new calf into the group without causing harm in several ways. First, they are very protective of their young calves and will fiercely defend them against any threats. When a new calf is born, the mother hippo will guard it and even kill predators that come too close. This helps to protect the calf from danger.

Second, the adult hippos are also very social creatures that live in groups, so the calf is welcomed into the group. The other members of the herd will look out for the calf and help protect it from predators. This helps the calf survive and grow up.

Third, hippos are very intelligent creatures that are able to recognize the difference between a threat and a friendly calf. They can distinguish between calves that are just being born and those that are already part of the group. The adults will only attack a calf that poses a threat.

So in summary, hippos are able to integrate a new calf into their group through their protective and social nature, while also being able to distinguish between friend and foe. This helps the calf survive and grow up in the group.
