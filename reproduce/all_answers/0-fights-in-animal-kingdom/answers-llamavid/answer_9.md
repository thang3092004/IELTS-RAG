In the Yellowstone Valley of Wyoming, the bison herd gathers around a precious waterhole during their mating season. The male bison, known as the Ridge Pride, has been dominating this area for years. However, the youngest male bison, Flip, is determined to gain access to the females. When he sees the Ridge Pride drinking at the waterhole, Flip charges at him in a bold attempt to intimidate him. The Ridge Pride is surprised but manages to escape. Flip is determined to impress the Ridge Pride and gain his acceptance. He continues stalking the Ridge Pride, hoping to show his worth. The Ridge Pride and his pack are initially amused by Flip's attempts, but Flip refuses to give up. Finally, the Ridge Pride is impressed and accepts Flip into the pack. Flip is now able to enjoy the benefits of being part of the pack, including access to the females. This dramatic fight between the two male bison illustrates the harsh reality of the mating season and the brutal competition that exists in the animal world.
