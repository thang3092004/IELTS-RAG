Japanese honeybees have evolved a unique heat-balling technique to survive the harsh Japanese climate. The hive's brood is kept at an optimal temperature of around 46 degrees Celsius. If the temperature exceeds this, the honeybees can overheat and die. To avoid this, the hive workers dig tunnels into the soil to excavate a ball of earth. Inside this ball, the bees hang fire and fry the outside of the ball, creating a protective shell. The heat inside the ball can reach 60 degrees Celsius, while the temperature outside is much cooler. This allows the bees to keep the hive warm and safe from the cold while also protecting them from the harsh winters. The heat-balling technique is a form of insulation, allowing the bees to survive the extreme temperatures and maintain their hive at the optimal temperature. The heat-balling technique is an adaptation to the extreme climate conditions of Japan, which is why it is so effective.
