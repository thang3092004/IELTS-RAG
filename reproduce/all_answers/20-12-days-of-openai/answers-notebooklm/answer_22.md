The small number of examples needed for reinforcement fine-tuning is significant because it is a departure from traditional machine learning and large language models. With traditional fine-tuning, a few dozen examples would be insufficient to achieve good results. However, with reinforcement fine-tuning, providing just a few dozen examples allows the model to **learn to reason in new and effective ways** over specific subject areas. This is accomplished by:

* Allowing the model to process a problem.
* Evaluating the model's answer. 
* **Reinforcing** thought processes that result in the correct answer. 
* **Discouraging** thought processes that result in an incorrect answer. 

Essentially, **reinforcement fine-tuning teaches the model *how to think* instead of just teaching it to mimic the examples it receives.**
