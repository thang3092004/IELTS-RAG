The 'Storyboard' feature allows you to describe the actions and events of a video, then generate the video from that description. Here's an example of how to use it:

1. First, open the 'Storyboard' tool and create a new storyboard.

2. In the storyboard, describe the initial scene and what happens in the first action. For example, you can describe the first scene where the crane is shown standing over the water. 

3. Then describe the second action where the crane dips its head into the water and catches a fish. You can describe the visuals and sounds for this action. 

4. Next, describe the next action where the crane picks up a fish and continues on to its next destination. 

5. You can repeat this process for as many actions as you want, describing the actions and visuals for each scene.

6. Once you have described all the actions in your storyboard, click 'Generate' and Sora will generate the video from your storyboard. You can then review the generated video and make any adjustments if needed.

The 'Storyboard' feature allows you to create a visual script for a video by describing the actions and events that should be included. The video can then be generated from that script, allowing you to see the video and make adjustments as needed.
