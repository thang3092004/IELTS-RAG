Thompson Reuters, a legal research company, used reinforcement fine-tuning to improve their Co-Counsel AI tool. Reinforcement fine-tuning is a technique that uses reinforcement learning algorithms to disincentivize lines of thinking that are not effective. The researchers curated a list of symptoms that doctors are trying to figure out given a patient's symptoms what gene might be responsible. They then trained their model on this data and fine-tuned it with reinforcement learning. The researchers used a set of validation data to grade the model's answers. The model was able to generate correct answers and explain why it thought certain genes were responsible. The researchers also tested the model's performance on a new data set of unseen symptoms. The model was able to generate correct answers and provide explanations for why it thought certain genes were responsible. The model was able to generate correct answers over 90% of the time. Reinforcement fine-tuning allowed the model to learn from the data and improve its performance over time.
