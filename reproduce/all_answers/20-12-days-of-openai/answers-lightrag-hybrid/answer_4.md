Linking feedback to specific parts of writing can be quite challenging without the use of tools like Canvas, primarily because of the lack of structured organization and interactive capabilities inherent to such platforms. Here are a couple of reasons illustrating this dilemma:

### Lack of Real-Time Interaction
Without a dedicated collaborative tool, feedback on written content often occurs in linear formats, such as traditional document edits or email threads. This can lead to difficulties in understanding which comments relate to specific sections of the text, as the feedback is typically not embedded within the document itself. In contrast, tools like Canvas allow users to annotate directly on the text, facilitating clearer and more immediate connections between feedback and the relevant portions of the writing.

### Difficulty in Structuring Feedback
In a conventional setting, feedback may require separate discussions and manual adjustments to align comments with the sections they pertain to. This not only makes the process time-consuming but can also result in important insights being overlooked. A platform like Canvas provides a more organized, visual interface that helps writers and reviewers to navigate through the document while instantly accessing comments, which streamlines the process of making revisions.

### Collaborative Discourse
Furthermore, without a comprehensive digital workspace, collaborative discourse tends to suffer. Traditional methods don’t foster an easily accessible dialogue that encourages immediate response to feedback adjustments. In platforms like Canvas, participants can work side by side, addressing feedback in real time and discussing changes within the context of the written work itself. This enhances comprehension and retention of the feedback.

### Conclusion
In summary, the absence of tools like Canvas complicates the feedback process by making it harder to establish direct relationships between comments and specific text sections, reducing the clarity and effectiveness of feedback. The traditional methods of interaction lack the immediacy and organization which collaborative writing and feedback platforms offer, ultimately inhibiting the improvement of written work.
