In the context of reinforcement fine-tuning, 'graders' are mechanisms or components used to evaluate and provide feedback on the performance of a model or agent. They assess how well the model's actions align with the desired outcomes or objectives. The feedback from graders is used to adjust the model's parameters, guiding it towards improved performance. Graders play a crucial role in reinforcement learning by helping to optimize the model's decision-making process through iterative evaluation and refinement.
