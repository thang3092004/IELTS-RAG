Graph neural networks can help overcome limitations of vector-based methods in several ways:

1. They can identify non-linear relationships between nodes and edges in the graph, which are difficult to model with vectors. This allows them to find meaningful subgraphs and connections.

2. They can identify patterns and features that are not represented in the input data. This allows them to find novel information and insights. 

3. They can identify errors and non-error plasticity in the graph, which helps improve the model.

4. They can identify "shortcuts" that allow the model to quickly jump to relevant information. This speeds up the retrieval process.

In summary, graph neural networks can identify complex relationships, patterns, features, and shortcuts that are difficult for vector-based methods to model. This allows them to improve information retrieval and provide more relevant and comprehensive answers.
