The DSP framework consists of three main phases: the 'demonstrate' phase, the 'search' phase, and the 'predict' phase.

The 'demonstrate' phase is the first phase, where the large language model (LLM) is used to provide the language model with a conceptual understanding for the retriever model. It is used to demonstrate the model's knowledge of a concept in context. The model is trained on a dataset of text, and the goal is to generate a response that shows it has a conceptual understanding of the concept.

The 'search' phase is the second phase, where the retriever model is used to search for data that can be used to answer the question. The retriever model searches through a large corpus of text, and the goal is to find relevant text that can provide answers to the question.

The 'predict' phase is the third phase, where the retrieved data is used to generate a response. The goal is to generate a response that answers the question based on the retrieved data.

In summary, the 'demonstrate' phase provides the model with a conceptual understanding of the concept, the 'search' phase finds relevant data, and the 'predict' phase generates a response that answers the question based on the retrieved data.
