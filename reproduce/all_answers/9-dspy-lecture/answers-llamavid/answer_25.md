In the Raptor framework, tree traversal and collapsed tree retrieval are two methods used to search through the vast amount of data generated by the large language models. Tree traversal involves traversing the graph structure of the data and retrieving relevant information. Collapsed tree retrieval involves breaking down the data into smaller subgraphs and then retrieving the relevant information from the subgraphs.

The tree traversal method works by traversing the graph structure and generating a chain of nodes that contain the relevant information. This chain of nodes is then used to retrieve the relevant information.

In contrast, collapsed tree retrieval involves breaking down the data into smaller subgraphs and then retrieving the relevant information from the subgraphs. The subgraphs are then collapsed to retrieve the relevant information. This method is faster and more efficient than tree traversal and can handle larger amounts of data.

In summary, tree traversal and collapsed tree retrieval are two effective methods used in the Raptor framework to search through the vast amount of data generated by large language models. They allow for efficient retrieval of relevant information from the data.
