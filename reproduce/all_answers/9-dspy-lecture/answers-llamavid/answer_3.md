Current RAG systems have several limitations that hinder their effectiveness in retrieving relevant information:

1. Limited knowledge domain: RAG systems are designed to retrieve information within a specific knowledge domain, but they cannot generalize to new domains. They require training on a new domain before they can provide accurate answers.

2. Short context length: RAG systems are unable to retrieve information for questions with context lengths longer than 2000 words, which limits their applicability. 

3. Lack of logical reasoning: RAG systems lack the ability to perform logical reasoning and causal inference, which is an important component of human reasoning.

4. Lack of creativity: RAG systems lack creativity and flexibility in generating new knowledge from the retrieved information.

In summary, RAG systems' effectiveness is limited by their domain specificity, short context length, lack of logical reasoning, and lack of creativity. These limitations restrict their ability to retrieve relevant information for complex questions.
