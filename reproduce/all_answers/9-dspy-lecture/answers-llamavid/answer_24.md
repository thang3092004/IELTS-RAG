When a large language model (LLM) is processing long texts, it can get "lost in the middle" of the text, as it struggles to keep track of the context and meaning. This can lead to errors and incorrect predictions. The LLM relies on its pre-trained knowledge and context to make predictions, but may not have enough information to accurately respond to the question. In some cases, the LLM may need to ask clarifying questions or provide additional context to properly understand the question. The LLM may also need to use its knowledge of related concepts to properly respond.
