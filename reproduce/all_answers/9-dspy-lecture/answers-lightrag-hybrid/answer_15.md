## Understanding Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton's laws are three principles that describe the relationship between the motion of an object and the forces acting on it, which are essential to grasping the dynamics involved in space travel. Key principles include:

1. **Inertia**: An object will remain at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a force.
2. **Force and Acceleration**: The force acting on an object is equal to the mass of that object multiplied by its acceleration (F = ma).
3. **Action-Reaction**: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

These laws form the theoretical backbone necessary for understanding how spacecraft can navigate through the vacuum of space.
