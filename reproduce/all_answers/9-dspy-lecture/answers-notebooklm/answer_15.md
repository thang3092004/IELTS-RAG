The sources describe a method developed at Stanford University in January 2023 called **Demonstrate, Search, Predict (DSP)**, which uses in-context learning to enhance the capabilities of large language models (LLMs). One example of DSP's use was to determine the influence of Newton's laws on modern space exploration. 

This was accomplished in a multi-step process.

*   **Initial Query Formulation:**  The LLM analyzed the query to identify key components, which were Newton's Law and its applications in space exploration. It was important to determine if the LLM had existing knowledge of this domain and to ensure that relevant external data sources such as NASA databases were accessible. 
*   **Retrieval Model Searches:** Using search methodologies such as keyword or semantic search, the retrieval model gathered additional information from the sources to create a demonstration dataset. This involved breaking the initial query into two parts: “What are Newton's Laws of Motion?” and “How are Newton’s Laws applied in space exploration?”. The model then retrieved relevant information, for example,  summarizing Newton’s three laws and describing how they are used to plan space missions. 
*   **Synthesize a Complex Demonstration:** The LLM used the retrieved information to create a demonstration that explained how Newton's historical scientific principles are connected to their modern applications in space exploration. This involved the LLM generating a new synthetic dataset in response to a prompt requesting a demonstration of the influence of Newton’s Laws on modern space exploration using the retrieved information. The LLM output included an answer to the prompt as well as a reasoning path outlining the connections between concepts.
*   **Future Career Applications:** This detailed demonstration, including the multi-step reasoning path, can help guide the DSP system when answering related questions, serving as a model for constructing reasoned answers that require connecting historical developments with their contemporary applications. 
*   **Continuous Improvement and Expansion:** The process fosters ongoing learning, expanding the system's knowledge base and its reasoning capabilities.

The example demonstrates DSP's advanced capability to self-generate demonstration synthetic training data for complex multi-hop questions to improve in-context learning. This process was referred to as retrieval augmented in-context learning (RAICL).
