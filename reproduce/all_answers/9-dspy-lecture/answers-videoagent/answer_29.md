The Demonstrate-Search-Predict (DSP) methodology enhances the ability of Large Language Models (LLMs) to handle complex tasks by breaking down the problem-solving process into three distinct phases. In the "Demonstrate" phase, the model is shown examples of how to perform a task, which helps it understand the task requirements and context. In the "Search" phase, the model explores different possibilities and retrieves relevant information or solutions from a large dataset or knowledge base. Finally, in the "Predict" phase, the model uses the information gathered to make informed predictions or decisions. This structured approach allows LLMs to tackle complex tasks more effectively by leveraging demonstration, exploration, and prediction in a systematic manner.
