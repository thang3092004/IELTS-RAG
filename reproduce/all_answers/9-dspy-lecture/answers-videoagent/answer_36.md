The primary reasons for the failure of certain LLMs in accurately processing text containing embedded tasks within longer documents include:

1. **Contextual Limitations**: LLMs have a maximum context window, and when documents exceed this limit, they may lose track of important context, leading to errors in understanding and processing embedded tasks.

2. **Complexity of Embedded Tasks**: Embedded tasks often require understanding nuanced instructions or multiple steps, which can be challenging for LLMs that are not specifically fine-tuned for task execution.

3. **Ambiguity and Ambivalence**: Longer documents may contain ambiguous language or conflicting information, making it difficult for LLMs to discern the correct interpretation or task.

4. **Lack of Task-Specific Training**: LLMs trained on general data may not have the specialized knowledge or training required to accurately process specific embedded tasks.

5. **Distraction by Irrelevant Information**: In longer documents, LLMs may get distracted by irrelevant information, leading to a failure in focusing on the embedded tasks.

6. **Inference and Reasoning Limitations**: Some tasks require advanced reasoning and inference capabilities, which may be beyond the current capabilities of certain LLMs.
