Digital Signal Processing (DSP) is used in modern space exploration to analyze and interpret data collected from spacecraft and satellites. Newton's laws of motion, which describe the relationship between the motion of an object and the forces acting on it, are fundamental to understanding and predicting the trajectories of spacecraft. DSP techniques are applied to process signals from sensors and instruments that monitor the motion and orientation of spacecraft, ensuring precise navigation and control. By filtering noise and extracting meaningful information from these signals, DSP helps engineers apply Newton's laws to maintain the desired trajectory and stability of spacecraft, enabling successful missions in space exploration.
