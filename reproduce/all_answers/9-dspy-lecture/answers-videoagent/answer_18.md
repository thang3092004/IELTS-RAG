Traditional vector embedding techniques in Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems have several limitations when it comes to retrieving new research data:

1. **Static Nature**: Traditional embeddings are often static, meaning they do not adapt to new data or changes in the underlying data distribution. This can be problematic for new research data, which is constantly evolving.

2. **Contextual Limitations**: These embeddings may not capture the full context or nuances of new research topics, especially if the training data did not include similar contexts or topics.

3. **Scalability Issues**: As the volume of research data grows, traditional vector embeddings may struggle with scalability, leading to inefficiencies in retrieval performance.

4. **Semantic Understanding**: Traditional embeddings might not fully understand the semantics of complex or highly specialized research topics, leading to less accurate retrieval results.

5. **Domain Adaptation**: They often require significant retraining or fine-tuning to adapt to new domains or specialized fields of research, which can be resource-intensive.

6. **Bias and Generalization**: Embeddings trained on existing data may carry biases that do not generalize well to new or diverse research data, potentially leading to skewed retrieval results.
