Extreme multi-label classification (XMC) is a type of machine learning problem where the task is to assign a set of relevant labels to an instance from an extremely large set of possible labels. Unlike traditional multi-label classification, where the number of labels is relatively small, XMC deals with scenarios where the number of labels can be in the millions. This makes the problem more challenging due to the high dimensionality and sparsity of the label space.

A real-world example of XMC is in product recommendation systems for e-commerce platforms. In such systems, each product can be associated with multiple labels or tags, such as categories, brands, or features. With millions of products and potential labels, XMC techniques are used to efficiently and accurately recommend relevant products to users based on their preferences and browsing history.
