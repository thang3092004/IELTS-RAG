Multi-hop searches within the DSP (Digital Signal Processing) framework refer to the process of retrieving information or data by making multiple sequential queries or "hops" across different data points or nodes. This approach is significant because it allows for the aggregation and synthesis of information from various sources, leading to more comprehensive and accurate results. In the context of DSP, multi-hop searches can enhance the ability to process and analyze complex signals by leveraging interconnected data pathways, ultimately improving decision-making and problem-solving capabilities.
