The concept of "Lost in the Middle" in the context of Large Language Models (LLMs) refers to the tendency of these models to struggle with retaining and accurately processing information that appears in the middle of long texts. LLMs often have a limited context window, meaning they can only consider a certain number of tokens (words or characters) at a time. As a result, when processing long texts, the information at the beginning and end of the text may receive more attention, while the information in the middle can be overlooked or forgotten. This can lead to a loss of coherence and accuracy in the model's output when dealing with lengthy documents..
