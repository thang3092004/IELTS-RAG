1. **Context Retention**: LLMs need to retain context over long passages to accurately understand and respond to tasks embedded within them. This can be challenging due to the model's limited context window, which may cause it to lose track of earlier information.

2. **Attention Mechanism**: The attention mechanism in LLMs might struggle to focus on the relevant parts of the text when the task is embedded in a long passage. This can lead to the model missing important details necessary for task completion.

3. **Distraction by Irrelevant Information**: Long texts often contain a lot of information that may not be relevant to the task. LLMs might get distracted by this irrelevant information, which can affect their performance on the task.

4. **Inference and Comprehension**: Understanding the task often requires inference and comprehension skills, which can be difficult if the task is not clearly defined or if the surrounding text is complex.

5. **Memory Limitations**: LLMs have a finite memory and may not be able to store all the necessary information from a long text, leading to incomplete understanding of the task.

6. **Prompt Engineering**: Crafting prompts that effectively guide LLMs to focus on the task within a long text can be challenging and requires careful design.
