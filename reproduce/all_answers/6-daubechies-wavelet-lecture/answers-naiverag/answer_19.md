In discussing the synchrosqueezing transform, Daubechies highlights the technique of multi-tapering alongside the use of Hermite functions for better data analysis. The method pertains to improving signal processing outcomes by minimizing issues linked to sharp cutoffs often encountered in Fourier analysis. 

### Key Terminology:
- **Multi-tapering**: This is a technique where multiple windows (or tapers) are used instead of just one. In the context of the synchrosqueezing transform, it helps reduce variance in the estimation of time-frequency representations.
- **Hermite Functions**: These functions serve as the tapered windows in the multi-tapering method, effectively enhancing the transformation's performance in identifying signal characteristics.

### Example Illustration:
Daubechies provides specific examples regarding the use of multi-taper synchrosqueezing. A key aspect mentioned is the effectiveness of Hermite functions, which help to concentrate the analytical focus on the signal’s relevant features while minimizing noise. By employing several orthogonal windows through this technique, she illustrates that it allows for an exploration of data with maximal effect. 

For instance, when analyzing complex signals, utilizing the Hermite functions through multi-tapering facilitated the ability to isolate and refine specific components within a noisy signal. This improvement demonstrates the capability of the synchrosqueezing transform not just to analyze data but to extract meaningful information from it, showcasing substantial reductions in ambiguities that arise during analysis, especially with signals that have both deterministic and stochastic components.

Ultimately, this methodological enhancement through multi-tapering and Hermite functions results in more reliable and clearer signal reconstructions, validating Daubechies' assertion of its practical applicability in advanced signal processing scenarios.
