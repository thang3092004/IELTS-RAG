Daubechies emphasizes the use of time-frequency analysis, particularly an adaptive approach, to extract the fetal heartbeat from the electrocardiogram (ECG) of a pregnant woman. This is diagnostically important because it can provide insights into the health of the fetus, especially for those suspected of having heart problems. 

Here are some key points regarding this application:

* **Challenge:**  The fetal heartbeat is a very faint signal with a low signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), making it challenging to detect, especially as it's dominated by the mother's much stronger ECG signal.
* **Importance:** Currently, directly measuring the fetal heartbeat during delivery for babies with potential heart issues involves invasive procedures with inherent risks. A non-invasive method based on signal analysis would be safer and more convenient.
* **Daubechies' collaborator's success:**  Daubechies' collaborator, a medical doctor with a PhD in applied math, developed a method using adaptive time-frequency analysis that successfully extracted the fetal heartbeat from the mother's ECG. This method is undergoing further evaluation for clinical use.
* **Benefits:** This application highlights the potential of advanced signal processing techniques to improve fetal health monitoring and diagnostics. 

Daubechies believes this application represents significant progress in the practical use of adaptive time-frequency analysis for real-world medical challenges.
