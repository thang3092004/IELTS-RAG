The Zak transform, named after the physicist Joshua Zak, is a mathematical tool used in signal processing and harmonic analysis. It is particularly useful in the study of periodic functions and lattice structures. The transform provides a way to represent a function in a two-dimensional periodic domain, which can simplify the analysis of signals with periodic or quasi-periodic structures. Ingrid Daubechies, a prominent mathematician known for her work on wavelets, has contributed to the development and application of the Zak transform in the context of wavelet theory. The purpose of the Zak transform is to facilitate the analysis and processing of signals by transforming them into a domain where their periodic properties are more easily examined and manipulated.
