Bird songs are interesting for analysis using time-frequency methods because they often contain complex patterns that vary over time and frequency. These methods allow researchers to visualize and analyze the frequency modulation, pitch, rhythm, and temporal structure of the songs. Researchers hope to address biological questions such as understanding the role of bird songs in communication, mate selection, territory defense, and species identification. Additionally, they aim to study how environmental factors and evolutionary pressures influence the development and variation of bird songs.
