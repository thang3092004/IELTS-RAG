Different window functions impact time-frequency analysis by affecting the trade-off between time and frequency resolution. Common window functions include rectangular, Hamming, Hanning, and Blackman windows. Each window function has its own characteristics:

1. Rectangular Window: Offers the best frequency resolution but poor time resolution, leading to spectral leakage.

2. Hamming and Hanning Windows: Provide a balance between time and frequency resolution, reducing spectral leakage compared to the rectangular window.

3. Blackman Window: Offers better suppression of side lobes, resulting in less spectral leakage, but at the cost of reduced frequency resolution.

The choice of window function depends on the specific requirements of the analysis, such as the need for better frequency resolution or reduced spectral leakage.
