The synchrosqueezing transform is a technique used in signal processing to enhance time-frequency analysis. It is an improvement over traditional time-frequency representations like the wavelet transform or the short-time Fourier transform. The key advantage of synchrosqueezing is that it provides a more precise localization of signal components in the time-frequency plane. This is achieved by "squeezing" the time-frequency representation to concentrate the energy of the signal components, making it easier to identify and analyze them. This enhancement is particularly useful for analyzing non-stationary signals, where the frequency content changes over time.
