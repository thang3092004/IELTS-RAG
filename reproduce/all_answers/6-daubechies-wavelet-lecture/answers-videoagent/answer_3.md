Orthonormal bases in time-frequency analysis, such as wavelets or Fourier bases, have certain limitations. One key limitation is the trade-off between time and frequency resolution, as dictated by the uncertainty principle. This means that achieving high resolution in time results in lower resolution in frequency, and vice versa. Additionally, orthonormal bases may not be well-suited for analyzing signals with non-stationary or transient characteristics, as they may not provide the most compact or efficient representation of such signals. Furthermore, the choice of basis functions can significantly affect the analysis, and finding the optimal basis for a given signal can be challenging.
