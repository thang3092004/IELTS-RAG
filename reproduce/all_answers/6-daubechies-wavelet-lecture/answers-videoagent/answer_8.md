The use of orthogonal windows in signal analysis is significant because they help in minimizing spectral leakage, which is the spreading of signal energy across different frequency components. Orthogonal windows, such as the Hamming or Hanning windows, ensure that the windowed segments of a signal do not overlap in a way that causes interference, allowing for more accurate frequency analysis. This is particularly important in applications like Fourier Transform, where precise frequency representation is crucial. By using orthogonal windows, one can achieve better resolution and reduce the distortion in the frequency domain, leading to more reliable signal processing outcomes.
