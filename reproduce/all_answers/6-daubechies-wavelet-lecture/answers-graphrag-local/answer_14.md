In the context of fetal health, Ingrid Daubechies highlights the application of time-frequency analysis primarily in the realm of electrocardiogram (ECG) data analysis, which is utilized to evaluate fetal heart activity. Time-frequency analysis allows for the detailed examination of signals over varying time and frequency domains, providing insights into the dynamic nature of fetal heart rate patterns.

This analytical approach is significant because it can aid healthcare professionals in monitoring the well-being of unborn children by pinpointing abnormalities in heart rate patterns. By using techniques like the short-time Fourier transform, medical practitioners can analyze changes in fetal heart frequencies that correlate with different states of fetal health, assisting in timely and informed decision-making regarding prenatal care.

Moreover, Daubechies' discussions emphasize how advanced signal processing techniques relate to the interpretation of ECG data, making it a vital tool for ensuring maternal-fetal health outcomes. This integration of complex mathematical concepts with clinical practice underlines the critical role that time-frequency analysis plays in enhancing healthcare strategies related to monitoring fetal health.
