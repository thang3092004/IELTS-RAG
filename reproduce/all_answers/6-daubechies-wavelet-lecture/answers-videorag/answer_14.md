In the context of fetal health, Daubechies emphasizes the medical application of time-frequency analysis specifically for analyzing fetal electrocardiogram (ECG) data. The technique of time-frequency analysis, particularly utilizing Daubechies wavelets, is instrumental in extracting relevant information from ECG signals during fetal monitoring. This is crucial for assessing the fetal heart's rhythm and for identifying potential issues that may arise during pregnancy or delivery.

Daubechies discusses how this approach allows clinicians to interpret the complex frequency components over time found in fetal ECG signals. The analysis helps to distinguish the fetal heartbeat from the maternal ECG, which typically has a much stronger signal, owing to the mother's heart activity. By focusing on the harmonics and using methods like wavelet transforms, it becomes feasible to monitor the fetus's health more precisely, even in cases of low signal-to-noise ratio that commonly occurs in such medical assessments.

Additionally, the information extracted through time-frequency analysis plays a significant role in understanding the development and status of the fetus, enabling timely and informed clinical decisions during monitoring. This application is vital for ensuring proper fetal health and managing any potential complications effectively.
