In the context of signal processing and the use of the cepstrum for extracting signal components, Daubechies acknowledges a few key limitations. These limitations are primarily related to the nature of the signals being analyzed and the inherent characteristics of the cepstrum method itself.

### Non-Constant Amplitudes

One significant limitation lies in dealing with signals that exhibit non-constant amplitudes. Traditional cepstrum analysis often struggles when the amplitude of the signal varies over time. This variability complicates the extraction of meaningful signal components, as the cepstrum relies on the assumption of constant amplitude for effective analysis.

### Overlapping Frequencies

Another acknowledged challenge is the presence of overlapping frequency components within the signals. The cepstrum method can encounter difficulties in distinguishing between these overlapping components, particularly when they are closely related in frequency. This is especially problematic in complex or multifaceted signals that are common in practical applications, such as biomedical signals or seismic data.

### Noise Sensitivity

Furthermore, Daubechies suggests that the cepstrum can be sensitive to noise within the signal. The introduction of noise can affect the stability and accuracy of the cepstrum analysis, potentially leading to unreliable results. Given that many real-world signals are contaminated with various forms of noise, this limitation represents a critical consideration in the practical application of the cepstrum for signal extraction tasks.

### Conclusion

Overall, while the cepstrum is a powerful tool in signal processing, these limitations highlight the need for careful consideration and potential modifications when applying this method to complex signals. Alternative techniques or additional preprocessing steps may be necessary to mitigate these challenges and achieve more accurate extraction of signal components.
