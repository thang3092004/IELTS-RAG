### Limitations of the Cepstrum in Signal Component Extraction

Daubechies highlights several notable limitations in the application of the cepstrum for extracting signal components, which are significant for anyone utilizing this technique in signal processing.

#### Difficulty with Overlapping Frequency Components

One primary limitation is that the cepstrum may not be effective in extracting all types of signal components, particularly when the signals exhibit overlapping frequency components. This scenario complicates the process of discerning and separating individual components from the overall signal. As such, users of cepstral methods shall remain cautious when dealing with signals that are closely interwoven in frequency.

#### Noise Interference

Another critical challenge associated with the cepstrum technique is its susceptibility to noise interference. Noise can obscure the true signal components that are being analyzed, thereby complicating the interpretation of results and potentially leading to inaccurate conclusions. This emphasizes the importance of maintaining signal integrity to ensure valid analyses when utilizing cepstral methods.

#### Need for Parameter Tuning

Daubechies further notes that cepstral methods may necessitate careful tuning of parameters to avoid misinterpretations of the signal components. This requirement for precise tuning introduces an additional layer of complexity in signal processing tasks and may not always lead to optimal results. Analysts and practitioners will need to be mindful of this complexity and its implications for their analyses.

### Conclusion

In summary, while the cepstrum is a powerful tool in signal processing, it is not without its limitations. Users should be aware of the challenges posed by overlapping frequency components, noise interference, and the need for careful parameter tuning. Addressing these limitations is crucial to improving the effectiveness and accuracy of cepstral methods in practical applications.
