Daubechies motivates the use of 'interval statistics' in signal analysis by drawing an analogy to the human auditory system. He argues that the auditory system works by taking a signal and breaking it down into its individual frequency components. He suggests that by applying interval statistics to a signal, you can extract the same components that the human auditory system extracts from a sound. This allows interval statistics to be used to extract the same 'fast modes' and 'local frequency' of a signal that are present in the human auditory system.
