Bird songs are fascinating for time-frequency analysis for several reasons. 

One major feature is the wide range of frequencies and harmonics present in the songs. Bird songs can include very high-pitched "tweets" as well as low-pitched "hoots". This allows researchers to analyze the frequency spectrum of the songs using Fourier analysis.

Another feature is the complex harmonics and overtones present in the songs. Bird songs often have multiple "harmonics" that repeat at different frequencies. This can be analyzed by decomposing the songs into their component frequencies and overtones.

Finally, the songs are complex in their structure, with different "parts" that repeat in different ways. This allows researchers to analyze how the different parts of the songs combine and interact with each other.

Overall, the wide range of frequencies, complex harmonics, and structural complexity of bird songs make them interesting for time-frequency analysis. Researchers hope to use this analysis to gain insights into the biology of bird songs, such as why some species have more complex songs than others.
