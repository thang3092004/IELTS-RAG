Orthogonal windows are an important concept in signal analysis that allows for localization in both time and frequency. They consist of a set of functions that are well-suited for a given application. When used in conjunction with the windowed Fourier transform, they allow for the extraction of the fast modes and the localization of the behavior in both time and frequency. 

This is useful for tasks such as speech recognition and compression of audio signals. By looking at the frequency response of the signals, one can determine the different frequency components and how they are localized.

In summary, orthogonal windows allow for localization of the behavior in both time and frequency, which is essential for applications such as speech recognition and audio signal compression.
