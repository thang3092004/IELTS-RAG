Daubechies describes two specific types of time-frequency patterns that indicate singularities in a signal:

1. The presence of a "bump" or "spike" in the signal's frequency spectrum. This indicates a localized region where the frequency spectrum is significantly different from the surrounding frequency spectrum. 

2. The presence of "fast modes" in the signal's time-frequency spectrum. Fast modes are localized regions where the frequency spectrum is dominated by a small number of dominant frequency components.

In the context of the movie, Daubechies explains that these time-frequency patterns are indicative of singularities in a signal because they correspond to localized regions where the signal exhibits unusual properties, such as being very dense, fast oscillations, or being localized in time and frequency.
