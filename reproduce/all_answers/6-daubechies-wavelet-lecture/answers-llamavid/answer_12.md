Daubechies demonstrates the effectiveness of synchrosqueezing by showing a signal from a maternal ECG and then performing a windowed Fourier transform on it. He explains that synchrosqueezing helps to localize the instantaneous frequency of the signal, which can then be used to extract the fetal heartbeat. He shows that by using multi-tapered windows and summing the results, the signal becomes more noisy and the fetal heartbeat is extracted more accurately. This demonstrates the power of synchrosqueezing in extracting fetal heartbeats from maternal ECGs.
