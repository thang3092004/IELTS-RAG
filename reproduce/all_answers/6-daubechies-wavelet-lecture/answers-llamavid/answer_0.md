The inherent limitations of time-frequency localization include:

1. It is limited to a single frequency range. It cannot capture signals that span a wide range of frequencies.

2. It is based on a fixed window function that is applied to the entire signal. This may not be an optimal choice for every signal. 

3. It assumes that the signal is stationary and periodic, which may not always be the case.

4. It relies on a fixed time-frequency grid that is fixed in advance. This may not match the true frequency structure of the signal.

5. It does not account for the time-frequency structure of the signal, which can change over time.

In summary, time-frequency localization is a powerful tool for analyzing signals in a limited frequency range, but it has inherent limitations due to its fixed nature and assumptions about the signal.
