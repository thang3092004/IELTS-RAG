The vloggers encountered distinct street food scenes in various regions of China, showcasing the country's culinary diversity.

In **Urumqi, Xinjiang**, the street food was heavily influenced by Central Asian flavors and featured dishes like:

* **Lamb-filled grilled bread:** This dish was a highlight for the vloggers, who found it very tasty with its thin dough and generous meat filling. 
* **Pomegranate juice:** The vloggers enjoyed the fresh pomegranate juice, which reminded them of their time in Almaty, Kazakhstan. 
* **Figs:** They tried fresh figs, which were not as strong as dried ones but still sweet. 
* **Walnut cake:** This densely packed walnut and raisin cake impressed the vloggers with its rich flavor and ingredients.
* **Sheep and lamb:**  The vloggers noticed that lamb and sheep were the dominant meats used in Urumqi street food.

**Turpan, Xinjiang** offered a different experience:

* **Cow buns:**  The vloggers enjoyed these grilled bread buns filled with beef. 

In **Yining, Xinjiang**: 

* The vloggers bought **freshly baked bread** from a hole-in-the-wall bakery.

Moving to **Chengdu, Sichuan**: 

* The focus shifted to **hot pot**, a spicy Sichuan specialty. 
* **Chang fen (rice noodles):** The vloggers sought out Michelin-recommended chang fen, appreciating the fresh, hot, and simple dish with generous fillings.

Finally, in **Guangzhou, Guangdong**, the street food showcased Cantonese cuisine's emphasis on fresh ingredients and subtle flavors:

* **Soy chicken and boiled chicken with ginger and green onion sauce:** The vloggers enjoyed the savory chicken dishes with aromatic ginger sauce.
* **Egg cakes and green bean cakes:** They were impressed by the soft, cloud-like texture and clean taste of these cakes.
* **Fake shark fin soup:** This affordable and delicious alternative to shark fin soup featured mushrooms, shredded meat, and sometimes fish.
* **Fish ball noodles:** A comforting and cheap bowl of noodles with various fish-based ingredients.
* **Durian:** The vloggers were delighted by the abundance and affordability of durian, a fruit they love but is not commonly eaten in their families.

The vloggers consistently noted the **generous portions** and **affordability** of the street food they encountered throughout China. They also observed **regional variations in ingredients, flavors, and cooking styles**, reflecting the country's vast and diverse culinary landscape.
