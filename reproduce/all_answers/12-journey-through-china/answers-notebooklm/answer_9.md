The Flaming Mountains, located in Turpan, China, are notable for their dramatic appearance and the legends associated with them. Formed over 50 million years ago from an active volcano, the mountains get their name from their distinct ridges, which were created by lava flows. Turpan is known for its extreme heat, with temperatures reaching 50°C (122°F) in the summer. The Turpan Basin, situated below sea level at 154 meters, is surrounded by 5,000-meter high mountains, creating a bowl-like effect that traps heat, making it one of the hottest and driest places in China.

There are a couple of legends that explain the name "Flaming Mountains." One legend is tied to the famous Chinese novel *Journey to the West*, featuring the mythical figure of the Monkey King, Sun Wukong. The story goes that the Monkey King, while in the heavens, knocked over an oven, and the embers flew to Earth, setting a mountain ablaze. This burning mountain is believed to be the Flaming Mountains. Another legend from *Journey to the West* recounts how the Monkey King and his companions, on their journey, had to pass through the scorchingly hot Flaming Mountains. They had to ask a princess to lend them her magical fan to cool down the area so they could pass. This legend explains the presence of a giant fan structure at the entrance of the Flaming Mountains park.
