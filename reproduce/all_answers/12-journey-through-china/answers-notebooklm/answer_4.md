The sources describe many differences between urban and rural areas in China, particularly focusing on the difficulty of traveling between different types of locations.

**Transportation:**

*   Many tourists travel to Xinjiang by private car or tour, since it can be difficult to travel independently due to the lack of public transportation. There is no public transportation to some attractions.   
*   In larger cities, public transportation options like subways and bikes are readily available, inexpensive, and efficient.   
*   However, certain cities, such as Chongqing, lack shared bikes due to safety concerns caused by the city’s mountainous landscape.

**Architecture and Landscape:**

*   Many buildings in rural areas like Ürümqi, especially in the old city, are culturally influenced and feature domes, in contrast to the more modern and futuristic buildings found in larger Chinese cities such as Shanghai.   
*   Ürümqi features buildings with a Middle Eastern aesthetic, featuring lots of bricks and stores selling rocks and dried fruits.   
*   Many cities are built on mountains, leading to more stairs and a different infrastructure than other cities.

**Tourism:**

*   The International Grand Bazaar in Ürümqi is specifically designed to attract tourists. The area features many opportunities for photographs and souvenirs. However, the area feels chaotic and is not enjoyable for everyone.  
*   Rural areas, like some parts of Xinjiang, seem to cater more to domestic tourism. Even Chinese tourists struggle to understand signs, and foreigners would find it impossible to travel without a tour guide.
*   Tourist attractions in China, particularly popular ones, tend to be crowded. Visitors must often follow a specific path designed to control the flow of people.   

**Food:**

*   Rural areas in Xinjiang are known for selling skewers of lamb, beef, and organ meats, along with sheep head soup. 
*   Ürümqi, in particular, is known for its textiles.
*   Turpan, located in the Turpan Basin, is known for grapes and other dried fruits, due to an irrigation system that draws water from the nearby Tianshan Mountains.  
*   Chengdu is known for its spicy Sichuan hot pot.   
*   Guangzhou, particularly the Shangxi Street, is famous for Cantonese cuisine.   
*   Cantonese food tends to be served hot and focuses on fresh ingredients. Dishes generally use less spice and sauces to allow the flavors of the food to come through. 
*   The availability of certain dishes varies in different regions of China. For example, the sources found shark fin soup in Guangzhou but not in other cities.



**Services:**

*   Public toilets in Guangzhou are generally clean and advanced, featuring machines that dispense toilet paper and automatic flushing.

**Overall Atmosphere:**

*   In general, smaller villages and towns have a more relaxed, peaceful, and quiet atmosphere compared to large cities. 
*   The sources report that people in smaller towns are friendlier and more welcoming than in larger cities. Locals frequently offered the travelers tea, snacks, and travel advice.

**Overall, the sources suggest that China offers a diverse range of experiences, from the bustling, modern cities to the peaceful, traditional villages. While transportation and tourism infrastructure may vary, visitors can find delicious food and welcoming people throughout the country.**
