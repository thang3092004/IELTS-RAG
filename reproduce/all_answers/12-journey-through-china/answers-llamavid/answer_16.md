The People's Liberation Monument in Chongqing is a monumental structure that commemorates the victory of the Chinese military against the Japanese in the Second Sino-Japanese War, also known as the War of Resistance against Japan. The monument was built in 1945 to celebrate the victory and to symbolize the restoration of independence for the Chinese people. It was originally named the War Victory Monument, but it was later renamed to the People's Liberation Monument. The monument is located in the central business district of Chongqing and is now a popular tourist attraction. While the monument itself is not particularly impressive, it holds historical significance as it represents the victory of the Chinese military against the Japanese in the war and the restoration of independence for the Chinese people.
