The Flaming Mountains in Turpan are significant because of their unique geological formation and historical importance. According to legend, the Monkey King knocked over a furnace in the heavens, which caused the formation of the mountains and the subsequent creation of the Flaming Mountain range. The name "Flaming Mountain" is said to be a result of the embers that were left behind by the furnace. This geological formation is also significant because it is one of the hottest and driest places in China, with temperatures reaching up to 50 degrees Celsius. This makes it an ideal location to grow grapes and raisins, which are used to make wine and other alcoholic beverages. The Flaming Mountains are also a popular tourist destination for both domestic and international tourists, with visitors coming to explore the unique landscape and cultural significance.
