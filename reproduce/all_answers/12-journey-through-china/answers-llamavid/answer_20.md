Nang is a traditional grilled bread made in Xinjiang. The process starts by grilling the dough over an open flame in a large pan. The dough is made from a mixture of wheat flour, water, and salt, which is then kneaded and shaped into a flat, thin sheet. A spatula is used to spread the dough onto the hot pan and then it is grilled until it becomes crisp and golden brown. The grilling time can vary depending on the thickness of the dough and the desired texture. Once grilled, the nang is cut into square-shaped slices and served with various toppings such as meat, cheese, and sauces. The grilling process is an important part of Xinjiang cuisine and is often accompanied by a glass of tea. The heat and smoke from the open flame gives nang its distinctive flavor and texture.
