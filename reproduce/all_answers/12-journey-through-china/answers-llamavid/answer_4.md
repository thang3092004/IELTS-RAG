China's urban and rural landscapes differ in several ways. 

In urban areas, there is a greater density of population and infrastructure, with more buildings, roads, and public transportation. The architecture is often more modern and technologically advanced, with high-rise buildings, skyscrapers, and large public transit systems. The pace of life is faster and more hectic, with a focus on work and business. 

In rural areas, there is a more relaxed pace of life, with fewer people and more open spaces. The architecture is often more traditional, with narrow roads, stone buildings, and simple public facilities. The pace of life is slower and more leisurely, with a focus on farming, crafts, and traditional customs.

Overall, the contrast between China's urban and rural landscapes is one of density and modernization versus openness and tradition. Urban areas are more technologically advanced and technologically sophisticated, while rural areas are more relaxed and traditional.

In summary, the key contrasts between China's urban and rural landscapes lie in the density of population and infrastructure, the pace of life, and the pace of work and business. Urban areas are more technologically advanced and technologically sophisticated, while rural areas are more traditional and leisurely.
