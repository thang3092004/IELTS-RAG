The People's Liberation Monument, located in Chongqing, holds substantial historical significance tied to China's modern history, particularly in relation to the Second Sino-Japanese War. Built in 1945, the monument commemorates the victory of the Chinese people against Japanese occupation, a period that is referred to in China as the War of Resistance against Japan. This conflict lasted from 1937 to 1945 and represents a pivotal moment in China's national history, emphasizing themes of resilience and liberation.

Chongqing served as the wartime capital of the Republic of China during the war, making the monument not just a symbol of military victory but also a representation of civilian endurance and sacrifice.
