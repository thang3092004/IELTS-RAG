### Research and Development Priorities for Saint-Gobain

Saint-Gobain, a global leader in construction and building materials, emphasizes several critical areas in its research and development (R&D) efforts to meet the changing demands of the market and the environment. Below are the main R&D priorities based on the information gathered.

#### 1. **Sustainability and Carbon Footprint Minimization**
One of the primary objectives of Saint-Gobain’s R&D is to minimize its carbon footprint. The company has established a commitment to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050, which involves:

- Implementing an internal carbon pricing strategy since 2016 to assess and influence R&D investments based on anticipated carbon emission costs.
- Focusing on projects aimed at reducing CO2 emissions, particularly in the construction sector, which accounts for nearly 40% of global CO2 emissions.
- Developing products with significant recycled material content, such as glass wool insulation made from 70% recycled content and plasterboards utilizing 50% recycled materials.

#### 2. **Energy Efficiency Innovations**
Saint-Gobain prioritizes the improvement of energy efficiency within the built environment. This includes:

- Researching advancements in thermal insulation technologies to enhance energy performance in construction.
- Promoting double glazing and glass fuel insulation solutions as effective measures for reducing energy consumption in residential and commercial buildings.

#### 3. **Integration of Digital Technologies**
The integration of digital applied sciences into construction is another focus area. This encompasses:

- The exploration of smart materials and technologies that can adapt to environmental changes, promoting smarter building solutions.
- Emphasizing the interdisciplinary nature of teams working on these initiatives, highlighting the collaboration between fields such as biology, physical sciences, and engineering.

#### 4. **Circular Economy Practices**
Saint-Gobain is committed to the principles of the circular economy. Key initiatives include:

- Developing durable products designed for reuse and recycling, which helps reduce waste and resource consumption.
- Investing in new methods to reclaim and recycle materials from buildings at the end of their lifecycle, thereby encouraging sustainable resource management.

#### 5. **Research Collaboration and Innovation**
The company maintains close collaboration with universities and research institutions, reflected in:

- Hosting programs for interns and researchers across various scientific disciplines at their research centers, fostering innovation through a diversified knowledge base.
- Developing partnerships with interdisciplinary teams to tackle the complex challenges associated with modern construction and sustainability.

### Conclusion
Saint-Gobain's R&D priorities are well-aligned with global trends towards sustainability and resource efficiency. By focusing on minimizing carbon impact, enhancing energy performance, and fostering innovation through collaboration, the company is positioning itself as a leader in sustainable construction practices and technologies for the future.
