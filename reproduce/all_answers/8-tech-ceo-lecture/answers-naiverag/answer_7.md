### Saint-Gobain's Organizational Structure and Its Impact on Sustainability Goals

Saint-Gobain's organizational structure is pivotal in advancing its sustainability objectives, primarily through its shift from a product-line-based framework to a country-based approach. This transition, implemented five years ago, signifies a strategic pivot in the way the company engages with its various markets and responds to broader environmental challenges.

#### Reorganization for Comprehensive Solutions

Previously, Saint-Gobain was structured by product lines, which only allowed for the sale of isolated products like wallboard, insulation, or flooring systems. This approach limited the company's ability to address complex customer needs comprehensively. However, with the reorganization to a country-based model, the company now emphasizes delivering integrated solutions that consider the entire construction ecosystem. By aligning its offerings to meet specific local needs, Saint-Gobain can better support sustainable building practices tailored to different regions, enhancing the effectiveness of its sustainability initiatives.

#### Focus on Sustainability Across Teams

The restructured organization fosters collaboration among various country teams, where 90% of the country CEOs are native to their respective countries. This native expertise allows for a deeper understanding of local environmental issues and regulatory contexts, thus promoting the development and implementation of sustainable practices that are culturally and environmentally appropriate. Such alignment of local knowledge with global sustainability objectives facilitates more effective actions towards reducing carbon emissions and resource usage.

#### Commitment to Long-term Sustainability Goals

Saint-Gobain has committed to ambitious sustainability targets, including becoming carbon neutral by 2050. Achieving these targets requires significant long-term capital investments and planning, particularly in sectors such as flat glass manufacturing where operational cycles extend over decades. The company acknowledges the need for strategic planning to implement necessary changes and invest in innovative, sustainable technologies. The organizational structure supports these goals by promoting internal collaboration and knowledge sharing across its extensive global operations, thus leveraging specific expertise across its market segments.

#### Innovations in Sustainable Practices

Saint-Gobain also utilizes its organizational structure to foster innovation in sustainable practices. The company invests approximately $600 million annually in research and development, creating an environment where innovative solutions for reducing CO2 emissions can thrive. This commitment enables the company to consider not only the immediate sustainability needs but also to anticipate future challenges, thus embedding sustainability into its core business strategy.

### Conclusion

In summary, Saint-Gobain's shift to a country-based organizational structure enhances its ability to meet sustainability goals by enabling a comprehensive approach to delivering solutions, fostering local expertise, committing to long-term sustainability objectives, and investing in innovation. This strategy not only responds to current environmental challenges but positions the company as a leader in sustainable construction practices for the future.
