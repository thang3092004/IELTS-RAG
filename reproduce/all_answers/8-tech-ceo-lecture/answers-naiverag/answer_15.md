## Concept of 'Tech' at Saint-Gobain

The concept of 'Tech' at Saint-Gobain represents a significant shift in leadership principles, emphasizing trust, empowerment, and collaboration within the organizational structure. As discussed by Benoît Bazin, the Chief Executive Officer of Compagnie de Saint-Gobain, 'Tech' is intended to streamline processes and enhance operational efficiency by moving away from traditional matrix management systems, which often led to slowed decision-making and complexity. 

This philosophy advocates for a clear delineation of authority, where individuals in leadership roles, such as country CEOs, are provided the autonomy to make decisions, encouraging a culture that promotes swift action and accountability. In a 'Tech' environment, the roles and responsibilities are restructured to allow leaders to focus on achieving their objectives with more agility and less bureaucratic interference.

## Example of Implementation

A specific example of implementing 'Tech' at Saint-Gobain includes the adjustment in the capital expenditure (capex) decision-making process. Previously, decisions regarding expenditures required consensus from multiple managers across different functions, which could delay initiatives and hinder performance. Under the 'Tech' initiative, the capex threshold was raised from $3 million to $10 million, allowing country CEOs the freedom to make investment decisions without excessive approvals. This change not only expedited the process but also fostered a sense of ownership and responsibility among the leaders.

Moreover, an intentional focus on alignment and trust among teams allows for greater collaboration. Leaders are encouraged to share knowledge and best practices across borders, fostering a global perspective that enhances innovation and efficiency. For instance, with various teams being empowered to leverage their local understanding while contributing to the global objectives of sustainability and performance, the organization demonstrates a dynamic interplay of local empowerment within the context of an overarching corporate strategy.

By instituting 'Tech', Saint-Gobain aims to cultivate a more energized workforce that can innovate and adapt quickly to market needs, ultimately driving the company toward greater operational success and sustainability.
