In the context of building a robust company culture, Rajesh Jain emphasizes two fundamental elements: the presence of an "icon" and a "storyteller." 

### The Icon

The "icon" refers to a person within the company who embodies its core values and ethos. This individual acts as a living representation of the company's culture, showcasing the principles such as honesty, transparency, and a humane environment. Jain believes that having an icon is crucial as this person serves as a model for the organization, influencing employees and helping them connect with the company's mission on a deeper level. An effective icon instills a sense of pride and commitment among team members, motivating them to uphold and propagate the culture.

### The Storyteller

The second essential element is the "storyteller," who plays a pivotal role in communicating and sharing the narratives that define the company. This individual is responsible for telling the stories that highlight the company's journey, successes, and values, thereby fostering a sense of belonging and continuity among employees. The storyteller helps new hires understand what makes the company unique, ensuring that the ethos is not only preserved but also passed on to newcomers. This storytelling aspect is vital for reinforcing the culture and creating a cohesive work environment where everyone feels connected to the overarching narrative of the company.

In summary, according to Rajesh Jain, a strong company culture is underpinned by the leadership of an icon who embodies the organization's values and a storyteller who keeps the culture alive through shared narratives. These elements are crucial for nurturing employee engagement and loyalty, ultimately contributing to the long-term success of the organization.
