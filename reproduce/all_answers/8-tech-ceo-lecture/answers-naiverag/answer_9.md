Saint-Gobain, under the leadership of its Chief Executive Officer Benoît Bazin, has taken significant steps to measure the impact of its sustainability initiatives primarily through a strategic focus on carbon emissions, resource preservation, and the overall reduction of its environmental footprint. 

### Carbon Intensity Reduction

One of the critical metrics that Saint-Gobain uses to gauge its sustainability performance is the reduction of carbon emissions. The company has reported a notable **27% decrease** in total emissions of CO2 across its Scope 1 and 2 categories over five years, while also achieving a **42% reduction** in carbon intensity per euro of turnover. This indicates that the company has managed to grow while simultaneously lowering its environmental impact, showing a commitment to both economic and ecological health.

### Internal Carbon Pricing

Saint-Gobain has implemented an **internal carbon pricing strategy** since 2016. This mechanism allows the company to assess investment decisions and project feasibility by incorporating a cost associated with carbon emissions, thereby encouraging more sustainable practices across its operations. This pricing includes different rates depending on the type of investment, enabling the firm to ensure that projects align with long-term sustainability goals.

### Sustainability Products and Circular Economy

The company actively focuses on the development and implementation of sustainable products, which play a significant role in their environmental metrics. For example, Saint-Gobain's glass wool insulation products, created from **70% recycled materials**, reflect its commitment to a circular economy. These products not only minimize waste but also help reduce CO2 content, thus showcasing tangible benefits of their sustainability initiatives.

### Carbon Neutral Commitment

Furthermore, Saint-Gobain has pledged to be **carbon neutral by 2050**, with interim targets set for 2030. The timeline for specific initiatives, such as pilot projects for carbon-reducing technologies, illustrates the company’s structured approach to sustainability. For instance, by 2025, they plan to initiate pilot projects to begin tackling long-term carbon control within their manufacturing processes.

### Conclusion

In summary, Saint-Gobain leverages key performance indicators, including reductions in carbon emissions, the adoption of internal carbon pricing, and the implementation of sustainable product lines, to effectively measure the impact of its sustainability initiatives. By focusing on both environmental performance and business growth, Saint-Gobain demonstrates a comprehensive approach to integrating sustainability into its core business practices, thereby setting a benchmark within the construction and materials industry for responsible operations.
