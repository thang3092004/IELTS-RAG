Rajesh Jain emphasizes that two essential elements are required to build a strong company culture: an "icon" and a "storyteller."

### Icon
The "icon" represents a person who embodies the values and principles of the company. This individual serves as a role model for the organization's culture, showcasing traits such as honesty, transparency, and humanity. The icon must live and exemplify the company's culture in their everyday actions, making it relatable and tangible for the rest of the team.

### Storyteller
The "storyteller," on the other hand, is the individual responsible for sharing the company’s narrative. This person communicates the stories and successes of the organization to new hires and current employees, reinforcing the core values and mission. By effectively narrating the company’s journey and achievements, the storyteller helps to build a collective identity and sense of belonging among team members.

Together, these two roles—icon and storyteller—play a critical role in fostering a strong and vibrant company culture that motivates employees and aligns them with the organizational goals.
