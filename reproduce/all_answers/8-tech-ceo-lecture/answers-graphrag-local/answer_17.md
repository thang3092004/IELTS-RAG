## The Marshall Plan Proposed by Benoît Bazin

Benoît Bazin, the CEO of Compagnie de Saint-Gobain, introduced the concept of the "Marshall Plan" in discussions regarding energy affordability and building retrofits, particularly in the context of low-income individuals in France. This proposal is centered around the urgent need to support vulnerable communities facing high energy costs and the consequent challenges in accessing necessary renovations for energy efficiency.

### Purpose of the Marshall Plan

The Marshall Plan aims to provide substantial public support specifically directed toward retrofitting initiatives. It calls for targeted financial assistance to help low-income homeowners and renters manage their high energy bills, which often lead to difficult choices between essential needs, such as food and shelter. By offering support for retrofitting projects, the plan seeks to reduce the financial burden on these populations, enabling them to improve the energy efficiency of their homes without facing exorbitant out-of-pocket expenses.

### Intended Impact on Building Retrofits

The proposed initiative emphasizes that a significant portion of the financial assistance should be redirected to low-income individuals, effectively lowering the initial costs associated with retrofitting. Bazin argues that if the financial contribution required from these individuals can be minimized—ideally down to about 20% of the total costs—they are more likely to pursue these energy-saving renovations. By tackling the financial barrier of retrofitting and providing a clearer framework of support, the plan could lead to increased participation in such programs.

The broader goal of the Marshall Plan is to not only foster economic stability for low-income communities by reducing their energy bills but also to contribute to environmental sustainability. By retrofitting buildings, the initiative indirectly addresses issues of CO2 emissions in the built environment, aligning with the overall push toward greener practices within the construction industry. This dual impact of economic support and environmental responsibility positions the Marshall Plan as a pivotal strategy for addressing both social and environmental challenges faced in urban settings. 

In summary, Bazin’s proposed Marshall Plan is a comprehensive approach aimed at empowering low-income households through enhanced access to financial support for building retrofits, ultimately leading to improved energy efficiency and a significant reduction in energy expenditures.
