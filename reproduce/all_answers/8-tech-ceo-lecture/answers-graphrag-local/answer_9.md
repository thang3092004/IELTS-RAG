Compagnie de Saint-Gobain has implemented a multi-faceted approach to measure the impact of its sustainability initiatives, emphasizing the importance of various metrics and methodologies to gauge success effectively.

### Reduction of CO2 Emissions

One of the key metrics utilized by Saint-Gobain is tracking the reduction of carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions. The company has reported a commendable achievement, with a 27% decrease in CO2 emissions over five years. This reduction is measured across various operational scopes, specifically addressing scope 1 and 2 emissions, which pertain to direct emissions from owned or controlled sources and indirect emissions from the generation of purchased energy, respectively. The company emphasizes that even while achieving this reduction, it has also grown its total turnover, highlighting the potential for sustainable growth alongside environmental responsibilities.

### Carbon Intensity Metrics

In addition to absolute emission reductions, Saint-Gobain measures carbon intensity, which reflects the amount of CO2 emissions per unit of turnover. Notably, the company has indicated a 42% drop in carbon intensity per euro of turnover, showing that it is possible to grow economically while minimizing the environmental footprint.

### Internal Carbon Pricing

Saint-Gobain has also integrated an internal carbon pricing mechanism in its strategy since 2016. This approach involves setting a cost on carbon emissions, which helps guide investment decisions and encourages projects to align with sustainability goals. The internal carbon price operates as an economic incentive, motivating the organization to prioritize low-carbon technologies and practices across its global operations.

### Sustainability Goals and Reporting

The company has set clear sustainability goals that track progress in key areas, including waste reduction, energy efficiency, and resource conservation. This structured framework allows for another layer of assessing the effectiveness of sustainability initiatives. By aligning their operational strategies with international environmental standards and UN Commitments, Saint-Gobain can further benchmark its outcomes against global best practices.

### Environmental Impact Assessments

Saint-Gobain also conducts comprehensive environmental impact assessments, particularly concerning the materials and processes used throughout production. These assessments evaluate the impact of innovative materials like low-carbon glass and other recycling initiatives across their supply chain.

### Collaboration and Continuous Improvement

Collaborations with educational institutions, such as Columbia University, serve not only to enhance knowledge sharing but also allow for feedback on best practices in measuring impact. Continuous research and development (R&D) projects enable Saint-Gobain to refine its methodologies further and adapt its strategies based on technological advancements and changing market needs. 

Through these comprehensive measures, Saint-Gobain demonstrates a committed approach to ensuring its sustainability initiatives are effectively monitored and improved over time, reinforcing its status as a leader in the sustainable construction sector.
