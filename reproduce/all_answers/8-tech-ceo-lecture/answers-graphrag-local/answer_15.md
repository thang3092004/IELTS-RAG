# Understanding 'Tech' as a Leadership Principle at Saint-Gobain

The concept of 'Tech' as a leadership principle at Saint-Gobain revolves around the strategic direction that integrates technology and innovation within the organization’s operational and managerial frameworks. This principle is dedicated to leveraging advanced technologies to enhance competitive advantage, drive sustainable practices, and shape the future of the construction materials industry. By prioritizing a tech-driven approach, Saint-Gobain aims to address pressing issues such as efficiency, environmental impact, and quality of service.

## The Role of Technology in Leadership

At Saint-Gobain, leadership is characterized by a robust emphasis on innovation and technology-driven solutions. Leaders are encouraged to incorporate various technological advancements which can streamline processes, optimize resource management, and improve operational efficiencies. This strategic focus aligns with their commitment to sustainability and carbon neutrality, reinforcing how tech direction is intertwined with corporate responsibility.

One critical way this principle manifests is through collaborative efforts with academic institutions, where leaders like Benoît Bazin engage in discussions that highlight technology's role in sustainable practices. These engagements showcase the importance of aligning technological advancements with business objectives while fostering a culture of innovation that permeates throughout the organization.

## Example of Implementation

An exemplary implementation of the 'Tech' principle at Saint-Gobain can be seen in their investment in research and development (R&D) projects focused on innovative building materials. The commitment to creating low-carbon products, such as electrochromic glass and Glass Wool Insulation, represents how technology is embedded into their product development strategy. These materials not only advance the quality and efficiency of construction but also address environmental challenges tied to conventional building practices.

Moreover, the introduction of an **Internal Carbon Price** model within their operational strategies exemplifies the application of tech in corporate governance. This unique approach incentivizes teams to innovate and reduce carbon emissions across all departments, showcasing how technology is utilized to align business practices with sustainability goals.

In summary, the integration of 'Tech' as a leadership principle at Saint-Gobain not only enhances their operational capabilities but also reinforces their position as a leader in sustainable construction practices, providing a clear pathway for innovation in the industry.
