### Saint-Gobain's Reorganization and Commitment to Sustainability

Saint-Gobain, a global leader in construction materials and solutions, has strategically reorganized its operational structure in alignment with its commitment to sustainability. This shift has involved moving from a product line-based organization to a country-based structure. This reorganization aims to enhance the company's responsiveness to local market needs while promoting a holistic approach to sustainability across its global operations.

#### The Rationale Behind Reorganization

The reorganization reflects Saint-Gobain's recognition that traditional methods of organization—which focused on specific product lines—were insufficient for addressing the complexities of modern construction and urban development. By restructuring into a country-focused organization, Saint-Gobain enables local teams, particularly country CEOs, to leverage their knowledge of regional markets, cultures, and customer needs. This model not only streamlines operations but also ensures that sustainable practices are tailored to local contexts, facilitating the implementation of solutions that are both environmentally and economically viable.

#### Enhancing Sustainability Efforts

The commitment to sustainability at Saint-Gobain is embedded in its core strategy. The company has identified three main sustainability challenges: reducing CO2 emissions, preserving natural resources, and accommodating the needs of a growing global population. During presentations at Columbia University, it was emphasized that the need for light and efficient construction methods is critical, especially as society grapples with the realities of urbanization and climate change.

By reorganizing, Saint-Gobain aims to innovate continuously in creating sustainable building materials and solutions that minimize the environmental footprint. For instance, the focus on utilizing materials with recycled content and the promotion of circular economy principles are central themes in their new strategic direction. Products like glass wool insulation and plasterboard, which are made from significant percentages of recycled materials, exemplify how the company is reducing its resource consumption and environmental impact.

#### Aligning Purpose with Action

Saint-Gobain's reorganization is not merely administrative; it aligns closely with the company's purpose of making the world a better home through responsible construction. The integration of a commitment to sustainability into the organizational structure empowers employees to think systemically about how their work contributes to broader environmental goals. It fosters collaboration across departments, thus maximizing innovation and resource efficiency.

The leadership at Saint-Gobain recognizes the importance of collective effort in sustainability, with plans to involve over 168,000 employees in this initiative. This diverse workforce is seen as a vital component in driving forward the company’s ambitious plans to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050, as reinforced by internal carbon pricing policies.

In conclusion, Saint-Gobain's reorganization is a thoughtful and strategic move that enhances its commitment to sustainability. By embedding sustainability into the fabric of its operations through a more responsive organizational structure, the company not only addresses present challenges but positions itself as a leader in building a sustainable future.
