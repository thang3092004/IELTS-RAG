Saint-Gobain has undertaken a variety of low-carbon initiatives aimed at reducing its environmental footprint and contributing to sustainability across its operations. Here are some notable initiatives:

### 1. Low-Carbon Glass Production
One of the significant advancements in Saint-Gobain's strategy includes the production of low-carbon glass. The company successfully launched its first low-carbon glass offering, which was made possible through innovative processes incorporating a greater proportion of recycled glass and the utilization of biogas. This initiative resulted in a reduction of CO2 emissions by 40%.

### 2. Carbon-Neutral Production Facilities
Saint-Gobain has established plants with aims of achieving carbon neutrality by utilizing renewable energy sources. For instance, a cluster board plant in Norway operates with zero carbon production thanks to hydropower availability. Plans are in place to replicate this model in Canada and California, focusing on sustainable practices in regions with sufficient renewable energy resources.

### 3. Circular Economy Practices
The company emphasizes circular economy principles by integrating recycled materials into its products. For example, their glass wool insulation products are composed of 70% recycled materials, and plasterboards contain 50% recycled content. This approach not only reduces waste but also minimizes raw material consumption and CO2 emissions.

### 4. Internal Carbon Pricing
Since 2016, Saint-Gobain has imposed an internal carbon price to guide its sustainability initiatives. This pricing strategy incentivizes projects that lead to lower carbon footprints and integrates sustainability considerations into investment decisions.

### 5. Long-Term Capital Investments for Sustainability
Saint-Gobain has committed to long-term capital investments to ensure its facilities can support low-carbon operations. The company is planning for future infrastructure that aligns with its carbon neutrality goals, recognizing that retrofitting and modernizing plants is essential for achieving these objectives long-term.

### 6. Commitment to Research and Development
The company invests approximately $600 million annually in research and development, focusing on innovative solutions that promote energy efficiency and reduce CO2 emissions. Their ongoing R&D efforts reflect a commitment to discovering new technologies and materials that contribute to sustainable construction practices.

### 7. Energy Efficiency in Existing Buildings
Saint-Gobain advocates for the retrofitting of existing buildings to enhance energy efficiency. Their approach highlights that 85-90% of the buildings in use today will still be standing in 2050, thereby making renovations crucial for reducing overall energy consumption and associated emissions.

Overall, these initiatives showcase Saint-Gobain's commitment to addressing environmental challenges through innovative processes, sustainable building practices, and concerted efforts towards achieving industry-leading low-carbon solutions.
