### Environmental Challenges Addressed by Saint-Gobain in the Built Environment

Saint-Gobain, a global leader in the construction and building materials industry, has recognized several critical environmental challenges within the built environment that it actively addresses. These challenges include CO2 emissions, natural resource preservation, and the need for sustainable construction methods to accommodate a growing global population.

#### CO2 Emissions

One of the primary concerns highlighted by Saint-Gobain is the significant contribution of the built environment to global CO2 emissions. Research indicates that approximately 37% to 40% of total CO2 emissions globally stem from building-related activities. Consequently, any nation aiming for carbon neutrality must prioritize reforming the construction sector and improving the lifecycle impacts of buildings. This includes implementing strategies that minimize the carbon footprint of construction processes and promoting energy-efficient building designs.

During various presentations, executives from Saint-Gobain emphasized the importance of adopting low carbon materials and innovative construction methods that led to substantial reductions in CO2 emissions. For instance, moving towards light construction practices, such as utilizing load-bearing structures instead of traditional heavy materials, allows for significant decreases in the embodied emissions associated with new developments.

#### Natural Resource Preservation

Natural resource preservation is another critical area of concern for Saint-Gobain. The company actively promotes the circular economy, which involves recycling materials and reducing waste in construction practices. By integrating recycled materials, such as glass wool insulation that utilizes up to 70% recycled content, Saint-Gobain seeks to minimize the impact on raw material extraction and promotes a more sustainable lifecycle for construction products.

The depletion of resources, particularly materials like sand, which were previously considered abundant, has garnered attention. Saint-Gobain acknowledges that sustainable resource management is essential to ensure that construction practices remain supported by adequate material availability without jeopardizing the environment.

#### Meeting Population Growth Challenges

As the world anticipates the addition of two billion people in urban areas over the next three decades, Saint-Gobain recognizes the urgent need for innovative solutions to meet housing and infrastructure demands. The construction industry must evolve to create better, more efficient buildings that cater to this exponential population growth while addressing environmental concerns.

Saint-Gobain's strategic focus involves integrating sustainability into every aspect of its operations, from product development to building practices. The emphasis on energy-efficient renovations and retrofitting existing structures, which represent a significant portion of the built environment, is a crucial part of their approach. By ensuring existing buildings are optimized for energy efficiency, they contribute to reducing the overall environmental footprint associated with housing and urban development.

### Conclusion

Saint-Gobain's commitment to tackling environmental challenges within the built environment encompasses a multifaceted approach. Addressing CO2 emissions, preserving natural resources, and preparing for urban growth are integral to their business strategy. Through innovative construction practices and sustainable solutions, Saint-Gobain aims to lead the industry toward a more environmentally responsible future, fulfilling its duty to minimize its ecological impact while providing quality building solutions.
