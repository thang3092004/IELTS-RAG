The concept of 'Tech' at Saint-Gobain embodies a leadership principle centered around "Trust, Empowerment, Collaboration." This framework aims to create an environment where team members are motivated and capable of making decisions autonomously, thus fostering innovation and enhancing organizational efficiency. The leadership approach encourages delegation of authority, allowing managers to function with a clearer focus on their specific responsibilities without unnecessary bureaucratic hurdles.

### Trust and Empowerment

In this context, 'Tech' emphasizes the importance of trust among team members and empowering them to take ownership of their tasks. For instance, the leadership structure was streamlined to reduce complexity in decision-making. Previously, significant financial decisions required multi-layer approvals. The threshold for capital expenditure (CAPEX) decisions, which previously stood at $3 million, was increased to $10 million. This adjustment allowed regional managers to make quicker decisions to address market needs and operational issues without waiting for extended approval processes. 

### Collaboration

Collaboration is another critical aspect embedded in the 'Tech' principle. By entrusting country leaders with the autonomy to manage their segments, Saint-Gobain encourages knowledge sharing and cooperative problem-solving across various regions. The organization leverages its extensive global network and promotes dialogue between different country heads, allowing them to exchange best practices and insights. This approach recognizes that challenges may vary by region, and local managers are best positioned to understand their markets.

### Example of Implementation

A practical example of the implementation of the 'Tech' leadership principle at Saint-Gobain involved the introduction of a 360-degree feedback system aimed at assessing management capabilities within the organization. To facilitate growth and development among its leaders, a psychologist was brought in to guide workshops tailored for executives. These sessions focused on the importance of building trust and the dynamics of adapting to diverse economic environments. This initiative serves to reinforce a culture of accountability and support, ultimately enhancing Saint-Gobain's ability to innovate and respond to market changes effectively.

In summary, the concept of 'Tech' at Saint-Gobain underscores the critical roles that trust, empowerment, and collaboration play in cultivating an agile and responsive leadership style. By simplifying decision-making processes and fostering a culture conducive to cooperation, the company positions itself to tackle sustainability and market challenges more efficiently.
