Rajesh Jain emphasizes the importance of two key components for building a strong company culture: **the presence of an "icon" and a "storyteller."** 

### The Icon
The "icon" is defined as a person who embodies the company's values and principles in their actions. This individual serves as a living example of the culture that the organization aims to instill among its employees. Their behavior and approach are crucial because they not only represent the company's ideals but also act as a model for employees to emulate. This aspect highlights the significance of leadership in nurturing a positive organizational atmosphere.

### The Storyteller
On the other hand, the "storyteller" plays a vital role by communicating the company's history, values, and mission to new and existing employees. This individual shares narratives that encapsulate what the organization stands for and helps reinforce a sense of belonging and purpose. Through storytelling, employees can connect with the company's culture on a deeper level, understanding not only what is expected of them but also why those expectations exist. 

Together, these two elements help create a robust and engaging company culture that fosters loyalty, understanding, and commitment among employees, thereby enhancing overall organizational effectiveness.
