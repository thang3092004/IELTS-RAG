Compagnie de Saint-Gobain employs a multi-faceted approach to address CO2 emissions in building materials, emphasizing sustainability and innovation across its operations. Here are some key strategies and initiatives that the company has implemented:

### Emission Reduction Targets
Saint-Gobain has set ambitious targets for reducing CO2 emissions, having successfully decreased its total emissions by 27% over the past five years. This reduction applies to scope 1 and scope 2 emissions, alongside a notable 42% reduction in carbon intensity per euro of turnover. This means that even as the company expands its operations, it is achieving a lower environmental impact in terms of carbon output.

### Sustainable Product Development
The corporation focuses on creating sustainable building materials that incorporate recycled content. For instance, products like glass wool insulation contain up to 70% recycled materials, which significantly reduces the carbon footprint associated with their production. This innovative practice showcases how the company integrates sustainability into its product development processes.

### Circular Economy Initiatives
Saint-Gobain advocates for circular economy principles, aiming to minimize waste and maximize resource efficiency in construction practices. The company promotes recycling and the use of reused materials in its processes. By repurposing materials and reducing raw material extraction, they contribute to less CO2 generation throughout their supply chain.

### Energy Efficiency Measures
Energy efficiency is a critical component of Saint-Gobain's strategy, which includes improving the energy performance of buildings. The company emphasizes renovations that enhance energy efficiency, subsequently lowering overall energy consumption and associated CO2 emissions in building operations. Moreover, they offer solutions that help to achieve energy efficiency in educational and residential settings.

### Internal Carbon Pricing
As part of its commitment to sustainability, Saint-Gobain has instituted an internal carbon price since 2016. This strategy serves as a financial incentive for various business segments to reduce carbon emissions, effectively guiding investment and operational decisions towards lower-impact alternatives.

### Commitment to Carbon Neutrality
Looking ahead, Saint-Gobain has committed to achieving carbon neutrality by 2050. This goal encompasses comprehensive strategies across all levels of production and logistical operations, reinforcing their dedication to mitigating environmental impact effectively.

### Active Involvement in Sustainable Discussions
Saint-Gobain, led by its CEO, actively participates in global forums and discussions on sustainability challenges, particularly in relation to the construction sector. These discussions aim to foster collaboration and share best practices for reducing CO2 emissions in building materials.

### Conclusion
Through these strategies, Compagnie de Saint-Gobain exemplifies a proactive and comprehensive approach to addressing CO2 emissions in building materials. By focusing on innovation, sustainable practices, and effective resource management, the company aims not only to enhance its product offerings but to play a critical role in the broader move towards sustainable construction and environmental stewardship.
