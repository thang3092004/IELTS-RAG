Anthropomorphic Test Devices (ATDs), commonly known as crash test dummies, play a crucial role in injury biomechanics research. They are used to simulate human responses to various physical forces during events such as car crashes. ATDs are equipped with sensors that measure the forces and accelerations experienced during impact, allowing researchers to assess the potential for injury. By analyzing the data collected from these devices, researchers can evaluate the effectiveness of safety features in vehicles, improve design standards, and develop better protective equipment to reduce injury risk in real-world scenarios.
