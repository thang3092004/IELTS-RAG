The "$100 Delta decision rule" refers to the idea that for decisions under $100, it is better to not spend too much time on them, as the outcome will likely be inconsequential. This rule is based on the principle that if a decision is less than $100, it is not worth the time and effort to overthink. The rule helps in prioritizing and simplifying decision-making by focusing on the most important and doable tasks. For example, if a decision is under $100, it is better to not spend much time analyzing it, as the outcome will not be consequential. This rule can be applied to both personal and professional life, helping individuals make quicker and more efficient decisions.
