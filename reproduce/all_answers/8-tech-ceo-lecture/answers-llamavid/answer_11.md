Saint-Gobain reorganized to address sustainability challenges by creating a cross-functional group that focused on energy efficiency, carbon reduction, and sustainable construction. They called this group "Sangava." The CEO, Mr. Benoit, explained that they reorganized by putting people from different departments together to work on the same goal. He said they wanted to define the large projects and then have the different departments run them. He also emphasized the importance of trust between the different departments and countries. Mr. Benoit stressed that management should empower people and give them the freedom to experiment and learn from each other. He said this is why they created "Sangava" which is a matrix organization with cross-functional teams working together. By creating this new structure, they were able to prioritize projects that would have the biggest impact and then scale them globally. Overall, the reorganization allowed Saint-Gobain to tackle the sustainability challenges they were facing by putting the right people in the right positions to drive change.
