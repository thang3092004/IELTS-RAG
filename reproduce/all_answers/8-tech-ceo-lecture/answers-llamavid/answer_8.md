Saint-Gobain has implemented a variety of low-carbon initiatives to reduce their environmental impact. Some of their initiatives include:

1. Using lightweight materials like concrete and gypsum wallboard instead of heavy concrete blocks. This reduces the amount of concrete needed to construct buildings. 

2. Developing new materials like clay concrete and rice husk wallboard, which have lower carbon emissions.

3. Launching a summer internship program for students from around the world to work on innovative projects. This helps develop the next generation of engineers.

4. Implementing a stage-gate process to define the go/no-go of new products, to reduce the risk of innovation.

5. Building a cross-functional team with representatives from different countries to drive innovation and prioritize projects.

6. Developing a system to measure the impact of energy efficiency renovations in buildings and classrooms, which helps guide investments in energy efficient products.

7. Sharing knowledge about their innovations with universities and other stakeholders to drive the adoption of more energy efficient materials.

In summary, Saint-Gobain has implemented a range of initiatives to reduce their carbon emissions and drive innovation in their products, while also engaging with students and other stakeholders to help develop the next generation of engineers.
