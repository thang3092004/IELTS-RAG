Saint-Gobain measures the impact of its sustainability initiatives in several ways. It has a dedicated sustainability team that defines its purpose, strategy, and targets. It has a carbon neutrality target for 2050 and a goal to reduce its carbon emissions intensity by 20%. It measures its emissions intensity and the carbon footprint of its products. It also measures the impact of its products in terms of energy efficiency, acoustic, and other environmental factors. It has developed innovative products like low-carbon concrete and gypsum wallboard made from rice husk. It also measures the impact of its initiatives in terms of economic and social value, such as creating jobs and improving the livelihoods of farmers. It has developed a digital and IT platform to scale up its innovation efforts. It has defined processes for innovation, industry performance, and digital and IT to ensure that the best ideas are selected and developed. It has also built trust across its global organization through cross-functional collaboration and communication. Overall, Saint-Gobain has developed a comprehensive approach to measure the impact of its sustainability initiatives through a combination of quantitative and qualitative measures.
