Saint-Gobain has a comprehensive strategy for reducing their carbon footprint in the production of flat glass. They have developed a low-carbon concrete pillar and a low-carbon concrete wallboard product. For the concrete pillar product, they have developed a new gypsum wallboard plant in India using rice husk as a biomass material, which generates no CO2 emissions. For the wallboard product, they have launched a plant in India using rice husk as a biomass material, which has a much lower carbon footprint compared to traditional cement. Saint-Gobain is also exploring innovations like using natural materials like clay and glass in their production process. They have also set a target to be carbon neutral by 2050.
