Saint-Gobain has several main strategies to achieve its goal of becoming carbon neutral by 2050:

1. Increase energy efficiency: Saint-Gobain is working to reduce the energy consumption of its products, such as concrete and glass, by developing new materials and coatings that use less energy. This includes investing in energy-efficient lighting systems and building designs.

2. Reduce the use of natural resources: Saint-Gobain is looking into recycling materials like PVC, aluminum, and glass to reduce its reliance on virgin resources. It is also exploring new materials that have a lower environmental impact.

3. Increase the use of renewable energy: The company is investing in renewable energy sources like solar and wind power to generate more of its electricity.

4. Reduce emissions from its operations: Saint-Gobain is working to reduce emissions from its manufacturing processes, such as by installing more energy-efficient equipment.

5. Develop new low-carbon products: The company is developing new products that use less carbon, such as low-carbon concrete and gypsum wallboard made from rice husk.

In summary, Saint-Gobain's main strategies involve improving the energy efficiency of its products, reducing its reliance on natural resources, and developing new products with lower emissions.
