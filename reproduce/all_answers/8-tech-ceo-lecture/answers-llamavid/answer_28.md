Anthropomorphic Test Devices (ATDs) are computer-based models of the human body that are used in injury biomechanics research to assess injury risk. They are designed to predict how an injury would occur and what the resulting injuries would look like. ATDs are based on anatomical and mechanical data about the human body, and they are used to simulate the mechanics of an injury by applying forces and motions to the model. This allows researchers to predict how an injury would occur and what the resulting injuries would look like. ATDs can be used to analyze injuries that have already occurred, as well as to predict injuries that could potentially occur in the future. They are an important tool in injury biomechanics research to gain a better understanding of how injuries occur and to develop strategies to prevent them.
