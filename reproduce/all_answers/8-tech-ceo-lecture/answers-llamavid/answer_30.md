Exponent addresses the challenge of studying injury tolerance in spines with pre-existing degenerative conditions by conducting experiments that simulate injuries on cadavers with both healthy and diseased spines. They use a test vehicle with a roof that is either extremely strong or extremely weak, and then crash it at high speeds to simulate injuries that would occur in a real car crash. By comparing the injuries sustained by healthy and diseased spines, they can determine the mechanical factors that increase the risk of injury. This allows them to better understand how injuries affect spines with pre-existing degenerative conditions and develop safer restraint systems for those spines. The experiments are expensive but necessary to gain insights into injury tolerance in a realistic crash test environment.
