Benoit Bazin proposed a Marshall Plan for the building retrofit sector, aiming to increase the rate of energy-efficient building renovations. The plan involved developing regulations that would make it mandatory for landlords and homeowners to invest in energy-efficient improvements. This would make it easier for the public to finance energy-efficient improvements, as they could get lower energy bills. The plan was designed to encourage more energy-efficient buildings, reduce emissions, and increase the demand for low-carbon materials. The regulations would be enforced by governments, and landlords and homeowners would be penalized for not investing in energy-efficient improvements. The goal was to make energy-efficient building renovations easier to finance, and to increase the rate of renovations, leading to more energy-efficient buildings.
