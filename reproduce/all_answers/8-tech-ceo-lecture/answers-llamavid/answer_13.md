Saint-Gobain reorganized its business model to place sustainability at the core of its strategy, reflecting its commitment to addressing the most pressing issues of our time. Under the new model, the company was organized around three main topics: energy efficiency, the circular economy, and low-carbon construction. This allowed it to tackle the challenges of reducing emissions, conserving natural resources, and promoting sustainability in the built environment. The reorganization enabled Saint-Gobain to focus on creating innovative solutions for its customers, like the low-carbon gas offer and the use of rice husk wallboard in India. The new model also allowed the company to leverage the expertise of its research centers, which developed innovative products like the electrocomic glass. The reorganization was part of a broader effort to change the company's culture and encourage innovation.
