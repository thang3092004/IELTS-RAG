2. Biomechanical testing: Exponent conducts experiments on crash dummies and human volunteers to test the biomechanical models. This provides real-world validation of the injury models.

3. Injury metrics: Exponent collects data on actual injuries and uses this to develop injury metrics that can be used to predict the severity of injuries.

4. Biomechanical testing: Exponent conducts experiments on crash dummies to study the injury mechanisms and how they can be prevented. This helps to understand what types of restraints and designs are needed.

5. Biomechanical imaging: Exponent uses imaging techniques like X-rays, CT scans, and MRI to study the injury mechanisms at the tissue level. This provides insights into how the injury happened.
