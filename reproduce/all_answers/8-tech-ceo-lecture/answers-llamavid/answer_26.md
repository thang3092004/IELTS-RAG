Exponent employs a multidisciplinary approach to conduct injury biomechanics investigations. This includes working with biologists, physical scientists, engineers, and other experts. Biologists provide expertise in human anatomy and tissue mechanics. Physical scientists are knowledgeable in mechanical engineering principles and the physics of materials. Engineers develop the experimental setup and perform the tests. Biomechanists analyze the data to determine the injury mechanisms and severity. Biologists and physical scientists work closely together to design the experiments and interpret the results. Biologists provide insights into the biological aspects of injury, while engineers focus on the mechanical aspects. This multidisciplinary approach allows Exponent to develop a comprehensive understanding of injury mechanisms and develop solutions that are both biologically plausible and technically feasible.
