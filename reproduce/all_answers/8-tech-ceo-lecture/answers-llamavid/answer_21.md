According to Rajesh Jain, entrepreneurs should identify problems that impact a large number of businesses or individuals, and then look for ways to create solutions for those problems. He advises entrepreneurs to "not have things, just do that." This means not spending too much time on distractions and focusing on the most important decisions and tasks. He also recommends taking time to reflect on the problem and how it can be solved. Jain suggests reading books, writing, and deep thinking as ways to help focus on the problem and avoid distractions. He also recommends building a team and working together to identify the most important problems and solutions.
