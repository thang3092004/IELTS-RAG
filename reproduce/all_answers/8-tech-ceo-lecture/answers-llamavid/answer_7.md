Saint-Gobain has a unique organizational structure that aids its sustainability goals. It is organized by function rather than product lines. This allows it to tackle the three key sustainability challenges - energy efficiency, low-carbon concrete, and circular economy - as a system, rather than as individual products. It has 800 manufacturing plants worldwide, which allows it to address the needs of local markets. The company also has a cross-functional team that works to align its purpose with its strategy and operations. Saint-Gobain has also set ambitious targets for its sustainability efforts, such as being carbon neutral by 2050.
