Exponent noticed that there was a gap in the data on injury tolerance of spines with pre-existing degenerative conditions. In the real world, many people have spines with degenerative conditions. To address this gap, Exponent researchers conducted a study on the injury tolerance of spines with **diffuse idiopathic skeletal hyperostosis (DISH)**, a condition in which the vertebrae fuse together.

To conduct this study, Exponent researchers obtained paired specimens with and without DISH. The specimens were then set up in a laboratory to **measure their stiffness, range of motion, and loading tolerance.**

Preliminary results show that:

*  Spinal units with DISH have **a much smaller range of motion** compared to healthy spinal units.
*  Spinal units with DISH can sustain a **much lower level of torque** compared to healthy spinal units.
*  Spinal units with DISH have a **much lower level of energy** compared to healthy spinal units.

By conducting this research, Exponent hopes **to contribute to basic science research** and **help others working on applications where there are gaps in the data.**
