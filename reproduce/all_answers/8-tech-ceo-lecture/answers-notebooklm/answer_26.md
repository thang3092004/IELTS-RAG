Exponent, a multidisciplinary engineering and scientific consulting firm, employs a comprehensive approach to injury biomechanics investigations, integrating expertise from various disciplines to provide end-to-end solutions. Here's a breakdown of their approach:

* **Collaboration with Vehicle Engineers:** Exponent's biomechanical engineers collaborate closely with vehicle engineers who reconstruct vehicle motions by examining scratch patterns, deformations, and other physical evidence. This provides a foundation for understanding the occupant's kinematic response to the vehicle's movements during the incident.
* **Medical Record Analysis:**  Meticulous analysis of medical records, including imaging studies and descriptions of injuries, is conducted to identify the specific injuries sustained, their severity, and locations. This information offers valuable clues about the forces and mechanisms involved in the injury event.
* **Static and Dynamic Testing:**  Exponent conducts both static and dynamic testing to investigate occupant kinematics and injury mechanisms. This includes using surrogates in static environments to evaluate factors like occupant excursion within restraints and using crash test dummies in dynamic rollover tests to measure forces and accelerations experienced during the event.
* **Injury Tolerance Data and Standards:** Exponent leverages extensive injury tolerance data gathered through decades of research, including cadaver testing, animal studies, crash test dummy experiments, real-world accident data, and non-injurious human volunteer testing.  This data is used to develop injury risk curves and correlate sensor measurements from crash tests to the likelihood of specific injuries.
* **Computational Modeling:**  While not always employed, computational models, including multibody and finite element models, can be utilized to simulate injury events and predict injury outcomes. This approach is gaining acceptance in regulatory environments and complements experimental methods.

This multidisciplinary approach, combining engineering, medical, and scientific expertise, allows Exponent to thoroughly investigate injury biomechanics, answer critical questions related to product safety, and provide comprehensive solutions in legal and regulatory contexts.
