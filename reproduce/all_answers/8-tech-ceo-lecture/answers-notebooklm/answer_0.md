The main research and development (R&D) priorities for Saint-Gobain are:

*   **Decarbonizing processes and systems:** Saint-Gobain aims to reduce their carbon footprint in the manufacturing process to align with their larger sustainability goals.
*   **Developing systems for light construction:** The company is pursuing innovative construction methods that reduce environmental impact.
*   **Circular economy:** They are heavily investing in ways to recycle materials and reduce waste, such as reusing demolition waste in the manufacturing process. 
*   **New applications for new materials:** Saint-Gobain is committed to researching and developing cutting-edge materials with novel applications in construction and related fields.

In addition to these four priorities, the company is prioritizing low carbon glass and plasterboard. They are also researching new uses for existing materials, such as using rice husk for plant energy.

They prioritize these innovations because they are facing challenges such as the urgency of climate change, the fragmented and sometimes conservative nature of the construction value chain, and the complexity of international regulations. To overcome these challenges, the company is focused on maximizing the positive impact of their products on homeowners, buildings, and the environment while simultaneously minimizing their own carbon footprint.
