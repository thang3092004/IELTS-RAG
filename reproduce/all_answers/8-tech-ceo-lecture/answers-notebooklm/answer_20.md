Rajesh Jain believes that **failure is an inherent part of the entrepreneurial journey** and that entrepreneurs should embrace it rather than fear it. He asserts that **most ventures fail**, and that entrepreneurs go out every day to reduce the risk of failure.  

Jain has experienced failure many times throughout his career, having failed over 30 times. He argues that **failure is a given when pursuing new ideas**, and that entrepreneurs should be comfortable with the idea that things will not always work out as planned. 

Here are some of Jain’s key ideas on the role of failure:

*   **Failure provides valuable lessons:** Every failure provides an opportunity to learn and grow. Entrepreneurs should reflect on their failures to understand what went wrong and how to avoid similar mistakes in the future.
*   **Failure is not the opposite of success:** Failure is simply a stepping stone on the path to success. Entrepreneurs should not be discouraged by their failures, but should instead use them as motivation to keep trying.
*   **Failure can lead to new opportunities:** Jain closed his image processing company when he realized it wasn’t going to work. This allowed him to start India World, a much more successful venture. 
*   **Entrepreneurs need to have a balance between persistence and knowing when to give up:** It's important to be persistent, but also to be able to recognize when an idea is not working. This can be difficult for entrepreneurs who are passionate about their ideas, but it's important to be able to detach emotionally and make rational decisions.
*   **Entrepreneurs should seek feedback from others:** People around entrepreneurs can often see their failures before they do. It's important to have a good feedback mechanism and to be able to listen to constructive criticism, even when it's difficult to hear.

Jain advises aspiring entrepreneurs to **imagine the worst-case scenario** and assess if they are willing to accept the cost of failure. He emphasizes the importance of **building a support system of friends and family** who can provide honest feedback and support. Jain believes that **by embracing failure, entrepreneurs can increase their chances of success and create something truly impactful.**
