Saint-Gobain, a company that focuses on light and sustainable construction, addresses three main environmental challenges in the built environment:

*   **CO2 emissions**: Buildings are responsible for 37% to 40% of global CO2 emissions. To address this, Saint-Gobain promotes **energy efficiency renovations**, which can reduce energy consumption by five to six times. The company also advocates for **light construction**, which can cut CO2 emissions by at least 50% by reducing the weight of building materials.
*   **Natural resource preservation**: To address the depletion of natural resources, Saint-Gobain focuses on **circular economy** practices. They aim to reuse materials in their construction processes, similar to ancient building methods. Saint-Gobain incorporates recycled materials in their products, such as **70% recycled glass in their European glass wool insulation and 50% recycled materials in their plasterboard**. They also strive to use sustainable energy sources like **hydropower in their manufacturing plants**.
*   **Population growth**: With a projected 2 billion population increase in developing countries over the next three decades, Saint-Gobain aims to build more efficiently and faster. They emphasize good planning and implementing sustainable practices from the start of construction.
