Saint-Gobain acknowledges that energy-efficient renovations can be expensive, especially for low-income homeowners who may be struggling to pay their energy bills. The company addresses these financial barriers by:

*  **Advocating for public support and subsidies specifically directed towards homes with low energy performance ratings.**  Saint-Gobain's CEO calls for a "Marshall Plan" to support retrofitting and suggests focusing subsidies on homes rated F and G, the lowest energy performance ratings. This targeted approach aims to reduce out-of-pocket expenses for low-income homeowners, making renovations more accessible. 

* **Highlighting the short payback period for energy-efficient renovations.**  With energy prices in Europe currently four to five times higher than in the US, the payback period for energy-efficient renovations has decreased to three to four years. Saint-Gobain emphasizes that the savings on energy bills over this short period can offset the initial renovation cost. 

* **Emphasizing the increase in real estate value that comes with energy-efficient upgrades.** Renovations not only reduce energy bills but also enhance the value of the property, providing a financial incentive for homeowners.

By advocating for targeted financial assistance, emphasizing the economic benefits, and highlighting the return on investment, Saint-Gobain aims to overcome financial barriers and encourage homeowners to embrace energy-efficient renovations.
