Saint-Gobain, a company that focuses on light and sustainable construction, aims to **maximize the impact of their sustainable solutions in buildings while minimizing their carbon footprint**. Here are some of the key elements of their approach:

*   **Reorganization by Country:** To better provide solutions for sustainable facades, acoustic performance, and other needs, Saint-Gobain reorganized from product line-based to country-based organization. This allows them to have the necessary building materials available in each country and manufacture them locally in their 800 plants around the world. 
*   **Energy Efficiency and Building Retrofits:** Saint-Gobain believes that a major aspect of sustainability comes from energy efficiency, especially for retrofits of existing buildings, which make up a large majority of the buildings that will exist in 2050. They are working on a number of solutions in this area, including retrofitting single-family homes in France, which has led to a 70% reduction in energy bills and CO2 emissions.
*   **Low-Carbon Products:** Saint-Gobain is also focusing on creating and improving their low-carbon products and processes. They launched the first-ever low-carbon glass, which uses more recycled glass and biogas in the manufacturing process to achieve a 40% reduction in CO2 emissions. They also opened the first zero-carbon plasterboard plant in Norway using hydropower and plan to open a second plant in Canada. 
*   **Circular Economy:** Saint-Gobain incorporates circular economy principles into their work. For example, they use 70% recycled materials for their glass wool insulation products in Europe and 50% recycled materials for their plasterboard products. They are also working on adjusting their manufacturing process parameters to better incorporate demolition waste in France.
*   **Carbon Neutrality:**  Saint-Gobain has committed to being carbon neutral by 2050 and is taking steps to reach that goal, including using hydrogen in a glass furnace in Germany, decreasing CO2 emissions by 27% over the past five years, and imposing an internal carbon price on all capital expenditures and R&D projects.

In addition to the above, they also work on measuring the impact of their solutions in areas like the acoustics of classrooms and on engaging all stakeholders, including homeowners, architects, and policymakers.
