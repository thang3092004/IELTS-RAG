Saint-Gobain made the commitment at the UN four years prior to the lecture to be **carbon neutral by 2050**. While that may seem far away, long-term capital planning is necessary in their industry. For example, a flat glass line lasts for 20 years and has to be rebuilt after that, with some lines lasting as long as 150 years with consistent rebuilding. Because of this long-term planning, by 2030, the company needs a plan in place to become carbon neutral by 2050. In fact, researchers and process engineers are already working to create a pilot by 2025 to achieve carbon neutrality on a flat glass line, which costs $150 million.  The company has already dropped total CO2 emissions by 27% and the carbon intensity per Euro of turnover has dropped by 42% over the past 5 years. The company believes it can take away so much carbon in its products and processes that it **does not consider buying carbon offsets**.
