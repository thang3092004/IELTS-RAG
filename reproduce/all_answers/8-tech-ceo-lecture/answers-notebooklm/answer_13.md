Saint-Gobain reorganized its company **from product lines to a country-based organization** five years before the lecture in source. The company did this so that each country CEO would have access to all of Saint-Gobain's building materials. This would allow them to **provide customers with a complete solution**, rather than individual products, better positioning them to **meet the company's sustainability commitments**. 

Here’s how the reorganization helped the company meet its sustainability commitments:

*   **System-focused solutions**: By having all building materials available in one place, country CEOs could design construction projects with sustainability in mind from the beginning. This is more effective than simply selling sustainable products, as it considers the entire system.
*   **Local expertise**: With country CEOs leading the charge, Saint-Gobain could better understand and respond to the specific needs and regulations of each market, ensuring their sustainability solutions were relevant and effective.
*   **Reduced transportation**: By manufacturing and sourcing materials locally, Saint-Gobain could reduce its carbon footprint from transportation.

The CEO states that this reorganization was a **“massive shift”** in organizational change, and that it was one of the key steps towards achieving the company’s sustainability goals. By focusing on system-level solutions, local expertise, and reduced transportation, Saint-Gobain has been able to **make significant progress in reducing its environmental impact**.
