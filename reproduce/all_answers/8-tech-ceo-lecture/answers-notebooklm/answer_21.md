Rajesh Jain, founder of Netcore Cloud, recommends entrepreneurs **focus on identifying problems worth solving, rather than becoming enamored by the technology or solution itself**. To identify and validate potential market opportunities, entrepreneurs can:

*   **Have conversations with potential customers to understand their needs and pain points.** Jain emphasizes the importance of understanding the language customers use to describe their problems and how they currently solve them. 
*   **Identify “blue ocean” opportunities within existing “red ocean” markets.** Jain encourages entrepreneurs to look for uncontested market spaces even within competitive industries. For example, Jain identified a “blue ocean” opportunity in improving customer retention within the crowded digital advertising market.
*   **Validate ideas by determining if enough customers are willing to pay for the proposed solution.** Jain believes the most important test of an idea is whether customers are willing to pay for it with either money or their time and attention.

Jain suggests entrepreneurs **read *The Innovator’s Solution* by Clayton Christensen to gain a deeper understanding of “jobs to be done”** framework. This framework encourages thinking about the underlying need a product fulfills for a customer, rather than focusing on the product itself. 

Jain also advises entrepreneurs to **attend conferences, mixers, and other events to connect with potential investors and customers**. These events can provide opportunities to network and pitch ideas to a wider audience.
