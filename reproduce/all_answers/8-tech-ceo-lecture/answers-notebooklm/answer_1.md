Saint-Gobain has implemented a variety of strategies to minimize its carbon footprint in the production of flat glass. 

*   **The company has pledged to be carbon neutral by 2050.** They have committed to building a carbon-neutral flat glass production plant by 2030. To achieve this ambitious goal, Saint-Gobain researchers and engineers are already working on a pilot project to be completed by 2025.  
*   **Saint-Gobain has achieved a world premiere by running a glass furnace with hydrogen in Germany.** 
*   **The company has developed the first low carbon glass offer.** The process reduces CO2 emissions by 40% in glass by increasing the amount of recycled glass used, using biogas, and modifying some of the process parameters.
*   **The company has implemented an internal carbon price since 2016.** The carbon price is factored into every capital expenditure and R&D project. The internal carbon price for capital expenditure is €100, above market price. For R&D projects, the price is €200, as they are further away in time and carbon prices are expected to increase.

In addition to these specific initiatives, Saint-Gobain is also working to reduce its carbon footprint by:

*   **Improving energy efficiency** in its manufacturing processes.
*   **Using more recycled materials** in its products.
*   **Developing new, more sustainable manufacturing processes.**

These efforts are part of Saint-Gobain's broader commitment to sustainability, which is reflected in its purpose of "making the world a better home."
