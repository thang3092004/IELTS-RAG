The '131' method is a problem-solving and delegation technique that encourages employees to take ownership of challenges and develop their leadership skills. Here's how it works and its benefits:

*   **1 Problem:** The employee brings **one** specific problem to their manager. This promotes focus and prevents overwhelming the manager with multiple issues at once.
*   **3 Options:** The employee presents **three** potential solutions to the problem. This demonstrates initiative, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills. The employee has already invested in exploring potential solutions, rather than relying on the manager to do all the work.
*   **1 Recommendation:** The employee recommends **one** of the three options as their preferred solution. This shows decision-making ability and ownership of the problem.

**Benefits of the 131 Method**

*   **Delegation:** It shifts the burden of problem-solving from the manager to the employee. The manager becomes a guide and mentor rather than a problem solver.
*   **Leadership Development:** It empowers employees to take ownership, think critically, and make decisions. This fosters leadership skills and prepares them for greater responsibility.
*   **Efficiency:** It streamlines the problem-solving process, focusing on solutions rather than simply presenting problems.

By encouraging employees to come prepared with potential solutions and a recommendation, the 131 method fosters a more proactive and efficient work environment. It promotes employee development and creates a more collaborative approach to problem-solving.
