Benoit Bazin, CEO of Saint-Gobain, proposes a "Marshall Plan" to address the challenges in the building retrofit sector, particularly focusing on dwellings with poor energy efficiency ratings (F and G). This plan draws inspiration from the historical Marshall Plan, which aimed to rebuild Europe after World War II.

Here's a breakdown of Bazin's proposed plan and its intended impact:

*   **Public Support for Retrofits:** The core of the plan involves directing public financial support towards the renovation of buildings with F and G energy ratings. These buildings often belong to or house low-income individuals who struggle with high energy bills.
*   **Reducing Out-of-Pocket Costs:** Bazin emphasizes the need to significantly reduce out-of-pocket expenses for homeowners undergoing energy efficiency renovations. He suggests that subsidies should cover a substantial portion of the costs, making retrofits more appealing and financially feasible for low-income households. 
*   **Addressing Housing Crisis:** The plan aims to prevent dwellings with poor energy ratings from becoming unrentable. By improving the energy performance of these buildings, the plan seeks to prevent them from exiting the rental market, mitigating the potential deepening of the housing crisis.
*   **Lowering CO2 Emissions:** The plan acknowledges that F and G-rated dwellings contribute significantly to CO2 emissions. By targeting these buildings for energy efficiency improvements, the plan intends to achieve substantial reductions in CO2 emissions.

The Marshall Plan proposed by Bazin aims to create a **"fair transition"** in the building retrofit sector. It recognizes the need to support low-income households in adopting energy-efficient solutions, while simultaneously contributing to broader societal goals of reducing CO2 emissions and alleviating the housing crisis.
