Saint-Gobain's organizational structure is designed to help the company achieve its sustainability goals. In the past, Saint-Gobain was organized by product lines. This meant that they were selling only pieces of a solution instead of complete building systems that could help advance sustainability. They reorganized by country so that each country CEO could offer all the products needed to make a sustainable building system like a facade, acoustic performance system for a classroom, etc..

**Reorganizing by country offered other benefits, including:**

*   **Empowering local CEOs to better service local markets.**
*   **Allowing Saint-Gobain to leverage local manufacturing plants.** They have 800 around the world and reorganizing by country keeps products from traveling long distances, which is better for sustainability.
*   **Hiring local CEOs who have knowledge of the region's needs.** 90% of country CEOs are natives of the country they manage.

**Saint-Gobain has also emphasized the importance of system-level thinking when it comes to sustainability.** The CEO even hired an architect to sit on the board of directors to challenge the company on its sustainability goals and push it to consider sustainable architecture in the future.
