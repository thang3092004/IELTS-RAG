The '$100 Delta decision rule' is a personal rule Rajesh Jain, founder of Netcore Cloud, uses to simplify decision-making. **Jain realized that he was often wasting time and energy agonizing over relatively small expenses.**

* To counter this, he created the '$100 Delta decision rule', which means that **if the difference in cost between two options is less than $100, he automatically chooses the more expensive option without further deliberation**. This eliminates time wasted on inconsequential decisions.

* Jain provided an anecdote where he was searching for a hotel room in New York. He wanted a room that cost under $200 but spent an hour searching without success. He realized that the extra $100 was insignificant compared to the cost of his trip and the value of his time. 

* Jain suggested that others **create their own threshold**, whether it’s $10 or $20, and apply this rule to simplify decisions and avoid wasting time. He applies this to purchasing books, reasoning that the cost is negligible compared to the time he’ll invest in reading.

The '$100 Delta decision rule' highlights the idea of **focusing on what’s truly important and letting go of minor decisions**.  It’s a practical approach to streamline decision-making and increase personal productivity.
