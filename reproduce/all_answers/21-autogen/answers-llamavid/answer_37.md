When using small open-source LLMs with less than 13 billion parameters for multi-agent systems, there are several challenges that can be encountered. First, small LLMs have a limited vocabulary and knowledge base compared to large models like GPT-3.5. This means they may struggle to generate complex, multi-step responses that require understanding of a wide range of concepts. Second, small LLMs are more prone to errors and incorrect information. They may provide answers that are factually incorrect or contain logical fallacies. Third, small LLMs may struggle to understand the context and nuances of the user's question, resulting in responses that are not tailored to the user's needs. Finally, small LLMs lack the ability to generate context and background information that is needed for multi-agent systems to be effective. This limits their ability to provide feedback to other agents and coordinate the workflow process.
