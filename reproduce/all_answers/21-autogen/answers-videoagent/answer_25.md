The concept of "flipping the script" involves changing the usual or expected course of events. In the context of understanding the evolution of AI agents, it can be applied by considering how AI has shifted from being a tool that follows human instructions to becoming an entity capable of learning, adapting, and making decisions autonomously. This shift represents a reversal of roles where AI agents are not just passive executors of tasks but active participants in problem-solving and decision-making processes. This evolution challenges traditional perspectives on control, creativity, and intelligence, prompting a reevaluation of how humans and AI interact and collaborate.
