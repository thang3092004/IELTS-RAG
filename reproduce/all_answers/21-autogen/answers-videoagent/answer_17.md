In an AutoGen workflow, the roles and responsibilities of different agents typically include:


1. User Proxy: The User Proxy acts as an intermediary between the user and the system. This agent is responsible for understanding user queries, translating them into actionable tasks, and ensuring that the system's responses align with user expectations. The User Proxy may also handle user authentication and manage user preferences.


2. Analyst: The Analyst is responsible for processing and analyzing data within the AutoGen system. This agent interprets the data, applies relevant algorithms or models, and generates insights or recommendations based on the analysis. The Analyst may also be involved in data validation and quality assurance.


3. Senior Analyst: The Senior Analyst oversees the analytical processes and ensures the accuracy and reliability of the results. This agent may provide guidance and support to the Analyst, review complex cases, and make high-level decisions. The Senior Analyst is also responsible for maintaining the integrity of the analytical framework and ensuring compliance with industry standards and best practices.


These roles may vary depending on the specific implementation and requirements of the AutoGen workflow.
