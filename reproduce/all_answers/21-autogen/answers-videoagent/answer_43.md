In AutoGen, prompting strategies for code generation and creative writing differ primarily in their focus and structure. For code generation, prompts are typically more structured and specific, often including explicit instructions, examples, and constraints to guide the model in producing syntactically correct and functional code. The emphasis is on precision, correctness, and adherence to programming standards.

In contrast, prompts for creative writing are generally more open-ended and flexible, encouraging the model to explore various narrative possibilities and styles. These prompts might include thematic elements, character descriptions, or initial story settings, but they allow for greater freedom in interpretation to foster creativity and originality. The focus is on engaging storytelling, emotional resonance, and imaginative expression.
