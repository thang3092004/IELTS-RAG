# Minimum IQ Requirement for Trading Success

The instructor emphasizes the importance of cognitive ability in successfully learning to trade. Specifically, they assert that a minimum IQ of **150** is necessary for individuals to effectively grasp the complexities involved in trading. This perspective indicates that a higher intellectual capacity is viewed as a critical requirement for navigating the intricacies of financial markets.

## Context of the Statement

In the discussions around trading, the instructor contrasts the notion that anyone can learn to trade without specific intellectual prerequisites. By stating that those with lower IQs or poor situational conditions, such as poverty, might find trading particularly challenging, the instructor underscores the need for a logical and analytical mindset in this high-risk domain. 

## Implications for Aspiring Traders

This high IQ benchmark serves as a guideline for potential traders, highlighting that while trading might seem accessible, it actually requires a unique set of cognitive skills and the ability to process complex information rapidly. Therefore, aspiring traders may benefit from self-assessment regarding their intellectual readiness alongside gaining relevant skills and strategies for successful trading. 

In summary, the instructor warns against the misconception that trading is easy and reiterates the importance of having a high IQ as a foundation for engaging in this demanding activity.
