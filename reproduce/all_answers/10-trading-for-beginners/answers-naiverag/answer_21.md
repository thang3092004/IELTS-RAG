When it comes to self-evaluation in trading, maintaining a comprehensive journal that goes beyond just technical performance is crucial. Here are several personal aspects traders should track:

### Emotional State
One of the most critical personal aspects to document is the emotional state during trades. Traders should consider noting how they felt on a scale from 1 to 10 regarding emotions such as fear, desperation, or confidence when entering a trade. This kind of tracking can help identify patterns in emotional responses that may impact decision-making. For instance, entering a trade while feeling overly desperate may lead to poor outcomes, whereas a sense of calm might correlate with better results.

### Decision-Making Process
Traders should also reflect on the decisions made leading up to and during each trade. This includes analyzing if the decisions were informed by their trading plan or if they deviated due to emotional prompts. Keeping track of moments when they deviated from the plan can provide insights into habitual issues that may require correction.

### Trade Evaluation
Each entry should contain an assessment of what went well and what went wrong during the trade. Traders can include specifics such as the execution of the trade, adherence to the trading strategy, and any mistakes made, such as not following stop-loss rules. This evaluation helps in recognizing areas of improvement and reinforces the importance of sticking to a plan.

### Learning and Adjustments
In addition to evaluating each trade’s performance, traders should document any actions taken to improve their skills or correct mistakes. This may involve noting additional research undertaken, new strategies tested, or educational materials consumed. By keeping a record of their learning journey, traders can track their evolution and development over time.

### Reflection and Goals
Finally, regular reflection on trading performance is vital. Traders can set short-term and long-term goals and assess their progress towards these objectives. Entries about personal development, motivational triggers, or obstacles faced can provide a well-rounded view of one’s trading journey and facilitate personal growth.

### Conclusion
Incorporating these personal aspects into a trading journal can enhance a trader's self-awareness and ultimately lead to better decision-making, emotional regulation, and improved performance. By tracking not just the technical details, but also emotional and cognitive processes, traders can develop a more holistic approach to their craft.
