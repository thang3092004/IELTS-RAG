A doji candle is a candlestick pattern in trading that indicates **indecision in the market**.  It is characterized by the **open and close prices being virtually the same**, resulting in a candle with a very small body or no body at all.

Here's why doji candles are significant:

* They represent a **stalemate between buyers and sellers**. Neither party was able to gain control of the market during the period represented by the candle.
* They often occur at **turning points in the market**, such as the top or bottom of a trend. This makes them a potential signal of a trend reversal, although not a guarantee.
* **Doji candles alone do not provide a trading edge**. However, they can be useful when combined with other technical analysis tools and indicators to confirm potential trading decisions. 

For example, a doji candle forming at a key support or resistance level, along with other confirming signals, could strengthen the case for a trade.
