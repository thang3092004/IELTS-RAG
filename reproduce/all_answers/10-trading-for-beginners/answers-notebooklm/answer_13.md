It is considered unwise to solely rely on trading strategies obtained from external sources because **traders have different personalities, risk tolerances, and trading styles**. What works for one trader may not work for another. To be a successful trader, you need to **develop a system that suits your individual needs and preferences.**  

Relying on external sources for trading strategies can also be problematic because:

* **You don't know how the strategy was developed or tested.** 
* **You don't have the mental capability to stick with the plan because you don't understand the probabilities involved.**
* **If the person providing the signals stops, you'll be left without a strategy.** 

Instead of relying on external sources, you should **focus on developing your own trading skills** by:

* **Learning the fundamentals of trading.** 
* **Testing and refining your own trading strategies.** 
* **Journaling your trades to track your progress and identify areas for improvement.** 

**Developing your own skills** will give you a **much better understanding of the market** and allow you to **make more informed trading decisions.**
