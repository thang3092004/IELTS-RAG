The instructor in the video "Trading for Beginners Part 1 - FULL TRADING COURSE TUTORIAL" says that a buy limit order and a buy stop order are both instructions given to a broker to execute a trade, but that **they differ in terms of the price point at which the trade is to be executed relative to the current trading price of an asset.**

*   A **buy limit** order is an instruction to buy an asset **if** the price **goes down to a specified limit** below the current trading price.
*   A **buy stop** order is an instruction to buy an asset **if** the price **goes up to a specified limit** above the current trading price.

The instructor gives the example of an asset whose current trading price is represented by a horizontal line on a chart. In the case of a **buy limit order**, the trader would set the limit below the line representing the current trading price, and the order would be executed if the price of the asset fell to that point. In the case of a **buy stop order**, the trader would set the limit above the current trading price, and the order would be executed if the price of the asset rose to that point.

The instructor emphasizes that **it's important for traders to understand how each type of order works** so that they can choose the right order for their trading strategy.
