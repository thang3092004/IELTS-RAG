Beyond technical performance, traders should track the following personal aspects in their journals for self-evaluation:

*   **How they felt on a scale of one to ten, in terms of how desperate they were to enter a trade, or how fearful they were to stay out of a trade.** Only enter trades when feeling a five or six on this scale.
*   **How they felt when placing and exiting trades.**
*   **Mistakes they made, including the details of the mistake and screenshots.**
*   **What actions they took to improve.**

The goal of this self-review is to trade a plan flawlessly, as determined by backtesting. Traders should note all trades that lose more than one percent of their account, especially if their trade plan dictates a one percent limit. Any deviation from the trade plan should be considered an error, and traders should aim for zero errors every single month.
