Besides identifying bullish and bearish trends, traders should also consider **ranging markets** before engaging in a trade setup. Ranging markets move sideways, bouncing between price highs and lows.  **Choppy and indecisive markets**, which are erratic and break structure, should be avoided. In such market conditions, traders don't have an edge. 

Here are the four main market conditions to be aware of: 

*   Bullish market 
*   Bearish market
*   Ranging market 
*   Choppy, indecisive market 

Trading strategies are most effective when applied to **bullish, bearish, or ranging markets.**  After identifying the market condition, traders need to **identify the market phase** to pinpoint high-probability entry points.  Phases include runs, which are extensions in the direction of the trend, and pullbacks, which are retracements or corrections before the trend continues.
