To accurately verify the order of price movements in a historical trading setup, it's crucial to analyze the price action on a lower time frame. Here's how:

* **Identify the specific candle** in the historical data where a potential trade setup occurred. Note the date and time of the candle.
* **Switch to a lower time frame chart**, such as the 15-minute or hourly chart, for the same currency pair.
* **Locate the exact time and date** of the identified candle on the lower time frame chart. This will allow you to see the detailed price movements within that candle.
* **Observe the price action** on the lower time frame chart. Determine if the price went up or down first, especially if there's any ambiguity about whether a stop-loss or entry was triggered first.

For example, imagine a situation where you're unsure if a trade would have resulted in a stop-loss or a profitable exit. By examining the lower time frame, you can see if the price went above the stop-loss level before triggering the entry order. If it did, then the trade would have been invalidated and you wouldn't have entered the trade.

This technique helps ensure accurate backtesting results by providing a clearer picture of how the price actually moved. It helps avoid mistakenly classifying a trade as a loss when it wouldn't have been taken in the first place.
