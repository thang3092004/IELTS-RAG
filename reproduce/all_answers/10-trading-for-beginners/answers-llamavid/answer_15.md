The length of a trade can significantly impact the potential accumulation of fees, such as rollover costs. Rollover costs are the fees charged by the broker for each trade made during the day. The longer the trade is open, the more opportunities there are for rollover costs to accumulate. For example, if a trade is open for 3 days, the broker will charge the fee for that day's trade, and then again for the next day's trade. The longer the trade is open, the more days there are to accumulate rollover costs. In contrast, if a trade is closed quickly, there are fewer opportunities for rollover costs to accumulate. This means that the longer a trade is open, the higher the potential fees. Therefore, shorter trades tend to accumulate fewer fees than longer trades.
