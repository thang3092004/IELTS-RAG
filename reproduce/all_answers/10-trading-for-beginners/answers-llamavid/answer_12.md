A "dumb donation" refers to the act of giving money to a trader or service without understanding the true cost of their fees. In the context of trading fees, this can mean giving money to a trader who charges a high fee, without properly considering the impact of these fees on the trader's returns over time. For example, if a trader charges a 1% fee and the trader performs identically to a fund with a 7% return, the trader would end up with 8.4 million while the fund would have 8.4 million. This difference of 3.2 million dollars is a "dumb donation" because the trader's fee is 7% of the trader's returns, while the fund's fee is only 1%. By not properly considering the true cost of fees, the trader may end up losing a significant portion of their returns over time.
