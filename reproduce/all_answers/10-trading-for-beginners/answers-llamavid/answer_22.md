A trading journal can be an invaluable tool for assessing the effectiveness of a chosen target placement strategy. By keeping track of all trades, including the entry price, stop loss, and target price, a trader can review their performance and identify areas for improvement. This can help them determine if their strategy is working as intended and if they need to adjust their approach. For example, if a trader's target placements are consistently too aggressive, they can identify this and adjust their position size accordingly. If they are not hitting their targets, they can look at their entry points to see if they are too far off the mark. By analyzing their journal data, a trader can identify patterns and trends in their performance and use this information to refine their strategy over time. A well-maintained trading journal can help a trader develop a better understanding of their own trading style and become a more consistent and profitable trader.
