In addition to tracking technical performance metrics like win rate, profit and loss, traders should also track personal aspects of their trading performance in their journals. Some of the key personal aspects that traders should consider tracking include:

1. Emotional performance - Traders should monitor their feelings of desperation or fear when entering a trade, and their ability to stay focused and manage their emotions. This can help identify when they need to adjust their trading approach. 

2. Mistakes - Traders should record any mistakes made, including errors in judgment or execution. This helps them identify areas for improvement.

3. Trade management - Traders should track how they manage their positions, such as when they take profits or cut losses, and how they adjust their position size. This helps them avoid overtrading.

4. Equity curve - Traders should track their equity curve, which shows their performance over time and can help them determine when to increase their position size.

5. Targets - Traders should track their targets and whether they hit them, which helps them determine if they are trading in an optimal manner.

6. Journaling - Traders should review their journal notes regularly to review their performance and make adjustments as needed.

In summary, traders should track personal aspects like emotional performance, mistakes, trade management, targets and journaling to gain insights into their trading performance and develop a better edge.
