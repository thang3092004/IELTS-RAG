Relying on trading strategies obtained from external sources can be unwise for several reasons. First, many of these strategies are not well-tested and may not perform as expected in real market conditions. The creator may have tested the strategy on historical data, but the market is constantly changing and different factors can impact performance. Second, the strategies are often not well-defined and lack clear rules for when to enter and exit a trade, making them difficult to implement correctly. This can lead to poor decision making. Third, the strategies are typically based on assumptions that do not always hold in real market conditions, which can lead to losses. Finally, many of these strategies are not tailored to the individual trader's goals, risk tolerance, and preferences, making them unsuitable. Ultimately, relying on strategies obtained from external sources can lead to disappointing results and the loss of money. It is important to develop a strategy that is well-defined, based on sound rules, and suited to the individual trader's needs.
