The 'ranging market' refers to a market where the price is moving in a choppy and erratic manner, with frequent bouncing between support and resistance levels. In technical terms, the price is not trending in a clear direction and instead is trading in a range-bound manner. The price can be seen as a tug of war between buyers and sellers, with neither side gaining the upper hand. The ranging market is characterized by frequent short-term pullbacks and bounces, with the market not moving in a consistent direction. In this context, a ranging market is one where the price does not trend consistently up or down, but instead moves in a zig-zag fashion between support and resistance levels.
