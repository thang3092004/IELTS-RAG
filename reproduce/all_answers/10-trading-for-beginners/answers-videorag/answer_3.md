In the instructional videos, the instructor differentiates between 'buy limit' and 'buy stop' orders by highlighting their distinct characteristics and applications within trading strategies.

### Buy Limit Order

A 'buy limit' order is defined as an instruction to the broker to purchase a security at a specified price or lower. This type of order is placed **below the current market price**, meaning that the trader believes the price of the asset will drop to that specific level before making a purchase. The fundamental premise behind a buy limit order is that the trader aims to get into a position at a more favorable price than what is currently available in the market. For instance, if a currency is trading at $100, and the trader sets a buy limit order at $95, the broker will execute the purchase once the price reaches or falls below $95.

### Buy Stop Order

Conversely, a 'buy stop' order is an instruction to purchase an asset once it surpasses a specific price level, which is set **above the current market price**. This type of order is utilized when a trader anticipates that the price will continue to rise if it breaks through a certain level, indicating bullish momentum. For example, if the current trading price is $100 and the trader believes that if the price rises above $105 it will continue to increase, they would set a buy stop order at $105. Once the price reaches this threshold, the order triggers, allowing the trader to enter the market as the price moves upward.

### Summary of Key Differences

1. **Position Relative to Market Price**:
   - **Buy Limit Order**: Placed below the current market price.
   - **Buy Stop Order**: Placed above the current market price.

2. **Execution Intent**:
   - **Buy Limit Order**: Used when the trader expects the price to fall, allowing them to buy at a lower price.
   - **Buy Stop Order**: Used when the trader believes the price will rise significantly once it exceeds a certain level.

3. **Market Behavior**:
   - **Buy Limit Order**: Triggers if the market price drops to the specified limit.
   - **Buy Stop Order**: Triggers if the market price rises to the specified level.

These distinctions help traders make informed decisions about their entry points into trades based on their market analysis and strategic outlook. Understanding these order types is crucial for managing risks and entering positions in a more controlled manner.
