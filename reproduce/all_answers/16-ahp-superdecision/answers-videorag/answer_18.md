To effectively define and utilize rating scales in SuperDecisions when implementing a ratings model, several systematic steps should be followed. This process ensures a structured approach to evaluating alternatives based on specific criteria. Below are the key steps to assist you in navigating this software effectively:

### Step 1: Create or Select Rating Scales

In SuperDecisions, rating scales are essential for quantifying the evaluation of different criteria. You can either create new scales or use existing ones:

- **Utilizing Existing Scales**: You can load a predefined scale from files (typically with the .rcp extension). To do this, navigate to the scales section in the software, select "Load from file," and choose an appropriate existing scale that fits your evaluation criteria. For example, scales such as "Excellent," "Very Good," "Good," "Average," "Below Average," and "Poor" are commonly used.
- **Creating New Scales**: If the predefined scales do not meet your needs, you can define new scales by selecting options to create a custom scale. When doing this, clearly label your scale (e.g., “Quality Assessment”) and choose the corresponding values for each rating level.

### Step 2: Define Rating Criteria

Each criterion under which the alternatives will be evaluated needs to be explicitly defined:

- Begin by selecting the main criteria relevant to your analysis (e.g., Cost, Performance, Reliability).
- As you define each criterion, ensure that you associate them with the corresponding rating scales you intend to use. This is vital for maintaining consistency across your assessments.

### Step 3: Input Judgments for Alternatives

Once the rating scales and criteria are set up, the next step involves inputting judgments for each alternative:

- Navigate to the "Ratings Table" within SuperDecisions and locate the section where alternatives are displayed alongside the selected criteria.
- For each alternative, assess its performance against each criterion and assign the appropriate rating based on your predefined scales. For instance, if assessing a candidate for a position based on 'Experience,' you might rate them as “Excellent” or “Good” based on your evaluations.

### Step 4: Pairwise Comparison (if needed)

In situations where the ratings model requires it, you might consider conducting pairwise comparisons to validate the assigned ratings:

- Use the pairwise comparison feature to compare different alternatives against each criterion. This can further refine the scores by establishing relative priorities among choices.
- The outcomes from pairwise comparisons can help adjust your initial ratings based on relative importance, helping to enhance the model’s robustness.

### Step 5: Synthesize Results

After entering all the necessary information and evaluations into the system, the next step is synthesis:

- Click on the “Synthesize” button to generate an overview of prioritization based on the assigned ratings.
- The output will typically present a matrix representing the priorities and may include graphical representations for better visual analysis.

### Step 6: Review and Adjust

Finally, review the synthesized results and ensure they align with your expectations:

- If necessary, make adjustments to individual ratings or scales based on insights gained from the synthesized data.
- Regularly check for consistency and reliability in your ratings model, particularly after updates or changes in input data.

### Conclusion

Utilizing rating scales in SuperDecisions involves a systematic approach—starting with defining and creating scales, assessing alternatives against defined criteria, and synthesizing results to aid decision-making. By carefully managing rating definitions and ensuring a broad understanding of the scales and their applications, you can enhance the effectiveness of your evaluations in any decision-making context. Regularly revisit the setup to ensure it remains relevant to the criteria you are assessing and the alternatives being considered.
