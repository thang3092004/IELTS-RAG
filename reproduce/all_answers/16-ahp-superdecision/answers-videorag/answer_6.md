## Incorporating Sub-Criteria into AHP Hierarchy Using Super Decisions Software

The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) allows for a structured approach to decision-making by breaking down complex problems into a hierarchy of goals, criteria, sub-criteria, and alternatives. Utilizing Super Decisions software enhances this method through its visual and systematic functionalities. Here, we detail the steps to incorporate sub-criteria into your AHP hierarchy effectively using this software.

### 1. **Starting with a New Model**

Begin by creating a new model within Super Decisions. You can do this by navigating to the software’s main interface and selecting the option to start a new project. Label your model appropriately (for example, "Job Selection" or "Car Selection") to reflect the decision-making scenario you are analyzing.

### 2. **Creating the Goal Node**

Once you have a new model, the first step is to define your primary goal. This goal acts as the apex of your hierarchy. For instance, if your primary goal is to "Select the Best Job," you would create a node labeled “Best Job” in the model.

### 3. **Adding Criteria Nodes**

Next, you add criteria nodes that correspond to the various factors considered in your decision. These criteria may include “Salary,” “Work Environment,” and “Growth Opportunities.” These nodes will serve as the next layer of your hierarchy, each representing different dimensions of the decision.

### 4. **Establishing Sub-Criteria**

To incorporate sub-criteria, select the relevant criteria node and create additional clusters or nodes beneath it. For instance, under the "Work Environment" criterion, you might add sub-criteria such as "Remote Work Flexibility" and "Company Culture." This hierarchical structuring allows for detailed comparisons between the sub-criteria in relation to their parent criteria.

#### **Interactivity:**

Within Super Decisions, use the following steps to add sub-criteria:
- Click on the relevant criteria node to open its properties.
- Choose the option to “Add Cluster” or “New Node” and name the node according to the sub-criterion.
- Save these changes to organize your model effectively.

### 5. **Connecting Criteria and Sub-Criteria to Alternatives**

Once both the criteria and sub-criteria have been established, create connections to the alternatives you’re evaluating (e.g., different job offers or car models). This step is essential as it allows you to assess how each alternative measures up against the criteria and sub-criteria you’ve set.

### 6. **Performing Pairwise Comparisons**

After establishing your hierarchy, the next step is to conduct pairwise comparisons. In Super Decisions, navigate to the "Judgments" tab and select the nodes (both criteria and sub-criteria) to begin the comparison process:
- Assess the importance of each sub-criterion relative to its main criterion.
- Determine the relative importance of alternative choices based on the criteria and sub-criteria.

This structured comparison technique employs a numerical scale (often 1 to 9) to express preferences, further refining the decision-making process.

### 7. **Synthesizing Results**

Finally, synthesize the results through the "Computation" options. Super Decisions facilitates the analysis of how well each alternative meets the established criteria and sub-criteria using algorithms that aggregate the data from pairwise comparisons. The software then provides a ranking of the alternatives based on the synthesizing process.

### Conclusion

Incorporating sub-criteria into the AHP hierarchy using Super Decisions software is a straightforward yet crucial step in enhancing decision quality. By systematically structuring the decision-making process and leveraging the powerful analytical capabilities of Super Decisions, you can derive robust, informed choices that are reflective of detailed evaluations. This method not only clarifies complex decisions but also ensures that every relevant factor has been duly considered in the final assessment.
