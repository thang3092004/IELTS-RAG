## Understanding Ratings and Intensities in the Absolute Measurement Process of AHP

The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured technique used for decision-making, particularly when dealing with complex problems involving multiple criteria. In this context, absolute measurement plays a significant role, especially through the use of ratings and intensities to evaluate alternatives.

### Role of Ratings in AHP

In the absolute measurement process of AHP, ratings are employed to quantify how well various alternatives meet specific criteria. Ratings provide a numerical representation of qualitative assessments, allowing for comparisons that facilitate decision-making. The process typically involves the following steps:

1. **Establishment of Criteria**: Initially, a set of criteria relevant to the decision at hand is established. For instance, in the selection of candidates for an MBA program, criteria such as CGPA, work experience (WE), letters of recommendation (LOR), and extracurricular activities (EA) might be considered.

2. **Rating Alternatives Against Criteria**: Each alternative is then rated against these established criteria. For example, in the context of student admissions, each candidate would receive a score based on how well they perform in each criterion. These scores are then recorded in a ratings matrix.

3. **Utilization of Superdecision Software**: The Superdecision software is often used to implement these ratings. The software allows users to define a rating scale for each criterion, which can either be an existing scale, a newly created scale, or an edited version of an existing one. This flexibility ensures that the chosen scale accurately reflects the decision-maker's evaluation mechanism.

### Understanding Intensities

Intensities, in this context, refer to the degree of preference assigned to each criterion when making comparisons. AHP allows decision-makers to assess how much more important one criterion is over another. For example, in evaluating car models based on criteria like price, mileage per gallon (MPG), and comfort, decision-makers can express intensity preferences. 

1. **Rating Intensity**: These intensity ratings help in prioritizing criteria and alternatives. The decision-maker can indicate, for instance, that price is significantly more important than comfort, leading to a higher intensity score for price. The intensity scores help create a hierarchy of priorities among the criteria.

2. **Mathematical Representation**: Intensity scores often take the form of values within a predefined range, which could signify how many times more important one criterion is compared to another. These scores are essential for synthesizing the overall priority of alternatives, which is a critical outcome of the AHP.

### Conclusion

In summary, ratings and intensities serve as fundamental components in the absolute measurement process of AHP. Ratings provide a structured way to evaluate how well alternatives satisfy criteria, while intensities help establish the importance of these criteria relative to one another. By integrating these elements through software like Superdecision, decision-makers can derive comprehensive insights that lead to informed decisions across various applications, from resource allocation to candidate selection.
