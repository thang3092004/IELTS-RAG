When converting an Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) model to an Analytic Network Process (ANP) model, several new connections need to be established to adequately capture the interdependencies between elements within the decision-making framework. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

### New Connections in ANP Models

1. **Removal of the Goal Node**: One of the primary changes in the ANP model is the removal of the goal node that is typically present in an AHP model. ANP does not feature a single overarching goal; instead, it allows for multiple clusters of criteria and alternatives.

2. **Connecting Alternatives to Criteria**: In an AHP model, criteria are often evaluated independently against a goal, while in an ANP model, there exists a need for direct connections between alternatives and criteria. This means that each alternative must be directly linked to every criterion to assess how each alternative performs with respect to the different criteria.

3. **Interdependencies Between Criteria**: ANP acknowledges that criteria can influence each other. Therefore, in this model, connections should be established not only between alternatives and criteria but also directly among criteria, allowing for evaluation of how the importance of one criterion may affect another.

4. **Feedback Loops**: These additional connections facilitate the creation of feedback loops, where the evaluation of one criterion can impact the weight and priority of others. This reflects the reality that decision-making often involves complex interactions among various factors, rather than a strictly linear hierarchy.

### Reflecting Interdependence

The connections made in the transition from AHP to ANP highlight the interdependence concept in several key ways:

- **Influence of Criteria on Alternatives**: By allowing alternatives to be connected to multiple criteria, the decision-making process reflects real-world scenarios where options are evaluated comparatively based on various factors. For instance, factors like price and comfort can significantly alter the perceived value of a car model based on individual consumer priorities.

- **Dynamic Interactions**: ANP models enable decision-makers to see how changing one criterion's importance can alter the evaluations of alternatives in real time. This dynamic interaction is essential in many applications, where the priority of one aspect may shift based on external influences or internal organizational shifts.

- **Complex Networks**: The network structure of ANP models captures the complexity and interrelatedness of the factors at play, enabling a more holistic analysis when making decisions. The ability to account for these relationships enhances the robustness of the model, allowing for better-informed decision-making and prioritization.

### Conclusion

Converting from AHP to ANP involves establishing crucial new connections that quantitatively and qualitatively reflect the complex interdependencies among criteria and alternatives. This transition promotes a more nuanced approach to decision-making, allowing for comprehensive evaluations that better represent the intricate nature of options and their influencing factors.
