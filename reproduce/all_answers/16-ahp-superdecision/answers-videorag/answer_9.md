To conduct sensitivity analysis for an Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) model using SuperDecisions software, follow these steps:

### Step 1: Load your AHP Model
- Begin by launching the SuperDecisions software and opening an existing AHP model or creating a new one.
- If you are starting from scratch, ensure that you've successfully structured your model with the goal, criteria, and alternatives specified.

### Step 2: Access Sensitivity Analysis Features
- Navigate to the menu bar at the top of the interface.
- Click on **Computations**, then select **Sensitivity** to access the sensitivity analysis tools.
- Depending on your needs, you may choose between **AHP Sensitivity** for static analysis or **Dynamic Sensitivity** for more advanced analysis.

### Step 3: Conduct Static Sensitivity Analysis
- For initial studies, select the **AHP Sensitivity** command.
- You will see graphical outputs that display how the weights of different criteria influence the ranking of alternatives. This typically includes a graphical representation with ranked alternatives and their respective weights.
- Analyze the output to determine how changes in the importance of specific criteria (like price or comfort) can affect the overall decision.

### Step 4: Identify Key Influential Factors
- Examine the results to identify criteria that significantly affect the alternative rankings. Look for "rank reversal" points in the graphs where the order of preferences changes due to changes in weights. This insight can help you understand which criteria are most critical in influencing decisions.

### Step 5: Perform Dynamic Sensitivity Analysis
- After the static analysis, you can delve into **Dynamic Sensitivity Analysis**. This can be selected through the **Node Sensitivity** option under the **Computations** menu.
- This feature allows you to adjust the weights of criteria and immediately observe how the priorities of alternatives change dynamically.
- This analysis may utilize sliders or input fields to alter the parameter values in real time for more interactive exploration.

### Step 6: Interpret Results
- Once you have conducted both static and dynamic sensitivity analyses, interpret the results.
- Consider how robust your model is and how sensitive it is to changes in the priority of specific criteria.
- Keep notes of significant findings that may affect your decision-making process.

### Step 7: Document Findings
- After conducting the analyses, ensure to document the findings clearly.
- This documentation is essential for informing other stakeholders or for future reference to improve the decision-making model over time.

### Conclusion
These steps encapsulate the process for performing sensitivity analysis in SuperDecisions for an AHP model. The sensitivity analysis not only enhances understanding of model robustness but is instrumental in influencing strategic decisions based on varying criteria priorities.
