## Understanding the Unweighted Supermatrix in AHP

The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured method for organizing and analyzing complex decisions. One of the critical components of AHP is the unweighted supermatrix, which serves as a pivotal tool in synthesizing information from various criteria to facilitate effective decision-making.

### What is the Unweighted Supermatrix?

The unweighted supermatrix is a mathematical representation of the relative priorities of different criteria or alternatives within a decision-making model. It consists of a matrix where the rows represent alternatives and the columns represent criteria. Each element in the matrix indicates how much one alternative contributes to a particular criterion, without any prior weighting applied. This initial setup allows for direct comparisons between relevant alternatives before any adjustments are made for priority or importance.

### How is the Unweighted Supermatrix Generated in Super Decisions?

1. **Input Judgments**: In Super Decisions software, users initially input their judgments regarding the performance of each alternative against the defined criteria. This is typically done through pairwise comparisons, where users assess the relative importance of different elements, forming a basis for the unweighted supermatrix.

2. **Matrix Construction**: Once the judgments are entered, Super Decisions generates the unweighted supermatrix by compiling these comparisons. This step involves organizing the judgments into a matrix format where each entry reflects the comparative judgment made by the user.

3. **Normalization**: At this stage, the software calculates the normalized values from the raw scores that have been input. This entails summing the values for each column and then dividing each entry by the sum to ensure all entries reflect a ratio of the total, totaling to one in each column. This normalization is crucial for achieving a fair representation of the priorities under consideration.

4. **Visual Representation**: The matrix is visually represented within the software interface, allowing users to easily view how alternatives are compared across various criteria. Users may also access visual aids or annotations that help highlight key priorities and comparisons made within the unweighted supermatrix.

5. **Validation**: Before proceeding, Super Decisions emphasizes the importance of verifying that all weights across the criteria are equal to ensure the integrity of comparisons made later on. This verification process ensures that subsequent analysis maintains fairness and accuracy.

6. **Transition to Weighted Supermatrix**: Once the unweighted supermatrix is established and validated, it can be transformed into a weighted supermatrix by applying the priority weights derived from user judgments. This transformation enables the incorporation of the relative importance of each criterion into the overall decision-making framework, leading eventually to a comprehensive synthesis of all alternatives.

### Conclusion

In summary, the unweighted supermatrix is a foundational element of the AHP methodology utilized in the Super Decisions software. It begins with user-inputted judgments and culminates in a structured, normalized matrix that facilitates a clear assessment of relative priorities among alternatives. By ensuring that all comparisons are fairly represented, Super Decisions aids users in navigating complex decision-making scenarios effectively.
