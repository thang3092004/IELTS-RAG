Establishing connections between different elements in an Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) hierarchy using SuperDecisions software involves a systematic approach to link goals, criteria, and alternatives. Here’s a structured guide on how to do this effectively:

### Step 1: Set Up Your Model
Initially, ensure that your AHP model is set up correctly within SuperDecisions. You’ll begin by creating clusters to represent different layers of your decision-making process—specifically the "Goal," "Criteria," and "Alternatives."

- **Create Clusters**: Use the “Add Cluster” option to define your main clusters. You typically have three: one for your goal, one for your criteria, and one for your alternatives.

### Step 2: Add Nodes to Clusters
After creating your clusters, you need to populate them with nodes that represent various components.

- **Add Nodes**: Access each cluster using the "Add Node" option and input the relevant nodes. For example, under the "Criteria" cluster, you can add nodes like "Price," "Comfort," "Miles Per Gallon," etc.

### Step 3: Establish Connections
Once your nodes are set up, it's time to create connections between them to show how they relate to the overall decision-making process.

- **Connect the Goal to Criteria**: Initially, connect your goal node to each of the criterion nodes. This establishes the relationship indicating that the criteria contribute towards achieving the goal.

- **Connect Criteria to Alternatives**: Next, establish connections from each criterion node to the alternative nodes. This is essential for comparing how well each alternative meets the respective criteria. In SuperDecisions, this is done by:

  1. Selecting a criterion node.
  2. Using the connection function (often denoted by a line or an arrow) to draw lines from that criterion to each relevant alternative.
  
### Step 4: Verify Connections
It's important to ensure there are no mistakes such as self-loops or incorrect links.

- **Check Connections**: After establishing all your connections, visually confirm that they represent the correct relationships—each criterion should connect to all applicable alternatives without looping back to itself or other criteria.

- **Use Connection Modes**: Enable connection mode if available within the software, which helps highlight or validate connections made.

### Step 5: Review and Save Your Model
After connecting all elements:

- **Finalize Your Model**: Review your network to ensure that everything is correctly linked and accurately reflects your decision-making framework.
- **Save Your Work**: Always save your model before proceeding to perform analyses, such as calculating priorities or synthesizing results.

### Conclusion
Using SuperDecisions for AHP modeling allows you to systematically establish connections between different elements. By carefully defining your clusters, adding nodes, and linking them appropriately, you can create a robust decision-making model. This structured approach not only aids in visualizing the relationships but also enhances the accuracy of your subsequent analyses. 

With these steps, you should be able to effectively use SuperDecisions to create and manage an AHP model tailored to your specific decision-making needs.
