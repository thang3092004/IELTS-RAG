In SuperDecisions, the process of synthesizing a model with sub-criteria differs significantly from a model without sub-criteria owing to the hierarchical complexity and the methods of comparison involved. Below are some key distinctions:

### Synthesis in Models with Sub-Criteria

1. **Hierarchical Structure**: 
   - In a model with sub-criteria, the structure becomes multi-tiered. Criteria are linked to various sub-criteria, which provide more granular detail about the considerations under each main criterion. For example, if "Comfort" is a criterion, sub-criteria might include "Driving Performance" and "Ride Quality." This hierarchy necessitates careful consideration of how each sub-criterion influences the overall criterion, leading to a detailed synthesis process.

2. **Pairwise Comparisons and Judgments**: 
   - When synthesizing with sub-criteria, users perform pairwise comparisons not just among the main criteria but also among the sub-criteria. This involves determining the relative importance of the sub-criteria to their respective parent criterion, which adds layers of complexity and requires additional judgments that must be made per criterion.

3. **Normalization of Priorities**: 
   - After creating pairwise comparisons, the next step involves normalizing the resulting priorities. In a model with sub-criteria, priorities are synthesized at several levels. They must be aggregated first at the sub-criteria level before they can influence the main criteria. Normalization processes will ensure that the importance of each sub-criterion affects its parent criterion proportionally.

4. **Synthesis of Global Priorities**: 
   - The synthesis concludes with the calculation of global priorities that reflect the outcomes based on both the main criteria and their corresponding sub-criteria. This means synthesizing results at multiple levels, leading to overall priorities that represent a big-picture view of the decision context.

### Synthesis in Models without Sub-Criteria

1. **Simpler Structure**: 
   - Models without sub-criteria tend to operate on a flat structure where each criterion connects directly to alternatives without intermediate nodes. This linear approach simplifies both interpretation and handling of the model.

2. **More Straightforward Comparisons**: 
   - In this case, users only need to perform pairwise comparisons between larger groups of criteria and their alternatives. The lack of sub-criteria means fewer comparisons are needed, simplifying the judgment input process.

3. **Direct Priority Calculation**: 
   - Priorities in a model without sub-criteria are calculated directly from the comparisons made at the criterion level without needing further normalization of sub-level data. This single-layer approach allows for faster syntheses as the overall priority for alternatives derives directly from criteria rankings.

4. **Less Complexity in Results Interpretation**: 
   - Since no hierarchical relationships with sub-criteria exist, interpreting the synthesized results can be more straightforward. The results will readily show how alternatives rank against the main criteria without additional layers of complexity.

### Conclusion

In summary, synthesizing a model with sub-criteria in SuperDecisions involves a more complex hierarchical structure, necessitating a multi-level approach to comparisons, normalization, and synthesis of priorities. In contrast, models without sub-criteria are simpler, leading to a more straightforward synthesis process. This complexity is crucial for providing a nuanced evaluation that can enhance decision-making by capturing additional layers of criterion relationships.
