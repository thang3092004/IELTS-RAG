The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a decision-making technique used to evaluate alternatives by breaking them down into criteria, sub-criteria, and alternatives. In AHP, global priorities of alternatives can be calculated by creating a super matrix that combines the priorities of all the criteria. 


To calculate the global priorities of alternatives in AHP using Super Decisions, first create an AHP model with criteria, alternatives, and sub-criteria. Then, in the judgment section, assign the priorities to the criteria and alternatives based on the judgments entered. Finally, in the synthesis section, the priorities can be calculated by using the super matrix.


The global priorities of alternatives calculated in this way can then be used to rank the alternatives, inform decision making, and evaluate the effectiveness of the decision.
