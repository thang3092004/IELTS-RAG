The unweighted supermatrix is a key component of the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) model. It is a matrix that shows the priority weights for each criteria and alternative. It is generated from the priority judgments made by the decision maker. In Super Decisions, the unweighted supermatrix is used to calculate the priorities and prioritized alternatives. The priority weights for each criteria are multiplied together to get the total priority weight. The priorities and prioritized alternatives are then used to calculate the overall priority weights for each alternative.
