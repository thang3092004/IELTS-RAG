The process of synthesizing an Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) model in SuperDecisions involves several structured steps that facilitate the evaluation of complex decision-making scenarios. This approach allows users to derive global priorities for alternatives based on a hierarchical breakdown of the decision problem.

### Initial Setup and Hierarchy Definition
The first step in synthesizing an AHP model is defining the goal of the decision-making process. This goal serves as the top-level focus from which the entire model is structured. Subsequently, users establish a hierarchy that includes criteria and sub-criteria relevant to the decision at hand, followed by alternatives that meet these criteria. This hierarchical arrangement ensures clarity regarding how different elements interact and influence the overall decision.

### Pairwise Comparisons
After establishing the hierarchy, users engage in pairwise comparisons of the criteria and sub-criteria to assess their relative importance. This is conducted using the SuperDecisions software, where users input numerical judgments that indicate how much more important one criterion is over another on a scale (commonly from 1 to 9). These comparisons are crucial since they convert subjective assessments into quantitative values, creating a weighted system that prioritizes criteria based on their significance toward achieving the decision goal.

Once the criteria are established, the next phase involves pairwise comparisons of the alternatives under each criterion. Users again input their preferences, generating a matrix that quantifies the relative performance of each alternative based on each specific criterion.

### Calculation of Local and Global Priorities
Upon completing the pairwise comparisons, SuperDecisions calculates local priorities or weights for each criterion and alternative. This computation includes normalizing the comparison results and deriving priority scores that represent each element’s contribution towards the primary goal of the AHP model.

The local priorities for both criteria and alternatives are then synthesized to yield global priority scores. This synthesis process uses mathematical techniques, such as the fundamental eigenvector method, to integrate the local priorities into a single global priority score for each alternative. This final ranking helps stakeholders identify which alternative offers the best overall benefit in relation to the defined goal.

### Sensitivity Analysis
Post-synthesis, users may conduct sensitivity analysis within SuperDecisions to assess how changes in the criteria weights or alternative ratings affect the overall global priorities. This analysis is fundamental for validating the robustness of the results, ensuring that decision-makers have comprehensive insights into the implications of their judgments.

### Conclusion
In conclusion, synthesizing an AHP model in SuperDecisions entails establishing a clear hierarchy, conducting pairwise comparisons to derive local priorities, and ultimately synthesizing these into global priorities for the alternatives. This systematic method not only structures decision-making but also enhances the reliability of the prioritized outcomes, enabling informed and confident decisions.
