To effectively connect criteria and sub-criteria to alternatives in the SuperDecisions software, a structured process must be followed. This process involves a series of steps aimed at establishing a clear hierarchy and ensuring that each alternative is appropriately linked to the relevant criteria. The following outlines these steps:

### Step 1: Establish Top-Level Criteria
Initially, users shall define the top-level criteria that represent the primary goals or objectives of the decision-making process. These criteria could include factors such as health impacts, socio-economic implications, or product-specific features relevant to the context of assessment.

### Step 2: Develop Sub-Criteria
The next step involves breaking down the main criteria into sub-criteria. This deeper analysis allows for a more detailed evaluation framework by identifying specific components related to each criterion. For example, a health impact criterion could be subdivided into aspects like accessibility, effectiveness, and cost.

### Step 3: Create a Hierarchical Structure
Utilizing the hierarchical capabilities of the SuperDecisions software, users shall create a structured model that connects the main criteria to their corresponding sub-criteria, and ultimately, to the alternatives being evaluated. This hierarchical representation enables visualization of relationships and ensures each alternative is assessed against the relevant criteria.

### Step 4: Link Alternatives to Sub-Criteria
Once the hierarchical structure is established, users must link each alternative to the appropriate sub-criteria. This connection is crucial for assessing how each alternative fulfills the specific needs outlined by the sub-criteria. Each alternative must be relevant and capable of addressing the overarching goals defined by the main criteria.

### Step 5: Validate Connections
It is vital to verify that all connections—between the main criteria, sub-criteria, and alternatives—are accurate and logically sound. This validation ensures the integrity of the decision-making process, as inaccuracies in linkage may lead to flawed evaluations.

### Step 6: Sensitivity Analysis
After establishing all connections, users may conduct sensitivity analyses to assess how changes in criteria weights or evaluations impact the rankings of alternatives. This step helps users understand the robustness of their evaluations and reaffirms the proper linkage within the hierarchy.

### Step 7: Review and Refine the Model
Finally, users should review the hierarchical model and refine the connections as necessary. This may involve adjustments based on feedback to optimize the evaluation process, ensuring clarity and effectiveness in decision-making.

By following these structured steps, users will create a comprehensive and cohesive evaluation framework in SuperDecisions that allows for systematic analysis of alternatives based on clearly established criteria and sub-criteria.
