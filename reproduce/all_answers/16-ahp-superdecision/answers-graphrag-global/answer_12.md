# Creating an AHP Hierarchy in SuperDecisions Software

The process of establishing an Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) hierarchy within the SuperDecisions software is methodical and involves multiple key steps. This structured approach aids in systematic decision-making by assessing various alternatives based on defined criteria. 

## Step 1: Define the Goal

The foremost step involves articulating the **goal** that the decision-making process aims to achieve. This overarching objective shall serve as the foundation for the hierarchy, guiding all subsequent steps and ensuring that all criteria and alternatives are aligned toward fulfilling this aim. A well-defined goal crystallizes the purpose of the evaluations and comparisons to follow.

## Step 2: Establish the Criteria

Once the goal is set, users shall proceed to define the **criteria** that will serve as benchmarks for evaluating the alternatives. These criteria must encompass both qualitative and quantitative measures relevant to the goal, providing a comprehensive framework for analysis. It is crucial that the criteria be ranked based on their relative importance, as this ranking will influence the assessments of the alternatives and ultimately the decision outcome.

## Step 3: Identify and Input Alternatives

With the criteria established, the next essential step is to identify and enter the **alternatives** that will be evaluated. These alternatives represent the different options available for achieving the defined goal. Each alternative shall be assessed against the established criteria, allowing for a careful consideration of how well each option meets the defined goal.

## Step 4: Conduct Pairwise Comparisons

SuperDecisions facilitates the process of conducting **pairwise comparisons** among the criteria and alternatives. This step is vital as it quantifies the relative importance of each criterion and evaluates how well each alternative satisfies these criteria. Through pairwise comparisons, users can create a priority vector, which enhances decision-making by clarifying which alternative is most suitable based on the structured analysis.

## Step 5: Generate Scoring and Visual Representation

After the pairwise comparisons, the software processes the data to produce an **overall score** for each alternative, along with a priority ranking for the criteria. Users may leverage visualization tools within SuperDecisions to clearly present the hierarchy and relationships among criteria and alternatives, facilitating better understanding and communication of the decision-making framework.

## Step 6: Perform Consistency Checks and Sensitivity Analysis

To ensure the integrity of the AHP hierarchy, users should utilize features such as consistency checks and **sensitivity analysis**. Consistency checks, such as the Inconsistency Report, allow users to confirm that their evaluations are coherent, while sensitivity analysis helps assess how changes in criteria weights or scores might impact the final decision-making outcome.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, creating an AHP hierarchy in SuperDecisions encompasses a structured series of steps: defining the goal, establishing criteria, identifying alternatives, conducting pairwise comparisons, generating scores, and performing checks and analyses to ensure a robust decision-making process. This systematic methodology enhances clarity and ensures that decisions are made based on a well-rounded assessment of all pertinent factors.
