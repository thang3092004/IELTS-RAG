In the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) using Super Decisions software, global priorities of alternatives are calculated by first establishing a hierarchy of criteria and alternatives. Pairwise comparisons are made at each level of the hierarchy to determine local priorities. These local priorities are then synthesized by multiplying them by the priorities of their respective criteria and summing them across all criteria to obtain the global priorities of the alternatives. The Super Decisions software facilitates this process by providing tools for inputting judgments, calculating priorities, and performing consistency checks.
