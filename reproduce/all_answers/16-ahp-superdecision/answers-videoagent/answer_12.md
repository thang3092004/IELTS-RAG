In SuperDecisions software, creating an AHP (Analytic Hierarchy Process) hierarchy involves several steps:


1. **Define the Goal**: Start by clearly defining the main objective or goal of the decision-making process. This goal will be at the top of the hierarchy.


2. **Identify Criteria**: Determine the criteria that will be used to evaluate the alternatives. These criteria should be relevant to the goal and can be organized into sub-criteria if needed. They form the second level of the hierarchy.


3. **List Alternatives**: Identify the possible alternatives or options that will be evaluated against the criteria. These alternatives are placed at the bottom level of the hierarchy.


4. **Construct the Hierarchy**: Use the SuperDecisions software to visually construct the hierarchy by placing the goal at the top, followed by the criteria and sub-criteria, and finally the alternatives at the bottom.


5. **Pairwise Comparisons**: Conduct pairwise comparisons of the criteria and alternatives to determine their relative importance or preference. This involves comparing each element against others at the same level with respect to their impact on the element above them.


6. **Calculate Weights**: The software uses the pairwise comparison data to calculate the weights of each criterion and alternative, reflecting their relative importance in achieving the goal.


7. **Synthesize Results**: Finally, synthesize the results to determine the best alternative based on the calculated weights and the overall hierarchy structure.


By following these steps, users can effectively utilize the AHP method in SuperDecisions to make informed and structured decisions.
