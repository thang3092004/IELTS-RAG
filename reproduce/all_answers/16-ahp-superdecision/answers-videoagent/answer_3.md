In the Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP), the unweighted supermatrix is used to represent the relationships and influences among elements in a decision-making hierarchy. It is generated by organizing the pairwise comparison matrices of all elements into a single matrix. In Super Decisions, the unweighted supermatrix is created by inputting the pairwise comparisons of elements, which are then used to calculate the priorities and synthesize the overall decision-making process. The unweighted supermatrix is later transformed into a weighted supermatrix and then a limit matrix to derive the final priorities.
