In SuperDecisions, a ratings model allows for the evaluation of alternatives based on a set of criteria using a predefined scale, which can be more efficient and less time-consuming than pairwise comparisons, especially when dealing with a large number of alternatives. The benefits of using a ratings model include simplicity, ease of use, and the ability to handle a large number of alternatives without the exponential increase in comparisons required by the pairwise approach. However, the pairwise comparison approach provides a more detailed and precise analysis by comparing each pair of alternatives directly, which can lead to more accurate results in complex decision-making scenarios. The choice between the two methods depends on the specific needs of the decision-making process, such as the number of alternatives, the desired level of precision, and the available time and resources.
