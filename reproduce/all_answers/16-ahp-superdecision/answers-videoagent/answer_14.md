Pairwise comparisons in the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) involve comparing elements (such as criteria or alternatives) in pairs to judge which element is more important or preferred and to what extent. This process helps in establishing priorities and making decisions based on multiple criteria. In SuperDecisions software, pairwise comparisons are conducted by creating a hierarchy of criteria and alternatives, and then using a scale (usually 1 to 9) to rate the relative importance of one element over another. The software then uses these comparisons to calculate weights and synthesize the results to determine the best decision or ranking of alternatives.
