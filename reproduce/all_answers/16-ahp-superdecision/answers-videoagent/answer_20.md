In SuperDecisions, connecting criteria and sub-criteria to alternatives involves the following steps:


1. **Define the Goal**: Start by defining the main goal of the decision-making process.


2. **Identify Criteria and Sub-Criteria**: Determine the criteria that will be used to evaluate the alternatives. Break down each criterion into sub-criteria if necessary to provide a more detailed evaluation framework.


3. **Create a Hierarchical Structure**: Organize the goal, criteria, sub-criteria, and alternatives into a hierarchical structure. The goal is at the top, followed by criteria, sub-criteria, and finally, the alternatives at the bottom.


4. **Link Criteria to Alternatives**: Connect each criterion and sub-criterion to the relevant alternatives. This involves specifying how each alternative will be evaluated based on the criteria and sub-criteria.


5. **Assign Weights**: Use pairwise comparisons to assign weights to the criteria and sub-criteria, reflecting their relative importance in achieving the goal.


6. **Synthesize Results**: Calculate the overall scores for each alternative by synthesizing the weights and evaluations, allowing for a comparison of the alternatives based on the defined criteria and sub-criteria.


By following these steps, you ensure that the criteria and sub-criteria are properly linked to the alternatives, facilitating a structured decision-making process in SuperDecisions.
