The implementation of absolute measurement in SuperDecisions software can be broken down into two key phases: the establishment phase and the evaluation phase. Each phase plays a crucial role in ensuring that the decision-making process is systematic and effective.

### Establishment Phase

During the establishment phase, users define the criteria and alternatives that will form the foundation of their decision-making model. This involves several important steps:

1. **Criteria Definition**: Users must identify and specify the criteria that will be used to assess the alternatives. Criteria may include quantitative measures like price, performance metrics, or qualitative factors like prestige and comfort.

2. **Alternative Identification**: Users must determine the specific alternatives that will be evaluated. These alternatives represent the options available for consideration, such as different car models or various disposal methods in nuclear waste management.

3. **Node Organization**: In the SuperDecisions interface, users create nodes corresponding to the defined criteria and alternatives. This organization allows for visual representation and helps in maintaining a structured approach to decision-making.

4. **Initial Rating Insertion**: Once nodes are established, users input initial ratings for each criterion and alternative. These ratings, often based on absolute measurements (like real numerical values), will be instrumental in later evaluations.

### Evaluation Phase

The evaluation phase focuses on analyzing the established criteria and alternatives through a systematic application of the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) and other methodologies provided by SuperDecisions:

1. **Pairwise Comparison**: Users conduct pairwise comparisons among the criteria and alternatives. This entails assessing the relative importance of each element, allowing for nuanced evaluations that consider multiple factors beyond simple numerical ratings.

2. **Sensitivity Analysis**: During the evaluation, sensitivity analyses are performed to ascertain how changes in ratings affect overall priorities. This process reveals the robustness of the measurements and ensures that the decision model remains valid under varying conditions.

3. **Inconsistency Reporting**: SuperDecisions includes features for reporting inconsistencies in user judgments. This is crucial in maintaining the integrity of the decision-making process, as it helps users identify and rectify any discrepancies that may undermine their evaluations.

4. **Final Decision Output**: After completing the evaluations and sensitivity analyses, SuperDecisions generates results that summarize the comparative advantages of each alternative based on the defined criteria and their respective ratings. This output supports informed decision-making.

### Conclusion

In summary, the two phases of implementing absolute measurement in SuperDecisions—establishment and evaluation—ensure comprehensive and structured decision-making. These phases leverage the power of defined criteria and alternatives, systematic comparisons, and analytical rigor to facilitate effective choices in various contexts. This systematic approach enhances the transparency and reliability of the decisions made within the software’s framework.
