### Calculating Global Priorities of Alternatives in AHP Using Super Decisions

The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured technique utilized in Super Decisions software to assist in organizing and analyzing complex decisions through pairwise comparisons. To calculate the global priorities of alternatives within this framework, several steps are typically followed, taking advantage of specific functionalities embedded in the Super Decisions software.

#### Step 1: Setting Up the AHP Model

Initially, you need to create a hierarchy that consists of multiple levels, comprising a goal node, criteria nodes, and alternatives nodes. In Super Decisions, this setup is comprehensively facilitated through the software’s interface. Users define the goal of their decision-making process and identify the criteria that will impact the alternatives. Each criterion should include various alternatives that will be evaluated against them.

#### Step 2: Conducting Pairwise Comparisons

After establishing the hierarchical structure, the next critical step is conducting pairwise comparisons among the criteria and among the alternatives under each criterion. This process is integral to AHP, as it enables the user to establish preferences quantitatively. In Super Decisions, this is accomplished through specific input methods that allow the user to assign numerical values reflecting their judgments about the relative importance of each criterion and the relative performance of each alternative concerning these criteria.

#### Step 3: Normalizing Values and Calculating Priorities

Once the pairwise comparisons are made, Super Decisions utilizes mathematical calculations to normalize the values derived from these comparisons. The normalization process involves converting the pairwise comparison matrices into priority vectors, which indicate the relative importance of each criterion and alternative. For example, this could involve computing the eigenvector corresponding to the largest eigenvalue for the matrix representing the comparisons.

#### Step 4: Synthesizing Results

Following the normalization, the software synthesizes the results, providing a structured output of the global priorities. This synthesis ensures that the priorities are reflected consistently across the different layers of the hierarchy. In Super Decisions, users can visualize these results through various reporting functions, which may include detailed tables, charts, or graphical representations of the priorities of alternatives.

#### Step 5: Conducting Sensitivity Analysis

To enhance the robustness of the decision-making process, conducting a sensitivity analysis is recommended. Super Decisions allows users to perform sensitivity analysis on the established model, providing insights into how changes in the criteria weights affect the global priorities of alternatives. This capability is particularly useful for assessing the stability and reliability of the final decision outcomes under varying scenarios.

### Conclusion

In summary, the calculation of global priorities for alternatives in AHP within Super Decisions involves setting up a hierarchical model, conducting pairwise comparisons, normalizing values, synthesizing results, and potentially carrying out sensitivity analyses. This structured approach not only aids in making informed decisions but also helps users understand the relative importance and impact of each alternative based on defined criteria, thus facilitating a comprehensive decision-making process.
