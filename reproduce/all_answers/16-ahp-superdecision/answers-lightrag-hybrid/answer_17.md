Using a ratings model in the SuperDecisions software presents several considerations and benefits, especially when compared to a pairwise comparison approach. Below is a detailed look at various aspects of this evaluation framework.

### 1. Structure and Clarity

The **ratings model** provides a structured approach where users can evaluate multiple alternatives against defined criteria using a numerical scoring system. This clarity allows decision-makers to see at a glance how alternatives stack up against the criteria, unlike pairwise comparisons that require users to make a series of comparisons between two options at a time.

### 2. Comprehensive Assessment

The ratings model facilitates a more comprehensive assessment where all options can be evaluated simultaneously. This contrasts with the pairwise comparison method, which can become cumbersome as it requires multiple rounds of comparisons, especially when the number of alternatives increases. The ratings model allows decision-makers to consider a broader spectrum at once, enabling a more holistic understanding of how each alternative performs across multiple criteria.

### 3. Enhanced User Experience

The user experience in the ratings model can often be more intuitive. Users can quickly assign scores to each criterion for an alternative and observe immediate outcomes. In a pairwise comparison, users may struggle with maintaining consistency across multiple comparisons, potentially leading to confusion or inconsistencies in judgments.

### 4. Agility in Decision-Making

In scenarios requiring swift decision-making, the ratings model allows for quicker data entry and analysis. Users can express their preferences without needing to engage in potentially complex pairwise comparisons for each pair of alternatives. This can be particularly useful in time-sensitive situations like candidate selection for admissions or job placements.

### 5. Flexibility in Criteria Application 

The ratings model allows for flexibility in defining and applying different rating scales, accommodating a variety of decision contexts. Conversely, pairwise comparisons can be rigid, as the model generally assumes a transitive preference which might not reflect the decision maker's actual thought process.

### 6. Consideration of Inconsistencies

The ratings model can include features that automatically analyze and flag inconsistencies in judgments, facilitating a more reliable decision-making process. Pairwise comparisons may inadvertently allow for inconsistencies to persist unnoticed due to the more fragmented nature of evaluating two alternatives at a time.

### 7. Analytical Depth

When integrated with sensitivity analysis, the ratings model allows users to see how shifts in their assessments can affect the overall rankings. This is beneficial in understanding the impact of different criteria weights on the final decision. While pairwise comparisons can also support sensitivity analysis, the direct application of scores in a ratings model can make it easier to visualize potential changes.

### Conclusion

In conclusion, while both the ratings model and pairwise comparison approach have their merits within the SuperDecisions software, the ratings model often offers a more straightforward, intuitive, and holistic method for evaluating alternatives. Its ability to provide comprehensive assessments quickly and facilitate clarity in decision-making makes it particularly advantageous in various contexts, such as candidate selection, project evaluation, or any scenario where multiple criteria must be weighed.
