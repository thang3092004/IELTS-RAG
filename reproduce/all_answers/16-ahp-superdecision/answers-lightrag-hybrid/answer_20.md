In SuperDecisions, connecting criteria and sub-criteria to alternatives involves a structured approach that facilitates the proper linkage in the decision-making hierarchy. Here’s a breakdown of the steps involved in this process:

### 1. **Define the Goal Node**
Begin by establishing the **Goal Node**, which represents the primary objective of your decision-making model. This node serves as the overarching aim that will guide the evaluation process.

### 2. **Create Criteria and Sub-Criteria**
Once the goal is defined, create **Criteria** that are essential to your evaluation. These criteria can include attributes such as Price, Prestige, Fuel Efficiency, and others relevant to the decision at hand. If needed, additional **Sub-Criteria** can be formed to provide more detailed assessments under each main criterion. For example, under the criterion 'Price,' sub-criteria might include 'Base Price' and 'Maintenance Costs.'

### 3. **Organize the Criteria**
Organize these criteria into **Clusters**. Each cluster groups related criteria and sub-criteria, enhancing the clarity of your decision framework. This helps in managing complex relationships and facilitates easier navigation during the evaluation process.

### 4. **Establish Connections**
With the criteria and sub-criteria in place, establish connections between them and the **Alternatives** you are evaluating. In SuperDecisions, this is done by linking each criterion and sub-criterion directly to the relevant alternatives. This linkage is crucial as it defines how each alternative will be assessed against the specified criteria.

### 5. **Utilize Node Comparisons**
After linking the criteria and alternatives, perform **Node Comparisons**. Here, you compare the alternatives concerning each criterion. This step often involves conducting pairwise comparisons to determine how much better one alternative is compared to another based on the given criteria.

### 6. **Apply Analytical Methodologies**
Utilize methodologies like the **Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP)** or the **Analytic Network Process (ANP)** to evaluate the relationships between the criteria and alternatives. These techniques help in structuring the evaluation process and aid in achieving a systematic ranking of the alternatives.

### 7. **Conduct Sensitivity Analysis**
After the initial evaluations, it's important to perform **Sensitivity Analysis**. This step assesses how changes in criteria weight impact the overall decision outcomes. This allows users to understand the robustness of their decisions and adjust priorities as necessary.

### 8. **Refine the Model**
Finally, refine your decision model as needed. Based on the outcomes from sensitivity analysis or additional insights gathered, you may adjust the weights of the criteria or even redefine the connections between the criteria and alternatives for improved clarity or accuracy.

### Conclusion
By following these steps, users can effectively establish and manage the connections between criteria and alternatives in SuperDecisions. This ensures that the hierarchical structure reflects the relationships and priorities necessary for informed and strategic decision-making. This structured approach not only enhances decision accuracy but also contributes to a comprehensive understanding of the evaluation process.
