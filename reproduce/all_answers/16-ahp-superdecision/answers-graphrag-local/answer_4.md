To calculate global priorities of alternatives using the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) with Super Decisions, you should follow a systematic approach that involves several steps:

### Step 1: Define the Problem and Structure the Model
Start by clearly defining the problem and identifying the goal of your decision. Create a hierarchical structure that organizes the criteria and alternatives involved. This involves establishing nodes for the goal at the top, followed by criteria that influence your decision, and then listing the alternatives at the bottom of the hierarchy.

### Step 2: Create Pairwise Comparisons
Once the hierarchical model is structured, the next step involves performing pairwise comparisons of the criteria and alternatives. In Super Decisions, you can do this by navigating to the comparisons section and selecting the criteria you wish to compare. You will input your judgments according to the AHP scale (usually ranging from 1 to 9), which helps in determining how much more important one criterion is over another.

### Step 3: Input Judgments and Generate Local Priorities
After completing the pairwise comparisons for each group of criteria and for the alternatives under each criterion, Super Decisions will generate local priority vectors. These vectors represent the relative importance of each criterion or alternative based on the judgments you provided.

### Step 4: Synthesize Local Priorities to Obtain Global Priorities
This is where the iterative process of AHP truly takes shape. The local priority values calculated for each criterion are now combined to derive global priorities for the alternatives. Super Decisions organizes this synthesis automatically, calculating how each alternative contributes to the goal based on the local priorities associated with the criteria.

### Step 5: Perform Sensitivity Analysis
Finally, it is advisable to conduct a sensitivity analysis within Super Decisions to see how changes in your judgments may impact the overall prioritization. This step helps in understanding the robustness of your decisions and the potential ramifications of altering any single comparison or priority.

### Summary
In summary, calculating global priorities in AHP using Super Decisions involves defining a structured model of alternatives and criteria, conducting pairwise comparisons, generating local priorities, synthesizing these to global priorities, and performing sensitivity analysis to ensure the validity of your decisions. By following these steps through the Super Decisions interface, users can facilitate complex decision-making processes in a systematic and analytical manner.
