# Steps for Performing Sensitivity Analysis in SuperDecisions for an AHP Model

SuperDecisions software is widely recognized for its capabilities in decision analysis, particularly using the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP). When conducting sensitivity analysis on AHP models within this software, several key steps are involved, ensuring that users can understand how changes in criteria can impact decision outcomes.

## 1. **Access the SuperDecisions Interface**

Begin by opening the SuperDecisions application. Familiarize yourself with the interface, specifically the "Main Network," "Analysis," and "Computations" tabs, as these will be pivotal during the sensitivity analysis process. 

## 2. **Setup the AHP Model**

Ensure that the AHP model has been established. This includes defining the goal, criteria, and alternatives associated with your decision context. Input all relevant data into SuperDecisions, allowing the software to generate a structured representation of the decision-making environment.

## 3. **Define Criteria and Alternatives**

Within the established model, critically assess and assign significance to criteria associated with the alternatives. This involves explaining how each criterion will affect the evaluations. Clear articulation of these definitions is crucial for the subsequent sensitivity analysis.

## 4. **Perform Sensitivity Analysis**

Navigate to the "Analysis" tab within SuperDecisions. Here, you can utilize the **AHP Sensitivity Analysis** feature, which allows you to adjust the weights assigned to different criteria. Follow these sub-steps:

- **Select Criteria**: Choose the criteria you wish to analyze in regard to their weights. 
- **Adjust Weights**: Modify the weights assigned to these criteria to see how changes impact rankings among the alternatives.
- **Dynamic Reference**: The "Dynamic Sensitivity" feature in SuperDecisions can illustrate how adjustments in the weight of one or multiple criteria influence the overall rankings of the alternatives. 

## 5. **Visualize Changes with Graphical Tools**

SuperDecisions provides various graphical elements, such as node comparisons and charts. These tools help visualize how changes in weights affect alternative rankings. Utilize these graphical representations to obtain a comprehensive overview of sensitivity impacts.

## 6. **Analyze Results**

Examine the outcomes provided by the sensitivity analysis. This includes noting shifts in alternative rankings based on your adjusted criteria weights. The analysis can uncover critical insights regarding which criteria the decision outcome is most sensitive to, allowing for informed adjustments.

## 7. **Iterative Refinement**

Based on the analysis results, iterate by adjusting weights and re-executing the sensitivity analysis. This iterative process is vital in reaching a final decision that accurately reflects stakeholder priorities and preferences.

## 8. **Export Findings**

After concluding the sensitivity analysis, users have the option to document their findings and export relevant data to formats such as Excel for further analysis or reporting.

## Conclusion

Performing sensitivity analysis in SuperDecisions for an AHP model is a structured process that enhances decision-making efficacy. By following these steps, users can better understand how changes in criteria impact alternative rankings, thereby fostering more robust and informed decisions in complex scenarios.
