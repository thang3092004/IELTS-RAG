# Defining and Utilizing Rating Scales in SuperDecisions

SuperDecisions is a robust software tool designed to facilitate structured decision-making through the implementation of ratings models. A critical aspect of this process involves defining and utilizing rating scales that allow users to assess various alternatives based on specific criteria. This guide provides an overview of the steps involved in defining and effectively utilizing rating scales within the SuperDecisions framework.

## Defining Rating Scales

1. **Select Your Criteria**: Before defining rating scales, users should identify the key criteria against which the alternatives will be evaluated. Criteria can include factors like price, performance, comfort, and environmental impact, depending on the context of the decision-making process.

2. **Create the Scale**: 
   - **Existing Scales**: Users can utilize existing scales loaded from previous sessions or files, which may save time and ensure consistency if similar evaluations have been made previously.
   - **New Scales**: Alternatively, users can develop new rating scales customized for specific criteria. This involves detailing what each level on the scale represents, such as a range from "Excellent" to "Poor" or a numerical range indicating levels of satisfaction or effectiveness.
   
3. **Guidance and Management**: The SuperDecisions interface provides various tools:
   - Tools like the "Manage Ratings" section help users oversee the different scales and modify them as needed.
   - Importing scales can be done through the "Load from file" feature, enhancing flexibility and ease of setup.

## Utilizing Rating Scales in the Ratings Model

1. **Incorporating Scales into Evaluations**:
   - Once the rating scales are defined, users can employ them when evaluating alternatives based on how well each option meets the selected criteria.
   - Users input values into the ratings table, applying the scales set for each criterion to assess the relative performance of alternatives systematically.

2. **Visualizing Ratings**: The software facilitates the display and management of ratings via the “Ratings Table.” This feature allows users to see all criteria and their corresponding ratings at a glance, facilitating transparent comparisons among the alternatives.

3. **Running Evaluations**: After entering ratings, users can synthesize the results to derive conclusions about the best alternatives based on the defined criteria and scales. The application of scales ensures that evaluations are quantitative and significantly increases the reliability of the decision-making process.

## Importance of Ratings Scales

Using structured rating scales is vital for maintaining objectivity and consistency in evaluations. By quantifying subjective measures, rating scales allow stakeholders to make informed, transparent, and defensible decisions. Additionally, SuperDecisions supports various analytical methods such as sensitivity analysis, enabling users to test the robustness of their decisions against different scenarios.

In summary, defining and employing rating scales in SuperDecisions is a systematic process that enhances decision-making quality across various domains, from nuclear waste management to educational admissions. By effectively utilizing the software’s features, users can ensure comprehensive evaluations that facilitate informed choices and optimal outcomes.
