## Understanding Pairwise Comparisons in AHP

Pairwise comparisons are a fundamental component of the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), a structured technique used for decision-making. In the context of AHP, pairwise comparisons involve analyzing two criteria or alternatives at a time to determine which one is preferred or more important based on a set of defined metrics. This method allows decision-makers to derive relative weights for each criterion, which is crucial in evaluating complex decisions where multiple factors are at play.

The process of conducting pairwise comparisons typically involves the following steps:

1. **Selection of Criteria**: Identify the criteria that need to be compared, which could stem from higher-level goals in a decision hierarchy.

2. **Rating the Importance**: For each pair of criteria, decision-makers review how one criterion compares to the other in terms of importance. This is usually expressed using a rating scale, where numbers represent the level of preference, such as 1 for equal importance to 9 for extreme importance.

3. **Creation of a Comparison Matrix**: The comparisons are organized into a comparison matrix, where each criterion is listed along the rows and columns. The values filled in the matrix reflect the ratio of preference between the criteria.

4. **Calculation of Weights**: Using the values in the comparison matrix, SuperDecisions computes the priority weights for each criterion, which can subsequently be used in decision analysis.

2. **Input of Judgments**: The software includes a 'Judgments Tab' where Users can input their pairwise comparisons. Depending on the requirements, Users can choose specific criteria and enter their relative importance ratings based on the predefined scale.

3. **Visual Assessment**: SuperDecisions displays the comparisons in a graphical layout, such as a Comparison Matrix, allowing Users to visualize how each criterion interacts with others, enhancing understanding and accuracy in their evaluations.

4. **Consistency Checking**: One crucial feature is the Inconsistency Report that highlights potential discrepancies in the Users' judgments. This function ensures that the comparisons reflect logical and coherent input by checking if the combined evaluations lead to consistent priority rankings.

5. **Final Calculations**: Once all pairwise comparisons are made and judged for consistency, SuperDecisions calculates the priority weights for each criterion automatically. These weights are integral for synthesizing decisions and are used in further analyses to evaluate alternatives.
