### Understanding Model Synthesis in SuperDecisions

The process of synthesizing a decision-making model using SuperDecisions can vary significantly between models that incorporate sub-criteria and those that do not. This variance primarily relates to the complexity and detail of the evaluations conducted within the framework of the software.

### Synthesizing Models with Sub-Criteria

When synthesizing a model that includes sub-criteria, the process involves several layers of evaluations. Sub-criteria represent more detailed metrics or standards that fall under broader evaluation metrics. For example, if the main criterion is "Comfort," sub-criteria may include aspects such as "Seat Quality," "Legroom," and "Noise Level." This hierarchical structure allows users to perform more nuanced assessments of alternatives.

In such cases, the synthesis process requires the user to:

1. **Define and Input Sub-Criteria**: This step involves categorizing sub-criteria under their main criteria. Each sub-criterion must be rated based on its importance and impact on the overall decision-making.
   
2. **Conduct Pairwise Comparisons**: Users compare alternatives not only based on primary criteria but also across the defined sub-criteria. This detailed comparison enhances the granularity of the analysis, allowing for a thorough evaluation of how each alternative performs concerning every specific metric.

3. **Aggregate Results**: The final step involves synthesizing the ratings at various levels, where the software combines the evaluations of both main criteria and their sub-criteria to reach a comprehensive decision. This layered synthesis enables more informed choices by reflecting the multi-dimensional nature of user preferences.

### Synthesizing Models without Sub-Criteria

In contrast, synthesizing a model without sub-criteria streamlines the process. The synthesis in such models typically revolves around broader criteria without the additional complexity of multiple layers. The following steps summarize the process:

1. **Simple Criteria Definition**: Users define fewer, broader criteria and proceed without the need to delve into detailed sub-criteria. For example, criteria may include "Cost," "Fuel Efficiency," and "Comfort" as standalone metrics.

2. **Pairwise Comparisons**: The comparison process focuses on these broad criteria, resulting in a simplified evaluation structure. Each alternative is assessed against these primary metrics directly without the need for deeper analysis.

3. **Direct Aggregation of Results**: The results are synthesized based on the primary criteria evaluations alone, which makes the overall process quicker but less detailed. Users receive a consolidated ranking based solely on these higher-level criteria.

### Conclusion

In summary, the key difference between synthesizing models with and without sub-criteria in SuperDecisions lies in the complexity and thoroughness of the evaluation process. Models with sub-criteria necessitate a more intricate framework for evaluation, encompassing detailed pairwise comparisons and a multi-layered synthesis process. This meticulous approach often leads to more nuanced insights and reflections of user preferences. Conversely, models without sub-criteria offer a more streamlined and straightforward process, resulting in quicker but potentially less comprehensive decision-making outcomes.
