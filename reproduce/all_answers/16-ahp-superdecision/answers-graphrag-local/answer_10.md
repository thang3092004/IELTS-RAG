The implementation of absolute measurement in the SuperDecisions software is structured around two main phases that facilitate decision-making through the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP). Here's a detailed look at each phase:

## Phase One: Setup and Configuration of the Decision Problem

In the first phase, users begin by defining the decision problem at hand. This involves identifying the various alternatives and criteria that will play a significant role in the decision-making process. The user is required to set up a structured model that clearly delineates these elements, allowing for a comprehensive breakdown of the decision problem. 

During this setup, the criteria—whether they be quantitative metrics like cost or qualitative assessments such as user satisfaction—are established. The software provides users with a graphical interface that aids in visualizing the relationships between the criteria and alternatives. By organizing this information hierarchically, users can effectively manage and present the complexity associated with their decision-making.

## Phase Two: Conducting Assessments and Evaluations

The second phase focuses on the actual evaluation and assessment of the alternatives based on the defined criteria. In this phase, users input data quantitatively, assigning numerical values to the criteria based on their importance in relation to each alternative. This involves conducting pairwise comparisons to determine preferences among the options, which is the cornerstone of the AHP.

SuperDecisions employs a systematic approach to process this input, calculating priority vectors that reflect the significance of each alternative. By using absolute measurement, users can evaluate choices without the ambiguity that can come from relative comparisons. This phase culminates in generating results that inform the decision-maker about the best alternatives based on the established metrics, ensuring a solid foundation for well-informed choices.

Overall, these two phases enhance the decision-making capabilities within the SuperDecisions software, leveraging structured analysis to facilitate effective outcomes in complex scenarios.
