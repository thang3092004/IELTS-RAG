The ratings mode in SuperDecisions software offers several advantages for absolute measurement in the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) framework. Here are some key benefits:

* **Handling a Large Number of Alternatives**: When dealing with a large pool of alternatives, the traditional relative measurement process in AHP can become cumbersome and time-consuming. The ratings mode excels in scenarios with numerous alternatives, providing a more efficient approach.
* **Simplified Evaluation**: Instead of making pairwise comparisons between alternatives for each criterion, the ratings mode allows you to directly evaluate each alternative against a predefined rating scale. This simplifies the judgment process, making it faster and potentially less prone to inconsistencies.
* **Reduced Cognitive Load**: By focusing on rating individual alternatives rather than comparing pairs, the ratings mode reduces the cognitive load on the decision-maker. This can lead to more consistent and reliable judgments, especially when dealing with complex decisions.
* **Flexibility in Rating Scales**: SuperDecisions software provides flexibility in defining rating scales for each criterion. You can use predefined scales, create custom scales with specific ranges and labels, or even utilize scales based on numerical values. This adaptability allows you to tailor the rating scales to the specific decision problem and criteria.
* **Streamlined Data Entry**: The tabular format of the ratings mode in SuperDecisions facilitates quick and organized data entry for performance ratings of alternatives. This can be particularly beneficial when handling a substantial number of alternatives and criteria. 
* **Focus on Ideal Performance**: The ratings mode encourages evaluating alternatives against an ideal performance level for each criterion. This helps to maintain consistency and objectivity in judgments, as the focus shifts from comparing alternatives to assessing their individual merits.

However, it's crucial to recognize that the effectiveness of the ratings mode relies on the expertise and ability of the decision-maker to assign accurate and consistent ratings to alternatives without being influenced by comparisons between them.
