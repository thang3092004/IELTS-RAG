The global priorities of alternatives in AHP can be calculated using Super Decisions software by synthesizing the entire model. The steps are as follows:

*   **Create a hierarchy** for the decision problem, including the goal, criteria, and alternatives.
*   **Connect the elements** in the hierarchy. For criteria with sub-criteria, connect the alternatives to the sub-criteria instead of the main criteria.
*   **Enter judgments** for pairwise comparisons of the criteria and alternatives. Judgments can be entered in various modes, including questionnaire mode, graphical mode, verbal mode, direct entry, and matrix view.
*   **Check for inconsistency** in the judgments and revise if necessary. The consistency ratio should be less than 0.1.
*   Once all comparisons are made and local priorities are calculated, go to **‘Computation’** and select **‘Synthesize the whole model’**. This will calculate the global priorities for the alternatives, showing their overall weights.

**The software will then display the results, showing the global priorities of the alternatives.** The results can be viewed in different ways, including a table, a bar chart, and a matrix. You can also use the software to perform sensitivity analysis to see how the results change when the input values are varied.

These steps can be applied to various decision-making problems, from personal decisions like choosing a job to complex government decisions like selecting a nuclear waste disposal strategy. The software helps to make the process of AHP analysis more efficient and user-friendly.
