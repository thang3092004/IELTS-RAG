Super Decisions offers various ways to visualize the results of an AHP analysis, highlighting the priorities of alternatives and criteria.

### Visualizing Results in Super Decisions

*   **Limit Matrix Graphical View**: This view displays the final priorities of the alternatives under the goal node, alongside the priorities of the criteria. The values shown are row values and are not normalized by the cluster. To see the normalized values, you can open the **Synthesize** view, which presents the same values as the Limit Super Matrix. 
*   **Unweighted Super Matrix**: This matrix showcases the set of judgments entered during the AHP process. It reveals the priorities under each criterion and the overall priorities of the criteria. For instance, the priorities derived from pairwise comparisons are copied into the super matrix, enabling calculations.
*   **Sensitivity Analysis**: After calculating global priorities for the alternatives, sensitivity analysis can be performed. This analysis helps understand how changes in criteria weights affect the ranking of alternatives. Super Decisions provides options for conducting sensitivity analysis, which are detailed in separate videos mentioned in the source.
*   **Plot Chart**: Sensitivity analysis results can be presented visually using a plot chart, which is commonly included in research papers. The plot chart illustrates how the ranking of alternatives changes as the weight of a specific criterion is varied. 
*   **Bar Chart, Pie Chart, and Horizontal Bar Chart**: In addition to the plot chart, Super Decisions also offers bar chart, pie chart, and horizontal bar chart options to visualize sensitivity analysis results. These charts provide alternative ways to represent the data and can be helpful in understanding the relationships between criteria weights and alternative rankings.

By employing these visualization tools, Super Decisions helps users gain insights into the AHP analysis results, enabling them to make informed decisions based on the priorities of alternatives and criteria.
