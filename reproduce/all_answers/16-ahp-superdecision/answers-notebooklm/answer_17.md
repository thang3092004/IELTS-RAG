When deciding whether to use a ratings model or a pairwise comparison approach in SuperDecisions, there are some key considerations and benefits to keep in mind. 

**Ratings Model Considerations:**

* **Expertise:** Using a ratings model effectively requires users to be **experts in the field** and possess a clear understanding of the ideal outcome. If users lack sufficient expertise, the results may become unreliable. 
* **Objectivity:** It's crucial to maintain objectivity when assigning ratings and avoid comparing the available alternatives.  The focus should remain on evaluating each alternative against the ideal, not against each other.

**Ratings Model Benefits:** 

* **Efficiency:** When dealing with numerous alternatives, the ratings model proves much **faster** for entering judgments compared to the pairwise comparison approach. Pairwise comparisons become time-consuming with a large number of alternatives, making the ratings model a more efficient choice.
* **Simplicity:** Ratings models are generally **easier to understand and implement**. The process of assigning ratings to alternatives based on predefined scales is straightforward and intuitive.

**Pairwise Comparisons Considerations**:

* **Suitability:** Pairwise comparisons are better suited for situations where there are a limited number of alternatives and a clear understanding of the relative importance of criteria.  

**Pairwise Comparisons Benefits**:

* **Accuracy:**  Pairwise comparisons can lead to more precise results because they force a more deliberate consideration of the trade-offs between alternatives.

**Overall:** The ratings model is generally favored when the number of alternatives is large, making pairwise comparisons impractical. If the decision-maker is confident in their expertise and ability to remain objective, a ratings model can provide a simpler and more efficient path to decision-making.
