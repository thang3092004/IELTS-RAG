Pairwise comparisons are a fundamental aspect of the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), a multi-criteria decision-making method. In AHP, the goal is to make a decision by considering various criteria and alternatives, and pairwise comparisons help establish the relative importance of these elements.

**Pairwise comparison involves comparing two elements at a time to determine their relative importance or preference.** For example, if you're choosing a car, you might compare the importance of "price" versus "fuel efficiency." You would judge whether price is more important, less important, or equally important to fuel efficiency.

SuperDecisions software facilitates AHP analysis, including pairwise comparisons. The software offers different modes for entering judgments:

*   **Questionnaire Mode:** This user-friendly mode presents pairs of elements and asks you to select the preferred one based on a scale (usually 1-9). For instance, it might ask, "With respect to buying a car, is prestige more important than price?"
*   **Graphical Mode:** You visually adjust bars or sliders to represent the relative importance of the elements being compared.
*   **Verbal Mode:** This mode uses descriptive terms (e.g., "strongly more important," "moderately more important") to capture your judgments. You drag and drop these terms to indicate your preference.
*   **Direct Entry Mode:** You directly input numerical values to express the relative importance (e.g., if price is three times more important than mileage, you'd enter a 3). This mode is particularly useful when you have quantifiable data.
*   **Matrix View:** This mode displays the pairwise comparisons in a matrix format, allowing you to directly input or adjust the numerical judgments.

The software automatically calculates priorities and consistency ratios based on your judgments. The **consistency ratio (CR)** measures the logical consistency of your comparisons. A CR value below 0.1 is generally considered acceptable, indicating that your judgments are reasonably consistent. If the CR is high, the software provides options to revise judgments and improve consistency.

SuperDecisions simplifies the process of conducting pairwise comparisons, making it easier to apply AHP for complex decision-making problems.
