To convert an AHP model to an ANP model in Super Decisions, you must first **remove the goal node and connect the alternatives to the criteria**. This is because, in ANP, criteria are prioritized by how important they are in the alternatives being considered. 

Next, **compare the importance of the criteria for each of the alternatives**. This will create a profile of how important each criterion is for each alternative. For instance, when comparing the criteria for an Acura TL, prestige may be more important than price. However, for a Honda Civic, price may be a better feature than miles per gallon.

Finally, **synthesize the model to get the overall priorities for the cars and criteria**. The synthesis process combines the judgments made for the criteria and alternatives to produce the final priorities. 

**In summary, the following steps and additional judgments are needed to convert an AHP model into an ANP model in Super Decisions:**

*   Remove the goal node.
*   Connect each alternative to all of the criteria.
*   For each alternative, compare the criteria with respect to that alternative.

This conversion allows for a more nuanced understanding of the decision problem by considering the interdependence between the criteria and alternatives.
