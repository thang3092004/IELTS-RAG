The consistency ratio (CR) in the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a measure of the logical coherence of the judgments made when comparing different elements in a decision hierarchy. **It helps ensure that the decision-maker's preferences are consistent and reliable**.

According to Thomas Saaty, the developer of AHP, **the CR value should be less than 0.1 for the judgments to be considered acceptable**. This threshold ensures that the inconsistencies in the decision-maker's judgments are within a reasonable range and do not significantly impact the reliability of the results.

SuperDecisions, a software tool for implementing AHP, provides features to address inconsistencies and maintain a CR value below the acceptable threshold:

* **Automatic CR Calculation:** SuperDecisions automatically calculates the CR value after each set of pairwise comparisons, providing immediate feedback on the consistency of the judgments.
* **Inconsistency Reports:** If the CR value exceeds 0.1, SuperDecisions can generate inconsistency reports that highlight the specific judgments contributing the most to the inconsistency. This report guides the decision-maker in revising their judgments to improve consistency.
* **Judgment Revision:** The software allows users to easily revise their judgments based on the inconsistency report. By adjusting the values of specific pairwise comparisons, the decision-maker can reduce inconsistencies and bring the CR value below the acceptable limit.
* **Sensitivity Analysis:** SuperDecisions offers sensitivity analysis tools that allow users to explore how changes in the judgments or weights of different criteria affect the overall ranking of alternatives. This analysis helps assess the robustness of the decision model and identify potential areas where inconsistencies might have a significant impact.

**By utilizing these features, SuperDecisions assists decision-makers in maintaining a low CR value, ensuring the reliability and validity of the AHP results.**
