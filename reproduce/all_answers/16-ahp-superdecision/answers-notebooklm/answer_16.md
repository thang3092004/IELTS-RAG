The process of synthesizing an AHP model in SuperDecisions involves calculating the global priorities for alternatives, which represent the overall ranking of choices based on their performance against a set of criteria. Here's a breakdown of the process, combining explanations from multiple sources:

**1. Completing Pairwise Comparisons:** Before synthesizing, ensure all pairwise comparison judgments are entered for criteria and alternatives. The software automatically calculates local priorities from these judgments. For example, if comparing car alternatives based on price, input judgments reflecting which car is more affordable, such as Acura costing $35,000, Camry costing $28,000, and Civic costing $20,000.  Remember to use the 'invert' button if a higher value in the criterion is less desirable, like in the case of price.

**2.  Synthesize the Model:** Once judgments are complete, initiate the synthesis by navigating to 'Computation' and selecting 'Synthesize the whole model.' This action triggers the calculation of global priorities for all alternatives.

**3.  Reviewing the Results:** Upon synthesis, SuperDecisions displays the global priorities of each alternative. These values represent the weighted importance of each alternative, considering their performance across all criteria. For instance, the results might show that Acura has a global priority of 0.348, company B has 0.400, and company C has 0.250, indicating company B as the best choice.

**4.  Additional Insights:** You can access further details through different views:
* **Limit Matrix Graphical View:** Displays final priorities of alternatives and criteria priorities.
* **Unweighted Supermatrix:** Shows the entered judgments and resulting local priorities.
* **Matrix Priorities View:** Provides a separate window displaying the calculated totals and priorities.

By following this process, you can utilize SuperDecisions to effectively synthesize your AHP model and determine the most favorable alternative based on its overall performance across all defined criteria.
