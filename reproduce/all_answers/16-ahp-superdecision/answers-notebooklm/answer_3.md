The unweighted supermatrix is a matrix that stores the local priorities derived from pairwise comparisons in an AHP model. Before any comparisons are made, all weights are distributed equally. For example, if there are four criteria, each criterion will have a weight of 0.25. **The unweighted supermatrix is used to calculate the final priorities of the alternatives in the AHP model using a process called synthesis.**

Super Decisions software can generate an unweighted supermatrix. The unweighted supermatrix will show the initial priorities of the criteria and alternatives. Once pairwise comparisons are made, the unweighted supermatrix is updated to reflect the local priorities. The exact priorities from pairwise comparisons are then copied into the supermatrix to perform calculations.  For instance, in the car selection example, the priorities derived from comparing Acura, Toyota Camry, and Honda Civic on comfort are displayed as a row in the supermatrix.
