# Connecting Criteria and Sub-Criteria to Alternatives in SuperDecisions

In SuperDecisions, establishing a robust decision-making hierarchy is crucial for accurate assessments. The software facilitates the connection of criteria and sub-criteria to alternatives through a structured approach. Below are detailed steps for linking these components effectively.

## Step 1: Create Your Hierarchical Structure

Initially, you need to ensure that you have a clear hierarchical structure set up in your SuperDecisions model:

1. **Open the Software**: Start by launching the SuperDecisions application and loading your project file.
2. **Define Clusters**: Create clusters for your goals, criteria, and alternatives.
3. **Add Nodes**: Within the defined clusters, add nodes representing each criterion and sub-criterion. For instance, you might have nodes labeled "Comfort", "Price", or "Performance" within your criteria cluster.

## Step 2: Establish Connections from Criteria to Alternatives

Once your nodes are in place, the next step is to connect your criteria to the alternatives:

1. **Select the Criteria Node**: Click on the criteria node that you wish to connect to your alternatives.
2. **Enable Connection Mode**: Activate the connection mode in SuperDecisions by clicking the corresponding icon at the top of the workspace. This will allow you to visualize and manage connections easily.
3. **Connect to Alternatives**: Click on the alternatives nodes that need to be connected to the selected criteria. It's recommended to connect each criterion to all relevant alternatives to ensure a comprehensive comparison.
4. **Check Connections**: After creating connections, deactivate the connection mode and visually inspect that all connections are established correctly. Nodes being highlighted in red typically indicate active connections.

## Step 3: Link Sub-Criteria to Alternatives

Mapping sub-criteria to alternatives follows a similar process:

1. **Select a Sub-Criteria Node**: Choose the node representing a sub-criterion.
2. **Enable Connection Mode Again**: As before, turn on the connection mode.
3. **Connect to Relevant Alternatives**: Proceed to connect this sub-criterion to relevant alternatives, similar to how you connected the main criteria. It’s important that each sub-criterion is linked back to the appropriate alternatives.
4. **Inspect Connections for Accuracy**: Once done, turn off the connection mode again and verify the connections made. Ensure that every alternative has the required sub-criteria linked.

## Step 4: Validate and Save the Model

After establishing all connections:

1. **Conduct a Review**: Look through your hierarchy, ensuring each connection accurately reflects the relationships you wish to model.
2. **Input Judgments**: For each node and connection, make necessary judgments based on the established comparisons (e.g., how important each criterion is relative to each alternative).
3. **Save Your Work**: Once satisfied with the connections and entered judgments, save the model to prevent any loss of data.

By following these steps, you can successfully connect criteria and sub-criteria to alternatives in SuperDecisions, allowing for a well-structured decision-making framework that supports systematic evaluation and prioritization.
