# Creating an AHP Hierarchy in SuperDecisions Software

The process of constructing an Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) hierarchy in SuperDecisions software involves several key steps, including defining the goal, establishing criteria, and identifying alternatives. This structured approach is designed to facilitate decision-making by breaking down complex problems into more manageable components. Below, we'll walk through the essential steps involved in creating this hierarchy.

## 1. Define the Goal

The first step in creating an AHP model is to establish the primary goal of your decision-making process. The goal serves as the apex of the hierarchy and encapsulates the main objective you seek to achieve. For instance, if you're trying to select a car, your goal would be stated as "Select the Best Car." 

To set the goal in SuperDecisions:

- **Open the Software**: Launch SuperDecisions and create a new project.
- **Create a Goal Node**: In the software interface, navigate to the section where you can create a new cluster or node. Click the '+' button to add your goal node, labeling it appropriately (e.g., "Select the Best Car").

## 2. Establish Criteria

Once the goal is defined, the next step involves determining the criteria that will guide the evaluation of alternatives. Criteria provide the framework for comparisons and can include multiple variables depending on the complexity of the decision. 

In the context of selecting a car, your criteria might include aspects like:

- **Price**
- **Comfort**
- **Fuel Efficiency (MPG)**
- **Prestige**

To incorporate criteria into your SuperDecisions model:

- **Create Criteria Nodes**: Add nodes underneath the goal node for each criterion you've identified.
- **Define Priorities**: As you create these nodes, you will also enter judgments to evaluate how important each criterion is relative to one another, laying the foundation for prioritization.

## 3. Identify Alternatives

The final step in constructing the hierarchy is identifying the alternatives that will be evaluated against the criteria. These are the options from which you will make your selection. For our example of car selection, the alternatives may include:

- **Acura TL**
- **Toyota Camry**
- **Honda Civic**

To add alternatives in SuperDecisions:

- **Create Alternatives Nodes**: Place these nodes beneath each criterion node to indicate how each alternative performs with respect to the established criteria.
- **Enter Judgments**: Similar to the criteria, you will enter judgments that compare how well each alternative meets each criterion.

## 4. Connect the Hierarchy

With the goal, criteria, and alternatives established, you will need to create connections between the nodes. This involves:

- **Connecting Nodes**: Use the software's interface to create links from the goal to the criteria and then from the criteria to the alternatives.
- **Judgment Entry**: Enter pairwise comparisons that allow the software to calculate priorities based on the judgments made about the importance of each criterion and how each alternative performs concerning these criteria.

## 5. Synthesize the Results

After entering your judgments and establishing the hierarchy:

- **Synthesize Options**: Utilize the software’s capabilities to synthesize the results, giving you a clear priority ranking of the alternatives based on the criteria and their respective judgments.
- **Review and Analyze**: Finally, review the synthesized priorities to analyze which alternative best meets your goal.

## Conclusion

Creating an AHP hierarchy in SuperDecisions software is a systematic process that facilitates informed decision-making. By clearly defining the goal, setting criteria, identifying alternatives, and establishing relationships among them, users can comprehensively evaluate their options and arrive at a well-structured decision. This structured approach not only enhances the clarity of the decision-making process but also aids in achieving more consistent and objective outcomes.
