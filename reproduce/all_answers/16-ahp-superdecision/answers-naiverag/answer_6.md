### Incorporating Sub-Criteria into the AHP Hierarchy with Super Decisions Software

Incorporating sub-criteria into the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) hierarchy using Super Decisions software involves a structured approach that allows decision-makers to evaluate complex alternatives more effectively. Here’s a step-by-step outline of how to integrate these additional layers into the AHP framework.

#### Step 1: Create the Main Model Structure

The first step in the process is to create a new model within the Super Decisions software. Start by defining the main goal of your decision-making process. For example, if the goal is to select the best car, you will set this as the top-level goal in your hierarchy.

#### Step 2: Define the Criteria

Once your goal is established, the next objective is to define the primary criteria that will help you evaluate the options available. In the car selection example, criteria might include aspects such as price, comfort, reliability, and fuel efficiency. Each of these criteria represents a separate node in the decision-making structure.

#### Step 3: Adding Sub-Criteria

After defining your main criteria, the next step is to establish sub-criteria for each main criterion. This is crucial for a more detailed analysis. In the case of price, sub-criteria could include purchase price, resell value, and maintenance costs. You can accomplish this by creating clusters for each criterion and then adding sub-criteria nodes under them.

1. **Create Clusters:** In the Super Decisions interface, you can create clusters for your criteria. Each cluster can contain related sub-criteria. 
2. **Define the Sub-Criteria:** Use a dialog box to define each sub-criterion, ensuring you provide appropriate names and descriptions for clarity.

#### Step 4: Establish Connections

Once both criteria and sub-criteria are defined, it is essential to create connections within your model. Connect the goal node to each criterion node and then connect each criterion to its respective sub-criteria. This network of connections illustrates the relationship between the criteria and their sub-criteria, providing a clear visual structure of your decision-making framework.

#### Step 5: Input Judgments

With the hierarchy established, the next step is to input judgments comparing the sub-criteria. This involves pairwise comparisons where you will evaluate the sub-criteria relative to one another, allowing you to assign weights based on their importance concerning each main criterion.

1. **Conduct Pairwise Comparisons:** Use the Super Decisions interface to rank the sub-criteria under each primary criterion through pairwise comparisons. This process helps to quantify the importance of each sub-criterion in relation to its parent criterion.
2. **Calculate Priorities:** After making the comparisons, the software will synthesize the results to calculate the priority weights for the sub-criteria.

#### Step 6: Analyze and Synthesize Results

After all judgments have been inputted and analyzed, the final step is to synthesize the results. Super Decisions will produce a summary table displaying the overall priorities, enabling the decision-maker to see how sub-criteria influence the outcome regarding the primary alternatives being assessed.

### Conclusion

Incorporating sub-criteria into the AHP hierarchy using Super Decisions is a critical aspect of detailed decision analysis. By creating a structured hierarchy, defining relevant criteria and sub-criteria, establishing connections, and inputting judgments, users are equipped to make well-informed, justified decisions. This method enhances clarity and precision, ultimately leading to improved outcomes in complex decision-making scenarios.
