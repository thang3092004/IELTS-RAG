The implementation of absolute measurement in the SuperDecisions software follows a structured methodology encompassing two primary phases. Below, I will outline each phase and its significance within the context of decision-making processes.

### Phase 1: Normalization of Values

In this initial phase, the primary activity revolves around normalizing the values present in the decision-making matrix. Normalization is crucial as it helps convert the raw scores or ratings associated with each candidate or alternative into a standardized format, ensuring that comparisons are meaningful and based on a uniform scale. This process typically includes:

1. **Calculating Normalized Columns**: Each criterion's scores are assessed to create normalized values. The highest score for each criterion is often used to scale all other scores within that column, allowing for clear relative comparisons.
   
2. **Identifying Idealized Alternatives**: After the normalization process, idealized alternatives or ideal scores are established. This step is important as it helps define what an optimal outcome looks like based on the normalized values, providing a benchmark against which all alternatives can be measured.

### Phase 2: Constructing the Decision Hierarchy

The second phase involves creating a comprehensive decision hierarchy that elaborates on the relationships between the goal, criteria, and alternatives. This phase includes essential actions such as:

1. **Building a Hierarchical Model**: Users are guided to organize their decision-making structure hierarchically. This involves connecting the main goal (e.g., selecting the best candidate) to various criteria (e.g., Admission Test Results (ATR), CGPA, Work Experience (WE), Letters of Recommendation (LOR), and Extracurricular Activities (EA)).
   
2. **Entering Judgments**: Each criterion is assessed against various alternatives (in this context, different candidates). Users enter their judgments or scores for how well each alternative meets the criteria, facilitating a comparison that drives the decision-making process.

3. **Synthesizing Results**: The culmination of this phase is synthesizing the collected judgments to calculate priorities, which further aids in determining the best alternative based on the established criteria.

### Conclusion 

Together, these two phases enable users of the SuperDecisions software to systematically implement absolute measurement in their decision-making. Phase one focuses on establishing a foundational understanding of values through normalization, leading to idealized benchmarks. Phase two shifts to a strategic arrangement of data, fostering a robust evaluation of alternatives through accessible comparisons and synthesized results. This structured approach guarantees informed and reliable decision-making outcomes.
