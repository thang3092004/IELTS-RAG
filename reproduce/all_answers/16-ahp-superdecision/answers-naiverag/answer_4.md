### Calculating Global Priorities of Alternatives in AHP using Super Decisions

The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured technique for organizing and analyzing complex decisions, based on mathematics and psychology. Super Decisions is a software tool that helps in implementing AHP by allowing users to create and manage decision frameworks. Here’s how to calculate global priorities of alternatives within the software:

#### Step 1: Define Your Hierarchy

1. **Create the AHP Model**: Begin by defining a clear hierarchy that includes at least three levels: the top level for your goal, the middle level for criteria, and the bottom level for alternatives. Each layer of your hierarchy allows you to break down your decision problem into manageable parts.
2. **Set Up Clusters**: Within Super Decisions, create clusters for each level of the hierarchy. For example, a goal cluster at the top, followed by criteria clusters, and then alternatives clusters at the lowest level.

#### Step 2: Pairwise Comparisons

1. **Conduct Pairwise Comparisons**: Use the software to input judgments regarding the relative importance of each criterion and alternative. This is typically done through pairwise comparison matrices that compare each element against others within the same cluster.
2. **Enter Judgments**: Each judgment is expressed using a scale (e.g., 1 to 9 scale) for consistency in rating the importance or preference. Once you have completed entering all comparisons for criteria and alternatives, the software will process these judgments.

#### Step 3: Synthesize Priorities

1. **Synthesize Local Priorities**: After conducting pairwise comparisons, Super Decisions synthesizes the local priorities for each cluster. Local priorities are essentially the rankings of each alternative based on the associated criteria.
2. **Calculate Global Priorities**: To calculate the global priorities, you need to multiply the local priorities of alternatives with the priorities of the corresponding criteria. This creates a composite score for each alternative, integrating its performance across all criteria.

#### Step 4: Analyze the Results

1. **Review Global Priorities**: Once the global priorities are calculated, Super Decisions displays these in a results section or synthesis table. This table presents a clear ranking of all alternatives, showing which options are the best based on the overall criteria.
2. **Sensitivity Analysis**: It may also be beneficial to conduct a sensitivity analysis to observe how changes in criteria weights affect the global priorities of alternatives. Super Decisions provides visual representations to help understand the robustness of these priorities.

### Conclusion

Using Super Decisions to implement AHP allows for systematic decision-making through clearly defined hierarchies, structured pairwise comparisons, synthesis of priorities, and analysis of results. By following these steps, users can efficiently determine global priorities for alternatives in complex decision scenarios, ensuring that all relevant factors are carefully considered and weighted in the decision-making process.
