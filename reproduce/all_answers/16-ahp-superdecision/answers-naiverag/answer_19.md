In SuperDecisions, various visualization options are available for displaying sensitivity analysis results. These options are designed to facilitate the interpretation of the data derived from the analysis, making it easier for users to understand how changes in criteria weights affect the prioritization of alternatives. Below are the primary visualization formats supported by the software:

### 1. **Bar Charts**
Bar charts are utilized to visually compare the priority levels of different alternatives based on selected criteria. Each alternative is represented by a colored bar, and the height of the bar correlates with its priority value. Users can alter parameters to observe how these changes impact the overall performance of the alternatives.

### 2. **Pie Charts**
Pie charts provide a circular representation of the distribution of priorities among the alternatives, showcasing the relative proportions of each option. This method is particularly useful for quickly grasping the overall distribution of priorities without delving into numerical specifics.

### 3. **Scatter Plots**
Scatter plots can be employed to display normalized values against parameter adjustments, allowing users to see how the values fluctuate across different criterion weights. This dynamic representation helps in identifying trends and correlations between the established parameters.

### 4. **Line Graphs**
Line graphs are effective for illustrating the changes in priorities over a range of criterion weights. Users can plot different lines for each alternative, offering a clear view of how prioritization shifts in response to variations in the criteria.

### 5. **3D Bar Charts**
Enhanced visualization techniques, such as 3D bar charts, can be used to present complex data sets visually. This offers an additional depth perception which can aid in comprehending relationships and variations among multiple alternatives and criteria.

### Conclusion
These various representation options enhance the analytical capabilities of the SuperDecisions software by allowing users to interactively explore how different factors influence decision outcomes. By leveraging these visualization tools, analysts can effectively communicate the robustness of their models and insights gained from sensitivity analysis, ultimately aiding in informed decision-making processes.
