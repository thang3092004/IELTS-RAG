### Understanding the Unweighted Supermatrix in AHP

The unweighted supermatrix is a fundamental concept in the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), specifically used in decision-making environments where hierarchical structures are present. This matrix serves as a tool for capturing and representing the relative priorities of alternatives under various criteria without applying any weights. Essentially, it allows decision-makers to visualize how alternatives rank concerning each criterion based on direct comparisons, making it easier to analyze and synthesize results later.

In AHP, alternatives are evaluated across several criteria, leading to a structured comparison matrix. Each element within the unweighted supermatrix reflects the priority of an alternative concerning a specific criterion. Consequently, the unweighted supermatrix is established in several steps, typically starting from a decision-making model where judgments and comparisons have been made.

### Generating the Unweighted Supermatrix in Super Decisions

In the SuperDecisions software, generating the unweighted supermatrix involves systematic entry of judgments and preferences pertaining to various alternatives under specific criteria. Here are the detailed steps to create this matrix:

1. **Inputting Judgments**: Users begin by entering judgments regarding the relative importance of the criteria and alternatives. This is achieved through pairwise comparisons where users evaluate how each alternative measures up against others based on established criteria.

2. **Normalizing Values**: Once the judgments are entered, the software processes these values, normalizing them to ensure consistency and comparability across the matrix. This normalization typically entails summing the judgments within a category and dividing each judgment by the total to provide a relative weight.

3. **Constructing the Supermatrix**: After normalization, the unweighted supermatrix is formed. Within SuperDecisions, users can visualize this matrix, which organizes the priorities of alternatives against the criteria comprehensively. The unweighted matrix merely reflects the priorities without applying any additional weights, which gives a clear snapshot of the relative standings among alternatives.

4. **Reviewing and Adjusting**: As users examine the unweighted supermatrix, they have the opportunity to make adjustments based on their insights or further data analysis. This interactive feature is key in ensuring the model reflects the most accurate assessment for decision-making.

5. **Final Analysis**: After constructing the unweighted supermatrix, further computations can be performed, such as synthesizing weights or conducting sensitivity analysis. This allows decision-makers to explore various scenarios and understand how the unweighted priorities might shift based on changes to the criteria weightings.

### Conclusion

The unweighted supermatrix is crucial in AHP as it provides a structured mechanism for assessing alternatives based on criteria without introducing biases through weights. In SuperDecisions, the process of creating this matrix is straightforward, starting from pairwise comparisons through normalization to the final visual representation. This software-driven approach facilitates robust decision-making by encapsulating complex evaluations into an easily interpretable format, ultimately aiding users in making well-informed decisions.
