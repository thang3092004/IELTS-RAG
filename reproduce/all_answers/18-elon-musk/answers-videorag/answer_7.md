In discussions related to the production of humanoid robots, Elon Musk compares it to the automotive industry, particularly referencing the production capacity of vehicles. He notes that the global capacity for vehicles is around a hundred million per year, stating that this figure reflects both the existing demand for vehicles and the overall capability of manufacturing them. He goes on to suggest that the potential utility and demand for humanoid robots might be even greater than that of cars, positing that humanoid robots could see production at a scale of possibly over a billion units per year. This comparison reflects the ambitious perspective Musk has regarding the future of robotics and its integration into daily life, emphasizing the vast possibilities for humanoid robots akin to that of passenger vehicles. 

This consideration of production parallels highlights the increasing importance and potential of humanoid robotics in future technological advancements and societal structures.
