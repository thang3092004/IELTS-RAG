In discussions regarding the production of humanoid robots, Elon Musk compares it to the automobile industry. He notes that the global capacity for vehicle production is approximately one hundred million vehicles per year, which sets a context for the potential scale of humanoid robot manufacturing. Musk suggests that, while the current demand for vehicles is around that number, the utility and demand for humanoid robots could be significantly higher, indicating that humanoid robots might reach production rates of one billion or more annually. This comparison highlights the advancements in technology and manufacturing capability that could support the mass production of humanoid robots in the future. 

This analogy emphasizes the transformative potential of robotics in various industries, akin to how automobiles revolutionized transportation and society as a whole. The aim is to underscore the feasibility of scaling up humanoid robot production to meet anticipated demand, similar to established automotive production rates.
