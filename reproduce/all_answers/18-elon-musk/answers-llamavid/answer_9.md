The first several years of Neuralink focused on solving basic neuron damage issues like quadriplegia and cerebral palsy. The first patient, Nolan Arbaugh, had severe spinal cord damage that left him paralyzed from the neck down. Neuralink aimed to restore some of his movement and sensation, though it was not able to give him complete mobility or feeling back. The team also focused on training the AI to recognize and decode the neuron signals and translate them into useful data. The first Neuralink device had a relatively low data rate of 1000 BPS, which was sufficient for basic communication, but not for more complex tasks. The team worked to improve the data rate and signal processing to enable more advanced applications.
