Elon Musk compares the production of humanoid robots to the production of cars in terms of global capacity. He notes that the production of cars is on the order of 100 million cars a year, while the production of humanoid robots is on the order of 1 billion robots a year. This is due to the significantly higher complexity and sophistication of humanoid robots compared to cars. Humanoid robots require much more engineering and programming to build, so they cannot be mass-produced at the same rate as cars. However, the potential for humanoid robots to be mass-produced at such a scale is exciting, as it could greatly benefit society by providing more advanced automation and artificial intelligence.
