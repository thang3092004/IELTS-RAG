Elon Musk compares the production of humanoid robots to the automotive industry, specifically referencing vehicle production rates. He notes that the global capacity for vehicles is about 100 million per year, suggesting that humanoid robots, due to their greater utility, could potentially reach production rates of over a billion per year. This analogy emphasizes the challenges and advancements related to automating and scaling the production of humanoid robots to meet significant demand, similar to existing automotive manufacturing capacities. 

Musk points out that while humanoid robots are still considered an extremely difficult problem in terms of engineering and production, he anticipates that with continued efforts, their production could become a substantial reality akin to that of cars. This comparison not only illustrates the ambitions behind robotic technology but also highlights the complexity and resource investment required to achieve similar output levels as found in the automotive sector.
