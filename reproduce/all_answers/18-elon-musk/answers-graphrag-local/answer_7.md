Elon Musk compares the production of humanoid robots to the automotive industry, specifically referencing vehicles in terms of global capacity. He discusses that the current global demand for vehicles is around 100 million per year, indicating that there is a substantial market and manufacturing capability within the automotive sector. Musk suggests that, similarly, humanoid robots could potentially see production rates exceeding a billion units per year, demonstrating a vision for scalability in humanoid robot manufacturing that parallels the automotive industry.

This comparison highlights both the challenges and opportunities in scaling up production for advanced technologies like humanoid robots, emphasizing the complexity and engineering effort required to achieve such goals. The automotive industry, with its established manufacturing processes, serves as a benchmark for Musk's ambitions in the field of robotics, particularly concerning the utility and integration of humanoid robots into everyday life.
