Slowing down communication drastically affects one's perception. Having to communicate at a slower pace can make one feel frustrated, as if they are being held back from expressing their thoughts efficiently. For example, if forced to speak at 1/10th the normal speed, one might find it "agonizingly slow." It can be compared to the feeling of impatience when using older computers with limited RAM and processing power.  Conversely, once accustomed to a faster pace of communication, like listening to podcasts at 1.5x speed, the original speed can feel "painfully slow." This suggests that our perception of the pace of information flow adapts to what we are accustomed to.
