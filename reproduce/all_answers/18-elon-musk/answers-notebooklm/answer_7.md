Elon Musk compares the production of humanoid robots to the **car industry** in terms of global capacity. The global capacity for vehicles is about 100 million per year.  Musk states that while this number could be higher, the demand is only around 100 million vehicles per year. There are roughly 2 billion vehicles in use. With an average vehicle lifespan of about 20 years, the production of 100 million vehicles per year makes sense for a 2 billion vehicle fleet.  Musk believes that because the utility of humanoid robots is much greater, they will be produced at a scale of **over a billion per year**.
