Elon Musk uses a **“tech tree” metaphor** to explain Neuralink’s development stages. Similar to how one needs literacy to have “Lord of the Rings”, Neuralink must start by focusing on basic functions, like restoring communication, before achieving higher-level functionalities, like memory enhancement.  Musk emphasizes that **Neuralink’s immediate focus is to solve basic neurological problems** like those experienced by individuals with spinal cord or neck damage, or near-complete loss of connection between the brain and body, as in the case of the first two Neuralink patients. These patients face significant challenges, and Neuralink aims to provide them with the ability to communicate more effectively, similar to how Stephen Hawking could have benefited from the technology.

 Musk acknowledges that **there are inherent risks associated with this new device, and these risks cannot be entirely eliminated**. Therefore,  **it’s crucial to prioritize solving issues with the highest potential reward for patients, given the inherent risks**. Starting with addressing fundamental neurological damage ensures that the **potential benefits outweigh the risks for patients.** Once the technology matures, the risks decrease, and thousands of individuals have used it for years, Musk suggests that expanding Neuralink’s applications to non-disabled individuals might become a possibility.
