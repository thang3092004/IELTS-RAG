The acceptance speeches of some developers at The Game Awards emphasize how game development is a collaborative effort. For example, when Nicolas Doucet from Team Asobi won Best Game Direction for "Astrobot," he highlighted that, in their case, **"it's really like every single person on the team coming and really bringing their own vision and being responsible for their own work."** Doucet emphasized that each person on the team acted as a game director, showcasing how success comes from the combined efforts of every individual. 

Furthermore, when accepting the award for Best Family Game for "Astrobot," Doucet **thanked "Team Asobi over in Japan, all 65 of you,"** acknowledging the significant number of individuals contributing to the game's development.  He went on to express the importance of making family games, as they often create people's first gaming memories. This sentiment highlights how game developers can impact players' lives through collaborative efforts that create meaningful experiences.
