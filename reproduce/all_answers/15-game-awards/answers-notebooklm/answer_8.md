The nominees for Best Ongoing Game at The Game Awards 2024 can offer insight into what keeps players engaged and coming back for more. Based on the nominated games, here are some key factors that contribute to a game’s longevity:

*   **Regular Updates and Fresh Content:**  Games like *Fortnite* thrive on constant updates. New content, like game modes, weapons, map changes, and seasonal events, provide players with reasons to revisit and stay invested. This prevents the experience from becoming stale and keeps players anticipating what’s next.
*   **Community Support and Engagement:**  Listening to player feedback and actively engaging with the community can foster a sense of ownership and investment in a game. This was a strong point for *Helldivers 2*, the winner of the Best Ongoing Game award. This can be achieved through community managers, social media interaction, and in-game events tailored to player preferences. 
*   **Live Service Elements:**  The “Games as a Service” model, popularized by titles like *Fortnite*, focuses on continuous content updates, seasonal battle passes, and in-game events. This creates a cycle of engagement where players are encouraged to participate in the latest offerings and potentially spend money on microtransactions. 
*   **Expanding Upon a Strong Foundation:** Successful ongoing games often start with a strong core gameplay loop that’s both enjoyable and has the potential for expansion. Building upon this foundation with meaningful additions and improvements can keep players hooked. 

It’s worth noting that *Diablo 4*, a game with a loyal following and ongoing content updates, was not the winner of this award. This suggests that even with regular updates, **capturing and retaining player interest over time requires more than just adding new content. Factors like innovation, addressing community concerns, and providing a consistently rewarding experience play a crucial role in a game’s longevity.**
