The games nominated for Best Ongoing Game demonstrate various strategies for maintaining player engagement and longevity. Some key factors include:

1. Regular updates and new content: Many of the nominated games, like Warframe and The Last of Us Part 2, release new updates and content regularly to keep players engaged. This keeps players interested and invested in the game world. 

2. Innovative gameplay: Games that offer unique and engaging gameplay mechanics, like Shadow of the Colossus's combat system, tend to keep players coming back. The innovation helps players feel like they are experiencing something new and exciting.

3. Strong narrative and storytelling: Games with compelling narratives and stories, like Animal Crossing: New Horizons and The Last of Us Part 2, help keep players engaged. A good story can create an emotional connection to the game world.

4. Supportive communities: Games that have active and supportive communities, like Warframe and The Last of Us Part 2, can help players feel connected to the game and keep coming back. Players enjoy discussing the game and sharing experiences with others.

5. Reasonable difficulty: Games that provide challenging but manageable difficulty, like Wukong and Astrobot, can keep players engaged for an extended period. Players feel rewarded for their efforts.

In summary, the nominated games show that a combination of regular updates, innovative gameplay, engaging narratives, supportive communities, and reasonable difficulty can help maintain player engagement and longevity. These factors help keep players invested in the game world and enjoy the experience.
