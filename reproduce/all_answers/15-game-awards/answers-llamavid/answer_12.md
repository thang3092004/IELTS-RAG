Accessibility features in modern games are becoming increasingly important as the gaming industry expands to include players with disabilities, special needs, and diverse backgrounds. Many developers now strive to make games that are inclusive and enjoyable for all players, not just those with perfect motor skills and vision. Some key aspects of modern game design that promote accessibility include:

- Inclusive controls: Games are being designed with accessible controls that allow players to play with a wide range of physical abilities. For example, games like A Way Out use swappable control schemes so players can adjust the difficulty and accessibility to suit their needs. 

- Larger text and clearer fonts: Larger text and clearer fonts are used in menus, dialogue, and other in-game elements to make them easier to read for players with vision issues.

- Accessible user interfaces: User interfaces like menus, dialogue boxes, and controls are designed to be easy to navigate for all players, not just those with perfect vision. 

- Inclusive game design: Games are being designed with accessibility in mind, using features like adjustable difficulty levels, difficulty sliders, and color contrasts to make them easier for players with disabilities.

- Support for assistive technologies: Many games now support accessibility technologies like closed captioning, subtitles, and screen readers.

In summary, accessibility features are becoming a major consideration in modern game design to ensure that all players, regardless of their abilities, can enjoy the full gaming experience.
