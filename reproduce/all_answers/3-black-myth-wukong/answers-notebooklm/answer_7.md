In Black Wind Mountain, the player encounters a variety of characters and events connected to the protagonist's journey to recover the six relics of Sun Wukong. 

**The player first encounters a large black bear guarding the entrance to Guanyin Temple**. The temple was once bustling with worshippers before it was ruined by fire. The bear tells the player that rebuilding the temple is useless because the goodwill of men has been burnt to ashes. 

**The player must ring three bells in the mountain**. These bells were set in the mountains since the Black Wind Demon's return, and no one is allowed to go near them. After ringing the third bell, the player discovers it is an ancient version of a bell they previously encountered.

**The player encounters the spirit of a dead snake who asks them to pass a message to his master.** The message warns that mortals who aspire to achieve immortality will surely conspire.

**The player learns about the story of an evil monk who was burnt to ashes**. However, his resentment caused him to turn into a Yaguar, a type of demon in Chinese mythology.

**The player fights a large black bear at the summit of the mountain**. After defeating him, the bear reveals that the player has freed a miserable soul and warns them to stay sharp and keep a lookout because the enemies are getting harder. The bear also teaches the player the cloud step, a move that allows the player to disperse into mist, escape, and execute a surprise attack.

**The player discovers the story of the black bear they defeated**. He was once a monk named Jin Chanzi, the second disciple to Buddha in the West. His arrogance led him to disregard the Dharma teachings, and as a result, Buddha cast him down to the Mortal realm. He was destined to face 81 trials throughout his journey, but his greed for a casiah, a type of sacred fruit, led to his demise. 

These events demonstrate the challenges and obstacles the player faces in recovering the six relics of Sun Wukong.
