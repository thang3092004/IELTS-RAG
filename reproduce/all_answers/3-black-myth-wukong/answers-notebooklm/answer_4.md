The first part of the "Black Myth Wukong" gameplay video describes several in-game mechanics, including: 

*   **Combat mechanics**. Actions, like attacking, consume stamina. When stamina is depleted, the ability to perform actions is limited.
*   **Spells**. A new spell, immobilize, is learned in this part by the player. Immobilize binds enemies for a short time and gives the player a chance to strategize.  The player also has access to other spells, like a ring of fire, that can be upgraded.
*   **Talents**. Players can use "Sparks" to awaken talents and level up. Sparks are not lost upon death.  There are different categories of talents that enhance different skills, like Vigor and stamina.
*   **Gourd**. This appears to be a healing item that players need to replenish.
*   **Transformations**. The player unlocks a transformation that allows them to turn into a golden cicada. This lasts for a limited time and allows the player to explore areas undetected. The player can also transform into a "powerful yaogu" by launching spirit skills.
*   **Spirit skills**. These transform the player into a "yaogu" to unleash signature abilities. Spirit skills cost "key" which can be recovered by absorbing it from enemies during combat.  They can be cultivated at Keeper Shrines to enhance them.
*   **Equipment**. The player is shown to have a staff that can be upgraded.
*   **Crafting**. The player is able to craft items like medicine and weapons. For example, there is a weapon called the "twin serpent staff."
*   **Relics**. Players receive relics for completing chapters. There are three options for each relic.

The video also demonstrates additional features of the game, including: 

*   **Keeper Shrines**. These act as save points. Players can offer incense here to restore their conditions, replenish their Gourd, and respawn enemies. They can also fast travel to any Keeper Shrine where incense has already been offered. Players can also reallocate their Sparks and choose their spells at a Keeper Shrine.
*   **Currency**. Players collect money throughout the game, depicted as gold. There is also a store where players can buy and sell.
