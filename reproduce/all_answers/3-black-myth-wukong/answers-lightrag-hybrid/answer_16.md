The camera behavior during combat scenarios in games like *Nioh 2* and *Dark Souls III* is a critical component that significantly enhances the player's immersive experience. Different enemy types can trigger various camera techniques, impacting how players perceive and engage during intense battles.

### Dynamic Camera Angles

During combat, dynamic camera angles shift to capture the action from different perspectives, emphasizing the scale and intensity of the encounters. For instance, as the Player Character engages with formidable enemies like the Armored Beast Creature or large insect-like beings, the camera may pull back to provide a broader view of the surrounding battlefield, allowing players to better assess their environment and position relative to their adversaries. This technique not only heightens the visual storytelling but also enhances the tactical awareness required to navigate through combat without losing track of enemy movements.

Moreover, when facing smaller, agile enemies, such as the Agile Enemy or various humanoid foes, the camera tends to shift in closer to follow the rapid movements and quick exchanges of strikes. This close-up perspective can create a sense of urgency, drawing players into the intensity of melee scenarios and highlighting moments of critical action, such as dodges and counterattacks.

### Engaging Visual Effects

The camera also works in conjunction with visual effects that respond to the player's actions and the enemies' attacks. For example, when a player executes a powerful strike or uses special abilities, the camera may zoom in or capture the action in slow motion, showcasing dramatic visual effects like energy bursts, sparks, or fire, enhancing the overall immersive experience. This synchronization between camera movements and visual effects amplifies the impact of each action taken during combat.

### Environmental Interaction

In addition to dynamic angles and visual enhancements, the camera's movement often interacts with the game's environments. For instance, when combat takes place in tight spaces like ancient temples or rocky terrains, the camera might adjust to avoid clipping through geometry, maintaining an unobstructed viewpoint. This adaptability ensures that players remain aware of their surroundings even during frenetic combat, allowing for effective use of environmental features and strategic maneuvers.

### Summary

Overall, the camera's behavior during combat with different enemies is designed to create a more engaging and responsive gameplay experience. Techniques such as dynamic angles, engaging visual effects, and environmental interactions work seamlessly to elevate the intensity of battles, ensuring players can fully immerse themselves in the action while effectively managing the challenges posed by a diverse range of adversaries.
