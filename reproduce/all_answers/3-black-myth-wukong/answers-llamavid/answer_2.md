The narrative explores the nature of power and its consequences through the characters and their actions. Some key examples:

- Wukong's quest for the relics represents the pursuit of power and enlightenment, which can be both beneficial and corrupting. 

- The characters face temptation and corruption, showing how power can be misused. 

- The characters' decisions to use the relics for their own gain or to help others reflect their values and priorities.

- The characters' struggles to balance power with morality and wisdom highlight the complex nature of power.

- The conflicts and confrontations between the characters demonstrate the power dynamics at play and the consequences of different choices.

- The ultimate lesson is that power is not absolute, and the consequences of using power can be either positive or negative, depending on how it is wielded.
