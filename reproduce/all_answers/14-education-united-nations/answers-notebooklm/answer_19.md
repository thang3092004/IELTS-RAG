The UN’s response to the Taliban’s restrictions on Afghan girls’ education has been multi-pronged, emphasizing advocacy, collaboration with stakeholders like the Organization of Islamic Countries (OIC), continued program implementation through partners, and highlighting the humanitarian crisis.

*   **Advocacy and Engagement**: UN representatives, including the head of Education Cannot Wait, have directly engaged with the Taliban, expressing concerns about the ban on girls' secondary education and emphasizing the importance of education for all children, especially girls. These discussions revealed internal struggles within the Taliban, with some supporting girls' education and others opposing it.
*   **Collaboration with the OIC**: The UN recognizes the potential role of the OIC in promoting education for Afghan girls, given the Taliban's self-identification as an Islamic Emirate. The UN believes that the OIC, representing the Muslim world where girls' secondary education is a norm, can effectively communicate Islamic values supporting education for all.
*   **Continued Program Implementation**: Despite the challenges, the UN, through agencies like UNICEF and partners like the Aga Khan Foundation and Swedish Committee for Afghanistan, continues to implement educational programs at the local level in multiple provinces. These programs, focusing on primary education and reaching out through civil society, ensure that some learning opportunities remain available.
*   **Highlighting the Humanitarian Crisis**:  The UN has consistently raised awareness about the dire humanitarian situation in Afghanistan, including the impact on education. The organization emphasizes the need for continued humanitarian assistance, including educational support, delivered directly to the vulnerable population. This approach underscores the urgency of the situation and the importance of not abandoning the Afghan people.

The UN’s response acknowledges the complexities of the situation, navigating the political landscape while prioritizing the needs of Afghan girls. This approach combines direct engagement with advocacy, practical program implementation, and a focus on the broader humanitarian context. While acknowledging the challenges and setbacks, the UN remains committed to supporting education for all Afghan children.
