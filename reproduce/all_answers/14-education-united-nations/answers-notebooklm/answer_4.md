The Transforming Education Summit (TES) aims to address the **global crisis in education**, focusing on **access, quality, and relevance**. 

The Summit seeks to generate **new ideas and renewed urgency to end the crisis**, and to **reimagine education systems to be fit for the 21st century**. The ultimate goal is to ensure that **education is accessible to every person in the world and empowers them to help shape a more peaceful, just, and sustainable future**. 

Some more specific goals mentioned in the sources include: 

*   **Renewing commitments to SDG4** (quality education for all). 
*   **Developing all the potential of all young people**.
*   **Addressing learning poverty** (the inability to understand a simple text by age 10), which has been exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic.
*   **Transforming education to meet the needs of the 21st century**, including skills needed for the digital age and climate action.
*   **Creating a global movement** involving governments, youth, civil society, teachers, the corporate sector, and donors.
*   **Increasing funding** for education.
*   **Ensuring inclusion**, particularly for marginalized groups such as girls, refugees, and learners with disabilities.

The sources show that the Transforming Education Summit aims to create a **global movement** to **reimagine education**, **transform learning systems**, and **revitalize global commitments to education for all**.
