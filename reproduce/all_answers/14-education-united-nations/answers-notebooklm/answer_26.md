Education systems can be made more resilient to shocks and crises through sufficient financial investment and inclusive policies. 

According to the UN, education is in crisis. Shocks and crises such as the COVID-19 pandemic and climate change have exacerbated this crisis. The speakers at the UN’s Transforming Education Summit in September 2022 emphasized the need to take immediate action to address this crisis, highlighting the need to include marginalized groups, adequately fund education, and ensure quality education for all. 

**Financial investment** is essential for educational resilience to shocks. Speakers at the Summit called upon governments to increase domestic investment in education by raising the percentage of the national budget allocated to education and canceling school fees and exam fees. They also asked international development partners to increase aid, cancel debts, and ensure a fair global tax system so low-income countries can invest more in girls’ education. Additionally, more financial resources must be directed to training and recruiting more teachers and paying teachers adequate salaries.

**Inclusion** is also key. Education should be accessible for everyone, including girls, children with disabilities, and refugees. In Afghanistan, the Taliban have banned girls from education. Countries should lift restrictions on girls’ access to education and work to overcome deeply rooted stereotypes and social norms that limit access to education for women and girls. Speakers also emphasized the need to include refugees in national education systems, particularly now as the number of refugees is increasing due to climate change, war, and hunger. This will require good, inclusive policies and more resources.  

Many speakers highlighted the essential role of **teachers** in building resilient education systems.  Teachers need to be trained in a variety of areas, including literacy and numeracy, psychosocial support, and gender equality.  They need to be given adequate resources and better working conditions, including higher salaries.

To transform education systems, they must be aligned with the world today. This means taking a multi-sectoral approach to education and **working across disciplines**, incorporating scientific inquiry and ensuring education systems provide the skills and knowledge needed to address today's challenges, such as climate change and gender inequality. 

Finally, speakers stressed the importance of **youth engagement**. Young people must be included at every stage of decision-making, not just consulted at the end of the process. Speakers called for intergenerational dialogue and co-creation of solutions, ensuring that young people are not just beneficiaries but partners.
