The sources provide several pieces of evidence to demonstrate progress in girls' education since Malala Yousafzai began advocating for the issue.

*   In her remarks at the Transforming Education Summit, Malala Yousafzai notes that more girls are in school than ever before. 
*   The Secretary-General of the United Nations also highlights this progress, stating that more countries have reached parity in education enrollment. 
*   Yousafzai offers a personal anecdote about her mother, who was forced to leave school at age six but was inspired by Yousafzai’s advocacy to resume her education as an adult. Yousafzai’s mother now attends English classes and is able to navigate her community independently, demonstrating the transformative power of education.

However, the sources also emphasize that significant challenges to girls’ education remain: 

*   While there has been progress at the primary level, **adolescent girls are still dramatically disadvantaged**. 
*   **130 million girls are not in school at all, and a large proportion of girls do not go on to secondary education**.
*   **Social and institutional barriers persist**, discouraging girls from pursuing careers in STEM fields.

While celebrating the progress made, the sources underscore **the need for continued action and investment to ensure that all girls have access to quality education**.
