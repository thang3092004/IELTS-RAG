The concept of “transformational teaching” is not explicitly defined in the sources. However, the sources do provide many insights into the roles and skills teachers need to foster student leadership and empowerment.

Teachers are essential to transforming education, playing a critical role in creating a more equitable, inclusive, and sustainable future. The sources highlight the following ways teachers can contribute to this transformation:

*   **Teachers should act as facilitators who promote learning, rather than just transmitting answers.** They should encourage student voice and participation in shaping the learning process.
*   **Teachers should promote the physical and mental health of students, including their sexual and reproductive health.** This includes creating a safe and supportive learning environment that is free from violence, stigma, and intimidation.
*   **Teachers should be trained to use digital technologies effectively and develop high-quality digital educational content.** This will help to ensure that all students have access to the benefits of the digital revolution.
*   **Teachers should be trained in strategies that ensure students with disabilities are included in the classroom.**
*   **Teachers need to be supported with decent working conditions, continuous training and learning opportunities, and adequate salaries.** This will help to attract and retain qualified teachers and improve the quality of education.
*   **Teachers must be trusted and given professional autonomy to interact with students and challenge them to become critical thinkers.**

**By adopting these roles and skills, teachers can become agents of transformational change in education, empowering students to become leaders and active citizens who can shape a more sustainable and just world.**

The sources also emphasize the importance of **collaboration between teachers, governments, young people, civil society, and other stakeholders to achieve this transformation.** This includes creating opportunities for dialogue, sharing best practices, and developing policies that support the professional development and well-being of teachers.

Specifically, the sources describe the following collaborative efforts to engage young people:

*   **School councils** bring together students and school leadership to facilitate communication and improve the school environment.
*   **Youth parliaments** allow young people to propose solutions to improve education.
*   **Youth reports** provide a platform for young people to ask questions and share their proposals for improving education.
*   **National councils of young people** include education committees that consider proposals from youth organizations and make them available to the Ministry of Education.
*   **Student assemblies** provide regular opportunities for students to discuss curricular options and share their views on how they learn best.
*   **Participatory budgeting** allows students to decide how a portion of the school budget is spent.

By working together, all stakeholders can ensure that education is truly transformative, empowering all learners to reach their full potential and contribute to a better future.
