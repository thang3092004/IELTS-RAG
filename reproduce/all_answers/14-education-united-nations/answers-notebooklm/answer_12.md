To address the global shortage of teachers and improve the quality of teaching, governments and teachers can take a number of actions, including:

**Governments** can: 

* **Increase funding for education**, making it the number one priority. This includes protecting education budgets, ensuring that expenditure translates into increased resources per student and improved results, and devoting at least 15% of public development aid to education. Governments should also work to alleviate the debts of poor countries, which can make it difficult to fund education.  
* **Work with international financial institutions** to provide resources to developing countries, allowing them budgetary flexibility to invest disbursements. 
* **Raise the status of teachers** and ensure they have decent working conditions and continuous training and learning opportunities.
* **Ensure teachers receive adequate salaries**.
* **Invest in resilient, inclusive, and shock-responsive public education systems**. This includes increasing or maintaining the share of public expenditure on education and taking additional measures to mitigate the effects of school closures, such as investing in learning, child nutrition, and programs to prevent violence. 
* **Work to ensure that schools are safe spaces** free from violence, stigma, or intimidation.
* **Adopt education and lifelong learning strategies, policies, and budgets that ensure gender equality in and through education**.
* **Support the training and recruitment of teachers**, as well as investing in their training and development. 
* **Promote digital technologies in education**, including access to broadband internet and technology devices, and incorporate digital competences into educational systems.

**Teachers** can: 

* **Engage in collaborative professionalism and organizing** to transform the cause of history.
* **Advocate for decent salaries, trust from policymakers, and professional autonomy**.
* **Challenge students to become critical human beings**.

Ultimately, **transforming education requires a collaborative effort between governments, teachers, students, and communities**.
