To effectively reach marginalized populations in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs) with agendas like the SDGs, NGOs and grassroots organizations should consider a bottom-up approach, focusing on collaboration with local organizations and empowering local advocates. 

*   **Working with local organizations provides valuable insights into the specific needs and challenges of marginalized communities**. These organizations often have established trust and relationships within those communities.
*   **Empowering and supporting local advocates**, like those involved in the Malala Fund's Gulmakai Network, allows for targeted efforts that are tailored to the specific context of each community. These advocates can effectively champion change within their communities because they understand the local dynamics.

Additionally, **collaboration and information sharing among NGOs** are crucial. 

*   By **working together**, NGOs can pool their resources, knowledge, and experience to create more impactful interventions. 
*   **Sharing information and best practices** allows organizations to learn from each other and refine their strategies for reaching marginalized populations.

This approach ensures that interventions are **community-driven and sustainable** and that **local voices are amplified in the pursuit of the SDGs**.
