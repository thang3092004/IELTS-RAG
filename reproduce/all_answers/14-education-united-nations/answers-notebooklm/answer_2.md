Education Cannot Wait (ECW) is a global fund for education in emergencies and protracted crises.  Here are some ways their model facilitates their reach:

* **Joint Programming:** EWC's model is built upon the U.N. Secretary-General's reform and common agenda, using joint programming where multiple actors contribute to a single program. This collaborative approach, with different U.N. agencies, civil society organizations, and local communities working together, eliminates competition and allows each actor to use their comparative advantage, maximizing their reach. For example, in the Central African Republic, where there are 12 different militia groups, EWC and its partners are able to reach children in militia-controlled areas with holistic, quality education through diplomacy, trust, and collaboration. 

* **Reaching the Most Vulnerable:** EWC focuses on children and youth in conflict, emergency, or forced displacement situations, as well as climate-induced disasters. This includes reaching children in refugee camps, internally displaced persons (IDP) camps, and even militia-controlled areas.

* **Resource Mobilization:** EWC leverages funding from various sources, including private sector, member states of the United Nations, high net worth individuals, and foundations. Their successful resource mobilization efforts have allowed them to reach millions of children and youth with quality education. For example, in 2021 alone, they mobilized close to 390 million dollars, a dramatic increase from previous years. In total, their trust fund has mobilized 1.1 billion dollars, and through joint programming in the country, an additional 1 billion dollars has been aligned with their programs. 

* **Holistic Approach:** EWC provides a holistic quality education that addresses not just academic needs, but also the mental health and psychosocial needs of children and youth. They have trained close to 30,000 teachers in various subjects, including literacy, numeracy, mental health, and psychosocial services. This holistic approach ensures that children receive the support they need to thrive in challenging circumstances. 

* **Focus on Gender Parity:**  ECW is committed to gender parity in education.  92% of their investments demonstrated gender parity, not just in numbers, but also by breaking down barriers toward girls' inclusion in education. They have also seen an increased rate of completion of education for girls.  This commitment to gender parity ensures that girls have equal opportunities to access and complete quality education.
