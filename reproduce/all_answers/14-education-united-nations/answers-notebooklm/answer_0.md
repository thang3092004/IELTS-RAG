Technology can be used to increase access to education, improve the quality of education, and make education more relevant to the 21st century. The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted that the digital revolution is the key to a development revolution in education. However, the technological revolution must benefit all school children.

Here are several ways that technology could be leveraged to transform education:

*   **Expand access to education for students in remote areas and crisis settings.** For instance, there is a potential to use virtual classrooms to connect students in impoverished places with teachers at leading universities around the world.
*   **Provide students in developing countries access to the same information as students in developed countries.** For instance, students can learn from educational platforms like Khan Academy.
*   **Improve the quality of education through better learning assessments and data collection.** For instance, education systems can collect, analyze, aggregate, and use data to inform policy decisions.
*   **Train teachers to utilize technology effectively.**

However, it's important to acknowledge that there are limitations to using technology in education, including:

*   Electricity and connectivity issues
*   Cost and maintenance of technology
*   Teacher training

The key is to empower governments to determine the best way to use technology given their individual circumstances. Technology is not a silver bullet solution, but it can be a powerful tool for transforming education when used strategically and in conjunction with other interventions.  It's crucial to understand where technology can play a role and to apply technological solutions in ways that don't displace the authority and role of the government.

It's also important to remember that **education is about more than just access to information and technology**. It's also about creating a protective environment for students, developing their social and emotional skills, and helping them to learn how to learn. Technology can be a valuable tool for achieving these goals, but it's not a substitute for quality teaching, strong curricula, and supportive learning environments.

The international community must come together to invest in education and support countries in using technology to transform their education systems. This includes providing funding for infrastructure, equipment, and teacher training. It also includes working with governments to develop policies that promote inclusive and equitable access to education for all learners.
