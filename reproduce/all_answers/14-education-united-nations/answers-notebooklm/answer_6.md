A redistribution of wealth could help achieve education goals by 2030 by providing more funding for education in poorer countries. While the average cost for industrialized countries to educate a child for one year is about $8,000 to $10,000, the global fund Education Cannot Wait is asking for $250 per child and doesn't even have that much funding. If governments, high-net-worth individuals, and private sector companies would redistribute some of their wealth, they could help the UN meet its education goals by 2030.
