According to Yasmine Sharif, the head of Education Cannot Wait, the Organization of Islamic Countries (OIC) can play an instrumental role in promoting education for girls in Afghanistan. Sharif stated that the OIC **“can play an instrumental role especially if if if if the de facto thought is defined themselves on a religious basis Islamic Emirate.”** Because the de facto authorities in Afghanistan call themselves the Islamic Emirate, the OIC, as a group of Muslim states, **“naturally would you know be a useful partner.”**

Sharif also stated that **“there is no Muslim country today in the world where secondary girls do not go to school except Afghanistan.”** Girls attend secondary school and go on to leadership positions and universities in every other Muslim country in the world. Sharif believes that **“it's important that their voice is heard and that their perspectives are shared with the de facto authorities in Afghanistan”** because Islam is based on brotherhood and sisterhood.
