The Youth Declaration, informed by the voices of over half a million young people from 170 countries, was a foundational document of the Transforming Education Summit. The declaration emerged from extensive consultations with young people across the globe, including those in challenging circumstances like Aileen in Lebanon who participated despite power outages and John Murray who helped collect input from over 80 young people in the Kakuma Refugee Camp in Kenya.  This document articulated young people’s demands for a transformed education system.

**Key aspects of the Youth Declaration’s significance:**

*  **It represented a global youth movement for change.** The Youth Declaration was more than just a document; it signaled the beginning of a global movement demanding the transformation of education. This mobilization illustrated the widespread dissatisfaction among young people with the state of education and their determination to be heard and included in decision-making processes.
*  **It served as a roadmap and accountability tool.** The declaration outlined the collective vision of young people for a transformed education system, providing a guide for leaders to follow. Importantly, the declaration served as an accountability mechanism, allowing young people to measure whether leaders honored their commitments. This commitment to holding leaders accountable underscored the urgency and seriousness young people attached to achieving a transformed education system.
*  **It emphasized the need for an intersectional approach.** The declaration called for a transformative approach to education based on human rights, sustainable development, gender equality, climate justice, inclusion, diversity, and solidarity. This intersectional approach recognized the interconnectedness of various challenges and the need for holistic solutions to address the complex realities facing young people.
*  **It established young people as equal partners in decision-making.** The declaration made a powerful statement by demanding that young people be acknowledged as equal partners in decision-making processes related to education. This demand challenged the traditional power dynamics in education, advocating for a more participatory and youth-led approach to shaping the future of learning.

The Youth Declaration was a **pivotal moment in the Transforming Education Summit**, marking a **shift in the conversation around education**, emphasizing the **urgent need for change**, and **positioning young people as key drivers in the transformation of education**.
