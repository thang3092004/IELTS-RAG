World leaders and young people agree that education is facing a global crisis, but their perspectives on the key challenges and solutions differ in several ways. 

**World leaders emphasize foundational learning, teacher training, and financing as key challenges, while young people focus on climate change, mental health, and inclusion as the most pressing issues.**

Here is a detailed comparison of their perspectives:

### World Leaders

* **Focus on foundational literacy and numeracy:** Leaders highlight the alarming statistic that 70% of 10-year-olds in poor countries cannot read a basic text, calling it a “learning crisis”. They emphasize the need to recover learning loss due to the pandemic and ensure all children learn foundational skills.
* **Teacher training and support are crucial:** World leaders acknowledge the importance of teachers and call for a renewed focus on their training, working conditions, and salaries.
* **Financing is a major barrier:**  Leaders stress the need for increased funding for education, urging governments to prioritize education budgets and calling on international donors to increase aid and debt relief.

### Young People

* **Climate change and education are interconnected:** Young people see climate change as a defining challenge of their generation and call for education systems to address it. They demand climate education be integrated into curricula and that schools become models of sustainability.
* **Mental health and well-being are critical:** Young people emphasize the need for education systems to support their mental health and well-being. They call for safe and inclusive learning environments free from violence, stigma, and intimidation.
* **Inclusion and equity must be prioritized:** Young people demand an intersectional approach to education that addresses the needs of all learners, regardless of gender, disability, displacement, or socioeconomic background. They call for equal access to quality education for girls and urge world leaders to lift all restrictions on girls’ education in Afghanistan.

### Common Ground

* **Education is key to achieving the SDGs:** Both groups recognize that education is fundamental to achieving all the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
* **Transformative change is needed:** Leaders and young people agree that incremental improvements are insufficient and call for bold action to transform education systems.
* **Youth engagement is essential:** Leaders and young people recognize the importance of meaningfully engaging youth in shaping the future of education. They support co-creation of solutions and acknowledge the need to go beyond tokenistic participation.

In summary, **world leaders and young people share a commitment to transforming education but have different priorities and perspectives.** Leaders tend to focus on systemic issues like financing and teacher quality, while young people highlight the need for education to respond to the specific challenges of their generation, including climate change, mental health, and inclusion.  **Both groups recognize the need for bold action and genuine youth engagement to ensure that education systems are fit for the 21st century.**
