The sources provide a few examples of the challenges young girls face in accessing education due to the climate crisis:

* **Increased workloads looking after their families**: As the effects of climate change worsen, girls are often tasked with additional domestic responsibilities, such as fetching water from increasingly distant sources, which cuts into their time for school.
* **Increased domestic violence and child marriages**: Climate change exacerbates existing inequalities, leading to a rise in domestic violence and child marriages. This severely limits girls' opportunities to access and complete their education.
* **Exposure to climate dangers and lack of infrastructure**: Many schools lack basic infrastructure, such as electricity, leaving students vulnerable to climate dangers like heat waves. This can disrupt their learning and pose risks to their health. 

The sources highlight the urgency of addressing these challenges to ensure that girls have equal opportunities to education, despite the escalating impacts of climate change.
