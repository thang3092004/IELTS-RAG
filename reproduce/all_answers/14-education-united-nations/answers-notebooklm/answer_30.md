Education is a key tool to empower girls and women, helping them reach their full potential and contribute to society. Education also helps break down harmful gender stereotypes that limit opportunities for girls and women. Here are some ways education empowers girls and women, illustrated by points made in the sources:

* **Education allows girls and women to become agents of change in their communities.**  In Ireland, the inclusion of women in the peace process led to the successful Good Friday Agreement in 1998. Women insisted that the agreement include provisions for the needs of victims, reconciliation, and equality, which have been critical to sustaining peace.
* **Education can improve the quality of life for girls and women and create pathways to decent work and leadership opportunities.** By boosting a woman’s quality of life, education can open doors to better jobs and provide women and girls with important life skills.
* **Education helps girls and women advocate for their own needs.** Many girls must choose between their education and their safety because humanitarian aid does not sufficiently address the need for quality education in emergencies. Young women are raising their voices globally, demanding change and advocating for issues like climate action, peace, human rights, equality, education, and sustainable development. 
* **Education can reduce child marriage rates, allowing girls to stay in school and reach their full potential.** In some communities, a girl’s father decides if she is permitted to attend school. Child marriage is a barrier to education and can be perpetuated by poverty, inadequate school sanitation facilities, and harmful cultural practices. 

The sources also point to the urgent need to address gender stereotypes:

* **There is an urgent need to challenge gender stereotypes that limit girls' access to STEM education.** While girls perform as well as boys in STEM subjects, social and institutional barriers discourage them from pursuing STEM careers. Despite research showing that educating girls benefits societies globally, world leaders have failed to adequately invest in girls' education.
* **Gender stereotypes regarding girls' roles in society also lead to a lack of investment in girls' education.** Girls need to be more than wives and mothers and should be able to pursue the path of their choice. Many girls leave school due to cultural expectations, early marriage, and pregnancy.

The sources also underscore that education is not just about enrollment numbers, but about providing girls and women with the tools and resources they need to succeed:

* **Access to quality education is essential for empowering girls and women.** Every girl should have access to quality education. Girls need safe learning environments where they feel encouraged and supported. Teachers should be motivated, and the curriculum should be high-quality and relevant.
* **Investing in girls’ education benefits everyone.** When girls are educated, it reduces conflict, improves public health, and promotes environmental sustainability. Educated women make communities safer, healthier, and wealthier. 
* **World leaders must take concrete action to ensure that girls have equal access to education.** World leaders need to increase their financial commitments and make girls' education a political priority. Guaranteeing 12 years of free, quality education for girls will unlock a cascade of positive change that will benefit everyone.
