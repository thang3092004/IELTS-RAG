Education Cannot Wait (ECW) receives funding from a diverse range of sources, including:

* **Member States of the United Nations:** Germany, the United Kingdom, the United States, Denmark, Norway, Canada, and the European Commission are among the top donors to ECW. Germany alone contributed almost $20-30 million in one year.

* **Private Sector:** The Lego Foundation is a major private sector contributor to ECW.

* **High-net-worth individuals:** ECW also receives funding from wealthy individuals.

ECW mobilizes resources through various channels, such as:

* **Trust fund:** The organization has a trust fund that has raised over $1.1 billion.
* **Joint programming in-country:**  ECW works with member states and other organizations to align funding for education programs, raising an additional $1 billion.
* **High-level financing conferences:**  ECW holds conferences to mobilize resources, such as the one planned for February 2023 in Geneva, co-hosted by Switzerland and other countries, with a goal of raising at least $1.5 billion. 

The sources highlight a need for increased financial investment in education, particularly for children and youth affected by conflict, emergencies, and climate-induced disasters.
