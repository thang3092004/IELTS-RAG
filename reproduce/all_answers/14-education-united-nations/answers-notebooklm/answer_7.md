Education Cannot Wait, or ECW, is a UN global fund for education in emergencies and protracted crises. ECW works with partners such as the UN Refugee Agency, UNICEF, the World Food Programme, UNESCO, Save the Children, and hundreds of local NGOs and civil society organizations to deliver quality education to children and youth. **In Afghanistan, girls face significant challenges in accessing secondary education, with the Taliban banning girls from attending secondary school in March of 2022.**  This ban has reversed progress made since the early 2000s, when girls were not allowed to attend school at all.  

Here are some specific challenges that girls in Afghanistan face in accessing education, and how Education Cannot Wait has worked to address these challenges:

*   **Taliban Ban on Secondary Education for Girls:** The most pressing challenge is the Taliban’s ban on girls attending secondary school.  This ban has resulted in millions of girls being denied access to education. ECW has engaged in dialogue with the Taliban to advocate for the rights of girls to attend secondary school.  During an all-women mission to Afghanistan, the head of Education Cannot Wait met with the minister of Education to discuss a plan to enable girls to return to school.  
*   **Cultural Barriers:** In some communities in Afghanistan, there are cultural barriers that prevent girls from attending school. ECW works with local communities to address these barriers and promote the importance of girls' education.
*   **Lack of Resources:** Many schools in Afghanistan lack basic resources, such as qualified teachers, textbooks, and safe learning environments. ECW has invested in teacher training and provided schools with learning materials to improve the quality of education. ECW emphasizes the need for "protected" schools where girls can feel safe attending.
*   **Poverty:** Poverty is a major barrier to education in Afghanistan. Many families cannot afford to send their daughters to school, as they need them to work or help with household chores. ECW has provided financial assistance to families to help them send their daughters to school.
*   **Conflict and Displacement:** Conflict and displacement have disrupted education for many children in Afghanistan. ECW has worked to provide education to children in conflict-affected areas, including by setting up temporary learning spaces and providing psychosocial support. 

Despite the many challenges that remain, ECW is committed to working to ensure that all girls in Afghanistan have the opportunity to access a quality education.  ECW continues to work in Afghanistan with partners like the UN, the Agha Khan Foundation, and the Swedish Committee for Afghanistan to implement education investments.
