The COVID-19 pandemic has had a devastating impact on literacy rates globally, exacerbating a pre-existing education crisis.  

**Even before the pandemic, global literacy rates were concerning:**  70% of 10-year-olds in poor countries were unable to read a basic text. This suggests they were either not attending school or not learning effectively while in school.

The pandemic dealt a further blow to education systems, causing widespread school closures. The disruption to education has led to **significant learning loss, particularly among vulnerable children already facing barriers to education.** Data published in July 2022 by UNESCO, UNICEF, and the World Bank reveals a stark reality:

*  The number of 10-year-old children unable to understand a simple written text has **risen from 57% before the pandemic to a staggering 70%.** 
* **In sub-Saharan Africa, this figure is even more alarming, reaching 89%.**

To address this crisis, several strategies have been proposed: 

* **Investment in foundational learning**:  A renewed focus on basic literacy and numeracy skills is critical to help children catch up on lost learning.
* **Support for teachers**: Recognizing the crucial role of teachers, increased investment in teacher training, resources, and improved working conditions are essential.
* **Bridging the digital divide**: Expanded access to technology and digital learning resources can help reach marginalized students and provide them with quality education.
* **Addressing the needs of vulnerable children**: Targeted interventions are necessary to support girls, children with disabilities, refugees, and those affected by conflict and humanitarian crises. This includes ensuring safe and inclusive learning environments.
* **Increased funding for education**: Governments and the international community must prioritize education funding, including debt relief for developing countries, to ensure adequate resources are available to implement these strategies.

**The urgency of the situation cannot be overstated. Transforming education systems to address learning loss and ensure inclusive, quality education for all children is essential, not just for individual well-being but for global progress.**.
