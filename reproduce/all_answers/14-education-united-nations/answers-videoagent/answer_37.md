Children with disabilities face several challenges in accessing quality education, including physical barriers, lack of specialized resources, inadequate teacher training, and social stigma. Physical barriers can include inaccessible school buildings and classrooms. Lack of specialized resources refers to the absence of tailored learning materials and assistive technologies. Inadequate teacher training means that educators may not be equipped to effectively teach students with disabilities. Social stigma can lead to discrimination and exclusion from educational opportunities.


To overcome these challenges, schools can implement inclusive education policies that ensure accessibility for all students. This includes modifying physical environments, providing specialized resources and assistive technologies, and training teachers to support diverse learning needs. Additionally, raising awareness and promoting acceptance within the school community can help reduce social stigma and foster an inclusive environment for children with disabilities.
