Education systems can be made more resilient to shocks and crises by implementing several strategies:


1. **Digital Infrastructure**: Invest in robust digital infrastructure to facilitate online learning. This includes providing access to devices and reliable internet for students and teachers.


2. **Flexible Curriculum**: Develop a flexible curriculum that can be adapted to different learning environments, whether in-person or remote.


3. **Teacher Training**: Provide teachers with training in digital tools and remote teaching methodologies to ensure they can effectively deliver education in any situation.


4. **Emergency Planning**: Establish clear emergency plans and protocols to quickly transition between different modes of learning during crises.


5. **Mental Health Support**: Incorporate mental health support for students and staff to help them cope with the stress and anxiety that may arise during crises.


6. **Community Engagement**: Engage with the community to ensure that education systems are responsive to local needs and challenges.


7. **Equity and Inclusion**: Focus on equity and inclusion to ensure that all students, especially those from marginalized communities, have access to quality education during crises.


8. **Partnerships**: Build partnerships with other educational institutions, NGOs, and government bodies to share resources and best practices.


By implementing these strategies, education systems can become more adaptable and better prepared to handle future shocks and crises
