The COVID-19 pandemic has significantly impacted literacy rates globally by disrupting education systems, leading to school closures and a shift to remote learning. This has exacerbated existing educational inequalities, particularly affecting students in low-income regions with limited access to digital resources. Strategies proposed to address learning loss include implementing targeted remedial programs, increasing access to digital learning tools, providing teacher training for effective remote instruction, and prioritizing the reopening of schools with safety measures in place. Additionally, there is a focus on supporting vulnerable populations to ensure equitable access to education.
