The speakers at the event highlighted the significant role that the Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC) can play in promoting education, particularly in the context of girls' and women's education in Muslim countries. Here are the key points articulated regarding the OIC's involvement:

### Advocacy for Educational Rights

1. **Educational Access for All**: It was emphasized that education is a fundamental right for every adolescent girl, and the OIC is seen as a potential advocate for ensuring that this right is upheld across its member states. The organization can work to influence policies that support educational access for girls, particularly in regions where barriers exist.

2. **Promoting Core Islamic Values**: The speakers noted that many Muslim countries are seeking to re-engage with the core teachings of Islam, which advocate for education ("Read" being the first instruction in the Quran). The OIC can champion initiatives that echo these values, promoting a narrative that aligns educational rights with Islamic teachings.

### Collaboration with Member States

3. **Collaboration with Governments**: The OIC has the potential to engage with member states to foster dialogue and collaboration on educational initiatives. This includes involving various stakeholders in discussions about best practices and common challenges faced in education.

4. **Influencing Educational Policies**: By uniting the voices of its member states, the OIC can exert influence over educational policies, encouraging governments to prioritize education, particularly for female students who may face systemic restrictions.

### Supporting Sustainable Development Goals

5. **Contribution to Global Goals**: The OIC's role extends to aligning educational efforts with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). This connection underscores the organization's commitment to global standards for education, which encompass quality and equitable education for all, a point reiterated throughout various speeches.

### Fostering International Dialogue

6. **Creating Platforms for Discussion**: By organizing conferences and forums, the OIC can provide a platform for discussing educational challenges and innovations specific to member states. This can also help visualize success stories and best practices in education that can inspire action across the board.

### Conclusion

In summary, the speakers recognized the OIC's unique position in advocating for educational rights and access, particularly for girls, within the framework of Islamic values. Through collaboration, policy influence, and alignment with global educational standards, the OIC can contribute significantly to advancing education as a foundational pillar for development in member countries.
