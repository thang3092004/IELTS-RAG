### The Crisis in Foundational Learning: A Global Perspective

The crisis in foundational learning is a pressing issue affecting education systems across the globe. Many children, particularly in impoverished or crisis-stricken regions, are unable to acquire basic literacy and numeracy skills, which are essential for their overall development and future opportunities. Reports indicate that approximately 70% of 10-year-olds in low-income countries are unable to read a basic text, a statistic that underscores the severity of this crisis (Transcript: 0.00s -> 13.00s). This situation is further exacerbated by systemic inequalities, which often see poorer populations and marginalized groups, such as refugees and girls, facing the highest barriers to quality education.

The ongoing impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic have amplified these issues. School closures and shifts to remote learning created significant learning disruptions, particularly for those already at risk of falling behind (Transcript: 18.28s -> 25.80s). Furthermore, education systems in many developed nations have in some cases perpetuated rather than alleviated these disparities, reinforcing existing inequalities across generations (Transcript: 13.00s -> 23.00s).

### Addressing the Crisis: Necessary Steps

To effectively tackle the crisis in foundational learning, several strategic approaches can be implemented:

1. **Investment in Quality Education**:
   Ensuring that every child has access to quality education must be a priority. This includes investing in teacher training to elevate their roles as facilitators of learning rather than mere transmitters of information (Transcript: 19.54s -> 26.18s). Additionally, adequate resources must be provided to schools, particularly in low-income areas.

2. **Support for Marginalized Groups**:
   Special attention should be given to marginalized communities, including girls and children in humanitarian crises. Efforts to lift restrictions on education access, especially in crises like those seen in Afghanistan, are crucial (Transcript: 25.76s -> 29.94s).

3. **Safety and Well-being in Schools**:
   Creating a safe and conducive learning environment is vital. Schools should be established as safe havens where children can learn free from violence, stigma, or intimidation, thereby encouraging attendance and participation (Transcript: 20.18s -> 26.28s).

4. **Focus on Early Intervention and Foundational Skills**:
   Education systems should place a stronger emphasis on early intervention programs that focus on foundational learning (Transcript: 12.72s -> 18.94s). Facilitating an environment where children receive the support they need early on can greatly reduce the deficit in learning outcomes.

5. **Leveraging Technology**:
   Adapting to new educational technologies can transform learning experiences, especially in remote or underserved areas (Transcript: 8.00s -> 17.00s). Digital tools and platforms can bridge gaps in education access, offering innovative solutions for both teaching and learning.

6. **Global Cooperation and Policy Commitment**:
   International collaboration among nations and organizations is essential to establish a shared framework for educational improvement. Policies must be geared towards comprehensive reforms that prioritize learning outcomes and equitable access for all children (Transcript: 0.00s -> 5.66s).

### Conclusion

The crisis in foundational learning is not merely an educational issue but one that impacts economic development and social equity worldwide. Addressing it requires a multi-faceted approach that encompasses investment in education quality, support for marginalized populations, creating safe learning environments, and leveraging technological advancements. The collective engagement of governments, educational institutions, and communities is necessary to create lasting change and improve outcomes for future generations.
