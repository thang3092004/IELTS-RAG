The Giga initiative is an effort to bridge the digital divide in education and provide high-speed broadband internet to every school, classroom, and student around the world. It is led by UNESCO and aims to ensure that all students have access to the internet and digital resources necessary to achieve quality education. The initiative is part of the UN's Sustainable Development Goal 4, which aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education for all. The Giga initiative aims to achieve this by providing high-speed broadband internet to schools, classrooms, and students in underserved areas. It also aims to ensure that all students have access to the resources necessary to learn 21st century skills and knowledge. The initiative is working with governments, private companies, and civil society organizations to achieve this goal. The goal is to ensure that all students, regardless of their background, have access to the same resources and opportunities in education. The initiative is part of the UN's efforts to make education inclusive and equitable.
