Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and grassroots organizations can effectively reach marginalized populations with agendas like the SDGs by working closely with local organizations and governments to identify their needs and priorities. In the video, Malala Yousafzai, the founder of the Malala Fund, emphasizes the importance of empowering local communities to take the lead in creating change. She advocates for a bottom-up approach that involves working with local organizations to identify the needs of their communities. NGOs can also provide resources, such as funding and training, to help local organizations implement their priorities. Additionally, NGOs can help amplify the voices of marginalized groups, such as girls and women, to ensure their perspectives are heard. By working with local organizations, NGOs can ensure that the resources and knowledge provided are tailored to the specific needs of the communities they are trying to reach. This bottom-up approach can help ensure that the resources and knowledge provided are effective in empowering marginalized populations.
