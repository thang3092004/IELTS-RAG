Funding for 'Education Cannot Wait' comes from a variety of sources, including the United Nations, governments, international organizations, private sector companies, and individuals. The report mentions that the United Nations and its partners have provided $1.5 billion for the fund since its launch in 2016. In 2020, the fund's total contributions reached $1.3 billion. Private sector companies such as Google, Facebook, and Microsoft have also provided $437 million. Individuals have also contributed over $350 million to the fund. Additionally, the report states that the fund has received over $45 million in cash contributions from governments, and over $100 million in in-kind contributions. This shows the importance of various sources of funding for the fund to be able to provide quality education to millions of children in crisis situations.
