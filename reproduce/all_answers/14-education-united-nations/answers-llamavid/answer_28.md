Teachers in conflict-affected areas face numerous challenges that can hinder their ability to provide quality education. Some of the major challenges include:

1. Insecurity - Teachers in conflict-affected areas often live under the threat of violence, which can make it difficult for them to plan and deliver lessons. 

2. Displacement - Displacement of students and teachers due to conflict can disrupt education. This makes it difficult to establish stable learning environments.

3. Infrastructure - Damage to schools and other facilities can make it difficult for teachers to teach effectively.

4. Lack of resources - Teachers often lack the resources needed to provide quality education, such as textbooks, computers, and teacher training.

5. Teacher training - Teachers may not have the opportunity to receive proper teacher training.

To address these challenges, the following strategies can be implemented:

1. Provide teacher training to help them improve their skills.

2. Ensure schools have the resources they need, such as computers, textbooks, and teacher training.

3. Establish stable learning environments by rebuilding damaged schools and providing the resources needed.

4. Invest in teacher training to help them provide quality education.

5. Support the re-establishment of education in conflict-affected areas to provide children and youth with an education.
