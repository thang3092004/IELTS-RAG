The 'Youth Declaration' is a document that was developed as part of the Transforming Education Summit to highlight the importance of education and to provide guidance for the implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals. The declaration was drafted by the youth representatives and signed by the UN Secretary-General and the youth representatives. It outlines the key issues and challenges in education and emphasizes the need for transformative change in education systems to ensure that all students, particularly girls and those in humanitarian settings, have access to quality education. The declaration also highlights the importance of innovation, inclusivity, and cross-cutting priorities for education. It calls for greater investment in education, improved teacher training, and the use of digital resources to improve education outcomes. The declaration is meant to provide guidance to governments, civil society organizations, and other stakeholders to help transform education systems to ensure that all students, particularly girls and those in humanitarian settings, have access to quality education.
