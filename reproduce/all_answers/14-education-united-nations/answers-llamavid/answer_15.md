Young girls around the world are facing increasing challenges accessing education due to the climate crisis. In Pakistan, climate change is causing floods and heatwaves that are forcing girls to walk longer distances to get to school. In Sierra Leone, the increased violence and child marriages are also preventing girls from staying in school. In Yemen, girls are being forced to leave school due to the war. In India, girls are being forced to work instead of going to school. In Uganda, girls are being denied access to menstrual hygiene products and safe menstrual management. In Niger, girls are being forced to marry young due to the crisis. These are just some examples of the many ways the climate crisis is impacting education for young girls around the world.
