Transformational teaching is an approach to education that focuses on creating a safe and supportive learning environment that empowers students to take an active role in their own learning process. It is based on the idea that students are more likely to achieve their full potential when they feel valued, respected, and involved in the decision-making process. This approach is designed to promote student engagement, motivation, and self-confidence. Transformational teaching emphasizes building relationships, developing student voice, and creating an environment where students feel a sense of ownership over their education. 

Some key principles of transformational teaching include:

1. Building relationships with students. The teacher should develop a positive and supportive relationship with each student, which is crucial for student engagement.

2. Creating an environment where students feel valued and respected. The teacher should foster an inclusive and respectful atmosphere where all students feel they belong. 

3. Encouraging student voice. The teacher should create an environment where students are encouraged to speak up, share ideas, and take an active role in their learning. 

4. Providing opportunities for student ownership. The teacher should give students the opportunity to make decisions about their learning, such as choosing the topics, pace, and activities. 

5. Fostering student empowerment. The teacher should empower students by helping them develop the skills and confidence to take charge of their education and their future.

In summary, transformational teaching is an approach to education that emphasizes creating an environment where students feel valued, respected, and involved in the decision-making process, which helps to promote student engagement, motivation, and self-confidence. This empowers students to take an active role in their own learning process.
