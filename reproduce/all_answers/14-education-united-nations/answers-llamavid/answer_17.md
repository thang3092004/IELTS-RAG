Arguments for:

- Paid internships can help attract a more diverse group of interns, as those from lower income backgrounds may not be able to afford unpaid internships. This can help bring in fresh perspectives and ideas.

- Paid internships can also help ensure that interns are not taking on additional financial burdens during their internship.

- Paid internships can help raise awareness of the importance of internships within the UN, as it can show that internships are valued and not just a "free" opportunity.

Arguments against:

- Paid internships may not be feasible for all UN agencies, especially those in conflict-affected countries. 

- Paid internships may not provide the same meaningful internship experience as unpaid internships, as interns may be more focused on the money than the learning opportunity.

- Paid internships may not be as effective at building relationships and networks, as interns may be less invested in their internship.

In summary, while there are arguments for and against paid internships within the UN, the most important factors are likely the feasibility of the internship, the learning opportunity, and the interns' ability to invest in the experience.
