The United Nations has a key role in coordinating efforts to achieve Sustainable Development Goal 4 (SDG4) which aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education for all. The United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) is responsible for setting global standards and norms for education. It works closely with the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) and other UN agencies to implement the SDGs. The United Nations also has a Global Partnership for Education which brings together governments, civil society and other stakeholders to improve education worldwide. The UNESCO Director-General leads the UNESCO Secretariat and is responsible for overseeing the organization's work. The UNESCO works with governments, NGOs and other partners to develop policies and plans to improve education systems and make them more inclusive. It also helps raise awareness of the importance of education and the need for investments in education. Overall, the United Nations plays a crucial role in coordinating efforts to achieve SDG 4 by working with member states, civil society and other stakeholders to provide quality education for all.
