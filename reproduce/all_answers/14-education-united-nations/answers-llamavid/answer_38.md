Education can play a crucial role in promoting global citizenship and a sense of shared responsibility for the future of the planet. This is evident from the many speakers at the Transforming Education Summit who highlighted the importance of education in achieving the United Nations's Sustainable Development Goals. By providing children and young people with a quality education, they are empowered to make informed decisions and take action to shape a better future. They can learn about global issues and challenges, understand the importance of cooperation and shared responsibility, and develop the skills to work together to create positive change.

Education can also help break down barriers between different cultures and countries. By fostering mutual understanding and respect, education can help reduce tensions and promote peace. This is why the speakers emphasized the need for education to be inclusive, accessible and relevant to all, not just the privileged few.

In summary, education can contribute to promoting global citizenship by empowering individuals to become active participants in shaping the future and by fostering a sense of shared responsibility for the planet. It can help bridge the gaps between cultures, build cooperation and understanding, and inspire action to tackle global challenges.
