The perspectives of world leaders and young people on the key challenges facing education today are quite different. 

World leaders tend to focus on the big picture - global goals like gender equality, climate change, and innovation. They emphasize the need for political will, international cooperation, and adequate funding.

Young people, on the other hand, are more concerned with the practical issues that affect their lives - lack of quality education, inadequate teacher training, and the need for relevant skills. They prioritize access, equity, and quality over abstract ideals.

For example, world leaders like Malala Yousafzai and Ban Ki-moon emphasize the importance of education for peace, human rights, and economic progress. They see it as a way to create a brighter future.

But young people like Sonita Alizadeh and Liza Posnegh focus more on the immediate challenges they face - lack of access to quality education, inadequate teacher training, and the need for skills to succeed in life. They see education as a practical tool to help them achieve their goals.

In summary, world leaders tend to have a broader, higher-level perspective, while young people have a more immediate, bottom-up view of the issues. They prioritize practical solutions over abstract ideals.
