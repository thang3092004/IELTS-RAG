The crisis in foundational learning, particularly in reading and writing, is affecting education systems globally in a number of ways. According to the UNESCO report on education, 70% of students are not learning properly due to the crisis. This has resulted in a significant decrease in the quality of education and a worsening of learning outcomes. 

To address this, governments and other stakeholders can take steps to improve education. First, they should prioritize education in their budgets and invest more in schools and universities. They can also increase funding for teacher training to ensure that teachers have the necessary skills to teach students. 

Additionally, governments can work with international organizations like UNESCO to implement reforms to improve the quality of education. This includes improving curricula, developing better assessment tools, and increasing teacher pay. They can also make sure that students have access to digital resources and technologies to facilitate learning.

Finally, governments should value the voices of students and teachers in the decision-making process. They should engage in intergenerational dialogues and use the insights from young people to inform education policy.

In summary, by prioritizing education, investing in teacher training, improving curricula, and valuing the voices of students and teachers, governments can take steps to address the crisis in foundational learning and improve education outcomes.
