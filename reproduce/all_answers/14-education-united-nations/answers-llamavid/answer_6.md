Redistributing wealth is an important way to achieve education goals by 2030. The United Nations has set a target of providing quality education for all by 2030, and wealth redistribution is a key part of this. Redistributing wealth can help in several ways:

1. It can provide funding to governments to increase investment in education. This can help increase teacher salaries, build new schools, and provide resources like textbooks and computers. 

2. It can provide scholarships and financial aid to students from poor families so they can afford to go to school. This can help reduce the number of children who miss out due to lack of funds.

3. It can help provide education to refugees and children living in conflict areas. This can help reduce the number of children who miss out on education due to displacement.

4. It can help improve teacher training and professional development. This can help increase the quality of education and the skills of teachers.

5. It can provide mental health and psychosocial support to students who have been traumatized. This can help improve learning outcomes.

In summary, redistributing wealth can help increase access to education, improve the quality of education, and provide support for students who have been traumatized. This is an important way to achieve the UN's education goals by 2030.
