Education Cannot Wait bridges the gap between humanitarian aid and development assistance by providing a combination of both short-term and long-term solutions to ensure that children and youth in crisis have access to quality education. Humanitarian aid often focuses on providing immediate needs like food, shelter, and healthcare, while development assistance focuses on long-term solutions to build sustainable education systems. However, the two are often siloed and do not work together.

Education Cannot Wait brings together different actors to provide both immediate relief and long-term solutions. For example, it provides funding to support humanitarian aid, like providing shelter and healthcare, while also investing in education infrastructure like schools, teachers, and textbooks. This allows it to address the needs of children and youth in crisis in a comprehensive way. 

Education Cannot Wait also bridges the gap between different actors by working with governments, international organizations, and civil society. It provides funding to support the efforts of national governments, international organizations, and local communities to ensure that children and youth have access to education. This helps to build the capacity of education systems in crisis-affected contexts.

In summary, Education Cannot Wait bridges the gap between humanitarian and development assistance by providing both immediate and long-term solutions, working with different actors, and investing in education systems. This comprehensive approach ensures that children and youth in crisis have access to the education they need.
