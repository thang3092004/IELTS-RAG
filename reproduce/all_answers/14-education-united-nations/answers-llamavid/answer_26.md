Education systems can be made more resilient to shocks and crises by incorporating digital technologies and other innovative approaches. For example, during the COVID-19 pandemic, education systems have been forced to rapidly shift to remote learning, with students using laptops and smartphones to access lessons. This has allowed millions of students to continue learning, even when schools were closed. In addition, education systems can be made more resilient to climate change by incorporating climate education into curriculums, providing students with the knowledge and skills to adapt to the changing environment. This can include learning about the effects of heat waves, floods, and other climate disasters. Education systems can also be made more resilient by developing new educational ecosystems that favor cooperation, rather than competition. This includes creating partnerships between schools, governments, and non-profits, and using digital resources to support learning. Overall, education systems can be made more resilient by investing in teacher training, providing students with the resources to learn, and creating more inclusive and flexible learning environments.
