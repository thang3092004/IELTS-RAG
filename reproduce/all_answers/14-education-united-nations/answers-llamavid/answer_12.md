There are a few key actions that governments and teachers can take to address the global shortage of teachers and improve the quality of teaching:

1. Invest in teacher training programs and ensure they are up-to-date with the latest research and best practices. This can involve providing incentives for teachers to pursue further education and professional development.

2. Invest in the recruitment and retention of qualified teachers. This can include providing scholarships and incentives to attract top talent to the teaching profession. 

3. Provide teachers with the resources and materials they need to teach effectively. This can include textbooks, computers, and other teaching aids.

4. Ensure teachers are paid a fair and competitive salary. This can help attract and retain high-quality teachers.

5. Involve parents and the community in the decision-making process. This can help ensure that the needs of the community are taken into account when it comes to teacher recruitment and retention.

6. Foster a culture of continuous improvement. This can involve providing teachers with feedback and support to help them improve their teaching skills.

7. Develop a system of accountability to ensure that teachers are held accountable for student learning outcomes. This can help ensure that students are receiving a quality education.

In summary, governments and teachers can take a holistic approach to addressing the global shortage of teachers by investing in teacher training, recruitment, retention, and accountability, while also involving parents and the community in the decision-making process.
