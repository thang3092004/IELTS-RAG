### Impact of Weather Conditions

1. **Surface Conditions**: Wet or muddy track conditions can severely impact vehicle traction. In such cases, cars may struggle to maintain grip, leading to increased risks of skidding or losing control while navigating sharp turns. Drivers must adjust their driving techniques, which can create more opportunities for overtaking or, conversely, for accidents to occur.

2. **Visibility Challenges**: Rainy conditions may reduce visibility for drivers, particularly at higher speeds. This can lead to more cautious driving as competitors navigate both the track and changing conditions. Reduced visibility can also affect strategic planning and timing for overtakes, as drivers may not clearly see their opponents' actions.

3. **Strategic Choices**: Drivers often have to make key decisions about tire selection based on prevailing weather conditions. The choice between wet tires or harder treads designed for dry conditions can be crucial and can shift the competitive balance among drivers, especially if the weather changes mid-race.

4. **Increased Risks of Accidents**: Bad weather typically elevates the unpredictability of rallycross events. Collisions may increase as vehicles find it harder to negotiate corners or come into contact when attempting to outmaneuver each other in slick conditions.

5. **Driver Experience**: Different competitors may have varying levels of experience and comfort driving in challenging weather. More experienced drivers can sometimes exploit poor weather conditions to capitalize on mistakes made by less experienced ones, thereby affecting the final standings.
