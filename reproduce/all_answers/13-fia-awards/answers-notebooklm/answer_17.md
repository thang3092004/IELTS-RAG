The FIA's presence in Africa for the first time in its 120-year history is a significant milestone for the organization and for motorsports on the continent. The **FIA President, Mohammed Ben Sulayem**, highlighted the importance of Africa in the world of motorsports, stating that a proper world championship cannot exist without representation from all continents. This event marks a turning point for motorsports in Africa, and the significance can be explained through the following points: 

*   **Recognition and Inclusion:** Holding the FIA Awards in Rwanda signifies the recognition of Africa's potential in motorsports. It demonstrates the FIA's commitment to expanding the sport's reach and inclusivity.
*   **Inspiration for Future Generations:** The presence of motorsports legends and the showcasing of various racing categories will inspire young Africans to pursue careers in motorsports. Nelly Mukay from the Rwandan Ministry of Sports emphasizes that the event is a unique opportunity to showcase Rwanda to the world and inspire young people. Queen Keno, Rwanda's first female rally driver, echoes this sentiment, expressing the inspiration she felt seeing her role models and iconic cars. 
*   **Investment and Economic Growth:** The event attracts international attention and investment to Rwanda, boosting tourism and creating economic opportunities.
*   **Development of Motorsport Infrastructure:** Rwanda's bid to host a Formula 1 Grand Prix further underscores the country's ambition to become a motorsport hub. This will lead to the development of racing infrastructure, training facilities, and a skilled workforce.
*   **Grassroots Development:** The FIA's focus on grassroots development programs, such as the karting initiative in Rwanda, will make motorsports more accessible and affordable, nurturing local talent and creating a pathway for African drivers to reach the highest levels of racing. The introduction of the Cross Car program, with the first car built in Rwanda using the FIA's blueprint, significantly reduces costs and promotes accessibility.

The FIA's commitment to Africa is not merely symbolic but extends to concrete initiatives aimed at fostering motorsports development on the continent. Initiatives like the Cross Car program and talent identification initiatives are already creating opportunities for young people in Rwanda. **This historical event marks a new era for motorsports in Africa, setting the stage for a future where the continent plays a prominent role in the global motorsports landscape.**
