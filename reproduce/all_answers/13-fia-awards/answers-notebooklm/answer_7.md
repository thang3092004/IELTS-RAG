The FIA has implemented a blueprint system to reduce the cost of entry-level motorsports like karting and cross-car racing. This blueprint provides a standardized design with FIA safety standards, allowing regions to produce cars locally at a significantly reduced cost. For example, the first cross-car built using this blueprint was manufactured in Rwanda by a technical school and **cost 75% less than traditional models**. This initiative aims to make motorsports more accessible to young people by addressing the high cost of entry-level categories, which can deter aspiring drivers.
