The FIA's support for grassroots motorsports initiatives contributes to the long-term sustainability and growth of the sport by making it more accessible and affordable. The FIA president, Mohammed Ben Sulayem, emphasizes the importance of grassroots development as a pathway to the pinnacle of motorsport, Formula 1. He highlights the challenge of high costs in motorsport, particularly karting, which serves as the foundation for Formula 1.

To address this, the FIA is providing blueprints with safety standards for cross cars and karting, enabling regions to produce affordable options. This is exemplified in Rwanda, where a technical school utilized the FIA blueprint to create a cross car costing 75% less than traditional models. 

By reducing costs and increasing accessibility, the FIA aims to:

• **Expand the talent pool by enabling individuals from diverse socioeconomic backgrounds to participate in motorsport.** This aligns with the FIA's commitment to diversity, demonstrated by bringing the FIA General Assembly and prize-giving to Africa for the first time.

• ** Foster the development of future motorsport professionals, including drivers, engineers, and team personnel.**

• **Cultivate a broader fan base by engaging more people in the sport at the grassroots level.** This can lead to increased viewership, sponsorship, and overall growth of motorsport.

The FIA's initiative in Rwanda exemplifies how supporting grassroots motorsports can create a sustainable model for the sport's future.  Lowering entry barriers allows more people to participate, nurturing talent, and expanding the motorsport community. This strategy ensures the sport's continued growth and success for generations to come.
