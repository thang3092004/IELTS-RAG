The FIA has taken a multi-pronged approach to promoting diversity and inclusivity in motorsports, as evidenced by the 2024 season and awards ceremony:

**Geographic Expansion:** 

* The 2024 FIA Awards ceremony was held in Kigali, Rwanda, marking the **first time the event has been held in Africa**. 
* The president of the FIA, Mohammed Ben Sulayem, acknowledged that the FIA had been “overdue to come to Africa” and emphasized the importance of representing all continents in world championships. 
* The 2024 World Rally Raid Championship included a second round in Africa with the South Africa Rally.

**Emphasis on Grassroots Development and Affordability:** 

* President Ben Sulayem stressed the importance of grassroots development, particularly in making motorsport more affordable. 
* He highlighted the FIA's initiative to provide blueprints for cross cars and karts with safety standards, enabling regions to produce these vehicles at a lower cost. 
* **A cross car built by students at a technical school in Rwanda cost 75% less than traditional models**.
* This initiative aims to increase participation by making motorsport accessible to individuals who cannot afford the high costs associated with traditional karting. 
* The president mentioned seeing 100 young girls trying out karting in Rwanda, emphasizing the FIA's commitment to reaching a broader audience.

**Recognition of Women in Motorsports:**

* **The ceremony highlighted the achievement of Queen Keno, Rwanda’s first female rally driver**. 
* Queen Keno discussed the “growing presence of women in motorsports” and their increasing competitiveness. 
* **Clara Anderson won the FIA World Rallycross Action of the Year award**, marking a significant achievement for women in the sport. 
* **Michelle Mouton, a pioneering rally driver, was awarded the FIA Lifetime Achievement Award**, recognizing her contributions to motorsports and her role as an example for women in the field.

**Celebrating Diversity and Heritage:**

* The awards ceremony celebrated the FIA’s 120th anniversary, acknowledging the organization's heritage and evolution. 
* The FIA Founding Members Club Heritage Cup was awarded to the Autodromo Nazionale di Monza, recognizing its iconic status in motorsport history. 
* The Heritage Promotion Cup went to the Classic Cars Exhibition at Baku’s Heydar Aliyev Center, honoring the preservation of automotive history. 

These initiatives suggest that the FIA is making a concerted effort to increase diversity and inclusivity across different facets of motorsports.  The 2024 season and awards ceremony served as a platform to showcase these efforts and celebrate the contributions of individuals and organizations from diverse backgrounds.
