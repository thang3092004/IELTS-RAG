The FIA's recognition of Michelle Mouton's lifetime achievements with the **FIA Lifetime Achievement Award** highlights the evolving role of women in motorsports by showcasing her pioneering career and her contributions to safety in the sport.  

* The FIA president, Mohammed Ben Sulayem, emphasized that Mouton's 1982 World Rally Championship performance was so remarkable that "**the 1982 Champion was Michelle Mouton**". This statement underscores her exceptional talent and the impact she had on the sport.

* Mouton's driving skills, particularly in the challenging Audi Quattro A2, inspired many girls worldwide.  Her fearless approach and success in a male-dominated field paved the way for future generations of female drivers.

* Mouton's dedication extended beyond her driving career. She spent 20 years organizing the Race of Champions and 15 years contributing to the FIA's safety initiatives. Her commitment to improving safety standards benefited all motorsport participants.

By honoring Mouton, the FIA acknowledges the increasing presence and influence of women in motorsports. Her story serves as a powerful example of the **achievements women can attain in motorsports** and the valuable contributions they make to the sport's evolution.
