The emotions and pressures experienced by a rally driver, as described by Ott Tänak, are very different from those of a Formula 1 driver.

Rally driving requires constant focus, precision, and quick decision making, with little room for error. The roads are often narrow, winding, and covered in mud, gravel, and snow. The conditions can change rapidly, making it difficult to maintain control of the car. Drivers have to navigate tight corners and jumps at high speeds while keeping their eyes on the road.

Formula 1 driving, on the other hand, is more about power, speed, and aerodynamics. The cars are faster, and the tracks are wider and more predictable. The focus is on winning races, while rallying is more about endurance and survival. The pressures are more about winning points and constructors championships.

So in summary, rally driving requires a different skill set and mindset compared to Formula 1, with a greater emphasis on precision, endurance, and resilience. The emotions and pressures experienced by a rally driver are different from those of a Formula 1 driver.
