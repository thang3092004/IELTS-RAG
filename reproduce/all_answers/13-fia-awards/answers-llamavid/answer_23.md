The FIA Drivers' Commission is an organization that advocates for the interests and safety of drivers in motorsport. It is made up of drivers from different disciplines and is responsible for promoting the interests of drivers through initiatives such as safety standards, technical regulations, and fair play. The commission also works to ensure that motorsport remains accessible to a broad audience and that the sport is not dominated by a select few teams or individuals. The commission is made up of 15 drivers, with each member representing a different discipline. The commission is responsible for the development of safety standards and regulations for different disciplines, such as Formula One, World Rally, and Formula E. It also works to promote fair play and equal opportunities for all competitors. The commission also monitors the progress of motorsport to ensure that the sport remains accessible and affordable to a wide audience.
