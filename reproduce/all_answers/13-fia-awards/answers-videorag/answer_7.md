### Measures Taken by FIA to Reduce Costs in Entry-Level Motorsports

The FIA (Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile) has implemented several strategies aimed at making entry-level motorsports, including karting and cross-car racing, more accessible and affordable for new participants. Below are some key measures that have emerged from various discussions and initiatives promoted by the FIA:

1. **Affordability Initiatives**: The FIA has recognized the high costs associated with motorsport participation and has actively pursued ways to lower those expenses. This includes introducing formats that require less financial commitment, making it easier for aspiring drivers to enter the sport. The FIA's recent focus on grassroots developments indicates an ongoing commitment to increasing accessibility for newcomers to motorsport.

2. **Blueprint Sharing**: As emphasized during formal discussions, part of the FIA's strategy includes sharing blueprints and safety standards related to karting and cross-car racing across different regions. This initiative allows clubs and teams to utilize standardized designs and guidelines, reducing costs associated with development and compliance while ensuring safety.

3. **Support for Growth**: The FIA's commitment extends to providing financial support and initiatives aimed at fostering growing grassroots motorsport. Reports indicate that over $10 million has been allocated towards grassroots efforts aimed at enhancing inclusivity and supporting young drivers from various backgrounds.

4. **Safety Standards**: By improving safety while promoting affordability, the FIA has strategically positioned itself to make participation in motorsport lower in cost without sacrificing safety. The organization collaborates with local motorsport bodies to implement FIA safety standards that do not impose excessive financial burdens on participants.

5. **Diversification of Options**: The FIA continues to promote various entry points into the motorsport environment, including cross-car racing and karting, which are recognized as ideal starting levels for new competitors. By creating diverse formats and categories, they help cater to a wider audience, encouraging participation from different socio-economic backgrounds.

6. **Encouraging Local Competitions**: FIA’s criticism of the existing barriers highlights a push towards fostering local competitions and championships, allowing participants to compete at a lower cost in a familiar setting rather than facing high expenses from international competitions.

Through these measures, the FIA aims to cultivate a more inclusive and financially viable environment for aspiring motorsport enthusiasts, thereby contributing to the long-term sustainability of the sport. These actions reflect a broader commitment to not only enhancing competitiveness but also ensuring that motorsport remains accessible to a new generation of drivers.
