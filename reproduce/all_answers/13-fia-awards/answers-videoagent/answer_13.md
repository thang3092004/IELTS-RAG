In endurance racing, such as the 24 Hours of Le Mans, drivers face challenges like maintaining concentration and stamina over long periods, managing the car's mechanical wear and fuel efficiency, and dealing with changing weather and light conditions. Teamwork is crucial as multiple drivers share the car, requiring seamless driver changes and strategy coordination. In contrast, sprint races like Formula 1 focus on short, intense bursts of speed, requiring drivers to push the car to its limits for a shorter duration. The challenges include precise car handling, quick decision-making, and managing tire degradation and fuel within a limited timeframe. While endurance racing emphasizes consistency and reliability, sprint racing prioritizes speed and precision.
