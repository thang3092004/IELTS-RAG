The adoption of clean energy technologies in developing countries is influenced by a variety of social and economic factors, including cultural norms, cost, access to infrastructure, and capacity for change. Interventions can be designed to address these barriers in several ways:

1. Cultural norms: Addressing cultural norms and beliefs that may be holding back adoption of new technologies, like the belief that gas lamps are better than electric lamps. This may require targeted messaging campaigns.

2. Cost: Providing affordable financing options for the purchase of new technologies, like the LPG stoves, and providing incentives for their use. This can help overcome cost barriers. 

3. Infrastructure: Ensuring that new technologies can be integrated into existing infrastructure, like providing the necessary electricity grid. This may require investments in grid upgrades.

4. Capacity for change: Supporting the capacity for change, like providing training and resources for the communities to implement and adopt new technologies. This may require capacity building efforts.

In summary, interventions that address the social and economic factors that influence the adoption of clean energy technologies in developing countries, by tackling barriers like cultural norms, cost, access, and capacity for change, can help accelerate the adoption of these technologies.
