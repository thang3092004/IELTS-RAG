Researchers at Columbia University are working to close data gaps in air pollution monitoring through a project called "Decarbonize Africa" led by Professor Vinayagamurthy. They are developing low-cost sensors that can provide accurate air quality data to help identify sources of pollution and track trends over time. The sensors are designed to be easy to deploy and use in a variety of settings. The sensors are equipped with temperature sensors to measure PM2.5, a key indicator of air quality, as well as other environmental conditions that can impact sensing accuracy. The sensors can be used to identify sources of pollution and track trends over time. The data collected can be used to help identify sources of pollution, track trends, and develop strategies to reduce emissions. The sensors are also equipped with Bluetooth and other communication capabilities to allow the data to be transmitted to the cloud for analysis. This helps provide insights into the impacts of air pollution on health and the environment.

The researchers aim to use the data to develop better strategies to reduce emissions and improve air quality. The data collected from the sensors can be used to inform public health initiatives, policies, and energy transition plans. By closing these data gaps, the researchers hope to better understand the sources of air pollution and develop targeted solutions to address the problem.
