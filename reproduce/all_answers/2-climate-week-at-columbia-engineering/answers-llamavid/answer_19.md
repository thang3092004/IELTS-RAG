There are several benchmark datasets and metrics used to evaluate the performance of AI-based climate models. These include:

1. The Climate Model Intercomparison and Assimilation ProgrammeR (CMIP) which provides data from multiple climate models to compare their performance. 

2. The World Climate Research Programme (WCRP) Global Climate Observing System (GCOS) which provides data from global climate monitoring networks.

3. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) which provides data and assessments of climate change.

4. The Global Climate Modelling and Assessment Programme (GLAM) which provides data and model outputs to evaluate climate models.

5. The Earth System Grid (ESG) which provides a global-scale modeling framework.

These benchmarks allow researchers to compare their AI-based models to existing climate models, assess their performance, and identify areas for improvement. The metrics used include metrics like accuracy, skill, and bias.

In summary, benchmark datasets and metrics are essential tools to evaluate the performance of AI-based climate models and identify areas for improvement.
