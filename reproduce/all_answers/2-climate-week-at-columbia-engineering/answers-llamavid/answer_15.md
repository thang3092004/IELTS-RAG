The spherical tokamak design for the Type 1 energy fusion reactor is significantly different from the traditional tokamak design. Traditional tokamaks use a cylindrical shape with a toroidal magnetic field and high-field magnets at the center. However, the spherical tokamak design uses a sausage-shaped plasma chamber and only requires four high-field magnets, instead of the many hundreds of magnets used in traditional tokamaks. This design has several potential advantages. First, it is much simpler and easier to construct, requiring fewer high-field magnets. Second, the spherical tokamak is easier to scale up in size, since the magnetic field is uniform and does not need to be carefully shaped. Finally, the spherical tokamak design has the potential to achieve much higher plasma temperatures and energy output compared to traditional tokamaks. However, it also faces challenges in achieving the same level of performance and reliability as traditional tokamaks.
