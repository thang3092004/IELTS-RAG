Fusion energy commercialization faces several key challenges that are hindering its development and deployment. Some of the main challenges include:

1. Technical difficulties: Fusion requires creating a stable, high-temperature plasma and maintaining it for a long time. Developing the materials and technologies needed to achieve this is very difficult.

2. Cost: Fusion is an expensive process that requires significant investment in research and development. The cost of building and operating a fusion reactor is still high, and the cost of electricity from fusion is uncertain.

3. Safety: Fusion reactors are complex and pose safety risks. There are concerns about radiation, containment, and accidents.

4. Infrastructure: The necessary infrastructure for fusion, such as the construction of a fusion power plant, does not yet exist. The necessary facilities and workforce are not yet in place.

5. Policy: Fusion is a new and untested technology. There are no policies in place to regulate it or provide incentives for investment.

In summary, the main challenges are technical, economic, safety, regulatory, and policy issues that need to be addressed before fusion can be commercialized.
