Fusion energy is a promising but challenging technology that has been under development for decades. While there has been significant progress in the past few years, there are still a few key engineering challenges that are currently hindering its progress. 

1. High temperatures: Fusion requires temperatures in the range of 10 million degrees Celsius. Creating materials that can withstand these temperatures is extremely difficult. This is why researchers are exploring new materials and coatings to improve the performance of fusion reactors.

2. High magnetic fields: Fusion reactions require strong magnetic fields to confine the plasma. But creating and maintaining these fields is extremely difficult and requires advanced superconducting materials.

3. Troubleshooting: Even with the best engineering efforts, fusion reactors can experience failures and damage. Researchers are developing new diagnostics and tools to detect and troubleshoot problems faster and more accurately.

4. Cost: Building and operating fusion reactors is extremely expensive. Researchers are exploring new ways to reduce costs while maintaining performance.

In summary, while there is progress, there are still significant engineering challenges that need to be addressed for fusion energy to become a practical and affordable source of electricity.
