The air sensors lab at Columbia's Lamont Doherty Earth Observatory is an initiative to build a network of low-cost air quality sensors to monitor air quality around the world. The idea is to provide accurate, cost-effective data on air pollution levels to help inform decisions and policies related to air quality and public health. The lab aims to build up a network of monitoring sites around the globe, with sensors that are affordable and easy to deploy. The sensors are designed to provide real-time data on air quality, including PM2.5 levels. The data collected will be used to inform research and policy around air quality, climate change, and public health. The lab is part of Columbia's efforts to build a networked ecosystem that can promote and scale up new technologies and practices related to air quality and climate change.
