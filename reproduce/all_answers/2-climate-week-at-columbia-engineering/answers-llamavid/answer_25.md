Integrating social and economic considerations into building energy models and decarbonization plans can help ensure that the plans are effective, equitable, and widely accepted. Some potential benefits include:

1. Addressing health and equity: Decarbonization plans that do not consider the health and well-being of the most vulnerable communities may not be effective. By including social and economic factors in the models, plans can be tailored to the specific needs of these communities.

2. Ensuring affordability: Decarbonization plans that are too expensive may be difficult to implement. Incorporating economic factors can help ensure that the plans are affordable and accessible to all stakeholders.

3. Achieving buy-in from stakeholders: Decarbonization plans that do not take stakeholders' perspectives into account may not gain the necessary support to be successful. Integrating social and economic factors can help gain the backing of different stakeholders.

4. Increasing adoption: By considering social and economic factors, plans can be designed to increase the likelihood of adoption and successful implementation. This can help accelerate the transition to a low-carbon future.

In summary, integrating social and economic factors into building energy models and decarbonization plans can help ensure that the plans are both effective and equitable, while also increasing the likelihood of successful adoption.
