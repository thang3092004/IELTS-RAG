There are several techniques being explored to improve the energy efficiency of data centers, particularly in the context of growing AI workloads. 

1. Optimizing hardware: Newer hardware designs are being developed that are more energy efficient. For example, AMD and Intel have released new CPUs that are more power-efficient. 

2. Using AI to optimize energy usage: AI can be used to optimize energy usage. For example, AI can be used to determine the most efficient frequency and voltage settings for a CPU, or to determine the most efficient number of cores to use.

3. Using AI to scale up hardware: AI can be used to scale up hardware to match the workload. This can help save energy.

4. Using AI to identify underutilized hardware: AI can be used to identify hardware that is not being used as efficiently as it could be. This can help save energy.

5. Using AI to identify overutilized hardware: AI can be used to identify hardware that is being used more than necessary, which can help save energy.

In summary, AI is being used in a variety of ways to help improve the energy efficiency of data centers. The hardware itself is becoming more energy efficient, while AI is being used to optimize energy usage, scale up hardware, identify underutilized hardware, and identify overutilized hardware.
