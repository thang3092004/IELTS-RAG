AI can improve the accuracy and resolution of climate predictions at regional and local scales by utilizing machine learning algorithms to analyze large datasets from various sources, such as satellite imagery, weather stations, and climate models. AI can identify patterns and correlations that may not be apparent through traditional methods, leading to more precise predictions. Additionally, AI can enhance the downscaling of global climate models to regional and local levels, providing more detailed forecasts. AI-driven models can also continuously learn and adapt from new data, improving their predictive capabilities over time.
