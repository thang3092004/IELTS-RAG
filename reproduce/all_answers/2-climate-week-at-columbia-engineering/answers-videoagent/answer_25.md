Integrating social and economic considerations into building energy models and decarbonization plans can lead to several potential benefits. These include increased public acceptance and support for decarbonization initiatives, as they are more likely to address the needs and concerns of diverse communities. It can also lead to more equitable outcomes by ensuring that the benefits and costs of decarbonization are distributed fairly across different social groups. Additionally, considering economic factors can enhance the cost-effectiveness and financial viability of decarbonization strategies, potentially leading to more sustainable and long-term solutions. Overall, this integration can result in more comprehensive and effective energy policies that align with broader societal goals.
