Low-cost air quality sensors present both challenges and opportunities for informing policy decisions and community action.

One of the biggest **challenges** is data quality. Low-cost sensors are often less accurate and reliable than research-grade equipment. However, this can be addressed by developing correction models and calibration factors using data science methods. By comparing data from low-cost sensors to regulatory monitors, researchers can develop algorithms that improve the accuracy of low-cost sensor data.

Another **challenge** is the need for training and capacity building. Communities and policymakers need to be trained on how to use and interpret data from low-cost sensors. This includes understanding the limitations of the technology and how to use the data to inform decisions.

Despite these challenges, low-cost air quality sensors offer significant **opportunities** for improving air quality.

One of the most important **opportunities** is closing data gaps in data-sparse areas. Low-cost sensors can be deployed in areas where traditional monitoring networks are limited, providing valuable data on air pollution levels. This data can then be used to inform policy decisions and community action.

Low-cost sensors can also be used to **pinpoint specific sources of pollutants**, providing actionable information for policymakers and communities. By identifying pollution hotspots, policymakers can develop targeted interventions to reduce emissions.

Finally, low-cost sensors can be used to **engage communities in air quality monitoring**. By providing communities with access to real-time data on air pollution levels, they can be empowered to take action to protect their health.

In addition to these opportunities, low-cost sensors can play a valuable role in **ground-truthing air quality models** and providing a baseline for future monitoring efforts.

The use of low-cost air quality sensors is a rapidly evolving field with significant potential to improve air quality. By addressing the challenges and embracing the opportunities, low-cost sensors can be a powerful tool for informing policy decisions and community action.
