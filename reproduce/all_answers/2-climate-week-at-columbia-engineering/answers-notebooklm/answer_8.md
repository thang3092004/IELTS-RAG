The air sensors lab at Columbia's Lamont Doherty Earth Observatory aims to address the limitations of low-cost air quality sensors and expand their utility for research and decision-making.

The lab focuses on:

* **Evaluating low-cost sensors:** The lab compares data from various low-cost sensors against reference monitors to assess their performance. This helps in understanding the accuracy and reliability of these affordable sensors.
* **Developing correction factors:** Researchers in the lab use data science methods to create correction factors that can be applied to the raw data from low-cost sensors. This enhances the data quality, making it comparable to regulatory-grade monitors.
* **Custom sensor development:** The lab also focuses on building their own low-cost sensors to further reduce the cost and enhance accessibility to air quality monitoring.

By addressing the data quality issues associated with low-cost sensors, the air sensors lab seeks to **democratize air quality data** and provide valuable insights for research, policy decisions, and community action.
