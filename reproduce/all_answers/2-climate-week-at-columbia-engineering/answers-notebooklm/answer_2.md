Fusion is viewed as having great potential for **solving climate change and enabling the exploration of space**, though it is important to note that fusion technology is still in development.

*   One panelist at the Columbia Engineering event referred to fusion as the “**ultimate energy source**” because of its potential to provide a **firm, dense form of primary energy that can scale** without producing carbon emissions.
*   Another panelist described fusion as a way to **manufacture energy on demand, anywhere it is needed**, using a ubiquitous, abundant, and inexpensive fuel source. This would **free humanity from its reliance on geographically limited energy sources** like solar, wind, oil, and coal.

Fusion energy also has the potential to **revolutionize space travel**.

 *   If humanity ever wants to **move off the planet and explore the universe**, one panelist argued, **fusion will need to be a part of that**.

These are some of the reasons why fusion research is attracting a growing amount of interest and investment from governments, private companies, and venture capitalists.
