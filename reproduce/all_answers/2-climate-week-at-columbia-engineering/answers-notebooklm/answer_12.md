The main challenges hindering fusion energy commercialization are:

*   **Developing radiation-resistant materials:** Fusion reactions produce high-energy neutrons that can damage the materials used to build fusion reactors. Developing materials that can withstand this radiation is a major engineering challenge.
*   **Managing correlated demand on the electricity grid:** Electrifying everything without proper management could double the peak demand on the grid. If fusion power plants are added to the grid, it will be important to manage the demand so that the grid is not overloaded.
*   **High upfront costs and long development timelines:** Fusion power plants are expensive to build and require long lead times. This makes it difficult to attract private investment and to compete with other forms of energy generation that are already commercially available.
*   **Attracting and training a skilled workforce:** Fusion energy requires a highly skilled workforce, including plasma physicists, engineers, and technicians. Universities and National Labs need to train the next generation of fusion energy workers.
*   **Public perception and understanding:** While there is growing public awareness of the potential benefits of fusion energy, there is still a lack of understanding of the technology. This can lead to public opposition to fusion power plants and make it difficult to secure funding for research and development.

The sources describe a number of ways that these challenges can be addressed:

*   **Public-private partnerships:** The DOE Milestone program is an example of a public-private partnership that is helping to accelerate the development of fusion energy. The program provides funding to private companies that are developing fusion power plants.
*   **Collaboration between industry and academia:** Companies are increasingly partnering with universities to develop new technologies and to train the next generation of fusion energy workers.
*   **Increased government funding:** Governments around the world are increasing funding for fusion energy research and development. This funding is essential to support the development of new technologies and to build the infrastructure needed for commercialization.

Despite the challenges, there is growing optimism that fusion energy will eventually become a reality. Fusion energy has the potential to provide a clean, safe, and nearly limitless source of energy. With continued investment and innovation, fusion energy could one day play a major role in meeting the world's energy needs.
