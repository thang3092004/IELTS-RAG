The **spherical tokamak design**, utilized by companies like Tokamak Energy, differs from traditional tokamaks by employing a more compact, sphere-like shape. This design results in several potential advantages, including:

*   **Reduced Magnet Usage:** The spherical design necessitates fewer magnets, which translates to lower capital expenditure (CAPEX), as magnets constitute a significant portion of the overall cost.
*   **Enhanced Physics Performance:** Spherical tokamaks benefit from a phenomenon known as bootstrap current, which enhances plasma confinement and improves efficiency.
*   **Faster Development:** The smaller size and simpler design of spherical tokamaks potentially enable faster iteration and experimentation compared to larger, more complex traditional tokamaks.

It's important to note that while spherical tokamaks offer these advantages, they, like all fusion concepts, are still under development and face technical challenges.
