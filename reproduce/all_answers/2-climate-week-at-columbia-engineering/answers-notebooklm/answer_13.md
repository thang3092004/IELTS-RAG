Here is a timeline for fusion pilot plants, considering both ideal and realistic scenarios:

**Ideal Scenario (sufficient money and talent):**

*   **5 years**: Real to Fusion aims to have a single machine demonstrating full-size magnets for a power plant operational.
*   **10 years**: Real to Fusion anticipates starting construction of a pilot plant.

**Realistic Scenario (funding and talent constraints):**

*   **Late 2020s - early 2030s:**
    *   The US Department of Energy's Milestone program expects multiple companies to have construction-ready pilot plant designs. This program involves cost reimbursement for companies that meet specific milestones.
    *   Companies like Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS) and Tokamak Energy LLC plan to deploy pilot plants in this timeframe.
*   **2040s (some possibly in the late 2030s):** Other countries like Japan, Korea, Germany, and the UK are aiming for fusion power plant deployment.

**Factors Influencing Timeline:**

*   **Funding:** Increased public and private investment could significantly accelerate development.
*   **Talent:** A larger workforce trained in fusion-related fields (including engineering, materials science, law, and economics) is crucial.
*   **Technology Maturation:** Continued advancements in areas like high-temperature superconducting magnets, additive manufacturing, and quantum computing will drive progress.
*   **Partnerships:** Collaboration among industry, academia, and government agencies (like the DOE Milestone program) is vital.

**Note:** The sources primarily focus on timelines for US-based companies. International efforts may have different timelines.
