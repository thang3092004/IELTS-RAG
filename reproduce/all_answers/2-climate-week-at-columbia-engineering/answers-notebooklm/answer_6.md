The Software Carbon Intensity (SCI) standard is currently being adapted to apply specifically to AI. While the standard is not yet finalized, it could look at metrics such as the energy consumption per token generated by a large language model (LLM). However, this metric has limitations, as it does not account for potential optimizations that could allow for the same task to be accomplished with fewer tokens. The goal is to develop a standard that would enable the classification of AI as “red AI” (high carbon intensity) versus “green AI” (low carbon intensity).
