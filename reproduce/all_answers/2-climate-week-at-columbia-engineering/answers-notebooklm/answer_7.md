Researchers at Columbia University's Lamont Doherty Earth Observatory are working to close data gaps in air pollution monitoring, especially in areas with limited data availability, like the global South and underserved areas in the United States. They aim to provide accurate and cost-effective data to understand the health burden and climate change impacts of air pollution.

Here are some key approaches taken by researchers at Columbia University:

*   **Next-Generation Monitoring Projects**: They implement projects in data-sparse regions, using both traditional and novel methodologies, to collect air quality data. For example, in Accra, Ghana, researchers installed PM2.5 monitors across 18 locations and found alarmingly high levels, consistently exceeding World Health Organization guidelines.
*   **Training and Capacity Building**: They conduct scholar exchanges, certificate programs, and training workshops for individuals worldwide, empowering them to collect and analyze air quality data. This includes collaborating with environmental decision-makers and stakeholders to build local expertise.
*   **Low-Cost Air Quality Monitors**: They utilize and promote the use of low-cost air quality sensors, which are significantly more affordable than traditional equipment, to expand data collection capabilities. Recognizing the data quality limitations of these sensors, researchers develop correction models and calibration factors using data science methods to enhance accuracy. This approach makes air quality data more accessible globally.
*   **Air Sensors Lab**: Columbia University hosts an air sensors lab where various low-cost sensors are tested, compared against reference monitors, and calibrated for improved data quality. This lab also focuses on building custom PM2.5 sensors using affordable components, further democratizing air quality data collection.

These initiatives demonstrate the commitment of Columbia University researchers to address the critical issue of air pollution data gaps by combining technological innovation, capacity building, and community engagement.
