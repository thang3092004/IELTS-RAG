### Structured Workflow for Resource Optimization  
At the heart of Decision Lens is its **structured workflow**, which is pivotal for effective resource management. This workflow consists of four essential stages: **Requirements Intake**, **Mission Prioritization**, **Budgeting & Funding**, and **Resource Capacity**. Each stage plays a critical role in ensuring that resources are allocated efficiently and strategically.

1. **Requirements Intake**: This initial stage involves gathering and defining the organization's needs. By clearly articulating requirements, organizations can establish a solid foundation for subsequent decision-making processes. This clarity is crucial for aligning resources with strategic objectives.

2. **Mission Prioritization**: Following the intake of requirements, organizations must prioritize missions based on their strategic importance. Decision Lens facilitates this process by providing tools that allow stakeholders to evaluate and rank missions, ensuring that the most critical initiatives receive the necessary resources.

3. **Budgeting & Funding**: In this stage, organizations face the challenge of aligning their budgetary constraints with prioritized missions. Decision Lens offers analytical tools that help organizations assess the financial implications of their choices, enabling them to make informed decisions that balance cost and strategic value.

4. **Resource Capacity**: The final stage involves assessing the available resources and their capacity to meet the prioritized missions. Decision Lens aids organizations in identifying gaps and reallocating resources as necessary, ensuring that all missions can be adequately supported.
