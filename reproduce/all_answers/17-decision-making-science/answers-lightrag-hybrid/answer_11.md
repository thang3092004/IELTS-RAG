## Criteria for Prioritizing Environmental Cleanup Projects by the National Park Service (NPS)

The National Park Service (NPS), through its Environmental Compliance and Cleanup Division (ECCD), employs specific criteria to prioritize cleanup projects aimed at restoring and protecting the environment within national parks. This structured approach facilitates effective decision-making in managing contaminated sites and enhancing public access to these cherished landscapes. 

### Key Goals and Objectives

1. **Protect Human Health**: One of the primary criteria is ensuring the protection of public health. Cleanup projects are assessed on their ability to mitigate risks to human health caused by contamination, ensuring that park visitors and local communities are safe from potential health hazards.

2. **Enhance Access to Public Lands**: The NPS recognizes the importance of public access to natural resources. Projects are prioritized based on their potential to improve access to parks and recreational areas, making them more available for public enjoyment and educational activities.

3. **Stimulate Economic Activity**: The economic implications of cleanup efforts play a significant role. Projects are evaluated based on their potential to boost the local economy, creating jobs related to environmental restoration, tourism, and recreational activities.

4. **Reduce Impacts on Park Missions**: The criteria also incorporate the need to minimize disruptions to the core missions of the parks. This includes considering how cleanup efforts might affect wildlife, habitats, and ongoing conservation initiatives.

5. **Reduce Contamination Issues**: Projects are prioritized based on their potential to significantly decrease contamination levels in national parks. This includes evaluating the extent of contamination and the anticipated effectiveness of proposed cleanup methods.

6. **Leverage Dollars for Enhanced Collaboration**: The NPS seeks to maximize available funding through collaboration with other agencies and private stakeholders. Projects that demonstrate the ability to leverage additional resources or partnerships are likely to receive higher priority.

7. **Shift Obligations to Another Public Entity**: There is an emphasis on transferring certain responsibilities to other government entities when feasible. This entails identifying those projects that can be handled more effectively by other state or federal organizations while still meeting compliance and environmental standards.

### Conclusion

In conclusion, the prioritization of environmental cleanup projects by the NPS through the ECCD revolves around a balanced approach that weighs public health protection, access enhancement, economic stimulation, and environmental restoration. By evaluating these critical goals and employing a comprehensive assessment process, the NPS ensures that its efforts lead to sustainable outcomes that benefit both nature and society. This methodical approach not only aids in addressing contamination but also aligns with broader conservation efforts within the National Parks.
