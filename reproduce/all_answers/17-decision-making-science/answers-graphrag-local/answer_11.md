# Criteria Used by the National Park Service for Environmental Cleanup Projects

The National Park Service (NPS), through its Environmental Compliance and Cleanup Division (ECCD), employs a systematic approach to prioritize environmental cleanup projects across national parks. This prioritization is crucial for effectively addressing contaminated sites while ensuring the safety and accessibility of public lands. Below are the key criteria utilized in the evaluation and prioritization process.

## 1. Protection of Human Health and Safety

A primary goal within the ECCD's criteria centers around the protection of human health. This involves assessing the potential risks contaminants pose to park visitors and surrounding communities. Projects that significantly mitigate threats to health take precedence, ensuring that parks remain safe spaces for public enjoyment.

## 2. Public Benefit

The concept of Public Benefit is integral to the prioritization framework. Projects that demonstrate a clear benefit to the community, such as improved recreational opportunities or enhanced ecological conditions, are prioritized. The ECCD seeks to maximize the positive outcomes of cleanup initiatives so that local populations can enjoy an improved environmental experience.

## 3. Environmental Protection

Environmental protection is a guiding principle in selecting cleanup projects. The ECCD evaluates how specific projects will reduce pollution, restore natural habitats, and maintain biodiversity. This criterion ensures that ongoing environmental integrity is preserved while addressing existing hazards.

## 4. Enhancement of Access to Public Lands

Facilitating public access to national parks is a key goal for the NPS. Cleanup projects that also aim to enhance accessibility for visitors are prioritized within the framework. The focus is on creating safe and inviting environments that encourage public engagement with natural spaces.

## 5. Economic Activity Stimulation

Another important aspect of project prioritization involves stimulating economic activity. The ECCD considers how cleanup initiatives might bolster local economies, for example, through increased tourism and related services. Projects that promise to drive economic benefits while addressing contamination are marked as high-priority.

## 6. Collaboration and Leveraging Resources

The ability to leverage funding and resources through collaboration with other organizations is also a vital criterion. Projects that engage multiple stakeholders or create partnerships can receive priority, as they demonstrate the potential for broader support and sustainability in environmental cleanup efforts.

## 7. Reduction of Backlog and Instability of Contaminated Sites

Addressing sites that contribute to a backlog in cleanup efforts is crucial for effective management. The ECCD gives weight to projects that can resolve or stabilize ongoing contamination issues, preventing further deterioration and ensuring resources are efficiently allocated.

## Conclusion

The prioritization of environmental cleanup projects by the National Park Service is shaped by a multifaceted set of criteria aimed at maximizing public health, safety, and ecological integrity. By evaluating initiatives based on these criteria, the NPS not only fulfills its responsibility to conserve national parks but also enhances the welfare of communities and ecosystems surrounding these lands. Through this strategic framework, the potential for more sustainable and effective environmental management is realized, ensuring that national parks remain accessible and safe for future generations.
