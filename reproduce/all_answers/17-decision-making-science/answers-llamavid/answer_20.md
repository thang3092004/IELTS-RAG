The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured decision-making tool that can help organizations make complex resource allocation decisions, especially when dealing with emerging industries or policy changes. In the context of the state of Washington, AHP can be used to evaluate different options for allocating resources to support emerging industries or address policy changes. For example, the state's Office of Financial Management uses AHP to evaluate the potential impact of investments in areas like the marijuana industry, public transportation, or healthcare. AHP allows the state to compare and prioritize investments based on a wide range of criteria, such as the potential impact, cost, and urgency of the investment. This helps the state make informed decisions that align with its strategic goals and priorities. In summary, AHP can be a powerful tool for the state of Washington to evaluate resource allocation decisions in a structured and systematic way.
