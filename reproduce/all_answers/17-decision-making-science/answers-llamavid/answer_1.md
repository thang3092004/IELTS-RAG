The definition of a 'powerful' company has evolved significantly from the 20th century to the 21st century. In the 20th century, being powerful meant having economic, political, and military power. Companies were considered powerful if they had significant resources, influence, and capabilities. However, in the 21st century, the definition has expanded to include technological power. Companies are now considered powerful if they have the ability to develop and deploy innovative products and services that can create new markets, change customer behavior, and disrupt existing business models. Technological power is now a major determinant of a company's power. For example, companies like Google, Amazon, and Microsoft have become powerful due to their ability to collect and analyze massive amounts of data, and their ability to develop AI-driven products and services. The shift reflects the increasing importance of technology in the modern world.
