The Peloton example demonstrates how customer engagement and value creation have evolved from a product-based to a service-based model. 

In the early days, Peloton sold exercise bikes. However, Peloton quickly realized that the real value was in the service they provided - the workouts and guidance. By shifting to a service model, Peloton was able to offer a more personalized and engaging experience that customers valued. 

Today, Peloton offers a subscription-based service that includes a live and on-demand workout library, personalized coaching, and a community of like-minded individuals. This allows Peloton to create more meaningful customer relationships and value than simply selling exercise bikes. 

The shift to a service model also allows Peloton to continuously improve its offerings, since they can iterate and update the service over time. This is a key advantage over a product-based model that has a fixed lifecycle.

In summary, the Peloton example shows how shifting to a service model allows companies to create more meaningful customer relationships and value, since the service can be continuously improved and updated.
