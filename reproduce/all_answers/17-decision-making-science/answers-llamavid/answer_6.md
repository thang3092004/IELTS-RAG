The concept of 'disrupting the economics of an attack' helps organizations prioritize security investments by considering the motivations and costs of attackers. By understanding attackers' goals, resources, and costs, organizations can identify the most effective security controls to implement that will make it difficult for attackers to monetize their attacks. This helps them focus investments on the most effective ways to protect against the most common types of attacks. 

For example, if an organization knows that attackers are primarily motivated by ransomware, they can invest in technologies that make it difficult for attackers to steal and sell data. This makes it less profitable for attackers to target that organization.

So in summary, by understanding attackers' economics, organizations can prioritize investments in controls that will disrupt the economics of their attacks, making it less attractive for them to continue targeting that organization. This can help them focus investments on the most effective ways to protect against common attacks.
