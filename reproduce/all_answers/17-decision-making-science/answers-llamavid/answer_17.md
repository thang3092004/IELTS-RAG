The transition from episodic to continuous planning is changing the way organizations use tools like Decision Lens and the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP). Traditional episodic planning involved making decisions and setting priorities for each project and budget cycle. However, continuous planning enables organizations to evaluate options across the entire portfolio of projects and investments, rather than just for individual projects. This allows organizations to make more strategic trade-offs and optimize their investments. For example, Decision Lens can be used to evaluate the overall portfolio of IT investments, not just individual projects. This helps organizations identify which projects are most important and which should be prioritized. Similarly, the AHP can be used to evaluate the entire portfolio of projects and investments, not just individual projects, enabling more strategic planning. Overall, continuous planning is changing the way organizations evaluate and prioritize investments.
