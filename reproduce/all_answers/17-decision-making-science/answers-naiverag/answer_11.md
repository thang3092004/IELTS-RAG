The National Park Service (NPS) operates under a framework established by its Environmental Compliance and Cleanup Division (ECCD) to effectively prioritize and manage environmental cleanup projects across its vast portfolio of contaminated lands. The criteria used in this process focus on various goals aimed at promoting human health, environmental integrity, and economical viability while ensuring adherence to legislative mandates and organizational missions.

### Key Goals and Criteria

1. **Protecting Human Health and Safety**: This is considered the foremost priority. Projects are evaluated based on their potential to safeguard human health by reducing exposure to hazardous materials and contaminants. The overarching aim is to ensure that national parks remain safe for visitors and surrounding communities.

2. **Enhancing Public Benefit**: Projects must demonstrate their ability to provide clear, tangible benefits to the public. This includes increasing access to public lands and ensuring that remediation efforts lead to enhanced recreational and educational opportunities.

3. **Stimulating Economic Activity**: The cleanup initiatives are also assessed on their potential to promote local economies. By revitalizing contaminated sites, the NPS can contribute to economic development through tourism and local job creation.

4. **Reducing Negative Impacts on Park Missions**: Every project is reviewed for its compatibility with the NPS mission, which focuses on conservation and public enjoyment of national parks. Projects that promise to align with long-term park management goals are prioritized.

5. **Leveraging Resources for Enhanced Collaboration**: The NPS encourages collaboration with other public entities, stakeholders, and private partners. Projects that can effectively shift obligations to other parties while leveraging additional funding or resources are given a higher priority.

6. **Reducing Backlog and Stabilizing Contaminated Sites**: There is a conscious effort to tackle projects that help reduce the backlog of contaminated sites. This not only streamlines the cleanup process but also addresses areas requiring immediate attention.

### Methodological Framework

To organize and evaluate these criteria systematically, the NPS applies tools such as the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP). AHP allows decision-makers to rank various projects based on the criteria mentioned above, taking into account the unique circumstances and potential impacts of each site. By utilizing a hierarchical model, the NPS can ensure that all relevant factors are considered, leading to more informed, effective decisions.

### Conclusion

In summary, the National Park Service employs a comprehensive set of criteria centered around safeguarding human health, enhancing public benefit, supporting economic activity, and ensuring alignment with conservation goals. By employing systematic methodologies like the Analytic Hierarchy Process, the NPS effectively prioritizes environmental cleanup projects to achieve sustainable results in environmental management and restoration within the national parks.
