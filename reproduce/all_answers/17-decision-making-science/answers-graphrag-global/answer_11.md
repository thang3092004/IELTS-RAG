The National Park Service (NPS) employs a well-defined set of criteria to prioritize environmental cleanup projects across national parks. This approach is fundamentally centered on safeguarding environmental health and ensuring public safety, the key areas wherein the Environmental Compliance and Cleanup Division (ECCD) operates.

### Criteria for Prioritization

1. **Environmental Health and Public Safety**  
   The NPS prioritizes cleanup efforts based on the urgency of addressing contamination that poses immediate threats to ecological balance. Areas that exhibit critical environmental health issues are addressed first to mitigate risks to both the environment and visitors.

2. **Removal of Contaminants**  
   Central to the NPS's compliance measures is the focus on removing harmful contaminants from public lands. This enhances not only the environmental condition of these areas but also aligns with adherence to established environmental standards. Ultimately, it aims at reinforcing public access and health benefits.

3. **Facilitation of Public Access**  
   The NPS considers how cleanup initiatives will impact public access to national parks. Projects that enhance safety and enjoyment for visitors are given priority, effectively ensuring that natural areas remain accessible and usable for recreational purposes.

4. **Stakeholder Engagement**  
   Collaborative efforts involving various stakeholders are critical in shaping priorities for cleanup projects. Engaging the community and other interested parties allows the NPS to consider diverse perspectives and potential community benefits, enhancing the overall effectiveness of cleanup initiatives.

### Implications of the Criteria

The criteria employed by the NPS suggest a balanced approach that does not merely focus on environmental restoration, but also takes into account the essential role national parks play in public recreation and health. By prioritizing areas based on urgency, contamination levels, and accessibility, the NPS ensures that their efforts align with broader goals of public service and environmental integrity.

In conclusion, the NPS’s method for prioritizing environmental cleanup projects reflects a comprehensive strategy rooted in protecting public lands while ensuring the safety and enjoyment of millions who visit national parks each year.
