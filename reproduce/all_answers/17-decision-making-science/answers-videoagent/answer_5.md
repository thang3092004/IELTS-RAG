The concept of 'agility' can be interpreted differently by various stakeholders depending on their perspectives, roles, and objectives. For instance, in a business context, a manager might view agility as the ability to quickly adapt to market changes, while an IT professional might see it as the capacity to implement new technologies swiftly. In the context of AHP models, which are used for decision-making, clear terminology is crucial because it ensures that all stakeholders have a common understanding of the criteria and objectives being evaluated. This clarity helps in accurately prioritizing and making decisions that align with the overall goals of the organization or project. Without clear terminology, there is a risk of misinterpretation, leading to inconsistent or suboptimal decision-making outcomes.
