Considering post-transplant outcomes, such as patient survival rates and the avoidance of 'futile transplants', is crucial in organ allocation decisions to ensure that the limited supply of organs is used effectively and ethically. By prioritizing patients who are more likely to have successful outcomes, healthcare providers can maximize the benefits of organ transplants, improve overall survival rates, and enhance the quality of life for recipients. Avoiding 'futile transplants', where the likelihood of success is minimal, helps prevent unnecessary medical procedures and ensures that organs are allocated to patients who have a better chance of long-term survival and recovery. This approach also helps maintain public trust in the organ allocation system by demonstrating a commitment to fairness and the responsible use of medical resources.
