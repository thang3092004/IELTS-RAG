John Yokim's early experiences with computers during his time at the University of Pittsburgh played a pivotal role in shaping his career trajectory. Enrolling in 1994, he pursued a Bachelor's degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering. While this path initially set him on a more traditional engineering route, Yokim's involvement in computer labs marked a significant turning point in his academic and professional journey. 

While studying, he worked part-time in campus computer labs, starting with basic tasks such as stocking paper and managing print jobs for fellow students. This experience ignited a passion for technology, as he quickly moved from entry-level tasks to more significant responsibilities in the IT field. This opportunity allowed him to immerse himself in the world of computing and technology, planting the seeds for a long-term career in the sector.

Despite committing to an engineering degree, Yokim realized that his newfound interest in computing diverged from his original aspirations. He described this shift in passion as he engaged in internships which helped him discover that analyzing soil samples and environmental contaminants was not what he envisioned for his future. Instead, he gravitated toward opportunities in information technology. His strong interest in computers ultimately led him to accept a full-time IT position immediately after graduation, despite still having 18 credits left to complete his degree.

Over the next 18 years, Yokim developed a robust career in IT, including impressive roles such as Chief Technology Officer and, eventually, leading high-level application innovation at Microsoft. His early experiences with computers at the University of Pittsburgh not only laid the groundwork for his technical skills but also influenced his career decisions, pushing him toward a successful trajectory in the tech industry. This pathway illustrates how a foundation built on initial exposure and practical experience can steer professional development, often leading to unforeseen but rewarding outcomes.
