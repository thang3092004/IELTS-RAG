Decision Lens software, based on the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), facilitates collaboration and consensus-building among stakeholders by providing a structured framework for capturing and synthesizing diverse perspectives and priorities, enabling informed decision-making.

The software offers unique capabilities that address the challenges of modern planning, moving from episodic to continuous and integrated processes.  Here’s how Decision Lens helps build consensus:

* **Connecting Across Time Horizons:**  Decision Lens allows for continuous planning, incorporating the dynamic nature of changing environments and priorities, rather than relying on static snapshots.
* **Connecting Across Functions:** The software breaks down silos by integrating planning across various departments, ensuring alignment towards common goals. For example, in the case of the US Navy, it enables integrated planning for ships, airplanes, bases, and personnel to support the overall mission effectively.
* **Connecting Up and Down the Organizational Hierarchy:** Decision Lens connects planning efforts across different organizational levels, from the strategic objectives of top leadership to the operational requirements at the ground level. This ensures alignment and support for organizational goals across all levels.
* **Facilitating Input from Diverse Stakeholders:** The software allows multiple stakeholders to weigh in on various factors and criteria relevant to the decision-making process. This can be observed in the example of IT portfolio management, where technical and business stakeholders provide input based on their specific expertise.

The software's ability to incorporate diverse perspectives and facilitate real-time adjustments ensures that planning remains relevant and responsive to changing circumstances, ultimately promoting consensus and informed decision-making.
