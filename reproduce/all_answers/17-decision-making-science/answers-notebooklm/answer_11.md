The National Park Service (NPS) uses the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) and decision lens software to prioritize environmental cleanup projects across over 3,000 contaminated sites in national parks. The criteria they use include: 

* **Does the project protect people?**
* **Does the project provide a public benefit?** 
* **Does the project protect the environment?** 
* **Does the project help reduce backlog and instability of contaminated sites?** 
* **Does the project leverage dollars and enhance collaboration?**

The “leverage dollars and enhance collaboration” criterion is further broken down into sub-criteria that consider whether a project: 

*  **Shifts the obligation to another public entity**, such as the Environmental Protection Agency.
*  **Shifts the obligation to a private party**, such as a mining company that was previously responsible for the contaminated site.
*  **Optimizes collaboration with stakeholders**.

The NPS aims to **optimize their portfolio to get the most out of their resources**, shifting obligations to other parties when possible to free up resources to address other contaminated sites.
