Decision Lens software uses the **Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP)** to help organizations determine the strategic value of different investment options. The software then **factors in the cost** of each option and runs **millions of permutations** to identify the combination of investments that yields the **highest strategic value** while staying within a given budget.

For example, the United Network for Organ Sharing (UNOS) uses Decision Lens to allocate organs to patients in need. Using AHP, they developed a model that considers factors like medical urgency, post-transplant outcomes, and equity in allocating organs. This model allowed them to **reduce the number of deaths** while waiting for a transplant by over 50%.

Another example is the National Park Service, which uses Decision Lens to prioritize cleanup projects at over 3,000 contaminated sites across national parks. Using AHP, they developed a model to determine which cleanup projects would provide the most value, considering factors such as protection of people and the environment, public benefit, and cost-effectiveness. This approach allowed them to **move away from spreading limited resources too thinly** and instead **focus on high-value projects.**

In summary, Decision Lens enables organizations to optimize resource allocation by:

*   Using AHP to determine the strategic value of different investment options.
*   Factoring in the cost of each option.
*   Running simulations to identify the best mix of investments that maximizes strategic value while staying within budget constraints.

This approach allows organizations to make **more informed and strategic decisions** about resource allocation, leading to **better outcomes.**
