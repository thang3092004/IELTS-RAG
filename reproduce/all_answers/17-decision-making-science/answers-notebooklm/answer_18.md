The conflict in Ukraine is a major geopolitical event that has caused many organizations, including the U.S. Navy, to reconsider their planning priorities and resource allocation.  The **need to plan in a dynamic, rapidly changing environment** means organizations must constantly make trade-offs in their portfolios and may even need to completely reframe the question they are trying to answer. For the U.S. Navy, this means **shifting priorities and resources around a whole variety of things** including: 

*  Ships 
* Airplanes 
* Bases 
* Personnel

Decision Lens software, which utilizes the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP), has been used to help organizations, including the U.S. Navy, **integrate the needs of different asset areas and plan across all levels of the organizational hierarchy**. This software **connects planning across time horizons, functions, and organizational levels**, allowing for the creation of a "movie" rather than a single snapshot of the planning process.  The **highest levels of the Navy are concerned with strategic influence in different theaters, while the lowest levels focus on keeping the lights on at their bases.** Decision Lens and AHP help **connect these two disparate levels and make sure that what is being done at the lowest levels is supporting the strategic objectives at the highest levels.**
