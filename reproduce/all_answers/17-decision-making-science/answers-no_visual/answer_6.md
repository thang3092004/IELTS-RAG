### Understanding the Economics of Cyber Attacks

The economic impact of cybersecurity threats extends beyond immediate financial losses. Organizations must consider the **direct costs** associated with breaches, such as remediation expenses, legal fees, and potential fines, as well as **indirect costs** like reputational damage and loss of customer trust. The presentation slide titled **"Cost of Attack Examples"** illustrates the relationship between the likelihood of different types of cyber attacks and their associated costs, providing a visual representation that aids organizations in assessing risk. This understanding allows organizations to prioritize threats based on their potential economic impact, leading to more informed decision-making regarding security investments.

### Prioritizing Security Investments

By leveraging the concept of disrupting the economics of an attack, organizations can evaluate the **likelihood and cost** of various cyber threats. This evaluation is crucial for determining which security measures will be most effective in mitigating risks.
